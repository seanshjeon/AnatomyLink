# Urinary System

> The urinary system produces, stores, and excretes **urine** — filtering ~180 L of plasma per day to produce ~1.5 L of urine. It is essential for fluid balance, electrolyte homeostasis, acid-base regulation, and blood pressure control.

## 🔑 Urine Formation Summary
1. **Filtration** — glomerulus filters 125 mL/min (GFR)
2. **Reabsorption** — PCT reabsorbs glucose, amino acids, Na⁺, water (~67%)
3. **Concentration** — Loop of Henle + collecting duct (ADH-regulated)
4. **Secretion** — H⁺, K⁺, drugs into tubular fluid

## 🏥 Key Clinical Conditions
- **UTI** — *E. coli* most common; ascending infection: cystitis → pyelonephritis
- **Nephrolithiasis** — calcium oxalate (most common); ureteric colic (loin to groin)
- **AKI / CKD** — acute vs. chronic kidney injury; KDIGO criteria
- **Incontinence** — stress (weak sphincter), urge (detrusor overactivity), overflow
- **Bladder cancer** — transitional cell carcinoma; painless haematuria
