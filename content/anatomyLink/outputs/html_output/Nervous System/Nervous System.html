Nervous System

The nervous system is the body's master communication and control network, divided into the Central Nervous System (CNS) and Peripheral Nervous System (PNS), with the Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) as a functional subdivision of the PNS.

🔑 Functional Overview

Central Nervous System (CNS)

The CNS comprises the Brain and Spinal Cord. It processes all incoming sensory information, integrates it, and generates motor commands. Protected by bone (skull and vertebral column), meninges (dura, arachnoid, pia mater), and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF).

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

Everything outside the CNS. Includes Cranial Nerves (12 pairs), spinal nerves (31 pairs organized into plexuses), and the ANS. Carries signals to and from the CNS.

Autonomic Nervous System (ANS)

Controls involuntary visceral functions. Sympathetic ("fight or flight") uses thoracolumbar outflow; parasympathetic ("rest and digest") uses craniosacral outflow.

🗺️ Subsections

Central Nervous System

- Brain — cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem - Spinal Cord — segments, tracts, reflex arcs

Peripheral Nervous System

- Cranial Nerves — 12 pairs from the brain - Brachial Plexus — C5–T1, upper limb innervation - Lumbar Plexus — L1–L4, anterior lower limb - Sacral Plexus — L4–S3, posterior lower limb and pelvis

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) lesion — spasticity, hyperreflexia, upgoing Babinski; lesion in CNS (brain or spinal cord) - Lower Motor Neuron (LMN) lesion — flaccidity, fasciculations, hyporeflexia, muscle atrophy; lesion in PNS (nerve roots, peripheral nerves) - Dermatome mapping — used to localize spinal cord or nerve root lesions - Autonomic dysreflexia — dangerous hypertensive response in high spinal cord injury (≥T6)

🧠 Central Nervous System — Detailed Structures

- Brain — Cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem - Brainstem — Midbrain, pons, medulla - Cerebellum — Coordination and motor learning - Basal Ganglia — Movement regulation circuits - Limbic System — Emotion, memory, hypothalamus - Spinal Cord — Sensory + motor relay - Meninges — Dura, arachnoid, pia + meningeal spaces - Ventricular System — CSF production and flow

🔀 Pathways

- Motor Pathway — Corticospinal tract; UMN vs LMN - Sensory Pathway — Dorsal columns + spinothalamic tract - Visual Pathway — Retina → LGN → V1; field defects - Subcortical Fiber Tracts — Internal capsule, corpus callosum

🤖 Autonomic Nervous System

- Autonomic Nervous System — Sympathetic + parasympathetic
