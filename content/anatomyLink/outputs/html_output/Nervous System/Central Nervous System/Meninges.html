Meninges

The meninges are three protective membranes surrounding the Brain and Spinal Cord: dura mater (outer), arachnoid mater (middle), and pia mater (inner). Between them are spaces that can fill with blood or infection.

🔗 Key Connections

- Brain — surrounds the entire brain - Skull — dura attaches to inner skull surface - Spinal Cord — meninges extend down the vertebral canal to S2 - Cranial Nerves — CN V innervates the dura (pain-sensitive)

🗂️ Three Layers

1. Dura Mater ("Tough Mother")

- Structure: Thick, fibrous, two layers (periosteal + meningeal) - Periosteal layer — adheres to inner skull (forms endosteum) - Meningeal layer — forms dural folds (reflections) - Innervation — CN V, C1–C3 (PAIN-SENSITIVE → headaches) - Blood supply — Middle meningeal artery (branch of maxillary a.) runs in epidural space

#### Dural Folds (Reflections)

Fold Location Function
Falx cerebri Midline; separates cerebral hemispheres Anchors brain; limits side-to-side shift
Tentorium cerebelli Horizontal; separates cerebrum from cerebellum Divides cranial vault into supra/infratentorial
Falx cerebelli Between cerebellar hemispheres Minor
Diaphragma sellae Covers sella turcica Roof of pituitary fossa

#### Dural Venous Sinuses

Sinus Location Drains to
Superior sagittal sinus Along falx cerebri (upper) Confluence → transverse
Inferior sagittal sinus Along falx cerebri (lower) Straight sinus
Straight sinus Falx + tentorium junction Confluence
Transverse sinus Along tentorium (bilateral) Sigmoid sinus
Sigmoid sinus S-shaped → jugular foramen Internal jugular vein
Cavernous sinus Beside sella CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI + ICA pass through

2. Arachnoid Mater

- Structure: Thin, avascular, spider-web-like trabeculae - Subdural space — potential space between dura and arachnoid (no fluid normally) - Subarachnoid space — between arachnoid and pia; filled with CSF - Arachnoid granulations — protrusions into dural sinuses; CSF reabsorption sites

3. Pia Mater ("Tender Mother")

- Structure: Delicate, closely adherent to brain/cord surface; follows all gyri and sulci - Vascular: Contains blood vessels supplying brain surface - Filum terminale — pia extension anchoring spinal cord to coccyx

🔑 Meningeal Spaces

Space Location Normal Contents Pathology
Epidural Between skull and periosteal dura Fat (spine); arterial vessels Epidural hematoma (middle meningeal a.)
Subdural Between dura and arachnoid Bridging veins Subdural hematoma (bridging veins)
Subarachnoid Between arachnoid and pia CSF SAH (berry aneurysm rupture); meningitis

🏥 Clinical Significance

Condition Space Cause CT Appearance
Epidural hematoma Epidural Middle meningeal artery rupture (temporal bone fracture) Biconvex (lens-shaped); doesn't cross sutures
Subdural hematoma Subdural Bridging vein rupture (trauma, elderly) Crescent-shaped; crosses sutures
Subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH) Subarachnoid Berry aneurysm rupture (most common) Blood in cisterns/sulci; "thunderclap" headache
Bacterial meningitis Subarachnoid N. meningitidis, S. pneumoniae Cloudy CSF; ↑WBC (PMN), ↑protein, ↓glucose
Viral meningitis Subarachnoid Enteroviruses most common Clear CSF; ↑lymphocytes; normal glucose
