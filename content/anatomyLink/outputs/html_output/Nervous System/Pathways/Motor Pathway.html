Motor Pathway (Corticospinal Tract)

The motor pathway transmits voluntary movement commands from the motor cortex down through the internal capsule, brainstem, and spinal cord to the lower motor neurons that innervate muscles. Lesions above the decussation cause contralateral deficits.

🔗 Key Connections

- Brain — origin in primary motor cortex (precentral gyrus) - Subcortical Fiber Tracts — passes through corona radiata and internal capsule - Brainstem — corticobulbar fibers branch off; pyramidal decussation in medulla - Spinal Cord — lateral corticospinal tract; synapse on anterior horn (LMN) - Cranial Nerves — corticobulbar fibers supply CN motor nuclei

🗂️ The Pathway

` Upper Motor Neuron (UMN): Primary Motor Cortex (Precentral gyrus, Brodmann area 4) ↓ Corona radiata ↓ Posterior limb of internal capsule (face → arm → leg, anterior→posterior) ↓ Cerebral peduncle (midbrain) ↓ Corticobulbar fibers branch → CN motor nuclei (bilateral, except lower face + tongue) ↓ Pyramids (medulla) ↓ Pyramidal decussation — 85–90% cross → Lateral corticospinal tract — 10–15% uncrossed → Anterior corticospinal tract ↓ Spinal cord Lower Motor Neuron (LMN): Anterior horn cells → Ventral root → Peripheral nerve → NMJ → Muscle `

🔑 UMN vs LMN Lesion Signs

Feature UMN Lesion LMN Lesion
Tone Increased (spasticity) Decreased (flaccidity)
Reflexes Hyperreflexia Hyporeflexia / areflexia
Babinski Positive (extensor plantar) Negative
Weakness Yes (less atrophy) Yes (severe atrophy)
Fasciculations Absent Present
Clonus May be present Absent
Location Brain, spinal cord Anterior horn, nerve root, peripheral nerve

🔑 Cortical Homunculus (Motor)

Somatotopic representation along precentral gyrus (medial → lateral): Leg → Trunk → Arm → Hand → Face (leg on medial wall, face lateral) - Large hand + face representation (fine motor = more cortical area) - Paracentral lobule (medial): leg representation → affected by parasagittal lesions (e.g., falx meningioma)

🔑 Corticobulbar Tract (Cranial Nerve Motor Control)

Most cranial nerve motor nuclei receive bilateral cortical input (protected): - Exception 1: Lower facial nucleus (CN VII) — only contralateral UMN input → UMN lesion spares forehead - Exception 2: Hypoglossal nucleus (CN XII) — mainly contralateral → UMN lesion deviates tongue away from lesion

🏥 Clinical Significance

Condition Lesion Site Pattern
Stroke (MCA) Internal capsule / corona radiata Contralateral hemiplegia (face + arm > leg)
Stroke (ACA) Paracentral lobule Contralateral leg weakness (arm/face spared)
Spinal cord injury Lateral corticospinal tract Ipsilateral weakness below lesion (already crossed)
ALS UMN + LMN both Mixed UMN + LMN signs; progressive
Bell's palsy LMN CN VII Entire ipsilateral face weak (including forehead)
UMN CN VII lesion Contralateral cortex Lower face weak; forehead spared (bilateral cortical input to upper face)
Brown-Séquard Lateral cord hemisection Ipsilateral UMN weakness + proprioception loss; contralateral pain/temp loss
