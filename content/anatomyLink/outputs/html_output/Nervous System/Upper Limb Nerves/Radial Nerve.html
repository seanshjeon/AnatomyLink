Radial Nerve

The radial nerve (C5–C8, T1) is the largest branch of the Brachial Plexus, arising from the posterior cord and innervating all extensor muscles of the arm and forearm; its hallmark injury is wrist drop.

🔗 Origin

- Root values: C5–C8 (T1) - From plexus: Brachial Plexus - Cord: Posterior cord (largest branch)

🗂️ Key Information

Property Detail
Root Values C5–C8, T1
Plexus Origin Brachial Plexus (posterior cord)
Course Descends posterior to axillary artery → enters spiral (radial) groove of Humerus between medial and lateral heads of Triceps Brachii → pierces lateral intermuscular septum → enters cubital fossa → divides into deep (posterior interosseous nerve, PIN) and superficial branches at the level of the lateral epicondyle
Sensory Territory Posterior arm, posterior forearm, anatomical snuffbox, dorsal hand (lateral 3.5 digits — proximal phalanges only)
Terminal Branches Deep branch (PIN) — motor to posterior forearm; Superficial branch — sensory to dorsal hand

💪 Muscles Innervated

- Triceps Brachii — elbow extension (innervated above the spiral groove; usually spared in mid-shaft fractures) - Brachioradialis — elbow flexion in mid-prone position - Extensor carpi radialis longus (ECRL) — wrist extension + radial deviation - Extensor carpi radialis brevis (ECRB) — wrist extension - Supinator — forearm supination - Extensor digitorum — finger extension at MCP joints - Extensor digiti minimi — little finger extension - Extensor carpi ulnaris (ECU) — wrist extension + ulnar deviation - Abductor pollicis longus (APL) — thumb abduction - Extensor pollicis longus (EPL) — thumb IP extension - Extensor pollicis brevis (EPB) — thumb MCP extension - Extensor indicis — index finger extension

🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships

- Winds around the spiral groove (radial groove) on the posterior aspect of the Humerus between the medial and lateral heads of Triceps Brachii - Pierces the lateral intermuscular septum ~10 cm above the lateral epicondyle - Enters the cubital fossa anterior to the lateral epicondyle of the Humerus - Deep branch (PIN) winds around the neck of the Radius through the supinator muscle (arcade of Frohse) - Superficial branch travels under brachioradialis along the lateral forearm

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Wrist drop — loss of wrist and finger extension; hallmark of radial nerve injury at the spiral groove - Cause: mid-humeral shaft fracture, "Saturday night palsy" (compression against hard surface during intoxicated sleep), "honeymoon palsy" (arm over partner's shoulder) - Motor deficit: loss of wrist extension, finger extension at MCP joints, thumb extension/abduction - Sensory deficit: dorsal hand (lateral 3.5 digits) — often mild clinically - Triceps usually SPARED (innervated proximal to the spiral groove) - Posterior interosseous nerve (PIN) palsy — compression at arcade of Frohse (fibrous edge of supinator) - Motor deficit: finger drop (MCP extension lost) but wrist extension preserved (ECRL spared) - No sensory loss (PIN is purely motor) - Causes: radial head dislocation, rheumatoid arthritis, lipoma - Superficial radial nerve entrapment (Wartenberg's syndrome) — sensory loss only over dorsum of hand; caused by tight watch/handcuff - Key test: Brachioradialis reflex (C5–C6); wrist extension testing
