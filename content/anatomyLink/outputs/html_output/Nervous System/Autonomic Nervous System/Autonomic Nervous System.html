Autonomic Nervous System (ANS)

The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions — heart rate, blood pressure, digestion, gland secretion, and pupil size. It has two main divisions: sympathetic ("fight or flight") and parasympathetic ("rest and digest"), plus the enteric NS (gut).

🔗 Key Connections

- Brain — hypothalamus is the master ANS controller - Spinal Cord — preganglionic neurons located in lateral horn (T1–L2 sympathetic; S2–S4 parasympathetic) - Cranial Nerves — CN III, VII, IX, X carry parasympathetic fibers - Brainstem — parasympathetic nuclei (Edinger-Westphal, superior/inferior salivatory, dorsal motor of vagus)

🗂️ Comparison

Feature Sympathetic Parasympathetic
Origin T1–L2 (thoracolumbar) CN III/VII/IX/X + S2–S4 (craniosacral)
Preganglionic Short Long
Postganglionic Long Short
Ganglion location Paravertebral/prevertebral (far from organ) Near/within target organ
Preganglionic NT ACh (nicotinic) ACh (nicotinic)
Postganglionic NT Noradrenaline (except sweat glands → ACh) Acetylcholine (muscarinic)
Effect Fight or flight Rest and digest

🔑 Sympathetic Division

Preganglionic Neurons

- Location: Lateral horn of spinal cord T1–L2 (intermediolateral cell column) - Exit: Via ventral roots → white rami communicantes → sympathetic chain ganglia

Sympathetic Chain (Paravertebral Ganglia)

- 22 paired ganglia running alongside vertebral column - Preganglionic fibers can: synapse at same level, travel up/down chain, or pass through to prevertebral ganglia

Prevertebral (Collateral) Ganglia

Ganglion Level Supplies
Celiac T5–T9 Stomach, liver, spleen, small intestine
Superior mesenteric T9–T11 Small intestine, proximal colon
Inferior mesenteric T12–L2 Distal colon, rectum, bladder, genitalia

Adrenal Medulla

- Modified sympathetic ganglion — preganglionic fibers synapse directly on chromaffin cells - Releases adrenaline (80%) + noradrenaline (20%) into blood

Sympathetic Effects

Organ Effect
Heart ↑HR (β1), ↑contractility
Blood vessels Vasoconstriction (α1) — skin, gut, kidney
Lungs Bronchodilation (β2)
Eyes Mydriasis (dilator pupillae), lid retraction
GI tract ↓Motility, sphincter contraction
Bladder Relaxation (detrusor), sphincter contraction
Sweat glands Secretion (cholinergic!)
Adrenal medulla Adrenaline/noradrenaline release

🔑 Parasympathetic Division

Cranial Outflow (CN III, VII, IX, X)

CN Nucleus Ganglion Target
CN III Edinger-Westphal Ciliary ganglion Pupil constriction + accommodation
CN VII Superior salivatory Pterygopalatine (lacrimal, nasal) + Submandibular ganglion Lacrimal, submandibular, sublingual glands
CN IX Inferior salivatory Otic ganglion Parotid gland
CN X (vagus) Dorsal motor nucleus In/near organ ganglia Heart (↓HR), lungs (bronchoconstriction), GI (↑motility) — to splenic flexure

Sacral Outflow (S2–S4 — Pelvic Splanchnic Nerves)

- Supplies: descending colon, rectum, bladder (detrusor contraction), genitalia (erection) - "Point and Shoot": Parasympathetic → erection (Point); Sympathetic → ejaculation (Shoot)

🔑 Neurotransmitters & Receptors

Receptor Location Effect
Nicotinic (Nn) All autonomic ganglia + adrenal medulla Excitatory
Muscarinic (M1–M5) Parasympathetic effectors Variable
α1 Vascular smooth muscle Vasoconstriction
α2 Presynaptic (feedback) ↓NE release
β1 Heart, kidney ↑HR, ↑contractility, renin release
β2 Lungs, vessels Bronchodilation, vasodilation

🏥 Clinical Significance

Condition Mechanism Features
Horner's syndrome Sympathetic chain lesion (central/preganglionic/postganglionic) Ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis (ipsilateral); causes: Pancoast tumor, carotid dissection, brainstem stroke
Autonomic dysreflexia Spinal cord injury ≥T6 Sudden severe HTN, bradycardia, flushing above lesion, pallor below; triggered by bladder/bowel distension
Orthostatic hypotension ANS failure (Parkinson's, diabetes) BP drop ≥20/10 mmHg on standing; dizziness, syncope
Adie's tonic pupil Ciliary ganglion damage Large, poorly reactive pupil; slow constriction to sustained near stimulus
Hirschsprung's disease Absent enteric/parasympathetic ganglia in distal colon Neonatal bowel obstruction; failed relaxation of internal sphincter
