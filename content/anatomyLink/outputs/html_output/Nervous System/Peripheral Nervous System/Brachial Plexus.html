Brachial Plexus

The brachial plexus is the network of nerve roots C5–T1 that forms all the major nerves of the upper limb. It is organised into roots → trunks → divisions → cords → terminal branches.

🔗 Key Connections

- Root values: C5–T1 (from Spinal Cord) - Formed between: anterior and middle scalene muscles (in the neck) - Passes through: posterior triangle of neck → beneath Clavicle → axilla - Terminal branches: Axillary Nerve, Radial Nerve, Median Nerve, Ulnar Nerve, Musculocutaneous Nerve - Relation to: Subclavian Artery (runs superior), Humerus (surgical neck — axillary nerve)

🗂️ Organisation (Roots → Trunks → Divisions → Cords → Branches)

Trunks (in posterior triangle of neck)

Trunk Roots Level
Upper trunk C5 + C6 Above clavicle
Middle trunk C7 Above clavicle
Lower trunk C8 + T1 Above clavicle

Cords (named by relation to axillary artery)

Cord From Key Branches
Lateral cord Anterior divisions of upper + middle trunks Musculocutaneous Nerve, lateral root of Median Nerve
Medial cord Anterior division of lower trunk Ulnar Nerve, medial root of Median Nerve, medial cutaneous nerves
Posterior cord All posterior divisions Axillary Nerve, Radial Nerve

Terminal Branches (the 5 main nerves)

Nerve Cord Roots Key Supply
Musculocutaneous Nerve Lateral C5–C6 Biceps Brachii, brachialis; lateral forearm sensation
Axillary Nerve Posterior C5–C6 Deltoid, teres minor (Rotator Cuff); regimental badge sensation
Radial Nerve Posterior C5–C8, T1 All extensors of arm + forearm; dorsal hand sensation
Median Nerve Lateral + Medial C6–T1 Anterior forearm flexors; thenar muscles; lateral 3.5 digits
Ulnar Nerve Medial C8–T1 FCU, FDP (medial); intrinsics; medial 1.5 digits

Pre-terminal Branches (arising before the cords)

Nerve Root Supply
Dorsal scapular C5 Rhomboids, levator scapulae
Long thoracic C5–C7 Serratus anterior (winging of Scapula if damaged)
Suprascapular C5–C6 Supraspinatus + infraspinatus (Rotator Cuff)
Nerve to subclavius C5–C6 Subclavius
Medial + lateral pectoral C5–T1 Pectoralis Major + pectoralis minor
Upper + lower subscapular C5–C6 Subscapularis (Rotator Cuff), teres major
Thoracodorsal C6–C8 Latissimus Dorsi

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Erb's palsy (upper trunk — C5, C6) — most common; birth injury (shoulder dystocia) or motorcycle fall; "waiter's tip" posture (arm adducted + internally rotated, elbow extended, wrist pronated + flexed); loss of shoulder abduction, elbow flexion, forearm supination - Klumpke's palsy (lower trunk — C8, T1) — traction injury with arm forced upward; "claw hand" (intrinsic minus); thenar + hypothenar wasting; Horner's syndrome (T1 sympathetic fibres) - Thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS) — compression between clavicle + 1st rib or by cervical rib; neurogenic TOS (most common): medial forearm + hand pain + weakness; vascular TOS: Subclavian Artery or vein compression - Winged scapula — long thoracic nerve (C5–C7) injury → serratus anterior paralysis → medial Scapula border wings out on wall push-up - Brachial plexus birth injury (BPBI) — overall incidence ~1–3/1000 live births
