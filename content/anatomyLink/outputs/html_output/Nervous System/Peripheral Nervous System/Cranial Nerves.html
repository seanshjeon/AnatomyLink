Cranial Nerves

The 12 pairs of cranial nerves arise directly from the Brain (mostly brainstem) and exit through foramina of the Skull. They carry sensory (S), motor (M), or both (B) fibers.

🗂️ The 12 Cranial Nerves

# Name Type Function Key Clinical Test Deficit
I Olfactory S Smell Identify familiar odors Anosmia
II Optic S Vision Visual acuity, visual fields Blindness, visual field defects
III Oculomotor M Eye movement (SR, IR, MR, IO), pupil constriction, eyelid Pupillary light reflex; eye movements Ptosis, mydriasis, "down and out" eye
IV Trochlear M Superior oblique (SO) — down & in Eye movement testing Vertical diplopia; head tilt
V Trigeminal B Face sensation (V1/V2/V3); mastication (V3) Corneal reflex; jaw clench Facial numbness; jaw deviation
VI Abducens M Lateral rectus (LR) — abduction Lateral gaze Medial deviation (esotropia)
VII Facial B Facial expression; taste (anterior 2/3 tongue); lacrimation/salivation Facial symmetry; close eyes Bell's palsy (LMN: all face); UMN: forehead spared
VIII Vestibulocochlear S Hearing + balance Rinne & Weber tests; Romberg Deafness, vertigo, nystagmus
IX Glossopharyngeal B Taste (posterior 1/3 tongue); pharyngeal sensation; parotid gland Gag reflex Loss of gag reflex
X Vagus B Pharynx/larynx motor; parasympathetic to thorax/abdomen; taste Voice quality; palate elevation Hoarseness; palatal deviation away from lesion
XI Accessory M Sternocleidomastoid (head turn), Trapezius (shoulder shrug) Resist head turn; shrug shoulders Weak head turn; drooped shoulder
XII Hypoglossal M Tongue movements Tongue protrusion Tongue deviates toward lesion

🔑 Mnemonic

Oh Oh Oh To Touch And Feel Very Good Velvet AH (I–XII) Type: Some Say Marry Money But My Brother Says Big Brains Matter More (S/S/M/M/B/M/B/S/B/B/M/M)

🔑 Key Clinical Relationships

- CN V3 — Innervates Masseter and other muscles of mastication (jaw) - CN XI — Innervates Sternocleidomastoid and Trapezius - CN III — First to be compressed in uncal herniation → fixed dilated pupil - CN VII — Bell's palsy: LMN lesion affects entire ipsilateral face (UMN spares forehead) - CN X — Vagus nerve is the primary parasympathetic nerve to thoracic + abdominal viscera

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Bell's palsy — Idiopathic CN VII LMN palsy; unilateral facial droop including forehead - Trigeminal neuralgia (tic douloureux) — Lancinating CN V pain; treated with carbamazepine or MVD - Acoustic neuroma (vestibular schwannoma) — CN VIII tumor in internal auditory canal; tinnitus + hearing loss - Horner's syndrome — Sympathetic chain interruption (not a cranial nerve but often tested with CN III) - Jugular foramen syndrome — CN IX, X, XI compressed together (tumor, thrombosis)
