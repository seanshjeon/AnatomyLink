Pulmonary Circulation

The pulmonary circulation is the circuit carrying deoxygenated blood from the right heart to the lungs for oxygenation, and returning oxygenated blood to the left heart. It is a low-pressure, high-flow system — the only circuit where arteries carry deoxygenated blood and veins carry oxygenated blood.

🔗 Key Connections

- From: Heart (right ventricle → pulmonary trunk) - Returns to: Heart (left atrium via pulmonary veins) - Gas exchange at: pulmonary capillaries (alveolar-capillary membrane) - Bounded by: Ribs, Sternum, Thoracic Vertebrae

🗂️ Circuit Overview

Step Structure Event
1 Right ventricle Pumps deoxygenated blood
2 Pulmonary trunk Arises from RV; bifurcates into L + R pulmonary arteries
3 Left + Right pulmonary arteries Enter each lung at hilum; branch alongside bronchi
4 Pulmonary capillaries Surround alveoli; O₂ in, CO₂ out
5 Pulmonary venules → veins Collect oxygenated blood; 4 pulmonary veins
6 Left atrium Receives 4 pulmonary veins
7 Left ventricle Pumps into systemic Aorta

🗂️ Pulmonary Pressures (Normal)

Parameter Value
Pulmonary artery systolic 15–30 mmHg
Pulmonary artery diastolic 4–12 mmHg
Pulmonary artery mean (mPAP) 8–20 mmHg
Pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP) 4–12 mmHg

Compare: systemic mean arterial pressure ~90 mmHg — pulmonary circulation is ~6× lower pressure

🔑 Unique Features

- Hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction (HPV) — low alveolar O₂ → local vasoconstriction → redirects blood to better-ventilated regions (opposite to systemic vessels); essential for V/Q matching - Zone model (West zones) — lung divided into zones based on PA vs. alveolar pressure; affects V/Q ratio from apex (zone 1, high V/Q) to base (zone 3, low V/Q) - Fetal circulation — foramen ovale + ductus arteriosus bypass the lungs (not yet inflated); close at birth

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Pulmonary embolism (PE) — thrombus (usually from leg DVT) lodges in pulmonary arteries → sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, haemoptysis; massive PE → obstructive shock + right heart strain - Diagnosis: CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA); ECG: sinus tachycardia, S1Q3T3 pattern - Treatment: anticoagulation; thrombolysis or embolectomy for massive PE - Pulmonary hypertension (PH) — mPAP >20 mmHg at rest - Primary (idiopathic PAH) — young women; progressive RV failure - Secondary — from chronic PE, left heart disease, hypoxic lung disease (COPD, OSA), connective tissue disease - Treatment: PDE-5 inhibitors (sildenafil), endothelin antagonists (bosentan), prostacyclins; lung transplant - Pulmonary oedema — elevated PCWP (>18 mmHg) from left heart failure → fluid into interstitium + alveoli → hypoxia; bat-wing shadowing on CXR - ARDS (acute respiratory distress syndrome) — non-cardiogenic pulmonary oedema (PCWP normal); diffuse alveolar damage; P/F ratio <300; treated with lung-protective ventilation
