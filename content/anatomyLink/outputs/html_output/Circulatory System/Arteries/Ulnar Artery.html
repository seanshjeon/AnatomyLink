Ulnar Artery

The ulnar artery is the medial and larger terminal branch of the Brachial Artery, running alongside the Ulna and Ulnar Nerve in the forearm, forming the superficial palmar arch in the hand.

🔗 Key Connections

- Arises from: Brachial Artery (at cubital fossa) - Runs alongside: Ulna and Ulnar Nerve (medial forearm) - Anastomoses with: Radial Artery (via palmar arches) - Forms: superficial palmar arch (primary)

🗂️ Key Information

Property Detail
Origin Brachial Artery bifurcation at cubital fossa
Course Passes deep to pronator teres → medial forearm with Ulnar Nerve → enters hand via Guyon's canal
Key Branch Superficial palmar arch → common palmar digital arteries → proper digital arteries
Pulse Palpable at wrist medial to flexor carpi ulnaris tendon

🔑 Palmar Arches

Arch Formed By Depth Supplies
Superficial palmar arch Ulnar artery + superficial radial branch Superficial to long flexor tendons Common digital arteries to medial 3.5 digits
Deep palmar arch Radial Artery + deep ulnar branch Deep to long flexor tendons Palmar metacarpal arteries

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Allen's test — tests ulnar artery patency (see Radial Artery); essential before radial access procedures - Hypothenar hammer syndrome — repetitive trauma to heel of hand (Guyon's canal) → ulnar artery thrombosis → digital ischemia; seen in mechanics, carpenters - Ulnar artery aneurysm — rare; pulsatile hypothenar mass
