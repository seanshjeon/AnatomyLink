Carotid Arteries

The carotid arteries are the primary arterial supply to the head and neck, arising from the Aorta on each side and bifurcating into internal (brain + eye) and external (face + scalp) branches at the level of C4 (Cervical Vertebrae).

🔗 Key Connections

- Arise from: Aorta (left common carotid directly; right via brachiocephalic trunk) - Supply: Brain (via internal carotid → anterior + middle cerebral arteries) - Relation to: Cervical Vertebrae, Skull (carotid canal → intracranial)

🗂️ Three Segments

Artery Course Supplies
Common carotid artery (CCA) Neck, within carotid sheath (with IJV + vagus) No branches in neck
Internal carotid artery (ICA) Enters carotid canal in Skull → cavernous sinus → Circle of Willis Brain (anterior 2/3), eye (ophthalmic artery), pituitary
External carotid artery (ECA) Branches in neck/face Face, scalp, pharynx, tongue, thyroid

🔑 External Carotid Branches (mnemonic: Some Angry Ladies Fight Over Pretty Maids)

1. Superior thyroid 2. Ascending pharyngeal 3. Lingual 4. Facial 5. Occipital 6. Posterior auricular 7. Maxillary (terminal) 8. Superficial temporal (terminal)

🔑 Carotid Sinus & Body

- Carotid sinus — dilation at bifurcation; baroreceptors → CN IX → nucleus tractus solitarius → regulate BP - Carotid body — chemoreceptor; detects low O₂, high CO₂, low pH → stimulates breathing

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Carotid artery disease (stenosis) — atherosclerosis at bifurcation → most common cause of embolic stroke and TIA - Amaurosis fugax — transient monocular blindness (ophthalmic artery embolus); classic warning sign - Carotid bruit — turbulent flow; heard with stethoscope over carotid triangle - Carotid endarterectomy (CEA) — surgical removal of plaque; indicated for symptomatic stenosis >50% or asymptomatic >70% - Carotid stenting — alternative to CEA - Internal carotid artery (ICA) dissection — traumatic or spontaneous; neck pain + Horner's + contralateral stroke; young patients - Carotid sinus hypersensitivity — vasovagal syncope triggered by collar pressure or head turning; diagnosed by carotid sinus massage - Carotid body tumour (paraganglioma) — slow-growing, pulsatile neck mass at bifurcation; "lyre sign" on angiogram
