Circulatory System

The circulatory system is the body's transport network, consisting of the Heart, blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries), and blood. It delivers oxygen and nutrients to every cell and removes metabolic waste.

🔗 Key Connections

- 00 - AnatomyLink Home - Heart — central pump - Pulmonary Circulation — heart ↔ lungs - Skeletal System, Muscular System, Nervous System — all supplied by this system

🗂️ Two Circuits

Circuit Path Function
Pulmonary Right heart → Pulmonary Circulation → Left heart Oxygenation of blood
Systemic Left heart → Aorta → body → Superior Vena Cava / Inferior Vena Cava → Right heart Delivery of O₂ + nutrients

🗂️ Vessel Types

Type Direction Wall Function
Arteries Away from heart Thick, muscular Carry oxygenated blood (except pulmonary artery)
Capillaries Tissue beds Single cell layer Gas + nutrient exchange
Veins Toward heart Thin, valved Return deoxygenated blood (except pulmonary vein)

🗺️ Subsections

Heart

- Heart — 4 chambers, 4 valves, conduction system - Coronary Arteries — blood supply to the heart itself

Arteries

- Aorta — main systemic artery; all others branch from it - Carotid Arteries — head & neck supply - Subclavian Artery — upper limb supply - Brachial Artery — arm - Radial Artery — lateral forearm & hand - Ulnar Artery — medial forearm & hand - Celiac Trunk — foregut supply (stomach, liver, spleen) - Renal Arteries — kidney supply - Iliac Arteries — pelvis & lower limb supply - Femoral Artery — thigh - Popliteal Artery — knee & leg

Veins

- Superior Vena Cava — drains upper body - Inferior Vena Cava — drains lower body - Portal System — gut → liver venous drainage - Jugular Veins — head & neck venous drainage

Pulmonary

- Pulmonary Circulation — right heart ↔ lungs ↔ left heart

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Atherosclerosis — plaque in arterial walls → ischemia; affects coronary, carotid, femoral arteries - Hypertension — increased systemic vascular resistance → damages vessels and heart - Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) → pulmonary embolism - Shock — failure of circulatory delivery of O₂ to tissues
