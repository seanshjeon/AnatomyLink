Coronary Arteries

The coronary arteries are the first branches of the Aorta, arising just above the aortic valve cusps in the aortic sinuses (of Valsalva). They supply the Heart muscle (myocardium) with oxygenated blood during diastole.

🔗 Key Connections

- Arise from: Aorta (aortic sinuses) - Supply: Heart (myocardium)

🗂️ Two Main Coronary Arteries

Left Coronary Artery (LCA)

Arises from the left aortic sinus → short left main stem → divides into:

Branch Territory Supplies
Left Anterior Descending (LAD) Anterior Anterior LV wall, anterior 2/3 of interventricular septum, anterior RV, apex
Left Circumflex (LCx) Lateral/posterior Lateral & posterior LV wall, left atrium; gives off obtuse marginal branches

Right Coronary Artery (RCA)

Arises from the right aortic sinus → travels in right AV groove:

Branch Territory Supplies
RCA main Right heart Right atrium, RV anterior wall
SA node artery SA node 55% from RCA; 45% from LCx
AV node artery AV node 85% from RCA (dominant)
Posterior Descending Artery (PDA) Posterior Posterior 1/3 of septum, posterior LV/RV walls

🔑 Coronary Dominance

- Right dominant (85%) — RCA gives rise to PDA - Left dominant (8%) — LCx gives rise to PDA - Co-dominant (7%) — both contribute

🔑 Venous Drainage

- Great cardiac vein → coronary sinus → right atrium (alongside LCA/LAD territory) - Middle cardiac vein → coronary sinus (alongside PDA) - Small cardiac vein → coronary sinus (alongside RCA) - Anterior cardiac veins → drain directly into right atrium

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Coronary artery disease (CAD) — atherosclerotic plaque → angina (stable or unstable) → MI - LAD occlusion — "widow maker"; anterior STEMI; most dangerous; can cause LV failure + septal defects - RCA occlusion — inferior STEMI (II, III, aVF on ECG); AV block (AV node ischemia); RV infarction - LCx occlusion — lateral STEMI (I, aVL, V5–V6); often electrically silent - STEMI territories on ECG: - V1–V4 = anterior (LAD) - II, III, aVF = inferior (RCA) - I, aVL, V5–V6 = lateral (LCx) - V1–V2 = posterior (RCA/LCx) — tall R waves - Percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) — balloon angioplasty ± stent; treatment for STEMI - CABG (coronary artery bypass graft) — internal mammary artery (IMA) used to bypass LAD (gold standard)
