Vertebral Column

The vertebral column consists of 26 bones forming the central axis of the body. It protects the spinal cord, supports body weight, and connects the skull to the pelvis.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Skull — Atlas (C1) articulates with the occipital bone at the atlanto-occipital joint - Ribs — Thoracic vertebrae (T1–T12) articulate with ribs at costovertebral joints - Sternum — Indirectly connected via the ribs - Hip Bone — Sacrum articulates with the ilium at the sacroiliac joint (SIJ)

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Erector Spinae — Primary extensors running along the entire spine - Trapezius — Attaches to spinous processes of cervical and thoracic vertebrae - Latissimus Dorsi — Originates from lower thoracic and lumbar spinous processes - Iliopsoas — Psoas major originates from lumbar vertebral bodies

Primary curves = present from fetal life / Secondary curves = develop postnatally

- Annulus fibrosus (outer fibrocartilage ring) + Nucleus pulposus (inner hydrated gel) - Absorb approximately 25% of axial load

🔑 Key Vertebral Landmarks

- Spinous Process — Posterior bony projections; palpable midline; ligament/muscle attachments - Transverse Process — Lateral projections; rib articulation (thoracic); muscle attachments - Intervertebral Disc — Fibrocartilage shock absorbers between vertebral bodies (23 total)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Disc herniation (HIVD) — Most common at L4/L5 and L5/S1 - Scoliosis — Lateral curvature >10° in the coronal plane - Spinal stenosis — Most common in the lumbar region - Spinal cord injury — Level of injury determines extent of paralysis
