Sacrum

The sacrum is a triangular flat bone formed by the fusion of 5 sacral vertebrae. It sits at the base of the spine, wedged between the two hip bones to form the posterior pelvis.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Lumbar Vertebrae — L5 articulates at the lumbosacral joint (L5/S1) - Coccyx — Sacral apex articulates with the coccyx at the sacrococcygeal joint - Hip Bone — Bilateral articulation at the sacroiliac joint (SIJ)

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Gluteus Maximus — Originates from the posterior sacrum and ilium - Erector Spinae — Attaches to the sacral crest

🗂️ Structure

Part Description
Base Superior; L5 articulation; anterior projection = sacral promontory
Apex Inferior; articulates with coccyx
Pelvic surface Concave; 4 pairs of anterior sacral foramina
Dorsal surface Convex; 4 pairs of posterior sacral foramina; median sacral crest
Auricular surface Bilateral; forms the sacroiliac joint

🔑 Sacral Foramina

- 4 pairs each (anterior and posterior) - Transmit sacral nerves S1–S4 - S2–S4: parasympathetic innervation to bladder, rectum, and genitalia

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Sacroiliitis — Early lesion in ankylosing spondylitis - Sacral fractures — High-energy trauma or insufficiency fractures in the elderly - Coccydynia — Tailbone pain after falls or childbirth - Caudal epidural anesthesia — Accessed through the sacral hiatus - Obstetrics — True conjugate determines pelvic inlet adequacy
