Humerus

The humerus is the longest bone of the upper limb, extending from the shoulder to the elbow.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Scapula — Glenohumeral joint (shoulder) — ball-and-socket; greatest ROM in the body - Radius — Humeroradial joint (elbow) - Ulna — Humeroulnar joint (elbow)

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Deltoid — Inserts on the deltoid tuberosity - Rotator Cuff — Attaches to the greater and lesser tubercles - Biceps Brachii — Long head passes through the bicipital groove - Triceps Brachii — Lateral and medial heads originate from the posterior humerus

🗂️ Key Structures

Proximal

Structure Description
Humeral head Spherical; articulates with glenoid cavity
Anatomical neck Groove between head and tubercles
Surgical neck Common fracture site
Greater Tubercle Supraspinatus, infraspinatus & teres minor attachment
Lesser Tubercle Subscapularis attachment
Bicipital groove Long head of biceps tendon

Shaft

Structure Description
Deltoid Tuberosity Deltoid insertion
Radial (spiral) groove Radial nerve & deep brachial artery

Distal

Structure Description
Capitulum Articulates with radial head
Trochlea Articulates with ulnar trochlear notch
Medial Epicondyle Ulnar nerve passes posteriorly
Lateral Epicondyle Common extensor tendon origin

📏 Shoulder Joint Range of Motion

Movement Range
Flexion/Extension 180° / 60°
Abduction/Adduction 180° / 45°
External/Internal rotation 90° / 90°

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Surgical neck fractures — Common in falls; axillary nerve injury risk - Radial nerve palsy — Mid-shaft fracture → wrist drop - Medial epicondyle fracture — Common in children; ulnar nerve at risk - Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis) — Global restriction of all shoulder movements
