Olecranon

The olecranon is the large posterior bony projection at the proximal end of the Ulna that forms the point of the elbow. It fits into the olecranon fossa of the Humerus during full elbow extension and is the insertion of the Triceps Brachii.

🔗 Key Connections

- Ulna — proximal posterior ulna - Humerus — articulates in the olecranon fossa (full extension) - Triceps Brachii — inserts on the olecranon (elbow extension) - Ulnar Nerve — passes in the cubital tunnel just medial to the olecranon

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Function Forms elbow point; locks into olecranon fossa in extension
Muscle insertion Triceps brachii
Palpation Prominent bony point of elbow
Olecranon bursa Superficial bursa overlies the olecranon

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Olecranon fracture — Usually direct blow or fall on outstretched hand; gap palpable; triceps avulsion - Olecranon bursitis (student's elbow) — Inflamed bursa; soft, fluctuant swelling at elbow point - Triceps tendon rupture — Rare; inability to extend elbow; often at olecranon insertion
