Lateral Epicondyle

The lateral epicondyle is the bony projection on the lateral side of the distal Humerus, serving as the common origin of the extensor muscles of the forearm.

🔗 Key Connections

- Humerus — distal lateral humerus - Radial Nerve — divides into superficial and deep (posterior interosseous) branches at the level of the lateral epicondyle - Extensor-supinator group — common extensor origin (ECRB, EDC, EDM, ECU, supinator) - Lateral collateral ligament — originates from lateral epicondyle

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow) — Overuse tendinopathy at common extensor origin (especially ECRB); lateral elbow pain - Radial tunnel syndrome — Posterior interosseous nerve compression near lateral epicondyle - Posterior interosseous nerve palsy — Finger/wrist drop (extensor weakness); no sensory loss (deep branch)
