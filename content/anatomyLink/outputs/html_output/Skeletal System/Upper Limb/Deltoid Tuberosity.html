Deltoid Tuberosity

The deltoid tuberosity is a roughened V-shaped area on the lateral midshaft of the Humerus where the Deltoid muscle inserts.

🔗 Key Connections

- Humerus — lateral midshaft, ~halfway down the humerus - Deltoid — inserts here (all three heads: anterior/middle/posterior) - Radial Nerve — the radial groove (spiral groove) passes posterior to the deltoid tuberosity level

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Lateral midshaft of humerus
Muscle Deltoid (all three portions)
Level ~Middle 1/3 of humerus

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Humeral shaft fracture — At the level of the deltoid tuberosity, the radial nerve is at risk in the spiral groove → wrist drop - Radial nerve palsy — Holstein-Lewis fracture (distal 1/3) or fracture at spiral groove level
