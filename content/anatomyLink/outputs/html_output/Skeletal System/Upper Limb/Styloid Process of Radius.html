Styloid Process of Radius

The styloid process of the radius is the pointed bony projection at the distal lateral end of the Radius, extending ~1 cm more distally than the ulnar styloid. It is the attachment for the brachioradialis tendon.

🔗 Key Connections

- Radius — distal lateral radius - Brachioradialis — inserts on the radial styloid - Ulna — ulnar styloid is ~1 cm proximal to the radial styloid (important in Colles' fracture assessment)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Colles' fracture — Distal radius fracture with dorsal displacement; radial styloid displaced → loss of normal radial inclination (normally 23°) - De Quervain's tenosynovitis — APL and EPB tendons pass through first dorsal compartment adjacent to the radial styloid - Chauffeur's fracture — Isolated radial styloid fracture from direct blow

Skeletal System → Radius → Styloid Process of Radius
