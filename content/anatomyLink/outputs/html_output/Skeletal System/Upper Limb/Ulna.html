Ulna

The ulna is the medial (little finger-side) bone of the forearm and the primary stabilizer of the elbow joint.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Humerus — Humeroulnar joint — elbow flexion/extension - Radius — Proximal & distal radioulnar joints — forearm rotation - Carpals — Indirect via the triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC)

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Triceps Brachii — Inserts on the olecranon

🗂️ Key Structures

Structure Location Description
Olecranon Proximal Posterior elbow prominence; triceps insertion
Coronoid Process Proximal anterior Anterior elbow stability
Trochlear notch Proximal Articulates with the humeral trochlea
Radial notch Proximal lateral Articulates with the radial head
Styloid Process of Ulna Distal Medial wrist prominence

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Monteggia fracture — Ulnar shaft fracture + proximal radioulnar joint dislocation - Olecranon fracture — Direct trauma or triceps avulsion - Cubital tunnel syndrome — Ulnar nerve entrapment; ring & little finger numbness - UCL tear — Medial elbow instability in throwing athletes (Tommy John surgery)
