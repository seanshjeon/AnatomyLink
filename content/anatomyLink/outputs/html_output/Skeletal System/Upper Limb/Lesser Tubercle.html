Lesser Tubercle

The lesser tubercle is the smaller bony prominence on the anterior-medial surface of the proximal Humerus, serving as the insertion site for the Subscapularis (the only anterior rotator cuff muscle).

🔗 Key Connections

- Humerus — anteromedial proximal humerus - Subscapularis — inserts on the lesser tubercle (internal rotation)

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Anteromedial proximal humerus
Muscle insertion Subscapularis only
Separated from greater tubercle by Intertubercular (bicipital) groove

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Subscapularis tear — Avulsion or tear at lesser tubercle insertion; loss of internal rotation strength - Gerber lift-off test — Tests subscapularis integrity at lesser tubercle insertion - Lesser tubercle fracture — Rare avulsion fracture from forceful internal rotation
