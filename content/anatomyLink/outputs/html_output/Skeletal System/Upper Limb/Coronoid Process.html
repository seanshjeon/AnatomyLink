Coronoid Process

The coronoid process is the anterior bony projection at the proximal end of the Ulna, forming the anterior wall of the trochlear notch and resisting posterior displacement of the ulna (elbow dislocation).

🔗 Key Connections

- Ulna — anterior proximal ulna - Humerus — articulates with the trochlea; coronoid enters coronoid fossa in full flexion - Brachialis — inserts on the ulnar tuberosity just distal to the coronoid process - Pronator Teres — medial head originates from the medial coronoid process

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Function Anterior wall of trochlear notch; resists elbow dislocation
Ulnar tuberosity Distal to coronoid; brachialis insertion

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Coronoid fracture — Often accompanies elbow dislocation ("terrible triad": coronoid + radial head + LCL) - Terrible Triad injury — Elbow dislocation + coronoid fracture + radial head fracture; highly unstable - Regan-Morrey classification — Grades I–III based on size of coronoid fragment
