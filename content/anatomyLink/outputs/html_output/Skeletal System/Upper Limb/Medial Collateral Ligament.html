Medial Collateral Ligament (Elbow UCL)

The medial collateral ligament (MCL) of the elbow — also called the ulnar collateral ligament (UCL) — is the primary stabilizer against valgus stress at the elbow, connecting the Medial Epicondyle of the Humerus to the Ulna.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Medial Epicondyle of Humerus - Insertion — Medial Ulna (sublime tubercle of coronoid process) - Three bundles — Anterior (strongest), posterior, transverse

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Tommy John surgery (UCL reconstruction) — UCL rupture in overhead throwing athletes (baseball pitchers); reconstructed with palmaris longus or gracilis tendon graft - Valgus stress test — Tests UCL integrity; medial elbow pain/gapping - Medial elbow pain — UCL sprain/tear from repetitive valgus load in throwing athletes

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Upper Limb → Humerus → Medial Epicondyle → Medial Collateral Ligament
