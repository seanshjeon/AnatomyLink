Carpals

The carpals are 8 short bones arranged in 2 rows forming the wrist. They create the carpal tunnel on their palmar surface.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Radius — Radiocarpal joint — primary wrist joint - Ulna — Indirect via the triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC) - Metacarpals — Carpometacarpal joints (CMC joints)

🗂️ The 8 Carpal Bones

Proximal Row (radial → ulnar)

Bone Feature
Scaphoid Largest; poor blood supply → AVN risk
Lunate Crescent-shaped
Triquetrum Ulnar side
Pisiform Sesamoid bone; flexor carpi ulnaris attachment

Distal Row (radial → ulnar)

Bone Feature
Trapezium Saddle joint with 1st metacarpal
Trapezoid Smallest carpal
Capitate Largest carpal; central position
Hamate Hook of hamate (Hamulus)

Mnemonic: "She Looks Too Pretty, Try To Catch Her"

🔑 Carpal Tunnel

- Floor: Carpal bones (concave palmar surface) - Roof: Flexor retinaculum - Contents: FDS (×4), FDP (×4), FPL (×1), median nerve — 10 structures total

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Scaphoid fracture — Most common carpal fracture (70%); AVN risk due to poor blood supply - Carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS) — Median nerve compression; thumb–middle finger numbness; thenar atrophy - Lunate dislocation — Compresses carpal tunnel → acute CTS - TFCC injury — Ulnar-sided wrist pain
