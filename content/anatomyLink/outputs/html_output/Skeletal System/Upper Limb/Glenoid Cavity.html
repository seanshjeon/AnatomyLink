Glenoid Cavity

The glenoid cavity (fossa) is the shallow cup-shaped articular surface of the Scapula that forms the socket of the glenohumeral (shoulder) joint, articulating with the head of the Humerus.

🔗 Key Connections

- Scapula — lateral surface of the scapula - Humerus — humeral head articulates here (glenohumeral joint) - Glenoid labrum — fibrocartilaginous ring that deepens the socket - Rotator cuff muscles — Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Subscapularis stabilize the joint

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Depth Shallow (~1/3 coverage of humeral head)
Labrum Fibrocartilage rim that deepens and stabilizes socket
Inclination Slightly anteverted and inferiorly tilted
Coverage Only ~25–30% of humeral head covered at any position

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Shoulder dislocation — Anterior (95%) most common; humeral head dislocates anteroinferiorly - Bankart lesion — Anterior-inferior glenoid labrum tear (from anterior dislocation) - Hill-Sachs lesion — Posterolateral humeral head compression fracture (from anterior dislocation) - SLAP tear — Superior Labrum Anterior to Posterior tear; common in overhead athletes - Glenoid fractures — Intra-articular fractures require surgery to restore congruity

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Scapula → Glenoid Cavity
