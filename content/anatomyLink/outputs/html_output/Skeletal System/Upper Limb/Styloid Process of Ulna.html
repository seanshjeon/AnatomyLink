Styloid Process of Ulna

The styloid process of the ulna is the pointed bony projection at the distal end of the Ulna, on the medial side of the wrist, serving as an attachment for the articular disc of the distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ).

🔗 Key Connections

- Ulna — distal medial ulna - Radius — distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ) — radius rotates around the ulnar head - Triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC) — attaches at the base and tip of the ulnar styloid

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Ulnar styloid fracture — Common with distal radius (Colles') fractures; if at base, TFCC is disrupted → DRUJ instability - TFCC injury — Ulnar-sided wrist pain; positive ulnar grind test - Ulnar impaction syndrome — Ulnar styloid impinges on triquetrum with ulnar-positive variance
