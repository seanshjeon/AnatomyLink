Radial Tuberosity

The radial tuberosity (bicipital tuberosity) is a roughened oval prominence just distal to the radial head on the anteromedial Radius, where the Biceps Brachii tendon inserts.

🔗 Key Connections

- Radius — proximal radius, distal to the radial head - Biceps Brachii — inserts here via the biceps tendon (flexion + supination)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Distal biceps tendon rupture — Avulsion at radial tuberosity; "popeye" deformity, weak supination - Surgical repair — Reattachment to radial tuberosity restores supination strength

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Radius → Radial Tuberosity
