Radial Head

The radial head is the disk-shaped proximal end of the Radius, articulating with the capitulum of the Humerus (radiocapitellar joint) and the radial notch of the Ulna (proximal radioulnar joint — PRUJ), allowing forearm rotation.

🔗 Key Connections

- Radius — proximal end - Humerus — articulates with capitulum (radiocapitellar joint) - Ulna — articulates with radial notch (proximal radioulnar joint) - Annular ligament — encircles the radial head; holds it against the ulna

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Radial head fracture — Most common elbow fracture in adults; fall on outstretched hand - Mason classification — I (non-displaced), II (partial, displaced), III (comminuted), IV (+elbow dislocation) - Nursemaid's elbow (annular ligament displacement) — In children <5: subluxation of radial head from annular ligament; arm held pronated after traction injury - Posterior interosseous nerve — Deep radial nerve wraps around the radial neck just distal to the head; at risk in fractures
