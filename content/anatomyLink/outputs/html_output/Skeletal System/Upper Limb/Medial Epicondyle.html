Medial Epicondyle

The medial epicondyle is the prominent bony projection on the medial side of the distal Humerus, serving as the common origin of the flexor-pronator muscle group and the site where the ulnar nerve passes in the cubital tunnel.

🔗 Key Connections

- Humerus — distal medial humerus - Ulnar Nerve — passes in the cubital tunnel posterior to the medial epicondyle - Flexor-pronator group — common flexor origin (FCR, PL, FCU, FDS, pronator teres) - Medial Collateral Ligament — UCL originates from medial epicondyle

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Medial distal humerus (easily palpable)
Nerve relation Ulnar nerve runs in cubital tunnel directly posterior
Common origin Flexor-pronator muscles of the forearm
"Funny bone" Striking the cubital tunnel → ulnar nerve tingling in ring + little finger

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow) — Overuse tendinopathy at the common flexor origin; medial elbow pain - Ulnar nerve entrapment (cubital tunnel syndrome) — Tingling in ring and little fingers; numbness at medial epicondyle - Medial epicondyle avulsion fracture — Common in adolescents; can trap ulnar nerve - UCL injury (Tommy John) — UCL originates here; rupture in overhead throwing athletes
