Skeletal System

The adult human body contains 206 bones. The skeleton provides structural support, protects vital organs, serves as muscle attachment points, produces blood cells, and stores minerals.

📌 Functions

1. Support — forms the body's structural framework 2. Protection — shields the brain, heart, lungs, and other organs 3. Movement — works with muscles to produce motion 4. Hematopoiesis — red blood cells produced in red bone marrow 5. Mineral Storage — stores calcium and phosphorus
