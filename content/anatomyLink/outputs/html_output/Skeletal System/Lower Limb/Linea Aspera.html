Linea Aspera

The linea aspera ("rough line") is a prominent longitudinal ridge on the posterior surface of the Femur midshaft, serving as an attachment for multiple thigh muscles and intermuscular septa.

🔗 Key Connections

- Femur — posterior midshaft ridge

- Adductor Magnus — inserts on the medial lip - Adductor Longus — inserts on the medial lip - Adductor Brevis — inserts on the medial lip - Biceps Femoris — short head originates from the lateral lip

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Posterior femoral midshaft; bifurcates above and below
Superior extension Spiral line → lesser trochanter; pectineal line
Inferior extension Medial + lateral supracondylar lines → medial/lateral condyles
Profunda femoris Deep femoral artery passes near the linea aspera via perforating branches
