Acetabulum

The acetabulum is the deep cup-shaped socket on the lateral Hip Bone, formed by the fusion of the ilium (40%), ischium (40%), and pubis (20%), that articulates with the femoral head to form the hip joint.

🔗 Key Connections

- Hip Bone — lateral surface; formed at the triradiate cartilage junction - Femur — femoral head articulates here (hip joint) - Acetabular labrum — fibrocartilaginous ring that deepens the socket - Hip joint capsule — iliofemoral, ischiofemoral, pubofemoral ligaments

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Formed by Ilium (40%), ischium (40%), pubis (20%)
Articular surface Lunate (horseshoe) area; acetabular fossa is non-articular
Depth Much deeper than glenoid cavity — inherently stable
Inclination ~45° lateral, 15° anterior

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Hip dislocation — Posterior (most common, 90%); femoral head exits posterior; sciatic nerve at risk - Acetabular fracture — High-energy trauma; classified by column (anterior/posterior) involvement - Hip impingement (FAI) — Cam (femoral head non-spherical) or Pincer (acetabular over-coverage) types - Developmental dysplasia of hip (DDH) — Shallow acetabulum → instability; Ortolani/Barlow tests in infants - Total hip arthroplasty — Acetabular cup replaces the socket

Skeletal System → Hip Bone → Acetabulum
