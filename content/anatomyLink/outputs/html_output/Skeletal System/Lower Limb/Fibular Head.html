Fibular Head

The fibular head is the proximal end of the Fibula, articulating with the posterolateral Tibia at the proximal tibiofibular joint, and serving as the attachment for the biceps femoris (lateral hamstring) and the fibular collateral ligament (FCL).

🔗 Key Connections

- Fibula — proximal end - Tibia — proximal tibiofibular joint (posterolateral)

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Palpation Palpable lateral knee, posterolateral
FCL attachment Fibular collateral ligament: lateral femoral condyle → fibular head
Common fibular nerve Wraps around fibular neck just below head

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Common fibular nerve palsy — Most commonly injured where nerve winds around fibular neck; knee dislocation, plaster cast, peroneal kick → foot drop (weakness of dorsiflexion/eversion) - Fibular head avulsion fracture (arcuate fracture) — Posterolateral corner injury; avulsion of FCL + biceps femoris - Proximal tibiofibular joint dislocation — Rare; direct blow or twisting; painful lateral knee lump

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Fibula → Fibular Head
