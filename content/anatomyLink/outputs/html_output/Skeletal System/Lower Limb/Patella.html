Patella

The patella is the largest sesamoid bone in the body, embedded within the quadriceps tendon. It increases the mechanical advantage of knee extension by ~50%.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Femur — Patellofemoral joint — glides along the femoral trochlear groove - Tibia — Indirectly via the patellar tendon to the tibial tuberosity

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Quadriceps Femoris — Inserts via quadriceps tendon onto the patella base; patellar tendon continues to tibial tuberosity

🗂️ Structure

Part Description
Base Superior; quadriceps tendon attachment
Apex Inferior; patellar tendon origin
Anterior surface Rough; subcutaneous
Articular surface Medial + lateral facets (contact femoral trochlea)

🔑 Functions

1. Increases mechanical advantage — extends the lever arm of the quadriceps by ~50% 2. Reduces friction — protects quadriceps tendon from bone contact 3. Protects the anterior knee joint

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Patellar fracture — Direct blow or forced quadriceps contraction - Patellofemoral pain syndrome (Runner's knee) — Anterior knee pain; common in young women and athletes - Patellar dislocation — Lateral dislocation most common; increased Q-angle is a risk factor - Patellar tendinopathy (Jumper's knee) — Repetitive jumping sports - Osgood-Schlatter disease — Tibial tuberosity apophysitis in adolescents

📍 Location in Skeletal System

Skeletal System → Lower Limb → Femur → Patella (Knee joint)
