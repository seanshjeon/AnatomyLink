Talus

The talus is the most superior of the Tarsals, forming the ankle joint above (with Tibia and Fibula) and the subtalar joint below (with the calcaneus). It has no muscle attachments — only ligamentous and cartilaginous surfaces.

🔗 Key Connections

- Tibia — superior/medial articular surface forms talocrural (ankle) joint - Fibula — Lateral Malleolus forms lateral ankle mortise - Medial Malleolus — medial mortise wall - Calcaneus — subtalar joint (below talus); primary site of hindfoot inversion/eversion - Navicular — talonavicular joint (midfoot)

🗂️ Ankle Joint

Surface Articulation
Trochlea (dome) Tibia (tibial plafond) — plantarflexion/dorsiflexion
Medial facet Medial Malleolus
Lateral facet Lateral Malleolus
Inferior surface Calcaneus (subtalar joint)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Talus fractures — Second most common tarsal fracture; AVN risk (65% in neck fractures) due to precarious blood supply - Ankle fractures — Talus trapped in mortise; malleoli fracture around it - Subtalar joint — Stiff after calcaneal fractures; site of subtalar arthritis - Osteochondral lesions of talus (OLT) — Cartilage + bone injury from ankle sprains; anteromedial or posterolateral dome
