Ischial Tuberosity

The ischial tuberosity is the roughened posterior bony projection of the ischium (part of the Hip Bone) on which you sit. It is the origin of the hamstring muscles and the adductor magnus.

🔗 Key Connections

- Hip Bone — posteroinferior ischium

- Adductor Magnus — hamstring portion originates here - Sacrotuberous Ligament — connects ischial tuberosity to sacrum

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Common name "Sit bone"
Palpation Palpable when hip is flexed; protected by gluteus maximus when standing
Bursa Ischial bursa overlies it

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Hamstring avulsion fracture — Forceful hip flexion with knee extension avulses the hamstring origin (sprinters, gymnasts); requires surgery if displaced - Ischial bursitis (weaver's bottom) — Chronic compression of ischial bursa in sedentary individuals - Proximal hamstring tendinopathy — Chronic pain at ischial tuberosity; common in cyclists and rowers

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Hip Bone → Ischial Tuberosity
