Anterior Superior Iliac Spine (ASIS)

The anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS) is the anterior bony projection at the front of the Hip Bone's iliac crest, serving as the origin of the Sartorius and the attachment of the inguinal ligament.

🔗 Key Connections

- Hip Bone — anterior iliac crest endpoint - Sartorius — originates from the ASIS (longest muscle in the body) - Tensor Fasciae Latae — originates just posterior to the ASIS - Inguinal ligament — extends from ASIS to pubic tubercle

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Palpation Prominent bony point at front of hip
Inguinal ligament ASIS → pubic tubercle
AIIS Anterior inferior iliac spine — 2 cm below ASIS; origin of rectus femoris

🏥 Clinical Significance

- ASIS avulsion fracture — Sartorius or TFL avulsion in adolescents; sprinting injury - Meralgia paresthetica — Lateral femoral cutaneous nerve compressed at ASIS/inguinal ligament → burning thigh pain - Anatomical landmark — Used to measure true leg length (ASIS to medial malleolus)
