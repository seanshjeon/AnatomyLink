Greater Sciatic Notch

The greater sciatic notch is a large concave notch on the posterior Hip Bone, converted into the greater sciatic foramen by the sacrospinous ligament. The sciatic nerve and multiple other neurovascular structures pass through it into the gluteal region.

🔗 Key Connections

- Hip Bone — posterior ilium and ischium - Sciatic Nerve — exits through the greater sciatic foramen (below piriformis in 85%) - Piriformis — passes through the greater sciatic foramen; key landmark - Superior gluteal nerve/vessels — exit above piriformis - Inferior gluteal nerve/vessels — exit below piriformis - Pudendal nerve — exits here, then re-enters via lesser sciatic foramen

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Piriformis syndrome — Sciatic nerve compressed at the greater sciatic notch/piriformis; sciatica-like symptoms - Posterior hip dislocation — Femoral head can injure sciatic nerve at the notch level - Gluteal injection safety — Injections must avoid the greater sciatic notch (upper outer quadrant)

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Hip Bone → Greater Sciatic Notch
