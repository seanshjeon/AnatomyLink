Metatarsals

The metatarsals are 5 long bones forming the forefoot, numbered 1 (big toe) to 5 (little toe).

🔗 Connected Structures

- Tarsals — Tarsometatarsal joints (Lisfranc joint) - Phalanges of Foot — Metatarsophalangeal joints (MTP joints)

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Tibialis Anterior — Inserts on 1st metatarsal base and medial cuneiform - Gastrocnemius — Plantarflexion during push-off involves metatarsal heads

🗂️ Structure

Each has a Base (proximal; tarsal articulation), Shaft, and Head (distal; forms ball of foot).

- 1st metatarsal — Shortest and stoutest; critical for weight bearing and push-off - 5th metatarsal base — Peroneus brevis insertion → avulsion fracture site

🏥 Clinical Significance

- 5th metatarsal base fracture (Dancer's fracture) — Ankle inversion; peroneus brevis avulsion - Jones fracture — 5th metatarsal metaphyseal-diaphyseal junction; nonunion risk - March fracture — 2nd–3rd metatarsal stress fracture; military recruits & distance runners - Metatarsalgia — Pain under the metatarsal heads - Hallux valgus — 1st MTP lateral deviation; bunion formation

📍 Location in Skeletal System

Skeletal System → Lower Limb → Tarsals → Metatarsals
