Lesser Trochanter

The lesser trochanter is the small conical bony projection on the posteromedial proximal Femur, just below the femoral neck, serving as the insertion for the iliopsoas (the primary hip flexor).

🔗 Key Connections

- Femur — posteromedial proximal femur - Iliopsoas — inserts on the lesser trochanter (iliacus + psoas major — primary hip flexor)

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Posteromedial proximal femur
Muscle Iliopsoas only
Level Roughly at the level of the intertrochanteric line (anterior) and crest (posterior)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Lesser trochanter avulsion — In adolescents; forceful hip flexion avulses iliopsoas insertion - Pathological fracture — Isolated lesser trochanter fracture in adults is a red flag for metastatic disease (common site for bone mets)
