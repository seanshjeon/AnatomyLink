Iliac Crest

The iliac crest is the superior curved border of the ilium (part of the Hip Bone), running from the anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS) to the posterior superior iliac spine (PSIS). It is the most commonly used bone graft donor site.

🔗 Key Connections

- Hip Bone — superior border of the ilium - Tensor Fasciae Latae — origin on the anterior iliac crest - Gluteus Medius — origin on the lateral iliac crest - Gluteus Maximus — origin on the posterior iliac crest - Quadratus Lumborum — inserts on the iliac crest - Iliotibial Band — attaches to the tubercle of the iliac crest

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Palpation Easily palpable "hip bones"; level of L4 vertebra (used for lumbar puncture landmark)
ASIS Anterior endpoint of the iliac crest
PSIS Posterior endpoint; dimples of Venus; level of S2
Bone graft Primary donor site for autologous bone grafts

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Iliac crest bone graft — Most common donor site for spinal fusion, nonunion repair - L4 landmark — Intercristal line (between highest points of iliac crests) passes through L4 vertebral body → used for lumbar puncture needle insertion level (L3–L4 or L4–L5) - Iliac crest apophysitis — In adolescents; avulsion injury at apophysis from muscle traction

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Hip Bone → Iliac Crest
