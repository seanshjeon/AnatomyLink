Tibial Tuberosity

The tibial tuberosity is the prominent bony elevation on the anterior proximal Tibia, just below the knee joint, where the patellar tendon (continuation of the Quadriceps Femoris via the Patella) inserts.

🔗 Key Connections

- Tibia — anterior proximal tibia - Patella — patellar tendon connects patella to tibial tuberosity - Quadriceps Femoris — force transmitted through patella → patellar tendon → tibial tuberosity → knee extension

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Anterior proximal tibia; easily palpable below kneecap
Insertion Patellar tendon (continuation of quadriceps mechanism)
Apophysis Growth plate in adolescents (Osgood-Schlatter site)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Osgood-Schlatter disease — Apophysitis (overuse) at the tibial tuberosity in adolescent athletes; painful prominence; resolves with skeletal maturity - Patellar tendon rupture — Avulsion or rupture at tibial tuberosity; unable to extend knee; infrapatellar gap - Tibial tuberosity osteotomy (TTO) — Surgical repositioning for patellar maltracking - Bump palpation — Tenderness at the tuberosity in Osgood-Schlatter; prominent ossicle may persist into adulthood
