Phalanges of Foot

The foot contains 14 phalanges. The great toe (Hallux) has 2; each of the other four toes has 3.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Metatarsals — Metatarsophalangeal joints (MTP joints) - Interphalangeal joints: - PIP joint — Proximal ↔ middle phalanx - DIP joint — Middle ↔ distal phalanx

🗂️ Components

Segment Count Description
Proximal phalanx 5 MTP & PIP joint formation
Middle phalanx 4 Absent in great toe; PIP & DIP joints
Distal phalanx 5 Supports toenail

🔑 Great Toe (Hallux)

- 2 sesamoid bones beneath the 1st MTP joint - Critical role in push-off phase of gait - Hallux valgus — lateral deviation at the 1st MTP joint

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Hallux valgus — 1st MTP lateral deviation; bunion; common in adult women - Hammer toe — PIP flexion deformity; dorsal corn formation - Claw toe — MTP hyperextension + PIP/DIP flexion - Toe fractures — Direct trauma (stubbing or dropped object) - Gout (Podagra) — 1st MTP joint; monosodium urate crystal deposition
