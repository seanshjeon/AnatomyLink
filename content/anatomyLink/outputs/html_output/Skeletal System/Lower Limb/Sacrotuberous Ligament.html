Sacrotuberous Ligament

The sacrotuberous ligament is a strong broad ligament connecting the sacrum/coccyx to the Ischial Tuberosity, converting the greater sciatic notch into the greater sciatic foramen and the lesser sciatic notch into the lesser sciatic foramen.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Sacrum + coccyx (lateral border) + PSIS - Insertion — Ischial Tuberosity - Related structures — Greater sciatic foramen (above), lesser sciatic foramen (below) - Biceps Femoris — Long head has a fibrous expansion into the sacrotuberous ligament

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Pelvic stability — Resists nutation of the sacrum (forward tilting) - SIJ dysfunction — Sacrotuberous ligament tension influences sacroiliac joint mechanics - Pudendal nerve — Pudendal nerve (S2–S4) exits greater sciatic foramen and re-enters through lesser sciatic foramen, close to sacrotuberous ligament
