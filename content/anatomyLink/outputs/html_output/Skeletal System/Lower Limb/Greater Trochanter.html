Greater Trochanter

The greater trochanter is the large, square bony prominence at the proximal-lateral Femur, serving as the insertion for the gluteal muscles and external rotators of the hip.

🔗 Key Connections

- Femur — proximal lateral femur - Gluteus Medius — inserts on the lateral and superior surfaces (primary hip abductor) - Gluteus Minimus — inserts on the anterior surface - Piriformis — inserts on the superior facet - Obturator Internus + gemelli — insert on the medial surface - Quadratus Femoris — inserts on the intertrochanteric crest

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Proximal lateral femur; palpable laterally at hip
Trochanteric fossa Medial deep pit; obturator externus insertion
Greater trochanteric bursa Between gluteus maximus and greater trochanter

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Greater trochanteric pain syndrome (GTPS) — Gluteus medius/minimus tendinopathy ± bursitis; lateral hip pain with lying on the side - Greater trochanteric bursitis — Inflamed bursa; lateral hip pain; Trendelenburg gait - Trochanteric fracture — Intertrochanteric or subtrochanteric fractures in elderly; treated with ORIF or IM nail - Trendelenburg sign — Gluteus medius weakness (insertion on greater trochanter); pelvis drops to contralateral side when standing on affected leg

Skeletal System → Femur → Greater Trochanter
