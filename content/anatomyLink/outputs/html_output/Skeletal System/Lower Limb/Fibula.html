Fibula

The fibula is the slender lateral bone of the leg, bearing minimal weight (~10%) but critical for lateral ankle stability.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Tibia — Proximal & distal tibiofibular joints + interosseous membrane - Tarsals — Ankle joint — lateral malleolus forms the lateral wall of the ankle mortise

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Tibialis Anterior — Runs along the anterior compartment lateral to the fibula

🗂️ Key Structures

Structure Location Description
Fibular Head Proximal Articulates with lateral tibial condyle; common fibular nerve wraps here
Fibular neck Proximal Common fibular (peroneal) nerve at risk
Shaft Middle Slender; interosseous membrane attachment
Lateral Malleolus Distal Lateral ankle prominence; sits lower than medial malleolus

🔑 Lateral Ankle Ligaments

- ATFL — Fibula anterior → talus; most commonly injured in ankle sprains - CFL — Fibula inferior → calcaneus - PTFL — Fibula posterior → talus; strongest of the three

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Lateral malleolus fracture — Ankle inversion; one of the most common fractures overall - Common fibular nerve injury — At fibular neck → foot drop - Fibular free flap — Reconstructive surgery with vascular pedicle - Maisonneuve fracture — Proximal fibula + distal tibiofibular disruption

📍 Location in Skeletal System

Skeletal System → Lower Limb → Fibula (alongside Tibia)
