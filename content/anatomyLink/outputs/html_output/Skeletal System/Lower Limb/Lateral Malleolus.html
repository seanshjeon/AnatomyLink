Lateral Malleolus

The lateral malleolus is the bony projection at the distal end of the Fibula, forming the lateral wall of the ankle mortise. It extends ~1 cm more distally than the Medial Malleolus, providing lateral stability to the ankle.

🔗 Key Connections

- Fibula — distal fibula - Tibia — distal tibiofibular syndesmosis (fibrous joint) - Talus — lateral talar surface articulates with the lateral malleolus - Lateral ankle ligaments — ATFL, CFL, PTFL (from lateral malleolus to calcaneus/talus) - Medial Malleolus — together form the ankle mortise

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Level ~1 cm more distal than medial malleolus
Lateral ligaments ATFL (most commonly injured), CFL, PTFL
Syndesmosis Connected to tibia by anterior/posterior tibiofibular ligaments + interosseous membrane

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Ankle sprain — Most common musculoskeletal injury; inversion → ATFL rupture (lateral malleolus origin) - Weber classification — Weber A (below syndesmosis), B (at syndesmosis), C (above syndesmosis = unstable) - High ankle sprain (syndesmosis injury) — Interosseous membrane + tibiofibular ligament torn; worse prognosis than lateral sprain - Ottawa Ankle Rules — Lateral malleolus posterior edge tenderness → X-ray required

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Fibula → Lateral Malleolus
