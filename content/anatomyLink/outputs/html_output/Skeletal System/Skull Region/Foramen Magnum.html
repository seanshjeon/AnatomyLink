Foramen Magnum

The foramen magnum ("great opening") is the large oval opening in the occipital bone at the base of the skull through which the brainstem transitions to the spinal cord.

🔗 Key Connections

- Skull — located in the occipital bone, posterior cranial fossa - Brain — brainstem passes through to become Spinal Cord - Spinal Cord — begins at the foramen magnum (continuous with medulla oblongata) - Vertebral Column — upper border of spinal canal

🗂️ Contents Passing Through

Structure Notes
Medulla oblongata Brainstem → spinal cord transition
Vertebral arteries (×2) Supply brainstem and posterior brain
Anterior spinal artery Supplies anterior spinal cord
Posterior spinal arteries (×2) Supply posterior spinal cord
Accessory nerve roots (CN XI) Ascending spinal roots enter skull
Meninges Dura, arachnoid, pia continue into spinal canal

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Tonsillar herniation — Increased ICP forces cerebellar tonsils through foramen magnum → brainstem compression → respiratory arrest - Chiari malformation — Cerebellar tonsils displaced below foramen magnum; causes headache, syringomyelia - Atlanto-occipital dislocation — Traumatic separation at the joint just above the foramen magnum

📍 Location

Skeletal System → Skull → Occipital bone → Foramen Magnum
