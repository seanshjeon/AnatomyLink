Skull

The skull is composed of 22 bones — 8 neurocranium bones encasing the brain and 14 viscerocranium bones forming the facial skeleton.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Vertebral Column — Occipital bone articulates with Atlas (C1) at the atlanto-occipital joint - Mandible — Temporal bone articulates with the mandible at the temporomandibular joint (TMJ) — the only freely movable facial joint

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Masseter — Inserts on the mandible; closes the jaw - Sternocleidomastoid — Inserts on the mastoid process; flexes/rotates neck - Trapezius — Originates from the occipital bone

🗂️ Component Bones

Neurocranium — 8 bones

Bone Count Location
Frontal bone 1 Forehead
Parietal bone 2 Top of skull
Temporal bone 2 Sides (temples)
Occipital bone 1 Back of skull
Sphenoid bone 1 Central cranial base
Ethmoid bone 1 Anterior cranial base

Viscerocranium — 14 bones

Bone Count
Nasal bone 2
Maxilla 2
Zygomatic bone 2
Lacrimal bone 2
Palatine bone 2
Inferior nasal concha 2
Vomer 1
Mandible 1

📐 Major Sutures

- Coronal suture — Frontal ↔ Parietal bones - Sagittal suture — Parietal ↔ Parietal bones - Lambdoid suture — Parietal ↔ Occipital bone - Squamous suture — Temporal ↔ Parietal bones

🔑 Key Foramina

Foramen Contents
Foramen Magnum Brainstem → spinal cord, vertebral arteries
Optic canal Optic nerve (CN II)
Jugular foramen CN IX, X, XI + internal jugular vein

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Mastoid Process — easily palpable behind ear; Sternocleidomastoid insertion - Sella Turcica — houses pituitary gland in sphenoid bone - Cribriform Plate — CN I olfactory filaments pass through ethmoid bone - Skull fractures — Linear vs. depressed fractures - Raised intracranial pressure (ICP) — Anterior fontanelle bulges in infants before suture closure - Temporomandibular disorder (TMD) — Jaw pain, clicking - Anterior fontanelle — Closes around 18 months of age
