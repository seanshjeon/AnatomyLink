Mandible

The mandible is the only freely movable bone in the facial skeleton. It is essential for mastication (chewing), speech, and breathing.

🔗 Connected Structures

- Skull — Articulates with the temporal bone via the temporomandibular joint (TMJ) - Teeth — Lower 16 teeth embedded in the mandibular alveolar bone

🔗 Associated Muscles

- Masseter — Inserts on the ramus and angle; primary jaw closer

🗂️ Structure

Part Description
Body U-shaped horizontal portion; holds lower 16 Teeth
Ramus Vertical portion, bilateral
Condylar process Forms the TMJ articulation
Coronoid process Temporalis muscle attachment
Mental foramen Mental nerve & vessels
Mandibular foramen Entry for inferior alveolar nerve

🦷 Joint

Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) - Type: Combined ellipsoidal + hinge joint - Movements: Opening/closing, protrusion/retrusion, lateral excursion - Contains an articular disc

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Mandibular fractures — Common in trauma (MVA, assault) - TMJ disorder (TMD) — Affects 10–15% of the population - Impacted wisdom teeth — Mandibular third molars commonly impacted - Inferior alveolar nerve block — Injected at mandibular foramen for dental procedures
