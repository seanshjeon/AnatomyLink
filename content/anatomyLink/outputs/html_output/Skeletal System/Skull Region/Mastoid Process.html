Mastoid Process

The mastoid process is a conical bony projection of the temporal bone, located posterior to the ear (behind the earlobe), serving as the primary attachment for the Sternocleidomastoid muscle.

🔗 Key Connections

- Skull — part of the temporal bone, posterolateral skull base - Sternocleidomastoid — inserts on the mastoid process (rotates/flexes neck) - Splenius Capitis — inserts on the mastoid process - Digastric — posterior belly attaches to mastoid notch

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Location Posterolateral skull base, behind external acoustic meatus
Bone Temporal bone (mastoid part)
Air cells Mastoid air cells communicate with middle ear via antrum
Palpation Easily palpable behind the ear

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Mastoiditis — Bacterial infection of mastoid air cells (complication of otitis media); tenderness + erythema behind ear - Mastoidectomy — Surgical removal of infected mastoid air cells - Facial nerve proximity — CN VII exits skull through stylomastoid foramen just anterior to mastoid
