Wisdom Tooth (Third Molar)

Wisdom teeth (third molars) are the last of the 4 Molars in each quadrant, normally erupting between 17–25 years. They are the most commonly impacted teeth in the mouth due to insufficient jaw space in modern humans.

🗂️ Basic Facts

Property Detail
Count 4 (one per quadrant: #1, #16, #17, #32)
Eruption 17–25 years ("age of wisdom")
Impaction rate ~70% of people have at least one impacted wisdom tooth
Congenitally absent ~20–25% of people are missing at least one
Roots Highly variable (1–5 roots, often fused)

🔑 Impaction Classification (Winter's Classification)

Angulation Description
Mesioangular Most common (~44%); tilted forward toward 2nd molar
Vertical Upright but no space to erupt (~38%)
Horizontal Lies horizontal; most difficult extraction
Distoangular Tilted backward; rarest

Depth classification (Pell & Gregory): - Class A/B/C — depth relative to occlusal plane - Class I/II/III — space between 2nd molar and ramus

🔑 Why Wisdom Teeth Are Impacted

Modern human jaws are smaller than ancestral jaws due to dietary changes (softer diet → less jaw development). Third molars often lack space to fully erupt, becoming trapped (impacted) against:

- Overlying gum/bone

🏥 Clinical Significance

Complications of Impacted Wisdom Teeth

Complication Description
Pericoronitis Gum infection around partially erupted wisdom tooth; pain + trismus
Caries Decay on the wisdom tooth itself or distal surface of 2nd molar
Periodontal disease Deep pocketing distal to 2nd molar
Dentigerous cyst Follicular cyst forming around impacted crown
Resorption External root resorption of adjacent 2nd molar
Crowding Debated — may contribute to anterior crowding

Extraction Considerations

- Inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) — Runs in the mandibular canal; lower wisdom teeth may be close to or touching the IAN → risk of lip/chin numbness (paresthesia) post-extraction - Lingual nerve — At risk in lower 3rd molar extractions → tongue numbness - Panoramic X-ray (OPG) — Standard pre-extraction imaging; evaluates root morphology + nerve proximity - CBCT — For high-risk cases where roots contact the mandibular canal - Dry socket (alveolar osteitis) — 2–5% of extractions; 30% of wisdom teeth; blood clot dislodges → severe pain day 3–5

When to Extract

- Recurrent pericoronitis - Non-restorable decay - Damage to adjacent 2nd molar - Pathology (cyst, tumor) associated with follicle - Prophylactically (before age 25 — easier + fewer complications)

🔗 Key Connections

- Molars — wisdom teeth are the 3rd molars - Teeth — part of the permanent dentition - Mandible — lower wisdom teeth; proximity to mandibular canal (inferior alveolar nerve) - Skull — upper wisdom teeth; proximity to maxillary sinus - Cranial Nerves — CN V3 (inferior alveolar + lingual nerve) at risk during lower extraction
