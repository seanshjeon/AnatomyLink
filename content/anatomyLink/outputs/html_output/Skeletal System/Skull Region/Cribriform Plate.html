Cribriform Plate

The cribriform plate (Latin: cribrum = sieve) is the horizontal portion of the ethmoid bone forming the roof of the nasal cavity and the floor of the anterior cranial fossa. Its numerous small foramina transmit the olfactory nerve fibers (CN I).

🔗 Key Connections

- Skull — ethmoid bone, anterior cranial fossa floor - Cranial Nerves — CN I (olfactory) filaments pass through cribriform foramina - Olfactory bulb — lies on the superior surface of the cribriform plate (intracranial)

🗂️ Key Facts

Property Detail
Bone Ethmoid (cribriform part)
Location Anterior cranial fossa floor / nasal cavity roof
Foramina Multiple small holes for olfactory nerve filaments
Crista galli Vertical midline projection; dural attachment

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Anosmia — Cribriform plate fractures sever olfactory filaments → loss of smell (CN I injury) - CSF rhinorrhea — Cribriform fracture creates pathway for CSF to leak into nasal cavity (halo sign on pillow; β2-transferrin test) - Meningitis risk — CSF leak = open pathway for bacteria from nasal cavity to meninges - Esthesioneuroblastoma — Olfactory neuroblastoma arising near cribriform plate
