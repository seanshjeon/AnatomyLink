Molars

Molars are the 12 large posterior teeth (6 upper, 6 lower) designed for grinding and pulverizing food. They have the largest crowns and multiple roots. The third molars (wisdom teeth) are the last to erupt and most commonly impacted.

🗂️ Types

Type Count Eruption Roots Notes
1st molar ("6-year molar") 4 6–7 years Upper: 3; Lower: 2 First permanent tooth to erupt
2nd molar ("12-year molar") 4 11–13 years Upper: 3; Lower: 2 Erupts ~6 years after 1st
3rd molar (Wisdom Tooth wisdom tooth) 4 17–25 years Variable (1–4) Most commonly impacted

Universal numbers: Upper #1–3, #14–16 / Lower #17–19, #30–32

🔑 Key Anatomy

- Cusps — Upper: 4–5 cusps; Lower: 5 cusps (lower 1st molar has 5 cusps) - Upper molar roots — 3 roots: mesiobuccal (MB), distobuccal (DB), palatal - MB2 canal — Additional canal in mesiobuccal root (found in ~70% of upper 1st molars; often missed!) - Lower molar roots — 2 roots: mesial + distal; mesial root often has 2 canals - Furcation — Area between roots; furcation involvement = advanced periodontitis

🔑 First Molar — Most Important Tooth

The upper and lower first molars are considered the most important teeth for: - Occlusal stability — Foundation of bite - Vertical dimension — Loss → collapsed bite - First to decay — Deep fissures trap bacteria before full eruption

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Caries — 1st molars most commonly decayed due to deep pit-and-fissure anatomy - Sealants — Applied to prevent pit-and-fissure caries in children - Root canal (endodontics) — Upper 1st molars most commonly treated (4 canals including MB2) - Furcation involvement — Sign of advanced periodontitis; poor prognosis - Early loss — Loss of 1st molar before age 10 → significant drift and malocclusion - Impacted 3rd molar — Wisdom teeth impaction → pericoronitis, caries on 2nd molar

🔗 Key Connections

- Teeth — 12 of 32 permanent teeth (largest group) - Wisdom Tooth — 3rd molars; most clinically significant subgroup - Mandible — lower molars (#17–19, #30–32) - Skull — upper molars (#1–3, #14–16) in maxilla; upper molars close to maxillary sinus - Cranial Nerves — CN V2 (upper, posterior superior alveolar nerve) + CN V3 (lower, inferior alveolar nerve) - Masseter — primary muscle generating force on molars
