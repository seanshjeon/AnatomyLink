Muscular System

The human body has over 600 skeletal muscles. Muscles work by attaching to bones via tendons — contracting to produce movement at joints.

📌 Key Concepts

- Origin — The fixed, proximal attachment point of a muscle - Insertion — The movable, distal attachment point - Action — The movement produced by contraction - Innervation — The nerve supplying the muscle - Agonist / Antagonist — Muscles working together or in opposition

🗂️ Muscles by Region

Head & Neck

- Sternocleidomastoid — Neck flexion & rotation - Masseter — Jaw closing (mastication)

Trunk

- Pectoralis Major — Shoulder flexion, adduction, internal rotation - Trapezius — Scapular elevation, retraction, depression - Latissimus Dorsi — Shoulder extension, adduction, internal rotation - Erector Spinae — Spinal extension and postural support - Rectus Abdominis — Trunk flexion, abdominal compression

Upper Limb

- Deltoid — Shoulder abduction - Rotator Cuff — Shoulder stability (SITS muscles) - Biceps Brachii — Elbow flexion & forearm supination - Triceps Brachii — Elbow extension

Lower Limb

- Iliopsoas — Hip flexion - Gluteus Maximus — Hip extension - Gluteus Medius — Hip abduction - Quadriceps Femoris — Knee extension - Hamstrings — Knee flexion & hip extension - Gastrocnemius — Plantarflexion & knee flexion - Tibialis Anterior — Dorsiflexion & foot inversion
