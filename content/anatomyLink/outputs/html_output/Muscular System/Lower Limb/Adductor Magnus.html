Adductor Magnus

The adductor magnus is the largest adductor, with two distinct parts: an adductor part (from pubis) and a hamstring part (from Ischial Tuberosity). The adductor hiatus in the muscle allows the femoral artery to pass from the anterior to posterior thigh.

🔗 Key Connections

- Adductor part origin — Inferior pubic ramus + ischiopubic ramus - Hamstring part origin — Ischial Tuberosity of Hip Bone - Insertion — Linea Aspera + adductor tubercle (medial femoral condyle) of Femur - Nerve — Adductor part: Obturator Nerve (L2–L4); Hamstring part: Sciatic Nerve (tibial, L4) - Action — Hip adduction; adductor part flexes; hamstring part extends hip

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Adductor hiatus — Opening between adductor and hamstring parts; femoral vessels pass to popliteal fossa - Adductor strain — Groin pain in athletes; "groin pull"
