Tensor Fasciae Latae (TFL)

The TFL is a small gluteal muscle that tenses the Iliotibial Band (ITB). It assists in hip flexion, abduction, and internal rotation.

🔗 Key Connections

- Nerve — Superior Gluteal Nerve (L4, L5) - Action — Hip flexion, abduction, internal rotation; tenses ITB

🏥 Clinical Significance

- IT band syndrome — TFL tightness causes ITB friction at lateral femoral condyle → lateral knee pain in runners - Hip abductor weakness — TFL overactivation compensating for weak gluteus medius
