Sartorius

The sartorius is the longest muscle in the human body (~50 cm), running diagonally across the anterior thigh from the ASIS to the medial tibia (pes anserinus). It flexes, abducts, and externally rotates the hip, and flexes the knee.

🔗 Key Connections

- Nerve — Femoral Nerve (L2, L3) - Action — Hip flexion + abduction + ER; knee flexion ("tailor's position")

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Pes anserinus bursitis — Inflammation at the pes anserinus insertion; medial knee pain - ASIS avulsion — Sartorius avulsion fracture in adolescents during sprinting
