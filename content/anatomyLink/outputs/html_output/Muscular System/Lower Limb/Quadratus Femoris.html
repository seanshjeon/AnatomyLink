Quadratus Femoris

The quadratus femoris is a flat, square-shaped deep external rotator of the hip, connecting the Ischial Tuberosity to the intertrochanteric crest of the Femur.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Ischial Tuberosity of Hip Bone (lateral aspect) - Insertion — Intertrochanteric crest of Femur - Nerve — Nerve to quadratus femoris (L4, L5, S1) - Action — Hip external rotation + adduction

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Ischiofemoral impingement — Quadratus femoris compressed between lesser trochanter and ischial tuberosity - Quadratus femoris tear/oedema — Seen on MRI; hip pain + external rotator weakness
