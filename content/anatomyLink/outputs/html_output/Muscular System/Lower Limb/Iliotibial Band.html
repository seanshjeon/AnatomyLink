Iliotibial Band (ITB)

The iliotibial band (IT band / iliotibial tract) is a thick band of fascia lata running down the lateral thigh from the Iliac Crest to Gerdy's tubercle on the lateral Tibia. It receives the insertions of Gluteus Maximus and Tensor Fasciae Latae.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Iliac Crest (tubercle); Gluteus Maximus + Tensor Fasciae Latae insert into it - Insertion — Gerdy's tubercle (anterolateral Tibia) - Crosses — Lateral femoral condyle at ~30° of knee flexion

🏥 Clinical Significance

- IT band syndrome (ITBS) — Most common running overuse injury; friction of ITB over lateral femoral condyle → lateral knee pain at 30° flexion; treated with stretching, activity modification - Segond fracture — Avulsion of lateral tibial rim by ITB (+ anterior capsule); associated with ACL tear

📍 Location

Muscular System → Lower Limb → Iliotibial Band
