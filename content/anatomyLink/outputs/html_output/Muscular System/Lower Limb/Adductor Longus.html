Adductor Longus

The adductor longus is the most anterior adductor, forming the medial border of the femoral triangle. It is the most commonly strained adductor.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Pubic body (below pubic crest) - Insertion — Middle third of Linea Aspera of Femur - Nerve — Obturator Nerve (L2–L4) - Action — Hip adduction + slight flexion

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Most commonly strained adductor — Groin strain in soccer, hockey players - Femoral triangle — Forms medial border (lateral = sartorius; superior = inguinal ligament)
