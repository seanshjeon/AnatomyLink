Biceps Femoris

The biceps femoris is the lateral hamstring muscle with two heads. It is the primary lateral knee flexor and a key hip extensor, inserting on the Fibular Head.

🔗 Key Connections

- Long head origin — Ischial Tuberosity of Hip Bone - Short head origin — Linea Aspera (lateral lip) of Femur - Insertion — Fibular Head (and lateral Tibia) - Nerve — Long head: Sciatic Nerve (tibial division, L5–S2); Short head: sciatic (fibular division, L5–S1) - Action — Knee flexion + lateral rotation; hip extension (long head)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Hamstring strain — Most common at the musculotendinous junction; sprinting injury - Common fibular nerve — Runs just posterior to the biceps femoris tendon at the knee → at risk in knee dislocations
