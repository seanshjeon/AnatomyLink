Piriformis

The piriformis is a key deep gluteal muscle that externally rotates and abducts the hip. It is the primary landmark of the greater sciatic foramen — the Sciatic Nerve typically exits just below it.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Anterior sacrum (S2–S4) - Insertion — Greater Trochanter (superior facet) of Femur - Nerve — Nerve to piriformis (S1, S2) - Action — External rotation + abduction of hip (when extended) - Relation — Sciatic Nerve exits below piriformis in 85%; above in 10%; through in 5%

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Piriformis syndrome — Sciatic nerve compression at the Greater Sciatic Notch/piriformis; buttock pain + sciatica; FAIR test positive - Injection landmark — Divides the greater sciatic foramen into superior (superior gluteal) and inferior (inferior gluteal + sciatic) compartments

📍 Location

Muscular System → Lower Limb → Piriformis (Deep Gluteal)
