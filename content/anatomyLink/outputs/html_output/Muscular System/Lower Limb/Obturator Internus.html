Obturator Internus

The obturator internus is a deep external rotator of the hip, originating within the pelvis and exiting through the lesser sciatic foramen to insert on the Greater Trochanter.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Inner surface of Hip Bone (around obturator foramen) - Exit route — Lesser sciatic foramen (makes a 90° turn around the ischial spine) - Insertion — Medial surface of Greater Trochanter of Femur - Nerve — Nerve to obturator internus (L5, S1, S2) - Action — Hip external rotation (extended); hip abduction (flexed)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Piriformis-like syndrome — Obturator internus spasm can irritate the sciatic nerve - Deep gluteal syndrome — One of the "POPS" muscles (Piriformis, Obturator internus, Gemelli, Quadratus femoris)
