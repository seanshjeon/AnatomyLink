Semimembranosus

The semimembranosus is the deepest medial hamstring, originating from the Ischial Tuberosity and inserting primarily on the medial Tibia condyle. It forms a major component of the posterior knee capsule (oblique popliteal ligament).

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Ischial Tuberosity of Hip Bone - Insertion — Posteromedial Tibia (medial condyle); contributes to posterior knee capsule - Nerve — Sciatic Nerve (tibial division, L5–S2) - Action — Knee flexion + medial rotation; hip extension; tightens posterior capsule

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Baker's cyst — Semimembranosus bursa enlarges → popliteal fossa swelling; associated with knee effusion
