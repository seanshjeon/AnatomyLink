Semitendinosus

The semitendinosus is the medial hamstring with a long tendon, originating from the Ischial Tuberosity and inserting at the pes anserinus on the medial Tibia.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Ischial Tuberosity of Hip Bone - Insertion — Pes anserinus (medial Tibia) - Nerve — Sciatic Nerve (tibial division, L5–S2) - Action — Knee flexion + medial rotation; hip extension

🏥 Clinical Significance

- ACL reconstruction graft — Semitendinosus (+ gracilis) tendons harvested for hamstring ACL graft - Pes anserinus bursitis — Insertion with sartorius and gracilis at medial tibia

📍 Location

Muscular System → Lower Limb → Hamstrings → Semitendinosus
