Brachialis

The brachialis is the primary elbow flexor, lying deep to the Biceps Brachii on the anterior humerus. Unlike the biceps, it inserts on the ulna (not the radius) and works regardless of forearm rotation.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Anterior distal half of Humerus (below deltoid tuberosity) - Insertion — Coronoid Process and ulnar tuberosity of Ulna - Nerve — Musculocutaneous Nerve (C5, C6); small lateral slip by Radial Nerve - Action — Elbow flexion (in all positions — pronated, supinated, neutral)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Pure elbow flexor — Works in all forearm positions (unlike biceps which supinates) - Anterior interosseous nerve — Not related, but brachialis is a landmark for AIN branches - Brachialis strain — "Climber's elbow" from repeated heavy elbow flexion
