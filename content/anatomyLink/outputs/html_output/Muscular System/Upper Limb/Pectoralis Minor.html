Pectoralis Minor

The pectoralis minor is a thin triangular muscle lying deep to Pectoralis Major, connecting the 3rd–5th ribs to the Coracoid Process of the Scapula. It depresses and protracts the scapula and elevates the ribs during forced breathing.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Ribs 3–5 (anterior surfaces near costal cartilages) - Insertion — Coracoid Process of Scapula - Nerve — Medial pectoral nerve (C8, T1) - Action — Depresses/protracts scapula; elevates ribs in forced inspiration

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Pectoralis minor syndrome — Neurovascular compression beneath the muscle → thoracic outlet syndrome variant - Pec minor tightness — Causes forward shoulder posture and scapular winging (anterior tilt) - Landmark — Axillary artery passes posterior to pectoralis minor (divides it into 3 parts)
