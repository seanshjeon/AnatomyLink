Brachioradialis

The brachioradialis is a forearm muscle that acts as an elbow flexor, particularly effective when the forearm is in the neutral (semi-pronated) position. It originates from the lateral supracondylar ridge of the Humerus and inserts at the Styloid Process of Radius.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Lateral supracondylar ridge of Humerus (proximal to lateral epicondyle) - Insertion — Styloid Process of Radius (distal radius) - Nerve — Radial Nerve (C5, C6) — only forearm flexor innervated by radial nerve - Action — Elbow flexion (strongest in neutral/semi-pronated position)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Radial nerve palsy — Brachioradialis tested for radial nerve function above lateral epicondyle - Lateral epicondylitis — Brachioradialis origin near lateral epicondyle may be involved - Distal radius fractures — Brachioradialis insertion at radial styloid may displace fragments
