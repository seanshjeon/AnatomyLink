Coracobrachialis

The coracobrachialis is a slender muscle of the medial arm, connecting the Coracoid Process to the medial humerus. It assists in shoulder flexion and adduction.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Coracoid Process of Scapula (with short head of Biceps Brachii) - Insertion — Medial surface of Humerus (midshaft) - Nerve — Musculocutaneous Nerve (C5, C6, C7) — penetrates coracobrachialis - Action — Shoulder flexion + adduction

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Musculocutaneous nerve — Pierces through the coracobrachialis; entrapment here is rare but possible - Coracobrachialis hypertrophy — Can compress musculocutaneous nerve
