Digastric

The digastric is a two-bellied (digastric) suprahyoid muscle. Its posterior belly attaches to the Mastoid Process; its anterior belly attaches to the mandible. Both bellies join at an intermediate tendon that loops through the hyoid bone.

🔗 Key Connections

- Posterior belly origin — Mastoid Process of temporal bone - Anterior belly origin — Digastric fossa of Mandible - Intermediate tendon — Loops through fibrous pulley attached to hyoid bone

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Swallowing — Digastric elevates hyoid during deglutition - Submandibular triangle — Posterior belly + stylohyoid form posterior boundary - CN VII landmark — Posterior belly is a key surgical landmark for the facial nerve
