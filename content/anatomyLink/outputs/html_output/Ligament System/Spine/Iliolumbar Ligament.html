Iliolumbar Ligament

The iliolumbar ligament is a strong fan-shaped ligament connecting the transverse processes of L4–L5 to the iliac crest. It stabilizes the lumbosacral junction and limits side-bending and rotation.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Transverse processes of L4 and L5 (Transverse Process, Lumbar Vertebrae) - Insertion — Iliac crest (anterior and posterior bands) - Related structures — Sacrum, Hip Bone, Iliac Crest, sacroiliac joint

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Low back pain — Iliolumbar ligament strain is a common source of mechanical low back pain - Lumbosacral instability — Resists excessive motion at the L5–S1 junction - Iliac crest bone graft — Harvest site can damage iliolumbar ligament → postoperative pain - Referred pain — Can refer pain to groin, testis, or posterior thigh
