Supraspinous Ligament

The supraspinous ligament is a strong fibrous cord running along the tips of spinous processes from C7 to the sacrum. It is continuous superiorly with the Ligamentum Nuchae in the cervical spine.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Tips of spinous processes (C7 → sacrum) - Superior continuation — Ligamentum Nuchae (C7 and above) - Adjacent — Interspinous Ligament (deep to it between each level) - Part of — Posterior Ligamentous Complex (PLC)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Lumbar puncture — First ligament pierced when needle enters intervertebral space - Flexion injury — Ruptures in severe hyperflexion/distraction injuries (seatbelt fractures) - Posterior instability — PLC failure (including SSL) indicates need for posterior spinal fusion
