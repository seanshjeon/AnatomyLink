Ligamentum Flavum

The ligamentum flavum ("yellow ligament") connects adjacent laminae throughout the spine. Its high elastin content (80%) allows it to recoil during extension and maintain upright posture without muscular fatigue.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Inferior border of upper lamina → superior border of lower lamina (bilateral, paired) - Levels — C2–C3 to L5–S1 - Related structures — Spinal Cord, Vertebral Column, epidural space (lies just anterior to LF)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Spinal stenosis — Hypertrophy (thickening) of LF narrows the spinal canal → neurogenic claudication - Epidural injection — Loss-of-resistance technique as needle passes through LF into epidural space - Lumbar disc disease — Thickened LF + disc herniation = combined central stenosis

📍 Location

Ligament System → Spine → Ligamentum Flavum
