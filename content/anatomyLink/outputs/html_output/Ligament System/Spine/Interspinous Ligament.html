Interspinous Ligament

The interspinous ligaments are thin fibrous bands connecting adjacent spinous processes throughout the spine. They blend anteriorly with the Ligamentum Flavum and posteriorly with the Supraspinous Ligament.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Between adjacent spinous processes (root to tip) - Levels — T1 to L5 (poorly developed in cervical spine) - Adjacent structures — Ligamentum Flavum (anterior), Supraspinous Ligament (posterior)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Flexion injuries — Rupture in hyperflexion (e.g., posterior ligamentous complex injury) - Lumbar puncture landmark — Needle passes through supraspinous → interspinous → ligamentum flavum → epidural → dural space - Posterior ligamentous complex (PLC) — Interspinous + supraspinous + facet capsules = PLC; injury here = spinal instability
