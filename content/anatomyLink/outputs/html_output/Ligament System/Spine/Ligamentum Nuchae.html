Ligamentum Nuchae

The ligamentum nuchae is a large, triangular fibroelastic ligament extending from the external occipital protuberance to the spinous process of C7. It is a continuation of the supraspinous ligament in the cervical region and serves as a muscle attachment site.

🔗 Key Connections

- Proximal — External occipital protuberance (occiput) - Distal — Spinous processes of C1–C7; continuous with Supraspinous Ligament below - Muscle attachments — Trapezius, Splenius capitis, Rhomboid minor attach to it - Related structures — Cervical Vertebrae, Skull

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Cervical stability — Passive support for the head; limits cervical flexion - Calcification — Can calcify with age; seen on lateral cervical X-ray - Quadruped analogy — Highly developed in grazing animals (e.g., ox) to support head without muscular effort
