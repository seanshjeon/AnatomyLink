Lateral Collateral Ligament (Elbow)

The lateral collateral ligament complex of the elbow provides varus stress resistance and rotatory stability. The most important component is the lateral ulnar collateral ligament (LUCL), which prevents posterolateral rotatory instability (PLRI).

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Lateral Epicondyle of Humerus - Components — Radial collateral ligament (RCL), lateral ulnar collateral ligament (LUCL), annular ligament (Annular Ligament) - LUCL insertion — Supinator crest of Ulna - Counterpart — Medial Collateral Ligament (medial side)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- PLRI (posterolateral rotatory instability) — LUCL insufficiency; elbow subluxes posterolaterally with supination + extension - Lateral pivot shift test — Reproduces PLRI - Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) — Extensor carpi radialis brevis origin at lateral epicondyle; LCL complex nearby - Elbow dislocation — LCL complex usually fails first in posterior elbow dislocation (Horii circle)
