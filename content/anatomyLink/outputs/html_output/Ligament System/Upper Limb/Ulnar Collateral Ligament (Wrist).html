Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist)

The ulnar collateral ligament (UCL) of the wrist is part of the TFCC (triangular fibrocartilage complex), connecting the Styloid Process of Ulna to the triquetrum and pisiform, providing ulnar-side wrist stability.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Styloid Process of Ulna - Insertion — Triquetrum and pisiform (Carpals) - Part of — TFCC (triangular fibrocartilage complex) - Counterpart — Radial Collateral Ligament (Wrist)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- TFCC tear — Ulnar-sided wrist pain with forearm rotation; common in falls or rotational injury - Ulnar styloid fracture — May avulse UCL + TFCC - Press test — Patient pushes up from chair → reproduces ulnar-sided TFCC pain - MRI wrist — Gold standard to assess TFCC and UCL integrity
