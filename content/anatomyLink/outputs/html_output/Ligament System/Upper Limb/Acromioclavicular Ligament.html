Acromioclavicular Ligament

The acromioclavicular (AC) ligament reinforces the AC joint capsule, providing horizontal stability between the acromion and the distal clavicle. Vertical stability is provided by the Coracoclavicular Ligament.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Distal Clavicle ↔ Acromion of Scapula - Joint — Acromioclavicular (AC) joint - Vertical stabilizer — Coracoclavicular Ligament (conoid + trapezoid) - Related structures — Rotator Cuff, subacromial space

🏥 Clinical Significance

- AC joint separation — Rockwood classification I–VI; Grade I = sprain, Grade III = AC + CC ligament rupture - Shoulder separation vs. dislocation — AC separation ≠ GH dislocation (common confusion) - Cross-body adduction test — Reproduces AC joint pain - Direct trauma — Common in contact sports / fall on outstretched hand
