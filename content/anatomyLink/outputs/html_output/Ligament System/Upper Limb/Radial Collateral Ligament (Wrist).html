Radial Collateral Ligament (Wrist)

The radial collateral ligament (RCL) of the wrist extends from the radial styloid to the scaphoid and trapezium, limiting ulnar deviation and providing radial-side stability to the radiocarpal joint.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Styloid Process of Radius - Insertion — Carpals (scaphoid, trapezium) - Counterpart — Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist) - Related structures — Radius, radiocarpal joint, scaphoid

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Radial styloid fracture — May avulse the RCL origin - De Quervain's tenosynovitis — Radial-sided wrist pain (EPB/APL tendons); RCL region proximity causes confusion - Wrist ligament injury — RCL sprain from radial wrist trauma
