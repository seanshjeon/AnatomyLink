Scapholunate Ligament

The scapholunate interosseous ligament (SLIL) connects the scaphoid and lunate, maintaining their normal relationship within the proximal carpal row. It is the most commonly injured intrinsic wrist ligament.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Scaphoid ↔ Lunate (dorsal, membranous, and palmar portions; dorsal band = strongest) - Part of — Proximal carpal row stabilization - Related structures — Carpals, radiocarpal joint, capitate

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Scapholunate dissociation — SL ligament tear → scaphoid flexes, lunate extends → DISI deformity (dorsal intercalated segment instability) - Terry Thomas sign / David Letterman sign — Widened scapholunate gap (>3 mm) on AP wrist X-ray - Watson test (scaphoid shift test) — Dorsal scaphoid pain/clunk with radial deviation while pressing on scaphoid tuberosity - Wrist arthritis — Chronic SL instability → SLAC (scapholunate advanced collapse) wrist arthritis
