Annular Ligament

The annular ligament is a strong ring-shaped band that encircles the radial head, holding it against the radial notch of the Ulna and allowing the radius to rotate freely during pronation/supination.

🔗 Key Connections

- Attachment — Both ends attach to the anterior and posterior margins of the radial notch of the Ulna - Wraps around — Radial Head - Related structures — Radius, Ulna, radiocapitellar joint, proximal radioulnar joint

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Nursemaid's elbow (radial head subluxation) — Annular ligament slips over radial head in children < 5 years when arm is pulled; treated by supination + flexion maneuver - Radial head fracture — May tear the annular ligament; assess stability after ORIF - Posterior interosseous nerve (PIN) — Deep branch of Radial Nerve passes through the arcade of Frohse near the annular ligament
