Coracoclavicular Ligament

The coracoclavicular (CC) ligament suspends the scapula from the clavicle, providing vertical stability to the AC joint. It has two components: the conoid (medial, cone-shaped) and trapezoid (lateral, quadrilateral).

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Coracoid Process of Scapula - Insertion — Inferior surface of distal Clavicle - Two parts — Conoid (posteromedial) + Trapezoid (anterolateral) - AC stabilizer — Acromioclavicular Ligament (horizontal component)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- AC separation Grade III+ — CC ligament rupture leads to inferior scapular displacement and "step deformity" - Coracoclavicular distance — X-ray measurement (normal < 13 mm); increased in Grade III+ - AC reconstruction — CC ligament reconstructed with tendon graft (e.g., gracilis) in high-grade separations - Piano key sign — Distal clavicle springs back up when pushed down
