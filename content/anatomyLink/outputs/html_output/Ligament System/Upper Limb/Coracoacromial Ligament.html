Coracoacromial Ligament

The coracoacromial ligament spans between the Coracoid Process and the Acromion, forming the coracoacromial arch — a rigid roof that prevents superior humeral head escape but also compresses the subacromial space.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Lateral Coracoid Process - Insertion — Anterior-inferior Acromion - Forms — Coracoacromial arch (with acromion and coracoid) - Contents below arch — Supraspinatus tendon, subacromial bursa, biceps tendon (long head)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Subacromial impingement — Coracoacromial arch compresses supraspinatus tendon between it and the humeral head → pain on elevation - Rotator cuff tear — Progressive abrasion from arch leads to supraspinatus tears - Subacromial decompression (SAD) — Acromioplasty may include CA ligament release to expand subacromial space - Acromial morphology — Type III (hooked) acromion + tight CA ligament = highest impingement risk
