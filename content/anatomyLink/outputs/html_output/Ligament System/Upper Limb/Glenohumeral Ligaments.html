Glenohumeral Ligaments

The glenohumeral ligaments (GHL) are three thickenings of the GH joint capsule that provide passive stability to the inherently unstable shoulder. The inferior glenohumeral ligament (IGHL) is the most important, acting as the primary stabilizer in abduction.

🔗 Key Connections

- Superior GHL (SGHL) — Origin: superior glenoid → Insertion: lesser tubercle; limits ER + inferior translation in adduction - Middle GHL (MGHL) — Oblique band; limits ER in mid-range abduction - Inferior GHL (IGHL) — Hammock structure; anterior and posterior bands; primary stabilizer in 90° abduction - Related structures — Glenoid Cavity, Humerus, Rotator Cuff, labrum

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Bankart lesion — Anterior IGHL + labrum avulsion from glenoid rim → anterior instability (most common shoulder dislocation) - HAGL lesion — Humeral Avulsion of Glenohumeral Ligament (IGHL off humeral side) - Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis) — Contracture of GHL + capsule → restricted passive ROM - Anterior dislocation — IGHL is the checkrein that fails with anterior GH dislocation
