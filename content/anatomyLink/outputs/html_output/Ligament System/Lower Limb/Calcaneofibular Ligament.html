Calcaneofibular Ligament (CFL)

The calcaneofibular ligament (CFL) is the middle component of the lateral ankle ligament complex, connecting the Lateral Malleolus to the lateral calcaneus. It stabilizes both the talocrural and subtalar joints against inversion.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Tip of Lateral Malleolus (Fibula) - Insertion — Lateral surface of calcaneus (Tarsals) - Lateral complex — Anterior Talofibular Ligament (ATFL) + CFL + PTFL - Crosses — Both ankle and subtalar joints (unique among ankle ligaments)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Grade II–III ankle sprain — CFL tears after ATFL in severe inversion injuries - Talar tilt test — CFL + ATFL; increased inversion on stress X-ray - Subtalar instability — CFL injury can cause subtalar (calcaneus-talus) instability - Peroneal tendon dislocation — Peroneal tendons lie posterior to CFL; both can be injured together
