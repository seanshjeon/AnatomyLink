Ligamentum Teres

The ligamentum teres (round ligament of the femur) connects the fovea capitis of the femoral head to the acetabular notch. It carries the artery to the femoral head (branch of obturator artery), critically important in children's vascular supply.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Acetabular notch + transverse acetabular ligament (Acetabulum) - Insertion — Fovea capitis of Femoral Head - Neurovascular — Carries medial circumflex femoral / obturator artery branch → supplies femoral head - Related structures — Hip Bone, Femur, acetabular labrum

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Avascular necrosis (AVN) — In children, ligamentum teres is critical blood supply; disruption (e.g., hip dislocation, Perthes disease) → AVN of femoral head - Hip dislocation — Ligamentum teres tears in traumatic hip dislocation - Adults — Less critical vascular role than in children; small contribution only - MRI hip — Ligamentum teres tears identified on MRI; associated with hip instability
