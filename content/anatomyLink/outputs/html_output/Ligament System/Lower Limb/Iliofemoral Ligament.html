Iliofemoral Ligament

The iliofemoral ligament (Y-ligament of Bigelow) is the strongest ligament in the human body, spanning the anterior hip joint. It prevents hip hyperextension and allows humans to stand erect with minimal muscle activity.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Anterior inferior iliac spine (AIIS) + acetabular rim (Acetabulum) - Insertion — Intertrochanteric line of Femur (two bands: superior → Greater Trochanter, inferior → Lesser Trochanter) - Hip capsule — Anterior reinforcement of hip joint capsule - Related ligaments — Pubofemoral Ligament, Ischiofemoral Ligament

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Anterior hip dislocation — Rare; requires rupture of iliofemoral ligament - Hip hyperextension — Iliofemoral tightness limits hyperextension → useful in standing posture (postural sway control) - Femoral neck fracture risk — Falls in elderly often involve hip hyperextension mechanism - Thomas test — Tests hip flexor tightness; when hip extends, iliofemoral ligament comes under tension
