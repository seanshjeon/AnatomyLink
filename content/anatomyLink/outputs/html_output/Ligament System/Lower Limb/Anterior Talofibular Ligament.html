Anterior Talofibular Ligament (ATFL)

The anterior talofibular ligament (ATFL) is the most commonly sprained ligament in the body, connecting the anterior Lateral Malleolus to the talar neck. It resists anterior talar displacement and inversion in plantar flexion.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Anterior border of Lateral Malleolus (Fibula) - Insertion — Lateral talar neck (Talus) - Lateral ankle complex — ATFL + Calcaneofibular Ligament + PTFL (posterior talofibular ligament) - Medial counterpart — Deltoid Ligament

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Lateral ankle sprain — ATFL is first ligament torn in inversion sprain (Grade I = stretch, Grade III = complete tear) - Anterior drawer test (ankle) — Anterior talar translation; ATFL integrity - Talar tilt test — Combined ATFL + CFL injury; increased inversion - Chronic ankle instability — Recurrent sprains from lax ATFL - Broström repair — Anatomic ATFL reconstruction for chronic instability
