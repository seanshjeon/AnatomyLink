Deltoid Ligament

The deltoid ligament is a strong triangular medial ankle ligament complex connecting the Medial Malleolus to the talus, navicular, and calcaneus. It resists eversion and is far stronger than the lateral ligaments.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Medial Malleolus (Tibia) - Four components — Tibionavicular, tibiocalcaneal, anterior tibiotalar, posterior tibiotalar (superficial + deep) - Insertion — Talus (Talus), navicular, sustentaculum tali of calcaneus - Counterpart — Lateral complex: Anterior Talofibular Ligament + Calcaneofibular Ligament

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Medial ankle sprain — Rare (only ~15% of ankle sprains); requires greater force than lateral - Ankle fracture — Medial malleolus fracture or deltoid ligament rupture in bimalleolar or Maisonneuve fractures - Pronation-abduction injury — Deltoid tears in pronation-type ankle fractures (Lauge-Hansen classification) - Medial ankle swelling — Deltoid tear → medial side bruising + tender medial malleolus region
