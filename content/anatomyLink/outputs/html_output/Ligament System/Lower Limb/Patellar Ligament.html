Patellar Ligament

The patellar ligament (often called patellar tendon) is the distal continuation of the Quadriceps Femoris tendon, connecting the inferior pole of the Patella to the Tibial Tuberosity. It transmits quadriceps force for knee extension.

🔗 Key Connections

- Origin — Inferior pole of Patella - Insertion — Tibial Tuberosity - Proximal — Continuous with Quadriceps Femoris tendon - Related structures — Infrapatellar fat pad (Hoffa's fat pad), infrapatellar bursa, Patella

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Patellar tendinopathy ("jumper's knee") — Overuse injury at inferior pole of patella; pain with loading - Patellar tendon rupture — Disrupts knee extension mechanism; gap below patella + patella alta on X-ray - Osgood-Schlatter disease — Traction apophysitis at tibial tuberosity in adolescents - ACL reconstruction — Bone-patellar tendon-bone (BTB) graft harvested from central third of patellar ligament - Hoffa's syndrome — Impingement of infrapatellar fat pad adjacent to patellar ligament
