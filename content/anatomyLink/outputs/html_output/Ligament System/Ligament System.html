🦷 Ligament System

Ligaments are dense connective tissue bands that connect bone to bone, providing joint stability and guiding motion. Unlike tendons (muscle→bone), ligaments are purely structural stabilizers.

---

Spine

Ligament Function
Anterior Longitudinal Ligament Prevents hyperextension
Posterior Longitudinal Ligament Prevents hyperflexion
Ligamentum Flavum Connects laminae; maintains posture
Ligamentum Nuchae Cervical spine support
Interspinous Ligament Limits flexion between spinous processes
Supraspinous Ligament Tips of spinous processes
Iliolumbar Ligament L4–L5 to iliac crest stability

---

Upper Limb

Shoulder

Ligament Function
Acromioclavicular Ligament AC joint horizontal stability
Coracoclavicular Ligament Vertical clavicle suspension
Glenohumeral Ligaments GH joint capsule reinforcement
Coracoacromial Ligament Superior shoulder arch

Elbow

Ligament Function
Medial Collateral Ligament Valgus stress stabilizer (UCL)
Lateral Collateral Ligament (Elbow) Varus + rotatory stability
Annular Ligament Holds radial head in ulna

Wrist

Ligament Function
Radial Collateral Ligament (Wrist) Radial deviation limit
Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist) Ulnar deviation limit
Scapholunate Ligament Carpal row stability

---

Lower Limb

Hip

Ligament Function
Iliofemoral Ligament Strongest ligament in body; prevents hyperextension
Pubofemoral Ligament Limits abduction + extension
Ischiofemoral Ligament Limits medial rotation
Ligamentum Teres Femoral head attachment; carries blood supply in children

Knee

Ligament Function
Anterior Cruciate Ligament Prevents anterior tibial translation
Posterior Cruciate Ligament Prevents posterior tibial translation
Medial Collateral Ligament (Knee) Valgus stress stabilizer
Lateral Collateral Ligament (Knee) Varus stress stabilizer
Patellar Ligament Quad mechanism; connects patella to tibia

Ankle & Foot

Ligament Function
Anterior Talofibular Ligament Most commonly sprained ligament
Calcaneofibular Ligament Lateral ankle stability
Deltoid Ligament Medial ankle stability complex
Plantar Fascia Foot arch support

Pelvis

Ligament Function
Sacrotuberous Ligament Sacrum–ischial tuberosity; pelvic stability
Sacrospinous Ligament Creates sciatic foramina

---

Head and Neck

Ligament Function
Transverse Atlantal Ligament Holds dens against atlas
Alar Ligament Limits rotation of head
Temporomandibular Ligament TMJ lateral stability
