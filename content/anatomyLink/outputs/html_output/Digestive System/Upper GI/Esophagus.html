Esophagus

The esophagus is a muscular tube ~25 cm long connecting the pharynx to the Stomach, passing through the neck, posterior mediastinum, and diaphragm. It has no mesentery and no serosal layer — crucial for surgical anastomosis leaks.

🔗 Key Connections

- Begins at: C6 (Cervical Vertebrae) — cricoid cartilage level - Passes through: posterior mediastinum alongside Thoracic Vertebrae - Pierces diaphragm at: T10 (oesophageal hiatus); relation to Ribs - Joins Stomach at: gastro-oesophageal (GO) junction (T11) - Posterior to: trachea, left main bronchus, Heart (LA) - Blood supply: inferior thyroid artery (cervical), oesophageal branches of descending aorta (Aorta), left gastric artery (Celiac Trunk) - Venous drainage: azygos vein → Superior Vena Cava (upper); left gastric vein → Portal System (lower) — portosystemic anastomosis

🗂️ Key Information

Property Detail
Length ~25 cm (15 cm from incisors to start, 40 cm to stomach)
Muscle type Upper 1/3: striated; Middle 1/3: mixed; Lower 1/3: smooth
Layers Mucosa → submucosa → muscularis propria → adventitia (NO serosa)
Innervation Vagus nerve (CN X) + sympathetic chain

🔑 Three Anatomical Narrowings (Sites of Foreign Body / Cancer)

1. Cricopharyngeal sphincter (C6) — upper oesophageal sphincter (UOS); 15 cm from incisors 2. Aortic arch + left main bronchus crossing — 25 cm from incisors 3. Oesophageal hiatus (T10) — 40 cm from incisors; lower oesophageal sphincter (LOS) zone

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD) — LOS incompetence → acid reflux → heartburn; risk: obesity, hiatus hernia, pregnancy - Hiatus hernia — stomach herniates through oesophageal hiatus; sliding (most common, 95%) or rolling (paraesophageal, 5%) - Barrett's oesophagus — metaplasia of squamous → columnar epithelium; complication of chronic GORD; precursor to adenocarcinoma - Oesophageal carcinoma — squamous (upper/mid, risk: smoking + alcohol) vs. adenocarcinoma (lower, risk: GORD + Barrett's) - Achalasia — failure of LOS relaxation + loss of peristalsis; dysphagia to liquids + solids; "bird's beak" on barium swallow; treat: pneumatic dilatation or Heller myotomy - Oesophageal varices — dilated veins at GO junction due to portal hypertension (Portal System); rupture → massive haematemesis; treat: terlipressin + endoscopic band ligation - Mallory-Weiss tear — mucosal tear at GO junction after forceful vomiting → haematemesis; usually self-limiting - Boerhaave syndrome — full-thickness oesophageal rupture after violent vomiting → surgical emergency; Mackler triad: vomiting + chest pain + surgical emphysema
