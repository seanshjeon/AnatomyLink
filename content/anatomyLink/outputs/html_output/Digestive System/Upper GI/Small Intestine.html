Small Intestine

The small intestine is a 6-metre tube where virtually all nutrient absorption occurs. It is divided into duodenum (25 cm), jejunum (~2.5 m), and ileum (~3.5 m). Its enormous absorptive surface (~200 m²) is achieved by villi, microvilli (brush border), and circular folds (plicae circulares).

🔗 Key Connections

- Receives from: Stomach (via pylorus → duodenum) - Empties into: Large Intestine (ileocaecal valve) - Blood supply: - Duodenum: Celiac Trunk (superior pancreaticoduodenal) + SMA (inferior pancreaticoduodenal) - Jejunum + ileum: Superior mesenteric artery (SMA) - Venous drainage: → Portal System (superior mesenteric vein) - Relations: Pancreas (posterior to duodenum), Lumbar Vertebrae (duodenum wraps around L1–L3)

🗂️ Three Segments

Segment Length Location Key Features
Duodenum 25 cm Retroperitoneal (C-shape around Pancreas head) Bile + pancreatic ducts open at ampulla of Vater (2nd part); Brunner's glands (alkaline mucus); most ulcers here
Jejunum ~2.5 m Intraperitoneal (left upper abdomen) Tall villi, thick wall, prominent plicae, active absorption; valvulae conniventes visible on imaging
Ileum ~3.5 m Intraperitoneal (right lower abdomen) Shorter villi; Peyer's patches (lymphoid follicles); terminal ileum absorbs B12 + bile salts (unique!)

🗂️ Specialised Absorptive Mechanisms

Nutrient Site Mechanism
Carbohydrates Duodenum/jejunum Disaccharidases (brush border) → monosaccharides → SGLT1/GLUT5
Proteins Duodenum/jejunum Peptidases → amino acids → Na⁺-linked cotransport
Fats Jejunum Micelles → fatty acids → chylomicrons → lacteals → lymph
Iron Duodenum Ferric (Fe³⁺) reduced to ferrous (Fe²⁺); absorbed as DMT1; ferroportin to blood
Calcium Duodenum Vitamin D-dependent (calcitriol → calbindin)
B12 (cobalamin) Terminal ileum Requires intrinsic factor (from Stomach parietal cells) → cubilin receptor
Bile salts Terminal ileum Active reabsorption → enterohepatic circulation

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Duodenal ulcer — most common peptic ulcer; anterior wall → perforation → peritonitis; posterior wall → bleeds (gastroduodenal artery) - Coeliac disease — autoimmune reaction to gluten → villous atrophy (duodenum/proximal jejunum) → malabsorption; anti-tTG antibodies; treat: gluten-free diet - Crohn's disease — transmural inflammation; any segment but terminal ileum most common; skip lesions; cobblestone mucosa; fistulas; strictures; treat: steroids, biologics, surgery - Small bowel obstruction (SBO) — most common cause: adhesions (post-surgery); also hernias; presents with colicky pain, distension, vomiting, absolute constipation; central dilated loops on AXR - Meckel's diverticulum — persistence of vitello-intestinal duct; 2% population; 2 feet from ileocaecal valve; 2 cm long; ectopic gastric/pancreatic tissue; presents with painless rectal bleeding (children) - Short bowel syndrome — <200 cm functional small bowel; malabsorption + TPN dependence; after massive resection (ischaemia, Crohn's, volvulus)
