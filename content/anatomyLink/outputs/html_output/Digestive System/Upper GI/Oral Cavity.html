Oral Cavity

The oral cavity is the entry point of the digestive system, bounded by the lips, cheeks, hard and soft palate, and floor of the mouth. It initiates mechanical digestion (chewing) and chemical digestion (salivary amylase), and is essential for speech and swallowing.

🔗 Key Connections

- Bounded by: Mandible (floor/lower teeth), Skull (hard palate / maxilla above) - Continues into: pharynx → Esophagus - Salivary glands: parotid (CN IX), submandibular, sublingual (CN VII) - Muscles: Masseter (mastication), tongue (CN XII) - Innervation: Cranial Nerves — CN V (sensation), CN VII (taste anterior tongue), CN IX (taste posterior tongue), CN XII (tongue movement)

🗂️ Key Structures

Structure Description
Lips Orbicularis oris (CN VII); red zone = vermilion border
Hard palate Anterior; bone (maxilla + palatine bones); separates oral from nasal cavity
Soft palate Posterior; muscular; uvula at midline; elevates during swallowing to close nasopharynx
Tongue Intrinsic + extrinsic muscles (genioglossus — main protrudes; CN XII); taste via CN VII (anterior 2/3), CN IX (posterior 1/3)
Teeth 32 permanent; incisors, canines, premolars, molars
Salivary glands Parotid (stensen's duct, opposite upper 2nd molar), submandibular (Wharton's duct), sublingual (multiple ducts)

🔑 Swallowing (Deglutition) — 3 Phases

1. Oral (voluntary) — tongue pushes bolus to pharynx 2. Pharyngeal (reflex) — soft palate closes nasopharynx; larynx elevates; epiglottis covers larynx; pharyngeal constrictors propel bolus 3. Oesophageal (involuntary) — peristalsis carries bolus to Stomach

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Oral cancer — most common on lateral tongue and floor of mouth; squamous cell carcinoma; risk: tobacco + alcohol; HPV (oropharynx) - Dental caries — Streptococcus mutans; demineralisation of enamel - Parotitis — parotid gland inflammation; mumps (paramyxovirus) or bacterial (blocked Stensen's duct); parotid swelling anterior to ear - Salivary gland calculi (sialolithiasis) — submandibular most common (alkaline saliva + dependent drainage); pain/swelling at mealtimes - Cleft lip / palate — failure of fusion of facial processes (week 6–7); most common craniofacial abnormality - CN XII palsy — tongue deviates toward the side of the lesion on protrusion
