Peritoneum

The peritoneum is the serous membrane lining the abdominal cavity (parietal peritoneum) and covering abdominal organs (visceral peritoneum). The potential space between them — the peritoneal cavity — normally contains ~50 mL of lubricating fluid.

🔗 Key Connections

- Contains: Stomach, Small Intestine, Large Intestine (transverse, sigmoid), Liver, Gallbladder, spleen, uterus - Retroperitoneal organs (NOT covered): Pancreas, ascending colon, descending colon, duodenum (2nd–4th parts), kidneys, adrenals, Aorta, Inferior Vena Cava - Relations: Lumbar Vertebrae (posterior), Ribs + diaphragm (superior), Hip Bone (pelvic peritoneum)

🗂️ Key Peritoneal Folds & Spaces

Structure Description Contents
Greater omentum Large fatty apron hanging from greater curvature of Stomach Fat, lymph nodes; "policeman of the abdomen" — walls off infection
Lesser omentum From liver → lesser curvature of Stomach + duodenum Hepatogastric + hepatoduodenal ligaments; hepatoduodenal contains portal triad (portal vein, hepatic artery, CBD)
Mesentery (small bowel) Fan-shaped; attaches jejunum + ileum to posterior abdominal wall SMA + SMV, lymphatics, nerves
Transverse mesocolon Attaches transverse colon Middle colic vessels
Sigmoid mesocolon Attaches sigmoid colon Sigmoid vessels; inverted V root
Hepatorenal recess (Morrison's pouch) Between liver and right kidney Most dependent space in supine position → fluid collects here first
Pouch of Douglas (rectouterine) Between rectum and uterus (or bladder in males) Most dependent pelvic space → blood/pus in pelvic pathology

🔑 Retroperitoneal Structures — Mnemonic: SAD PUCKER

Suprarenal glands, Aorta/IVC, Duodenum (2nd–4th), Pancreas, Ureters, Colon (ascending + descending), Kidneys, Esophagus (abdominal), Rectum

🔑 Peritoneal Attachments & Surgical Significance

- Pringle manoeuvre — compresses hepatoduodenal ligament (in lesser omentum) to control hepatic inflow → controls liver haemorrhage - Hartmann's pouch — recess at GB neck; site of stone impaction - Mesenteric ischaemia — SMA embolism → sudden severe abdominal pain; "pain out of proportion to tenderness"; surgical emergency

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Peritonitis — infection/inflammation of peritoneum; causes: perforated peptic ulcer, appendicitis, diverticulitis, Boerhaave; generalised guarding + rigidity + rebound tenderness; surgical emergency - Ascites — fluid accumulation in peritoneal cavity; causes: cirrhosis (portal hypertension + low albumin), malignancy, cardiac failure, TB; diagnostic tap (SBP if neutrophils >250/mm³) - Primary peritoneal cancer — serous carcinoma arising from peritoneum; similar to ovarian cancer; treat: platinum-based chemo - Pneumoperitoneum — free air under diaphragm on erect CXR → perforated viscus until proven otherwise - Hernia — protrusion of peritoneum (peritoneal sac) with contents through abdominal wall defect; inguinal (most common), femoral, umbilical, incisional
