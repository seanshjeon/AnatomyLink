Digestive System

The digestive system is a 9-metre muscular tube and its accessory organs, responsible for ingestion, mechanical and chemical digestion, absorption of nutrients, and excretion of waste. It is divided into the GI tract and accessory organs.

🗂️ Accessory Organs

Organ Function Duct
Liver Bile production, detoxification, metabolism Bile ducts → common bile duct
Gallbladder Bile storage + concentration Cystic duct → common bile duct
Pancreas Exocrine (digestive enzymes) + endocrine (insulin, glucagon) Pancreatic duct (Wirsung) → ampulla of Vater

🔑 Peritoneum & Mesenteries → Peritoneum

Most GI organs are covered by peritoneum. Mesenteries carry vessels and nerves to mobile organs. Retroperitoneal organs (duodenum, ascending/descending colon, Pancreas, kidneys) are fixed.

🔑 Embryological Divisions & Blood Supply (Key Exam Topic)

- Foregut (mouth → proximal duodenum + liver, gallbladder, pancreas) → Celiac Trunk - Midgut (distal duodenum → proximal 2/3 transverse colon) → Superior mesenteric artery (SMA) - Hindgut (distal 1/3 transverse colon → upper anal canal) → Inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Upper GI bleed — haematemesis / melaena; causes: peptic ulcer, varices, Mallory-Weiss tear - Lower GI bleed — haematochezia; causes: diverticulosis, colorectal cancer, IBD, haemorrhoids - Bowel obstruction — small (adhesions, hernia) vs. large (cancer, volvulus) - Peritonitis — peritoneal inflammation → surgical emergency
