Liver

The liver is the largest solid organ in the body (~1.5 kg), occupying the right upper quadrant under the diaphragm. It performs over 500 functions including bile production, detoxification, protein synthesis, glycogen storage, and cholesterol metabolism.

🔗 Key Connections

- Protected by: Ribs (under right dome of diaphragm, ribs 5–10) - Relations: Stomach (left lobe), Gallbladder (inferior), Inferior Vena Cava (posterior groove) - Blood supply (dual): - Portal vein (75%) — nutrient-rich from Portal System - Hepatic artery proper (25%) — oxygenated from Celiac Trunk - Drains into: hepatic veins → Inferior Vena Cava → Heart - Bile flows: bile canaliculi → hepatic ducts → common hepatic duct → Gallbladder (via cystic duct) or Small Intestine (via common bile duct)

🗂️ Anatomical Lobes

Lobe Location Notes
Right lobe Large, right side Separated from left by falciform ligament (anatomical) or middle hepatic vein (functional)
Left lobe Smaller, left of falciform Overlies stomach
Caudate lobe Posterior (segment I) Drains directly into IVC; may enlarge in Budd-Chiari
Quadrate lobe Between GB and falciform Functionally = left lobe

🗂️ Couinaud Segments (Surgical)

8 functionally independent segments (I–VIII), each with its own portal triad (portal vein + hepatic artery + bile duct) and hepatic vein drainage — enables segmental resection.

🗂️ Key Functions

Function Detail
Bile production ~500–1000 mL/day; bile salts emulsify fats for absorption
Protein synthesis Albumin, clotting factors (II, VII, IX, X + protein C, S), fibrinogen; NOT factor VIII (endothelium) or vWF
Gluconeogenesis + glycogenolysis Maintains blood glucose between meals
Detoxification CYP450 enzymes metabolise drugs, alcohol, ammonia → urea
Cholesterol metabolism Synthesis + bile salt conjugation
Iron storage Ferritin; haem catabolism → bilirubin → bile

🔑 Hepatic Triad (Portal Triad)

Each lobule has portal triads at corners: portal vein + hepatic artery + bile duct. Blood flows portal vein → sinusoids → central vein → hepatic veins. Bile flows opposite direction: hepatocytes → canaliculi → bile ducts.

🏥 Clinical Significance

- Hepatitis — viral (A–E), autoimmune, alcoholic; raised transaminases (AST, ALT); jaundice - Cirrhosis — end-stage liver fibrosis; causes: alcohol, NAFLD, viral hepatitis, PBC, PSC, haemochromatosis, Wilson's; complications: portal hypertension, varices, ascites, hepatic encephalopathy, hepatorenal syndrome, HCC - Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) — risk: cirrhosis + hepatitis B; AFP raised; treat: ablation, resection, TACE, sorafenib, transplant - Jaundice — pre-hepatic (haemolysis), hepatic (hepatitis, cirrhosis), post-hepatic (obstructive: gallstones, cancer); unconjugated vs. conjugated bilirubin - Liver abscess — pyogenic (E. coli — portal spread from appendicitis/diverticulitis) or amoebic (Entamoeba histolytica); RUQ pain + fever + raised ALP - Liver failure — coagulopathy (↓clotting factors), encephalopathy, hypoglycaemia; PT/INR best marker of synthetic function - Liver transplantation — indicated for acute liver failure, cirrhosis (MELD score), HCC (Milan criteria)
