# Brachioradialis

> The **brachioradialis** is a forearm muscle that acts as an elbow flexor, particularly effective when the forearm is in the neutral (semi-pronated) position. It originates from the lateral supracondylar ridge of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) and inserts at the [Styloid Process of Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Styloid Process of Radius.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Lateral supracondylar ridge of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) (proximal to lateral epicondyle)
- **Insertion** — [Styloid Process of Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Styloid Process of Radius.md) (distal radius)
- **Nerve** — [Radial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Radial Nerve.md) (C5, C6) — only forearm flexor innervated by radial nerve
- **Action** — Elbow flexion (strongest in neutral/semi-pronated position)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Radial nerve palsy** — Brachioradialis tested for radial nerve function above lateral epicondyle
- **Lateral epicondylitis** — Brachioradialis origin near lateral epicondyle may be involved
- **Distal radius fractures** — Brachioradialis insertion at radial styloid may displace fragments
