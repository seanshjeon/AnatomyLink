# **Deltoid**

> The deltoid is the **large, triangular muscle forming the rounded contour of the shoulder**. It is the prime mover of shoulder abduction and has three distinct functional portions.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Clavicle.md) (lateral 1/3 — anterior part) + [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Scapula.md) (acromion — middle part + spine of scapula — posterior part)
- **Insertion**: [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) (deltoid tuberosity, mid-lateral shaft)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Lateral [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Clavicle.md) + acromion + scapular spine ([Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Scapula.md)) |
| Insertion | Deltoid tuberosity ([Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md)) |
| Innervation | **[Axillary Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Axillary Nerve.md) (C5, C6)** |
| Blood Supply | Posterior circumflex humeral artery + deltoid branch of thoracoacromial artery |

## 🔑 Three Portions

| Part | Fibers | Primary Action |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Anterior (clavicular) | Flexion fibers | Shoulder **flexion** + internal rotation |
| Middle (acromial) | Abduction fibers | Shoulder **abduction** (15°–90°) |
| Posterior (spinal) | Extension fibers | Shoulder **extension** + external rotation |

> Note: The first 15° of abduction is initiated by the **supraspinatus** ([Rotator Cuff](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Rotator Cuff.md)); deltoid takes over from 15°–90°. Above 90° requires scapular upward rotation ([Trapezius](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Trunk/Trapezius.md) + serratus anterior).

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Axillary nerve injury** — Surgical neck fracture of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) or anterior shoulder dislocation
  - Result: deltoid paralysis → flat shoulder contour ("shoulder pad sign"), loss of abduction, loss of sensation over deltoid (regimental badge area)
- **Deltoid injection site** — IM injection in the middle deltoid (lateral arm, 2–3 fingers below acromion)
- **Deltoid wasting** — Axillary nerve injury or C5 root lesion
- **Anterior deltoid overuse** — Common in bench press, overhead pressing
