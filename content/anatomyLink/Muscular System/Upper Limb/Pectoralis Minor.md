# Pectoralis Minor

> The **pectoralis minor** is a thin triangular muscle lying deep to [Pectoralis Major](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Trunk/Pectoralis Major.md), connecting the 3rd–5th ribs to the [Coracoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Coracoid Process.md) of the [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Scapula.md). It depresses and protracts the scapula and elevates the ribs during forced breathing.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Ribs 3–5 (anterior surfaces near costal cartilages)
- **Insertion** — [Coracoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Coracoid Process.md) of [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Scapula.md)
- **Nerve** — Medial pectoral nerve (C8, T1)
- **Action** — Depresses/protracts scapula; elevates ribs in forced inspiration

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Pectoralis minor syndrome** — Neurovascular compression beneath the muscle → thoracic outlet syndrome variant
- **Pec minor tightness** — Causes forward shoulder posture and scapular winging (anterior tilt)
- **Landmark** — Axillary artery passes posterior to pectoralis minor (divides it into 3 parts)
