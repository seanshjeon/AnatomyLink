# Coracobrachialis

> The **coracobrachialis** is a slender muscle of the medial arm, connecting the [Coracoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Coracoid-Process.md) to the medial humerus. It assists in shoulder flexion and adduction.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Coracoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Coracoid-Process.md) of [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md) (with short head of [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md))
- **Insertion** — Medial surface of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) (midshaft)
- **Nerve** — [Musculocutaneous Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Upper-Limb-Nerves/Musculocutaneous-Nerve.md) (C5, C6, C7) — penetrates coracobrachialis
- **Action** — Shoulder flexion + adduction

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Musculocutaneous nerve** — Pierces through the coracobrachialis; entrapment here is rare but possible
- **Coracobrachialis hypertrophy** — Can compress musculocutaneous nerve
