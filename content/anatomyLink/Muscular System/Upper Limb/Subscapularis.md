# Subscapularis

> Subscapularis is the **anterior rotator cuff muscle** — the largest and strongest of the four — responsible for **internal (medial) rotation** of the shoulder and stabilising the humeral head anteriorly.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Muscular System](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Muscular System.md), [Rotator Cuff](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Rotator Cuff.md)
- Inserts on: **Lesser Tubercle** of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) (only rotator cuff on lesser tubercle)
- Nerve: **Upper + Lower subscapular nerves (C5, C6, C7)** — from posterior cord of [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md)

## 🔬 Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Subscapular fossa (anterior/costal surface of [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Scapula.md)) |
| Insertion | [Lesser Tubercle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Lesser Tubercle.md) of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) + anterior capsule |
| Action | **Internal (medial) rotation**; adduction of shoulder; stabilises humeral head anteriorly |
| Nerve | Upper subscapular nerve (C5–C6) + lower subscapular nerve (C5–C6) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Subscapularis tear** — less common than supraspinatus; often missed; from forced external rotation or fall
- **Belly-press test / Napoleon test** — unable to press abdomen with wrist straight → subscapularis tear
- **Bear-hug test** — inability to hold arm against abdomen suggests subscapularis pathology
- **Internal rotation weakness** — common in anterior shoulder instability
