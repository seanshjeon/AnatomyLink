# Brachialis

> The **brachialis** is the primary **elbow flexor**, lying deep to the [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Biceps Brachii.md) on the anterior humerus. Unlike the biceps, it inserts on the ulna (not the radius) and works regardless of forearm rotation.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Anterior distal half of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) (below deltoid tuberosity)
- **Insertion** — [Coronoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Coronoid Process.md) and ulnar tuberosity of [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md)
- **Nerve** — [Musculocutaneous Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Musculocutaneous Nerve.md) (C5, C6); small lateral slip by [Radial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Radial Nerve.md)
- **Action** — Elbow flexion (in all positions — pronated, supinated, neutral)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Pure elbow flexor** — Works in all forearm positions (unlike biceps which supinates)
- **Anterior interosseous nerve** — Not related, but brachialis is a landmark for AIN branches
- **Brachialis strain** — "Climber's elbow" from repeated heavy elbow flexion
