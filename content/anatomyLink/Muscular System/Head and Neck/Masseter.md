# Masseter

> The masseter is the **most powerful muscle of mastication** relative to its size. It is the primary jaw-closing muscle and is easily palpable when clenching the teeth.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: [[Skull]] (zygomatic arch — zygomatic bone & temporal bone)
- **Insertion**: [[Mandible]] (lateral surface of ramus & angle)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Zygomatic arch ([[Skull]]) |
| Insertion | Ramus & angle of [[Mandible]] |
| Action | **Jaw elevation** (mouth closing); slight protrusion |
| Innervation | Masseteric nerve (CN V3 — mandibular division of trigeminal) |
| Blood Supply | Masseteric artery (from maxillary artery) |

## 🔑 Muscles of Mastication (all CN V3)
| Muscle | Primary Action |
|--------|---------------|
| **Masseter** | Elevation (jaw closing) |
| Temporalis | Elevation + retrusion |
| Medial pterygoid | Elevation + medial excursion |
| Lateral pterygoid | Depression (jaw opening) + protrusion |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Bruxism** — Masseter hypertrophy from teeth grinding; TMJ and temporal pain
- **Masseter botox** — For jaw slimming or bruxism management
- **Trismus (Lockjaw)** — Masseter spasm; seen in tetanus, peritonsillar abscess
- **TMJ disorder** — Masseter tenderness is a key examination finding
- **Parotid gland** — Overlies the masseter; parotid duct crosses the masseter
