# Temporalis

> Temporalis is a **large fan-shaped muscle of mastication** filling the temporal fossa on the lateral skull. It is the **primary muscle of jaw closure** and the principal **retractor** of the mandible.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Muscular System](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Muscular System.md)
- Related: [Mandible](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Skull Region/Mandible.md), [Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Skull Region/Skull.md), [Temporomandibular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Head and Neck/Temporomandibular Ligament.md), [Masseter](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Head and Neck/Masseter.md)
- Nerve: **Deep temporal branches of mandibular nerve (CN V3)** — from trigeminal nerve
- Blood supply: Deep temporal arteries (from maxillary artery)

## 🔬 Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Temporal fossa (inferior temporal line, temporal fascia) |
| Insertion | Coronoid process + anterior border of ramus of [Mandible](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Skull Region/Mandible.md) |
| Action | **Elevation (closure) of mandible**; posterior fibres **retract** mandible |
| Nerve | Deep temporal nerves (CN V3 — mandibular) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) dysfunction** — temporalis spasm contributes to jaw pain, clicking, limited opening
- **Temporal arteritis (GCA)** — temporal artery in scalp (superficial to muscle) becomes inflamed; temporal headache + jaw claudication during chewing → ischaemia of temporalis
- **Trismus** — jaw lock/spasm; tetanus, pericoronitis, TMJ arthritis
- **Temporal flap** — temporalis used in reconstructive surgery (skull base, orbit)
