# Sternocleidomastoid (SCM)

> The SCM is the **largest and most superficial muscle of the neck**, forming the anterior-lateral boundary of the posterior triangle. It is the key muscle for neck rotation and flexion.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: [Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md) (manubrium — sternal head) + [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md) (medial 1/3 — clavicular head)
- **Insertion**: [Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Skull-Region/Skull.md) (mastoid process + superior nuchal line of occipital bone)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Manubrium ([Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md)) + medial [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md) |
| Insertion | Mastoid process + superior nuchal line ([Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Skull-Region/Skull.md)) |
| Action | **Bilateral**: neck flexion; **Unilateral**: ipsilateral lateral flexion + **contralateral rotation** |
| Innervation | Spinal accessory nerve (CN XI) + C2–C3 (proprioception) |
| Blood Supply | Occipital artery, superior thyroid artery |

## 🔑 Anatomical Triangles
The SCM separates:
- **Anterior triangle** (medial to SCM)
- **Posterior triangle** (lateral to SCM)

The **spinal accessory nerve (CN XI)** runs through the posterior triangle → innervates the [Trapezius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Trapezius.md).

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Torticollis (Wry neck)** — Unilateral SCM spasm or fibrosis; head tilts ipsilaterally, chin rotates contralaterally
- **Congenital torticollis** — SCM fibrosis from birth injury
- **CN XI injury** — During posterior triangle surgery → SCM and trapezius weakness
- **Cervical lymph nodes** — Run along the deep surface of SCM; enlarged in infection/malignancy
