# Quadriceps Femoris

> The quadriceps femoris is a group of **4 muscles** forming the anterior thigh — the **sole extensor of the knee** and one of the most powerful muscle groups in the body.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: All 4 originate from the [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) or [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)
- **Insertion**: All 4 converge into the **quadriceps tendon** → [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Patella.md) → **patellar tendon** → [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md) (tibial tuberosity)

## 🗂️ The Four Muscles

| Muscle | Origin | Special Feature |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Rectus femoris | AIIS ([Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)) | Only head crossing the hip joint |
| Vastus lateralis | Lateral [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) (linea aspera + greater trochanter) | Largest of the four |
| Vastus medialis | Medial [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) (linea aspera) | VMO stabilizes the patella medially |
| Vastus intermedius | Anterior [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) shaft | Deep; beneath rectus femoris |

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Insertion | Tibial tuberosity ([Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md)) via quadriceps tendon → [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Patella.md) → patellar tendon |
| Action | Knee **extension** (all heads); hip flexion (rectus femoris only) |
| Innervation | **[Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) (L2–L4)** — all four heads |
| Blood Supply | Femoral artery (muscular branches) |

## 🔑 Vastus Medialis Oblique (VMO)
The distal fibers of vastus medialis run obliquely (VMO) and are the primary **medial stabilizer of the patella**. VMO weakness → lateral patellar tracking → patellofemoral pain syndrome.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Quadriceps tendon rupture** — Usually in patients >40 years; inability to extend knee; palpable defect above patella
- **Patellar tendon rupture** — Usually in patients <40 years; Jumper's knee progression
- **Femoral nerve injury** — Loss of knee extension; difficulty walking on stairs
- **Patellofemoral pain syndrome (PFPS)** — VMO weakness + lateral patellar tilt
- **ACL injury rehabilitation** — Quadriceps strengthening is a key component
