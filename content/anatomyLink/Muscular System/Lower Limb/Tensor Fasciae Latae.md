# Tensor Fasciae Latae (TFL)

> The **TFL** is a small gluteal muscle that tenses the [Iliotibial Band](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliotibial-Band.md) (ITB). It assists in hip flexion, abduction, and internal rotation.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [ASIS](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Anterior-Superior-Iliac-Spine.md) and anterior [Iliac Crest](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Iliac-Crest.md)
- **Insertion** — [Iliotibial Band](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliotibial-Band.md) (ITB) → lateral [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md) (Gerdy's tubercle)
- **Nerve** — [Superior Gluteal Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Superior-Gluteal-Nerve.md) (L4, L5)
- **Action** — Hip flexion, abduction, internal rotation; tenses ITB

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **IT band syndrome** — TFL tightness causes ITB friction at lateral femoral condyle → lateral knee pain in runners
- **Hip abductor weakness** — TFL overactivation compensating for weak gluteus medius
