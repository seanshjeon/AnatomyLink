# Adductor Magnus

> The **adductor magnus** is the largest adductor, with two distinct parts: an adductor part (from pubis) and a hamstring part (from [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Ischial-Tuberosity.md)). The adductor hiatus in the muscle allows the femoral artery to pass from the anterior to posterior thigh.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Adductor part origin** — Inferior pubic ramus + ischiopubic ramus
- **Hamstring part origin** — [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Ischial-Tuberosity.md) of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)
- **Insertion** — [Linea Aspera](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Linea-Aspera.md) + adductor tubercle (medial femoral condyle) of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md)
- **Nerve** — Adductor part: [Obturator Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Obturator-Nerve.md) (L2–L4); Hamstring part: [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md) (tibial, L4)
- **Action** — Hip adduction; adductor part flexes; hamstring part extends hip

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Adductor hiatus** — Opening between adductor and hamstring parts; femoral vessels pass to popliteal fossa
- **Adductor strain** — Groin pain in athletes; "groin pull"
