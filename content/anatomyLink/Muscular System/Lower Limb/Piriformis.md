# Piriformis

> The **piriformis** is a key deep gluteal muscle that externally rotates and abducts the hip. It is the primary landmark of the **greater sciatic foramen** — the [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md) typically exits just below it.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Anterior sacrum (S2–S4)
- **Insertion** — [Greater Trochanter](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Greater Trochanter.md) (superior facet) of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md)
- **Nerve** — Nerve to piriformis (S1, S2)
- **Action** — External rotation + abduction of hip (when extended)
- **Relation** — [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md) exits below piriformis in 85%; above in 10%; through in 5%

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Piriformis syndrome** — Sciatic nerve compression at the [Greater Sciatic Notch](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Greater Sciatic Notch.md)/piriformis; buttock pain + sciatica; FAIR test positive
- **Injection landmark** — Divides the greater sciatic foramen into superior (superior gluteal) and inferior (inferior gluteal + sciatic) compartments
