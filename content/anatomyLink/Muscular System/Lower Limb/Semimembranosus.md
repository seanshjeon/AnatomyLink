# Semimembranosus

> The **semimembranosus** is the deepest medial hamstring, originating from the [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Ischial Tuberosity.md) and inserting primarily on the medial [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) condyle. It forms a major component of the posterior knee capsule (oblique popliteal ligament).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Ischial Tuberosity.md) of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md)
- **Insertion** — Posteromedial [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (medial condyle); contributes to posterior knee capsule
- **Nerve** — [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md) (tibial division, L5–S2)
- **Action** — Knee flexion + medial rotation; hip extension; tightens posterior capsule

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Baker's cyst** — Semimembranosus bursa enlarges → popliteal fossa swelling; associated with knee effusion
