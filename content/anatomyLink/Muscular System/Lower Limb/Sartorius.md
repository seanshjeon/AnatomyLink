# Sartorius

> The **sartorius** is the longest muscle in the human body (~50 cm), running diagonally across the anterior thigh from the [ASIS](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Anterior-Superior-Iliac-Spine.md) to the medial tibia (pes anserinus). It flexes, abducts, and externally rotates the hip, and flexes the knee.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [ASIS](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Anterior-Superior-Iliac-Spine.md) of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)
- **Insertion** — Pes anserinus (medial [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md)) — with gracilis + semitendinosus
- **Nerve** — [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) (L2, L3)
- **Action** — Hip flexion + abduction + ER; knee flexion ("tailor's position")

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Pes anserinus bursitis** — Inflammation at the pes anserinus insertion; medial knee pain
- **ASIS avulsion** — Sartorius avulsion fracture in adolescents during sprinting
