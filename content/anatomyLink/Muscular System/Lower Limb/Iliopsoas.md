# Iliopsoas

> The iliopsoas is the **most powerful hip flexor**, formed by the union of two muscles: **iliacus** and **psoas major**. It is essential for walking, running, and sitting up.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**:
  - Psoas major: [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (vertebral bodies T12–L5 and transverse processes)
  - Iliacus: [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) (iliac fossa)
- **Insertion**: [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) (lesser trochanter)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (psoas) + iliac fossa ([Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)) (iliacus) |
| Insertion | Lesser trochanter of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) |

| Innervation | Psoas major: anterior rami L1–L3; Iliacus: [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) (L2–L4) |
| Blood Supply | Lumbar arteries (psoas); iliac branches (iliacus) |

## 🔑 Anatomical Course
The iliopsoas runs from the abdomen/pelvis, under the inguinal ligament, to insert on the lesser trochanter. It is separated from the hip joint only by the **iliopectineal bursa**.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Iliopsoas abscess** — Psoas abscess from vertebral osteomyelitis (TB, Staphylococcus); flank/groin pain + fever
- **Iliopsoas tendinopathy** — Internal snapping hip (coxa saltans interna); snapping sensation at the lesser trochanter
- **Hip flexor tightness** — Anterior pelvic tilt; lumbar hyperlordosis; common in sedentary populations
- **Thomas test** — Assesses hip flexor contracture
- **Femoral nerve stretch** — Psoas spasm can compress the femoral nerve → anterior thigh symptoms
