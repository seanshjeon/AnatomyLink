# Tibialis Posterior

> Tibialis posterior is the **deepest and most central muscle** of the posterior leg compartment. It is the **primary invertor** of the foot, supports the **medial longitudinal arch**, and is a key stabiliser during the stance phase of gait.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Muscular System](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Muscular System.md)
- Tendon passes: Behind medial malleolus ([Medial Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Medial Malleolus.md)) → plantar foot
- Nerve: **Tibial nerve (L4, L5)** — from [Tibial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Tibial Nerve.md)
- Blood supply: Posterior tibial and peroneal arteries
- Acts opposite to: [Fibularis Muscles](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Fibularis Muscles.md) (evertors)

## 🔬 Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Posterior [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (lateral to vertical line), posterior [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) (upper 2/3), interosseous membrane |
| Insertion | Navicular tuberosity (primary); cuneiforms, cuboid, bases of metatarsals 2–4 (fan-like) |
| Action | **Inversion** of foot; plantar flexion; supports medial longitudinal arch |
| Nerve | Tibial nerve (L4–L5) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tibialis posterior tendon dysfunction (TPTD)** — most common cause of **adult-acquired flatfoot**; medial ankle pain + progressive arch collapse; "too many toes sign" from behind; Rx: orthotics → surgical reconstruction
- **Medial ankle tenderness** — posterior tibial tendon; distinguish from deltoid ligament injury ([Deltoid Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Deltoid Ligament.md))
- **Stage classification** — Stage I (tendinopathy, flexible flatfoot) → Stage IV (rigid flatfoot + ankle arthritis)
