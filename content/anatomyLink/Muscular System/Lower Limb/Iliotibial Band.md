# Iliotibial Band (ITB)

> The **iliotibial band** (IT band / iliotibial tract) is a thick band of fascia lata running down the lateral thigh from the [Iliac Crest](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Iliac-Crest.md) to Gerdy's tubercle on the lateral [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md). It receives the insertions of [Gluteus Maximus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Maximus.md) and [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Tensor-Fasciae-Latae.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Iliac Crest](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Iliac-Crest.md) (tubercle); [Gluteus Maximus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Maximus.md) + [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Tensor-Fasciae-Latae.md) insert into it
- **Insertion** — Gerdy's tubercle (anterolateral [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md))
- **Crosses** — Lateral femoral condyle at ~30° of knee flexion

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **IT band syndrome (ITBS)** — Most common running overuse injury; friction of ITB over lateral femoral condyle → lateral knee pain at 30° flexion; treated with stretching, activity modification
- **Segond fracture** — Avulsion of lateral tibial rim by ITB (+ anterior capsule); associated with ACL tear
