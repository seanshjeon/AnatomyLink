# Obturator Internus

> The **obturator internus** is a deep external rotator of the hip, originating within the pelvis and exiting through the lesser sciatic foramen to insert on the [Greater Trochanter](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Greater-Trochanter.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Inner surface of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) (around obturator foramen)
- **Exit route** — Lesser sciatic foramen (makes a 90° turn around the ischial spine)
- **Insertion** — Medial surface of [Greater Trochanter](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Greater-Trochanter.md) of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md)
- **Nerve** — Nerve to obturator internus (L5, S1, S2)
- **Action** — Hip external rotation (extended); hip abduction (flexed)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Piriformis-like syndrome** — Obturator internus spasm can irritate the sciatic nerve
- **Deep gluteal syndrome** — One of the "POPS" muscles (Piriformis, Obturator internus, Gemelli, Quadratus femoris)
