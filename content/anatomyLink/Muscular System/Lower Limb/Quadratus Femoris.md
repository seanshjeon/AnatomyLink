# Quadratus Femoris

> The **quadratus femoris** is a flat, square-shaped deep external rotator of the hip, connecting the [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Ischial-Tuberosity.md) to the intertrochanteric crest of the [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Ischial-Tuberosity.md) of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) (lateral aspect)
- **Insertion** — Intertrochanteric crest of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md)
- **Nerve** — Nerve to quadratus femoris (L4, L5, S1)
- **Action** — Hip external rotation + adduction

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Ischiofemoral impingement** — Quadratus femoris compressed between lesser trochanter and ischial tuberosity
- **Quadratus femoris tear/oedema** — Seen on MRI; hip pain + external rotator weakness
