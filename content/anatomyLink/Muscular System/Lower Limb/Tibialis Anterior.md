# Tibialis Anterior

> The tibialis anterior is the **largest and most medial muscle of the anterior compartment of the leg**. It is the primary dorsiflexor and invertor of the foot, and is critical for the swing phase of walking.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (lateral surface of upper 2/3) + interosseous membrane
- **Insertion**: [Tarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tarsals.md) (medial cuneiform — medial and inferior surfaces) + [Metatarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Metatarsals.md) (1st metatarsal base)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Lateral [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (upper 2/3) + interosseous membrane |
| Insertion | Medial cuneiform ([Tarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tarsals.md)) + 1st metatarsal base ([Metatarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Metatarsals.md)) |
| Action | Foot **dorsiflexion** (primary); foot **inversion** |
| Innervation | **[Deep fibular (peroneal) nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Common Fibular Nerve.md) (L4, L5)** |
| Blood Supply | Anterior tibial artery |

## 🔑 Role in Gait
- **Swing phase** — Dorsiflexes the foot to clear the ground (prevents foot drop)
- **Heel strike** — Eccentrically controls plantarflexion to lower the forefoot to the ground

## 🔑 Anterior Compartment Muscles
Tibialis anterior runs with:
- Extensor hallucis longus
- Extensor digitorum longus
- Fibularis (peroneus) tertius

All supplied by the **deep fibular nerve** and **anterior tibial artery**.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Foot drop (Steppage gait)** — Deep fibular nerve or common fibular nerve injury → inability to dorsiflex → foot slaps the ground
- **Tibialis anterior tendinopathy** — Overuse in runners; pain along anterior ankle
- **Anterior compartment syndrome** — Increased pressure in the anterior compartment compresses tibialis anterior and the deep fibular nerve → foot drop + intense anterior leg pain
- **L4 nerve root lesion** — Tibialis anterior weakness is the key clinical finding; tested by asking patient to walk on heels
