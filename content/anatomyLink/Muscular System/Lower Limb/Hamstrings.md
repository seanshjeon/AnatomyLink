# Hamstrings

> The hamstrings are a group of **3 muscles** on the posterior thigh that flex the knee and extend the hip. They are the **most commonly strained muscles** in athletes.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: All 3 originate from the [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md) (ischial tuberosity); biceps femoris short head from [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md) (linea aspera)
- **Insertion**:
  - Biceps femoris: [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) (fibular head)
  - Semimembranosus: [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (medial tibial condyle)
  - Semitendinosus: [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (medial shaft — pes anserinus)

## 🗂️ The Three Muscles

| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Unique Feature |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Biceps femoris (long head) | Ischial tuberosity | Fibular head | Two heads; lateral |
| Biceps femoris (short head) | Linea aspera ([Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md)) | Fibular head | Does NOT cross hip |
| Semimembranosus | Ischial tuberosity | Medial tibial condyle | Deep; broad tendon |
| Semitendinosus | Ischial tuberosity | Medial tibial shaft (pes anserinus) | Long, cord-like tendon |

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Action | Knee **flexion**; hip **extension**; medial (semi-) and lateral (biceps) tibial rotation |
| Innervation | **[Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md)** — tibial division (semis + long head BF); common fibular division (short head BF) |
| Blood Supply | Perforating branches of deep femoral artery |

## 🔑 Pes Anserinus
Semitendinosus + gracilis + sartorius insert together on the medial proximal tibia — the **"goose foot" (pes anserinus)**. This is a site of bursitis in osteoarthritis patients.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Hamstring strain** — Most common athletic soft tissue injury; proximal myotendinous junction at ischial tuberosity
- **Hamstring avulsion** — Ischial tuberosity avulsion in adolescents or high-energy sprinting
- **ACL injury** — Hamstrings act as dynamic stabilizers against anterior tibial translation; strengthening is key in ACL rehab
- **Pes anserinus bursitis** — Medial knee pain in obese/osteoarthritic patients
- **Sciatic nerve** — Runs between hamstring muscles in the posterior thigh; hamstring tightness can mimic sciatica
