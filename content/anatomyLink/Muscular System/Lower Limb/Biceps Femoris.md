# Biceps Femoris

> The **biceps femoris** is the lateral hamstring muscle with two heads. It is the primary lateral knee flexor and a key hip extensor, inserting on the [Fibular Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Fibular-Head.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Long head origin** — [Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Ischial-Tuberosity.md) of [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)
- **Short head origin** — [Linea Aspera](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Linea-Aspera.md) (lateral lip) of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md)
- **Insertion** — [Fibular Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Fibular-Head.md) (and lateral [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md))
- **Nerve** — Long head: [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md) (tibial division, L5–S2); Short head: sciatic (fibular division, L5–S1)
- **Action** — Knee flexion + lateral rotation; hip extension (long head)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Hamstring strain** — Most common at the musculotendinous junction; sprinting injury
- **Common fibular nerve** — Runs just posterior to the biceps femoris tendon at the knee → at risk in knee dislocations
