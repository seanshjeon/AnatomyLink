# Adductor Brevis

> The **adductor brevis** is the shortest adductor, lying deep to adductor longus. It is the key landmark for dividing the obturator nerve into anterior and posterior divisions.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Inferior pubic ramus
- **Insertion** — Upper [[Linea Aspera]] and lesser trochanteric line of [[Femur]]
- **Nerve** — [[Obturator Nerve]] (L2–L4)
- **Action** — Hip adduction + slight flexion

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Obturator nerve division** — Adductor brevis divides obturator nerve into anterior (superficial) and posterior (deep) branches
