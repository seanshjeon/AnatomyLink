# Soleus

> Soleus is a **broad, flat muscle** deep to [[Gastrocnemius]], forming part of the **triceps surae** group. Unlike gastrocnemius, it crosses only the ankle (not the knee) and is the **primary plantar flexor during standing and slow walking** — the "workhorse" of ambulation.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Muscular System]]
- Together with [[Gastrocnemius]]: Forms triceps surae → shared tendon = **Achilles tendon (calcaneal tendon)**
- Inserts via: Achilles tendon → posterior calcaneus ([[Talus]] area / calcaneum)
- Nerve: **Tibial nerve (S1, S2)** — from [[Tibial Nerve]]
- Blood supply: Posterior tibial, peroneal arteries

## 🔬 Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Posterior head of [[Fibula]], soleal line + medial border of [[Tibia]] |
| Insertion | Posterior calcaneus (via Achilles tendon) |
| Action | **Plantar flexion** (ankle only; single-joint muscle); postural stability |
| Nerve | Tibial nerve (S1–S2) |
| Fibre type | Predominantly slow-twitch (type I) — fatigue-resistant |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Deep vein thrombosis (DVT)** — soleal veins (within soleus) are common sites for DVT formation; calf DVT
- **Achilles tendon rupture** — usually at soleus/gastrocnemius junction (watershed zone); Thompson test (calf squeeze)
- **Achilles tendinopathy** — overuse; insertion vs. mid-portion; Rx: eccentric exercises
- **Compartment syndrome** — posterior deep compartment; raised intracompartmental pressure
