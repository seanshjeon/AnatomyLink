# Fibularis Muscles (Peroneal)

> The fibularis (peroneal) muscles occupy the **lateral compartment of the leg**, running along the [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md). They are the **primary evertors** of the foot and assist plantar flexion. They are innervated by the **superficial fibular (peroneal) nerve**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Muscular System](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Muscular System.md)
- Lie along: [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) (lateral aspect)
- Nerve: **Superficial fibular (peroneal) nerve (L5, S1)** — from [Common Fibular Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Common Fibular Nerve.md)
- Blood supply: Peroneal (fibular) artery
- Tendons pass: Behind lateral malleolus ([Lateral Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Lateral Malleolus.md)) → held by superior + inferior fibular retinacula

## 🔬 Details

| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action | Nerve |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Fibularis Longus** | Upper 2/3 lateral [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) | Base of 1st metatarsal + medial cuneiform (crosses plantar foot) | Plantar flexion + **eversion**; supports lateral longitudinal + transverse arches | Superficial fibular (L5–S1) |
| **Fibularis Brevis** | Lower 2/3 lateral [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) | Styloid process (base of 5th metatarsal) | Plantar flexion + **eversion** | Superficial fibular (L5–S1) |
| **Fibularis Tertius** | Lower anterior [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) (with EDL) | Dorsum of 5th metatarsal | Dorsiflexion + eversion | Deep fibular nerve |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Lateral ankle sprain** — inversion injury stretches/tears fibular tendons and [Anterior Talofibular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Anterior Talofibular Ligament.md); fibularis brevis avulsion fracture at styloid process of 5th metatarsal
- **5th metatarsal avulsion fracture** — "dancer's fracture" from fibularis brevis pull during inversion; X-ray essential
- **Peroneal nerve palsy** (superficial branch) — loss of eversion + sensory loss over dorsal foot; foot drop affects deep branch
- **Fibularis tendon subluxation** — snapping sensation at lateral malleolus; superior retinaculum tear
