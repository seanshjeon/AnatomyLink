# Quadratus Lumborum (QL)

> The **quadratus lumborum** is a deep posterior abdominal wall muscle connecting the [[Iliac Crest]] to the 12th rib and lumbar transverse processes. It laterally flexes the trunk and fixes the 12th rib during respiration.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [[Iliac Crest]] (posterior part)
- **Insertion** — 12th rib + [[Transverse Process]] of L1–L4
- **Nerve** — Subcostal nerve + lumbar plexus (T12, L1–L4)
- **Action** — Lateral trunk flexion; fixes 12th rib during respiration; maintains lumbar posture

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Low back pain** — QL is a very common source; trigger point pain refers to the SI joint, lateral hip, and groin
- **Hip hike** — QL shortening causes ipsilateral hip elevation (apparent leg length discrepancy)
