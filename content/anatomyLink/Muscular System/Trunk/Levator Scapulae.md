# Levator Scapulae

> Levator scapulae is a **strap-like muscle** in the posterior neck and back, connecting the [Cervical Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Cervical-Vertebrae.md) to the superior angle of the [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md). It **elevates the scapula** and laterally flexes the neck.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Muscular System](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Muscular-System.md)
- Related: [Cervical Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Cervical-Vertebrae.md), [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md), [Trapezius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Trapezius.md), [Rhomboids](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Rhomboids.md)
- Nerve: Dorsal scapular nerve (C4–C5) + direct branches from C3–C4

## 🔬 Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Transverse processes of C1–C4 |
| Insertion | Superior angle and medial border (upper portion) of [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md) |
| Action | **Elevates scapula**; downward rotation of glenoid; lateral flexion + ipsilateral rotation of neck (when scapula fixed) |
| Nerve | Dorsal scapular nerve (C4–C5); C3–C4 direct branches |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Levator scapulae syndrome** — common cause of neck + shoulder pain; trigger points at superior angle of scapula; exacerbated by prolonged sitting/computer work
- **Torticollis** — if unilateral spasm → head tilted to same side, rotated to opposite side
