# Pectoralis Major

> The pectoralis major is the **large, fan-shaped muscle of the anterior chest wall**, forming the anterior axillary fold. It powerfully moves the shoulder in flexion, adduction, and internal rotation.

## 🔗 Bone Attachments
- **Origin**: [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md) (medial 1/2 — clavicular head) + [Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md) (sternal head) + [Ribs](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Ribs.md) (costal cartilages 1–6)
- **Insertion**: [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) (lateral lip of intertubercular groove)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Medial [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md) + [Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md) + costal cartilages 1–6 ([Ribs](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Ribs.md)) |
| Insertion | Lateral lip of bicipital groove ([Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md)) |
| Action | **Clavicular head**: shoulder flexion; **Sternal head**: extension from flexed position; **Both**: adduction + internal rotation |
| Innervation | Medial & lateral pectoral nerves (C5–T1) |
| Blood Supply | Pectoral branch of thoracoacromial artery; anterior intercostal arteries |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Pectoralis major tear** — Bench press injury; "popping" sensation + sudden weakness + ecchymosis
- **Pectoralis major flap** — Reconstructive surgery for head/neck defects and sternal wounds
- **Breast anatomy** — Breast tissue lies anterior to pec major; relevant in mastectomy
- **Poland syndrome** — Congenital absence of the sternal head of pectoralis major
