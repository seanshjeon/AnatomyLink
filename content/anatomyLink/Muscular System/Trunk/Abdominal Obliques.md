# Abdominal Obliques

> The **external oblique** and **internal oblique** are two of the four muscles forming the anterolateral abdominal wall. Together with [[Rectus Abdominis]] and transversus abdominis, they protect abdominal viscera, flex the trunk, and generate intra-abdominal pressure.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Muscular System]]
- Layer (outside → in): External oblique → Internal oblique → Transversus abdominis → [[Rectum and Anal Canal|peritoneum]]
- Forms: Inguinal ligament (EO aponeurosis); conjoint tendon (IO + transversus); rectus sheath

## 🔬 External Oblique

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Outer surfaces of ribs 5–12 (lower 8 ribs) |
| Insertion | Linea alba (via aponeurosis), pubic tubercle, anterior half of iliac crest |
| Action | Bilateral: trunk flexion, ↑ intra-abdominal pressure; Unilateral: **ipsilateral lateral flexion, contralateral rotation** |
| Nerve | Intercostal nerves T7–T12, subcostal (T12) |
| Mnemonic | "Hands in front pocket" — fibre direction |

## 🔬 Internal Oblique

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Thoracolumbar fascia, anterior 2/3 of iliac crest, lateral inguinal ligament |
| Insertion | Inferior borders of ribs 10–12; linea alba (aponeurosis); pubic crest (conjoint tendon with transversus) |
| Action | Bilateral: trunk flexion, ↑ IAP; Unilateral: **ipsilateral lateral flexion, ipsilateral rotation** (opposite to EO) |
| Nerve | Intercostal nerves T7–T12, subcostal, iliohypogastric, ilioinguinal (L1) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Inguinal hernia** — weak point in inguinal canal; conjoint tendon reinforces posterior wall
- **Abdominal wall hernias** — Spigelian hernia (through semilunar line); incisional hernia
- **Rectus sheath haematoma** — bleeding into sheath; sudden abdominal pain; usually after coughing or anticoagulation
- **Abdominal wall repair** — component separation technique uses these muscles
