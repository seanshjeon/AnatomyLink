# Lungs

> The lungs are **paired, spongy, cone-shaped organs** occupying the left and right pleural cavities of the thorax. They are the primary site of **gas exchange** between inspired air and the pulmonary circulation, and together form the largest organ of the respiratory system.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Respiratory System]]
- Airway: [[Trachea]] → [[Bronchi]] → bronchioles → alveoli
- Vascular: [[Pulmonary Circulation]] (deoxygenated blood in, oxygenated out); bronchial arteries from [[Aorta]] (nutritional)
- Covered by: [[Pleura]] (visceral layer directly on lung)
- Skeletal: [[Ribs]] (lateral), [[Sternum]] (anterior), [[Thoracic Vertebrae]] (posterior), [[Diaphragm]] (inferior)
- Lymphatics: Hilar and mediastinal nodes → right lymphatic duct / thoracic duct

## 🔬 Anatomy

### Right Lung (larger, ~625 g)
- 3 lobes: **Upper (RUL), Middle (RML), Lower (RLL)**
- Separated by: Oblique fissure (RLL from RUL+RML), Horizontal fissure (RUL from RML)
- 10 bronchopulmonary segments

### Left Lung (~565 g)
- 2 lobes: **Upper (LUL), Lower (LLL)** (no middle lobe)
- Oblique fissure only
- **Cardiac notch** — concavity on anterior-inferior border accommodates heart
- **Lingula** — tongue-like projection of LUL; homologue of RML
- 8–10 bronchopulmonary segments

### Surfaces & Borders
| Surface | Relation |
|---------|----------|
| Costal | Against [[Ribs]] and intercostal muscles |
| Mediastinal | Against heart, great vessels, oesophagus, trachea |
| Diaphragmatic (base) | Rests on [[Diaphragm]] |
| Apex | Projects ~2.5 cm above clavicle (cervical pleura) |

### Hilum (Root of Lung)
Structure order (anterior → posterior): Pulmonary veins (× 2) → Pulmonary artery → Main bronchus
- **Right hilum:** Artery ABOVE bronchus; upper lobe bronchus ABOVE artery (eparterial bronchus)
- **Left hilum:** Artery ABOVE bronchus

## ⚙️ Gas Exchange
- ~300 million alveoli; total surface area ~70 m²
- Alveolar-capillary membrane (0.2–0.5 μm): type I pneumocytes (gas exchange) + type II pneumocytes (surfactant)
- **Surfactant** (phosphatidylcholine): reduces surface tension, prevents alveolar collapse; produced from ~24 weeks gestation

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
| Condition | Key Points |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Pneumonia** | Lobar (S. pneumoniae) vs. bronchopneumonia; consolidation on CXR; air bronchograms |
| **Lung cancer** | Adenocarcinoma (periphery, never-smokers), SCC (central, smokers), SCLC (central, paraneoplastic) |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | V/Q mismatch; pleuritic pain; CTPA diagnostic |
| **Atelectasis** | Lung collapse; post-operative (subsegmental), lobar (mucus plug/endobronchial mass) |
| **ARDS** | Diffuse alveolar damage; bilateral infiltrates; PaO₂/FiO₂ <300 |
| **Surfactant deficiency** | Neonatal RDS in premature infants; ground-glass on CXR; Rx: exogenous surfactant |
| **Pleural effusion** | Fluid in pleural space; blunts costophrenic angle on CXR |
