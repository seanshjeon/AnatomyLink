# Respiratory System

> The respiratory system conducts air between the atmosphere and the alveoli for **gas exchange** (O₂ uptake, CO₂ elimination), and regulates acid-base balance via CO₂. It is divided into the **conducting zone** (dead space, ~150 mL) and the **respiratory zone** (gas exchange).

## 🔑 Lung Volumes & Capacities
| Measurement | Value | Description |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Tidal volume (TV) | ~500 mL | Normal breath |
| IRV | ~3000 mL | Extra inhaled after normal inspiration |
| ERV | ~1100 mL | Extra exhaled after normal expiration |
| RV | ~1200 mL | Air remaining after maximal exhalation |
| TLC | ~6000 mL | TV + IRV + ERV + RV |
| FRC | ~2300 mL | ERV + RV (resting lung volume) |
| VC | ~4800 mL | IRV + TV + ERV |

## 🏥 Key Clinical Conditions
- **Asthma** — reversible bronchoconstriction; ↑ FEV₁/FVC on bronchodilator
- **COPD** — emphysema + chronic bronchitis; irreversible obstruction; FEV₁/FVC <0.70
- **Pneumonia** — consolidation; lobar vs. bronchopneumonia; fever + productive cough
- **Pulmonary embolism** — thrombus lodges in pulmonary artery; V/Q mismatch; CXR may be normal; CTPA diagnostic
- **Pneumothorax** — air in pleural space; tension = surgical emergency
- **Lung cancer** — squamous (central), adenocarcinoma (peripheral), SCLC (paraneoplastic)
