# Trachea

> The trachea is a **cartilaginous and fibromuscular tube ~10–12 cm long**, extending from the [Larynx](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Larynx.md) (C6) to the **carina** (T4–T5), where it bifurcates into the left and right main [Bronchi](./anatomyLink/Respiratory System/Bronchi.md). It is the principal conduit for airflow between the larynx and lungs.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Respiratory System](./anatomyLink/Respiratory System/Respiratory System.md)
- Above: [Larynx](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Larynx.md) (cricoid cartilage, C6)
- Below: Bifurcates at **carina** (T4/T5 level, sternal angle) → Left & Right Main [Bronchi](./anatomyLink/Respiratory System/Bronchi.md)
- Anterior: Thyroid gland (isthmus overlies rings 2–4), sternothyroid muscles, manubrium
- Posterior: [Esophagus](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Esophagus.md) (shares trachealis muscle posteriorly)
- Right side: Azygos vein arch, right vagus nerve
- Left side: Aortic arch, left recurrent laryngeal nerve
- Blood supply: Inferior thyroid artery (upper); bronchial arteries (lower)
- Innervation: Recurrent laryngeal nerves (mucosa + trachealis); sympathetic (bronchodilation)

## 🔬 Structure
- **16–20 C-shaped hyaline cartilage rings** — open posteriorly
- **Trachealis muscle** — smooth muscle bridging the posterior gap; contracts to ↑ airflow velocity during cough
- **Mucosa** — pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium + goblet cells; mucociliary escalator
- **Carina** — internal ridge at bifurcation; highly sensitive (cough reflex); widened/distorted in lymphadenopathy

## ⚙️ Function
- Air conduction (conducting zone — no gas exchange)
- **Mucociliary escalator** — cilia beat upward, trapping inhaled particles in mucus
- **Warming and humidifying** inspired air

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tracheal intubation** — ETT tip should sit 2–4 cm above carina (T4 level); confirm with CXR
- **Tracheostomy** — surgical airway through rings 2–4; bypasses upper airway obstruction
- **Right main bronchus** — wider, shorter, more vertical than left → foreign body aspiration preferentially lodges here
- **Tracheal stenosis** — post-intubation, post-tracheostomy; stridor on exertion
- **Tracheomalacia** — softening of cartilage; dynamic collapse on expiration
- **Carinal angle** — normally <70°; widened (>90°) suggests left atrial enlargement
