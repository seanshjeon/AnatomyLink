# Vulva

> The vulva comprises the **external female genitalia** — all structures visible externally in the perineum, from the mons pubis to the perineal body. It includes erectile, glandular, and cutaneous components homologous to male external genitalia.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Reproductive-System.md)
- Internal continuation: [Vagina](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Female/Vagina.md) (vaginal orifice / introitus)
- Blood supply: Internal pudendal artery (labial + clitoral branches)
- Venous drainage: Internal pudendal vein → internal iliac vein
- Lymphatics: **Superficial inguinal nodes** (then deep inguinal → external iliac)
- Innervation: Pudendal nerve (S2–S4) — dorsal nerve of clitoris, posterior labial nerves; ilioinguinal nerve (L1) — anterior labia

## 🔬 Components

| Structure | Homologue (Male) | Description |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Mons pubis** | — | Fatty eminence over pubic symphysis; pubic hair |
| **Labia majora** | [Scrotum](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Scrotum.md) | Outer fatty folds; skin + hair follicles |
| **Labia minora** | Ventral shaft skin | Inner thin folds; no hair; sebaceous glands |
| **Clitoris** | [Penis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Penis.md) | Erectile organ; glans, body, crura; richly innervated |
| **Vestibule** | Penile urethra (distal) | Space between labia minora; contains urethral + vaginal orifices |
| **Bartholin's glands** | [Bulbourethral Glands](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Bulbourethral-Glands.md) | Bilateral; secrete mucus for lubrication; ducts open at 4 & 8 o'clock of vaginal orifice |
| **Skene's glands** | Prostate | Periurethral glands; minor secretion |
| **Vestibular bulbs** | Bulb of penis + corpus spongiosum | Paired erectile bodies flanking vaginal orifice |
| **Hymen** | — | Thin mucosal fold at vaginal introitus |

## ⚙️ Function
- Sexual arousal — clitoral erection, Bartholin's secretion (lubrication), vestibular bulb engorgement
- Protection of vaginal and urethral orifices

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Vulvitis / vulvovaginitis** — common; candida, contact dermatitis, lichen sclerosus
- **Bartholin's cyst/abscess** — duct obstruction → cyst; superinfected → abscess (commonest organisms: *E. coli*, *N. gonorrhoeae*); Rx: marsupialization
- **Vulval cancer** — squamous cell carcinoma (most common); HPV-related; post-menopausal; drains to inguinal nodes
- **Lichen sclerosus** — white atrophic plaques; intense pruritus; ↑ vulval cancer risk
- **Female genital mutilation (FGM)** — WHO classification; significant health consequences
- **Episiotomy** — surgical cut at vaginal orifice during delivery; mediolateral preferred
