# Cervix

> The cervix is the **inferior cylindrical portion of the [Uterus](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Uterus.md)** (~2.5–3 cm), projecting into the vault of the [Vagina](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Vagina.md). It is a critical barrier between the uterine cavity and the vagina, and is the most common site of **gynaecological cancer** worldwide.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Reproductive System.md)
- Above: [Uterus](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Uterus.md) (internal os)
- Below: [Vagina](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Vagina.md) (external os)
- Lateral supports: Cardinal ligaments (Mackenrodt's), uterosacral ligaments
- Blood supply: Cervical branch of uterine artery (from internal iliac)
- Lymphatics: Internal iliac, external iliac, obturator, presacral nodes
- Innervation: S2–S4 (cervical pain poorly localised; referred to sacrum)

## 🔬 Structure

| Region | Epithelium | Key Feature |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Ectocervix** | Stratified squamous (non-keratinised) | Visible on speculum exam |
| **Endocervix** | Simple columnar (mucus-secreting) | Produces cervical mucus |
| **Transformation zone (TZ)** | Squamocolumnar junction (SCJ); metaplastic squamous | **Most common site of cervical cancer** |

**Cervical os:** External os (vaginal) — round in nulliparous, slit-like after delivery; Internal os — opens into uterine cavity

## ⚙️ Function
- **Mucus plug** — in pregnancy, thick mucus ("show") prevents ascending infection; lost before labour
- **Cervical mucus cycle** — mid-cycle (oestrogen): thin, watery, sperm-friendly (Spinnbarkeit); luteal (progesterone): thick, hostile to sperm
- **Labour** — cervical ripening (effacement and dilation from 0 → 10 cm) allows fetal descent
- **Barrier** — prevents ascending infection outside ovulatory window

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Cervical cancer** — predominantly squamous cell carcinoma (80%); HPV 16 + 18 cause ~70%; screened by Pap smear + HPV testing; FIGO staging; Rx: LLETZ, radical hysterectomy, chemoradiotherapy
- **CIN (Cervical Intraepithelial Neoplasia)** — premalignant changes in TZ; CIN 1–3; managed by surveillance or ablation/excision
- **Cervicitis** — *N. gonorrhoeae*, *C. trachomatis*; mucopurulent discharge
- **Incompetent cervix** — painless cervical dilation in 2nd trimester → preterm birth; treated with cervical cerclage
- **Ectropion (erosion)** — columnar epithelium visible on ectocervix; benign; bleeds on contact
