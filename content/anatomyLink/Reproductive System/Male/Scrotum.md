# Scrotum

> The scrotum is a **cutaneous fibromuscular sac** housing the [Testes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Testes.md), [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Epididymis.md), and lower [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md). Its primary role is **thermoregulation** — maintaining testicular temperature ~2–3°C below core body temperature, essential for spermatogenesis.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Reproductive System.md)
- Contains: [Testes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Testes.md), [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Epididymis.md), lower [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md)
- Blood supply: External pudendal arteries (anterior), perineal arteries (posterior)
- Venous drainage: External pudendal veins → femoral / long saphenous
- Lymphatics: **Superficial inguinal nodes** (not para-aortic — clinically distinct from testicular lymphatics)
- Innervation: Anterior — ilioinguinal nerve (L1); Posterior — perineal branch of pudendal nerve (S3), posterior scrotal nerves

| Tunica vaginalis (parietal) | Peritoneum |

## ⚙️ Function
- **Thermoregulation** — dartos muscle (cold → contracts, wrinkling skin; warm → relaxes)
- **Cremasteric reflex** — elevates testis via cremaster
- Works in tandem with **pampiniform plexus** countercurrent exchanger in [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Scrotal oedema** — can result from cardiac failure, hypoalbuminaemia, lymphoedema, or local infection
- **Fournier's gangrene** — necrotising fasciitis of perineum and scrotum; life-threatening; surgical emergency
- **Scrotal haematoma** — post-trauma or post-vasectomy
- **Lymph node drainage** — scrotal skin drains to **inguinal nodes** (vs. testes → para-aortic); scrotal skin tumour metastasises to inguinal nodes; testicular tumour to para-aortic nodes
