# Prostate Gland

> The prostate is a **walnut-sized fibromuscular glandular organ** (~20 g, 3 cm) encircling the proximal urethra at the bladder neck. It secretes **prostatic fluid** (~25% of semen volume) containing PSA, zinc, and citric acid, and is the most clinically significant male reproductive organ.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Reproductive System]]
- Superior: Urinary bladder neck
- Inferior: Urogenital diaphragm / external urethral sphincter
- Posterior: [[Rectum and Anal Canal]] (palpable on DRE)
- Receives: Ejaculatory ducts (from [[Vas Deferens]] + [[Seminal Vesicles]])
- Expels into: Prostatic urethra (via 20–30 prostatic ducts)
- Blood supply: Inferior vesical artery (internal iliac) + middle rectal artery
- Venous drainage: Prostatic venous plexus → internal iliac veins
- Lymphatics: Internal iliac + obturator nodes
- Innervation: Sympathetic → smooth muscle contraction; parasympathetic → secretion

## 🔬 Zones (McNeal Classification)

| Zone | Volume | Clinical Significance |
|------|--------|-----------------------|
| **Peripheral zone** | ~70% | Most common site of **prostate cancer** |
| **Central zone** | ~25% | Surrounds ejaculatory ducts; rarely cancerous |
| **Transition zone** | ~5% | Site of **benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)** |
| Anterior fibromuscular stroma | — | No glandular tissue |

## ⚙️ Function
- Secretes **PSA** (prostate-specific antigen; serine protease) — liquefies semen coagulum
- Secretes citric acid, zinc, spermine, fibrinolysin
- Smooth muscle contracts during ejaculation, expelling secretion into urethra

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **BPH** — transition zone hypertrophy → urinary outflow obstruction; LUTS (nocturia, weak stream, hesitancy); treated with α-blockers, 5α-reductase inhibitors, TURP
- **Prostate cancer** — most common male cancer; peripheral zone; screened with PSA; Gleason grading; stages from T1–T4; Rx: watchful waiting, RP, radiotherapy, ADT
- **Prostatitis** — acute bacterial (*E. coli*), chronic bacterial, chronic pelvic pain syndrome, asymptomatic
- **Digital rectal exam (DRE)** — palpates posterior lobe; hard nodule → cancer until proven otherwise
- **PSA** — elevated in cancer, BPH, prostatitis; not fully specific
