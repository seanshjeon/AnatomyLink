# Testes

> The testes are paired **male gonads** housed in the [Scrotum](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Scrotum.md), responsible for **spermatogenesis** and **androgen secretion**. They develop retroperitoneally and descend into the scrotum by week 28–36 of gestation.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Reproductive-System.md)
- Contained in: [Scrotum](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Scrotum.md)
- Supplied by: [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Spermatic-Cord.md) (testicular artery from abdominal aorta at L2)
- Drained by: Pampiniform plexus → testicular vein → right IVC / left renal vein
- Lymphatics: Para-aortic nodes (NOT inguinal — clinically important)
- Covered by: Tunica albuginea (fibrous capsule), tunica vaginalis (peritoneal derivative)
- Leads to: [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Epididymis.md)

## 🔬 Structure

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tunica albuginea | Dense fibrous capsule; sends septa dividing testis into ~250 lobules |
| Seminiferous tubules | Site of spermatogenesis; lined by Sertoli + spermatogenic cells |
| Sertoli cells | Nursing cells; blood–testis barrier; secrete inhibin B + androgen-binding protein |
| Leydig cells | Interstitial cells; produce **testosterone** under LH stimulation |
| Rete testis | Anastomosing channels collecting sperm → efferent ductules → [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Epididymis.md) |

## ⚙️ Function
- **Spermatogenesis** — takes ~64–74 days; requires temperature 2–3°C below core body temperature
- **Steroidogenesis** — Leydig cells produce testosterone (→ DHT in target tissues via 5α-reductase)
- **Hormonal feedback** — Inhibin B from Sertoli cells suppresses FSH at pituitary

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Testicular torsion** — twisting of [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Spermatic-Cord.md); ischaemia within 4–6 h → surgical emergency
- **Cryptorchidism** — undescended testis; ↑ risk of seminoma; treat with orchiopexy by age 1
- **Testicular cancer** — commonest solid malignancy in men 15–35; germ cell tumours (seminoma vs. non-seminoma)
- **Orchitis** — mumps orchitis → atrophy; can cause azoospermia
- **Hydrocele** — fluid between layers of tunica vaginalis
