# Penis

> The penis is the **male external genital organ** and the terminal segment of the urinary and reproductive tracts. It consists of three erectile cylinders and serves as the conduit for both **urine** and **semen**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Reproductive-System.md)
- Receives urethra from: [Prostate Gland](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Prostate-Gland.md) → membranous urethra → spongy (penile) urethra
- Receives duct from: [Bulbourethral Glands](./anatomyLink/Reproductive-System/Male/Bulbourethral-Glands.md) (bulbar urethra)
- Blood supply: Internal pudendal artery → dorsal artery, deep artery of penis (cavernosal artery)
- Venous drainage: Deep dorsal vein → prostatic venous plexus
- Lymphatics: Superficial inguinal nodes (penile skin), deep inguinal / internal iliac nodes (glans/urethra)
- Innervation: Dorsal nerve of penis (pudendal, S2–S4); autonomic via cavernous nerves (pelvic plexus)

## 🔬 Structure

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Corpora cavernosa** (×2) | Paired dorsal erectile cylinders; enclosed by Buck's fascia + tunica albuginea |
| **Corpus spongiosum** (×1) | Ventral; surrounds spongy urethra; expands into **glans penis** |
| Glans penis | Distal expansion of corpus spongiosum; most sensitive region |
| Prepuce (foreskin) | Retractile skin fold covering glans |
| Bulb of penis | Proximal expansion of corpus spongiosum; attached to perineal membrane |
| Crura of penis | Proximal attachment of corpora cavernosa to ischiopubic rami |

## ⚙️ Erection Mechanism
1. **Parasympathetic stimulation** (S2–S4, pelvic splanchnic nerves → cavernous nerves)
2. NO released → cGMP ↑ → smooth muscle relaxation in helicine arteries
3. Sinusoidal spaces fill with blood → compression of emissary veins → tumescence
4. **Ejaculation** = sympathetic (L1–L2) → smooth muscle contraction of vas deferens, seminal vesicles, prostate

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Erectile dysfunction (ED)** — vascular (most common), neurogenic (cavernous nerve damage post-prostatectomy), hormonal, psychogenic; treat with PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil)
- **Priapism** — sustained painful erection without stimulation; sickle cell, medications; emergency decompression required to prevent fibrosis
- **Peyronie's disease** — fibrous plaque in tunica albuginea → penile curvature
- **Phimosis** — non-retractile prepuce; treat with circumcision or topical steroids
- **Penile cancer** — squamous cell carcinoma; associated with HPV, phimosis; rare
- **Hypospadias** — urethral meatus on ventral surface; commonest male urogenital anomaly
