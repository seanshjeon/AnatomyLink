# Vas Deferens

> The vas deferens (ductus deferens) is a **muscular duct ~45 cm long** that carries sperm from the [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Epididymis.md) to the **ejaculatory duct**. It is the primary conduit of the [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md) and the target of vasectomy.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Reproductive System.md)
- Origin: Tail of [Epididymis](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Epididymis.md)
- Travels via: [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md) → inguinal canal → pelvis
- Joins: Duct of [Seminal Vesicles](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Seminal Vesicles.md) → **ejaculatory duct** → opens into prostatic urethra
- Relations: Crosses ureter ("water under the bridge") at pelvic brim
- Blood supply: Artery of the vas deferens (from superior vesical artery)
- Embryological origin: Wolffian (mesonephric) duct

## 🔬 Structure

| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Mucosa | Pseudostratified columnar epithelium with stereocilia |
| Muscularis | Thick; 3 layers (inner longitudinal, circular, outer longitudinal) — generates powerful peristalsis |
| Adventitia | Loose connective tissue |

**Course:** Epididymis (cauda) → ascends in [Scrotum](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Scrotum.md) → [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md) → inguinal canal → pelvic brim (crosses ureter) → dilates into **ampulla** → joins seminal vesicle duct → **ejaculatory duct**

## ⚙️ Function
- Rapid transport of sperm during ejaculation via strong peristaltic contractions (sympathetic innervation)
- Ampulla provides short-term sperm storage

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Vasectomy** — ligation/cutting in the scrotum for male contraception; highly effective, potentially reversible
- **Congenital bilateral absence of the vas deferens (CBAVD)** — associated with *CFTR* mutations (cystic fibrosis); obstructive azoospermia; can use TESE for IVF
- **Vas deferens palpation** — felt as a firm cord in the posterior scrotum; absence suggests CBAVD
