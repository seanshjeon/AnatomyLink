# Epididymis

> The epididymis is a **highly coiled duct** (~6 m uncoiled) lying on the posterolateral surface of each testis. It is the site of **sperm maturation** and **storage**, and connects the testis to the [Vas Deferens](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Vas Deferens.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Reproductive System](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Reproductive System.md)
- Receives sperm from: [Testes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Testes.md) (via efferent ductules)
- Continues as: [Vas Deferens](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Vas Deferens.md)
- Contained within: [Scrotum](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Scrotum.md)
- Blood supply: Testicular artery (from [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md))
- Embryological origin: Wolffian (mesonephric) duct

## 🔬 Structure

| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Head (caput) | Receives 12–20 efferent ductules from [Testes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Testes.md); initial sperm processing |
| Body (corpus) | Long coiled segment; primary site of maturation |
| Tail (cauda) | Sperm storage reservoir; becomes [Vas Deferens](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Vas Deferens.md) |

**Histology:** Pseudostratified columnar epithelium with **stereocilia** (long microvilli for reabsorption and secretion)

## ⚙️ Function
- **Sperm maturation** — sperm acquire motility and fertilisation capacity over ~12 days
- **Sperm storage** — cauda stores mature sperm until ejaculation
- **Fluid reabsorption** — absorbs ~90% of fluid from testicular lumen

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Epididymitis** — commonest cause: *Chlamydia trachomatis* (young) or *E. coli* (older); posterior scrotal pain; treated with antibiotics
- **Epididymo-orchitis** — spread of infection to [Testes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Testes.md)
- **Epididymal cyst** — benign; transilluminates; lies superior to testis (vs. hydrocele)
- **Vasectomy** — division of [Vas Deferens](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Vas Deferens.md) just distal to the epididymis
