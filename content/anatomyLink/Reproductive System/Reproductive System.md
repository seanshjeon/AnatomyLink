# Reproductive System

> The reproductive system consists of **organs, ducts, and accessory glands** responsible for producing, storing, and transporting gametes, and — in females — supporting fertilisation, gestation, and parturition. It is closely related to the urinary system (→ [Urinary System](./anatomyLink/Urinary System/Urinary System.md)) and is innervated by the autonomic nervous system.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [00 - AnatomyLink Home](./anatomyLink/00 - AnatomyLink Home.md)
- Arterial supply: Internal iliac artery → branches to pelvic viscera
- Venous drainage: Gonadal veins → IVC (right) / left renal vein (left)
- Lymphatics: Para-aortic nodes (gonads), internal iliac nodes (uterus, prostate)
- Innervation: [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Sacral Plexus.md), hypogastric plexus (sympathetic), pelvic splanchnic nerves (parasympathetic)
- Skeletal relations: [Coccyx](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Coccyx.md), [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Lumbar Vertebrae.md), pelvic bones ([Ischial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Ischial Tuberosity.md), [Anterior Superior Iliac Spine](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Anterior Superior Iliac Spine.md))
- Hormonal control: Hypothalamic–pituitary–gonadal (HPG) axis → GnRH → LH / FSH

## 🔑 Key Embryological Origins
- Gonads (both sexes) develop from **urogenital ridge** (intermediate mesoderm)
- **Müllerian (paramesonephric) ducts** → uterus, uterine tubes, upper vagina (female)
- **Wolffian (mesonephric) ducts** → epididymis, vas deferens, seminal vesicles (male)
- External genitalia are indifferent until week 9; DHT drives male differentiation

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

| Condition | Structure Involved | Key Point |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Testicular torsion | [Spermatic Cord](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Spermatic Cord.md) | Surgical emergency; bell-clapper deformity |
| Varicocele | Pampiniform plexus (left > right) | Dilated venous plexus; cause of male infertility |
| Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) | [Prostate Gland](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Prostate Gland.md) | Median lobe → urinary outflow obstruction |
| Prostate cancer | [Prostate Gland](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Male/Prostate Gland.md) | Posterior zone; screened with PSA |
| Ectopic pregnancy | [Uterine Tubes](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Uterine Tubes.md) | Ampullary region most common; surgical emergency |
| Ovarian torsion | [Ovaries](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Ovaries.md) | Sudden pelvic pain; requires urgent surgery |
| Cervical cancer | [Cervix](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Cervix.md) | HPV 16/18; screened with Pap smear |
| Uterine fibroids | [Uterus](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Uterus.md) | Leiomyoma; most common benign uterine tumour |
| Endometriosis | [Uterus](./anatomyLink/Reproductive System/Female/Uterus.md), ovaries, peritoneum | Ectopic endometrial tissue; cyclical pain |
