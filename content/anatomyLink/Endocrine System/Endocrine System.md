# Endocrine System

> The endocrine system is a network of **ductless glands and tissues** that secrete **hormones** directly into the bloodstream to regulate metabolism, growth, reproduction, fluid balance, stress response, and many other physiological processes. It acts in concert with the [Nervous System](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Nervous-System.md) (neuroendocrine integration).

## 🔑 Major Axes
- **HPG axis** — Hypothalamus GnRH → Pituitary LH/FSH → Gonads (sex steroids)
- **HPA axis** — Hypothalamus CRH → Pituitary ACTH → Adrenal cortex (cortisol)
- **HPT axis** — Hypothalamus TRH → Pituitary TSH → Thyroid (T3/T4)

## 🏥 Key Clinical Conditions
- **Diabetes mellitus** — Type 1 (insulin deficiency), Type 2 (insulin resistance); complications: neuropathy, nephropathy, retinopathy
- **Hypothyroidism** — fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance; ↑TSH, ↓T4
- **Hyperthyroidism** — weight loss, heat intolerance, tremor; Graves' (TSH-R antibodies)
- **Cushing's syndrome** — cortisol excess; central obesity, striae, HTN, osteoporosis
- **Addison's disease** — adrenal insufficiency; hyperpigmentation, hypotension, hyperkalaemia
- **Primary hyperaldosteronism** — Conn's syndrome; HTN + hypokalaemia
- **Acromegaly** — GH excess in adult; enlarged hands/feet/jaw; pituitary adenoma
