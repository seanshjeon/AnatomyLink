# Endocrine System

> The endocrine system is a network of **ductless glands and tissues** that secrete **hormones** directly into the bloodstream to regulate metabolism, growth, reproduction, fluid balance, stress response, and many other physiological processes. It acts in concert with the [[Nervous System]] (neuroendocrine integration).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[00 - AnatomyLink Home]]
- Master control: [[Hypothalamus]] → [[Pituitary Gland]] (HPG/HPA/HPT axes)
- Related: [[Nervous System]] (autonomic innervation of glands), [[Reproductive System]] (sex hormones), [[Urinary System]] (ADH, aldosterone, EPO)
- Skeletal landmark: [[Sella Turcica]] (houses pituitary)

## 🗂️ Glands & Hormones

| Gland | Location | Key Hormones |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| [[Pituitary Gland]] | Sella turcica, sphenoid bone | GH, TSH, ACTH, LH, FSH, prolactin, ADH, oxytocin |
| [[Thyroid Gland]] | Anterior neck, C5–T1 | T3, T4 (metabolism), calcitonin |
| [[Parathyroid Glands]] | Posterior thyroid (×4) | PTH → ↑ Ca²⁺ |
| [[Adrenal Glands]] | Retroperitoneal, superior to [[Kidney]] | Cortisol, aldosterone (cortex); adrenaline, noradrenaline (medulla) |
| [[Pancreas]] (endocrine) | Retroperitoneal, C2 level | Insulin (β-cells), glucagon (α-cells), somatostatin (δ-cells) |
| [[Pineal Gland]] | Epithalamus, posterior to [[Thalamus]] | Melatonin (circadian rhythm) |

## 🔑 Major Axes
- **HPG axis** — Hypothalamus GnRH → Pituitary LH/FSH → Gonads (sex steroids)
- **HPA axis** — Hypothalamus CRH → Pituitary ACTH → Adrenal cortex (cortisol)
- **HPT axis** — Hypothalamus TRH → Pituitary TSH → Thyroid (T3/T4)

## 🏥 Key Clinical Conditions
- **Diabetes mellitus** — Type 1 (insulin deficiency), Type 2 (insulin resistance); complications: neuropathy, nephropathy, retinopathy
- **Hypothyroidism** — fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance; ↑TSH, ↓T4
- **Hyperthyroidism** — weight loss, heat intolerance, tremor; Graves' (TSH-R antibodies)
- **Cushing's syndrome** — cortisol excess; central obesity, striae, HTN, osteoporosis
- **Addison's disease** — adrenal insufficiency; hyperpigmentation, hypotension, hyperkalaemia
- **Primary hyperaldosteronism** — Conn's syndrome; HTN + hypokalaemia
- **Acromegaly** — GH excess in adult; enlarged hands/feet/jaw; pituitary adenoma
