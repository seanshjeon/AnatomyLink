# Femoral Vein

> The femoral vein is the **main deep vein of the thigh**, a continuation of the popliteal vein at the adductor hiatus. It receives the [[Great Saphenous Vein]] at the saphenofemoral junction (SFJ) and continues as the **external iliac vein** above the inguinal ligament.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Circulatory System]]
- Inferior: Popliteal vein (continuation through adductor hiatus)
- Receives: [[Great Saphenous Vein]] (at SFJ, 4 cm inferolateral to pubic tubercle) + profunda femoris vein
- Superior: Becomes **external iliac vein** (above inguinal ligament)
- Relations in femoral triangle: **NAVY** (lateral → medial): Femoral **N**erve → Femoral **A**rtery → Femoral **V**ein → **Y**-front (lymphatics/empty space)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **DVT (deep vein thrombosis)** — common in femoral and iliac veins; virchow's triad; calf/thigh swelling, pain, warmth; Wells score + D-dimer; Doppler USS confirmation; Rx: LMWH/DOAC; risk of PE
- **Femoral vein cannulation** — emergency central access; below inguinal ligament medial to artery; complications: arterial puncture, DVT, infection
- **Femoral hernia** — medial to femoral vein in femoral canal; more common in women; high risk of strangulation
