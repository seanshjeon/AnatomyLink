# Jugular Veins

> The jugular veins are the **primary venous drainage of the head and neck**, draining into the subclavian vein and then into the [[Superior Vena Cava]]. The internal jugular vein (IJV) is the larger, deeper vessel running alongside the [[Carotid Arteries]] in the carotid sheath.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Drain: brain (via dural venous sinuses → IJV), face/scalp (EJV)
- Drain into: subclavian vein → brachiocephalic vein → [[Superior Vena Cava]] → [[Heart]]
- Accompany: [[Carotid Arteries]] (IJV runs lateral to carotid in carotid sheath)
- Relation to: [[Cervical Vertebrae]] (posterior), [[Skull]] (jugular foramen — IJV origin)

## 🗂️ Two Main Jugular Veins

| Vein | Origin | Course | Drains |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Internal jugular vein (IJV)** | Jugular foramen ([[Skull]]) — continuation of sigmoid sinus | Within carotid sheath (lateral to carotid, medial to SCM); joins subclavian at sternoclavicular joint | Brain (dural sinuses), face, thyroid, pharynx |
| **External jugular vein (EJV)** | Behind angle of mandible (posterior auricular + retromandibular veins) | Superficial, crosses [[Sternocleidomastoid]] obliquely; visible when raised JVP | Scalp, face, superficial neck |

## 🔑 Dural Venous Sinuses → IJV
Superior sagittal sinus + inferior sagittal sinus → straight sinus → confluence of sinuses (torcular Herophili) → transverse sinuses → sigmoid sinuses → **internal jugular veins**

## 🔑 Jugular Venous Pressure (JVP)
The JVP reflects right atrial pressure. Assessed by observing the EJV or IJV pulsation with patient at 45°:
- **Normal**: <3 cm above sternal angle (= ~8 cmH₂O above right atrium)
- **Raised JVP**: right heart failure, cardiac tamponade, SVC obstruction, fluid overload
- **Kussmaul's sign**: JVP rises on inspiration (constrictive pericarditis, RV infarction)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Internal jugular vein cannulation** — most common central venous access site; landmarks: apex of triangle between two heads of [[Sternocleidomastoid]]; ultrasound-guided placement now standard
- **Raised JVP** — key sign in heart failure, tamponade, SVC obstruction
- **IJV thrombosis** — septic thrombophlebitis (Lemierre's syndrome after tonsillitis); neck pain, swelling, fever, septic pulmonary emboli
- **Jugular venous waveforms** — a wave (atrial contraction), c wave (tricuspid closure), x descent, v wave (venous filling), y descent; cannon a waves in complete heart block
