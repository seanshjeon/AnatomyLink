# Hepatic Veins

> The hepatic veins are **3 major veins** (right, middle, left) draining venous blood from the [[Liver]] directly into the [[Inferior Vena Cava]] at the level of T8. Unlike most abdominal veins, the hepatic veins drain into the IVC, not into the portal system.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Circulatory System]]
- Drain from: [[Liver]] (hepatic sinusoids → central veins → hepatic veins)
- Drain into: [[Inferior Vena Cava]] (at T8 level, just below diaphragm)
- Distinct from: [[Portal System]] (hepatic portal vein carries nutrient-rich blood TO liver)
- Blood flow: Portal vein + hepatic artery → hepatic sinusoids → **hepatic veins** → IVC → heart

## 🔬 Three Major Hepatic Veins

| Vein | Drains |
|------|--------|
| Right hepatic vein | Right lobe (posterior segments) |
| Middle hepatic vein | Segments IV (left lobe) + V, VIII (right lobe) |
| Left hepatic vein | Left lobe (lateral segments II, III) |

Plus multiple short veins draining directly from caudate lobe (segment I) — important in Budd-Chiari

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Budd-Chiari syndrome** — hepatic vein thrombosis → hepatic outflow obstruction; painful hepatomegaly, ascites, jaundice; caudate lobe may hypertrophy; Rx: anticoagulation, TIPS, transplant
- **TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt)** — creates channel from hepatic vein to portal vein through liver parenchyma; relieves portal hypertension
- **Hepatic vein in liver resection** — major hepatectomies defined by hepatic vein planes (Couinaud segments)
- **Congestive hepatopathy** — right heart failure → hepatic vein hypertension → centrilobular necrosis, nutmeg liver
