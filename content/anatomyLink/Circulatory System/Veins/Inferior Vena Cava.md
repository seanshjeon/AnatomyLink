# Inferior Vena Cava

> The inferior vena cava (IVC) is the **largest vein in the body**, returning deoxygenated blood from the lower body (abdomen, pelvis, lower limbs) to the right atrium of the [Heart](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Heart/Heart.md). It lies to the right of the [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Drains into: [Heart](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Heart/Heart.md) (right atrium)
- Formed by: union of right and left common iliac veins (at L5, right of [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md) bifurcation)
- Drains: lower limbs (via [iliac veins](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Iliac-Arteries.md)), kidneys (renal veins), liver (hepatic veins), gonads
- Relation to: [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md) (to the left), [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (posterior), liver (IVC groove)

## 🗂️ Key Tributaries

| Tributary | Level | Drains |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Common iliac veins | L5 | Pelvis + lower limbs |
| Lumbar veins | L1–L4 | Posterior abdominal wall |
| Right gonadal vein | L2 | Right testis/ovary (left gonadal → left renal vein) |
| Renal veins | L1–L2 | Kidneys |
| Right suprarenal vein | Above renal | Right adrenal (left → left renal vein) |
| Hepatic veins (×3) | T8 | Liver (via [Portal System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Veins/Portal-System.md)) |

## 🔑 Left-Sided Asymmetry
- **Left gonadal vein** → **left renal vein** → IVC (not directly into IVC)
- **Left suprarenal vein** → left renal vein → IVC
- Left-sided IVC: rare congenital variant; important in retroperitoneal surgery

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **DVT → Pulmonary embolism** — thrombus from femoral/iliac veins propagates to IVC → pulmonary arteries
- **IVC filter** — placed in infrarenal IVC to trap emboli; indicated when anticoagulation contraindicated or fails
- **IVC obstruction** — Budd-Chiari syndrome (hepatic vein/IVC occlusion → liver congestion → ascites, jaundice, RUQ pain); IVC compression by tumour (renal cell carcinoma — "tumour thrombus" extends into IVC)
- **Renal cell carcinoma** — can invade right renal vein and extend as tumour thrombus into IVC and even right atrium
