# Great Saphenous Vein

> The great saphenous vein (GSV) is the **longest vein in the body** (~70 cm), running from the dorsum of the foot to the femoral vein at the saphenofemoral junction (SFJ) in the femoral triangle. It is the primary superficial vein of the lower limb and drains into the deep venous system.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Circulatory System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Circulatory System.md)
- Origin: Medial dorsal venous arch (dorsum of foot)
- Termination: **Saphenofemoral junction (SFJ)** → [femoral vein](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Femoral Artery.md) at femoral triangle (4 cm inferolateral to pubic tubercle)
- Accompanies: Saphenous nerve (L3–L4 branch of femoral nerve) in the lower leg — medial aspect

## 🔬 Course
1. Anterior to medial malleolus ([Medial Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Medial Malleolus.md))
2. Posteromedial [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md)
3. Posterior to medial femoral condyle
4. Anteromedial thigh → SFJ

**Small Saphenous Vein (SSV):** Origin — lateral dorsal venous arch → passes behind lateral malleolus ([Lateral Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Lateral Malleolus.md)) → ascends in posterior calf → drains into popliteal vein at saphenopopliteal junction (SPJ) behind knee

## ⚙️ Function
- Superficial venous drainage of lower limb
- Connects to deep system via **perforating veins** (valves direct flow from superficial → deep)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Varicose veins** — incompetent valves at SFJ → retrograde flow → tortuous dilated superficial veins; GSV most commonly affected; Rx: foam sclerotherapy, laser/radiofrequency ablation, phlebectomy
- **DVT** — thrombophlebitis can ascend to deep system; distinguish from superficial thrombophlebitis (tender cord)
- **GSV as conduit** — used as **coronary artery bypass graft (CABG)** and peripheral vascular bypass (leg); harvested before surgery
- **Saphenous nerve injury** — during GSV stripping/harvesting; medial lower leg sensory loss
- **Trendelenburg test** — assesses SFJ incompetence
