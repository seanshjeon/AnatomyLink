# Superior Vena Cava

> The superior vena cava (SVC) is the **large venous trunk returning deoxygenated blood from the upper body** (head, neck, upper limbs, thorax) to the right atrium of the [Heart](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Heart/Heart.md). It has no valves.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Drains into: [Heart](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Heart/Heart.md) (right atrium)
- Formed by: right and left brachiocephalic veins (each formed by subclavian + internal jugular veins)
- Drains: [Jugular Veins](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Veins/Jugular-Veins.md) (head/neck), subclavian veins (upper limbs), azygos system (posterior thorax)
- Relation to: [Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md) (behind manubrium), ascending [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md) (to the left), right lung hilum

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Formed by | Right + left brachiocephalic veins (behind right 1st costal cartilage) |
| Length | ~7 cm; enters right atrium at the level of the 3rd costal cartilage |
| Tributaries | Azygos vein (drains posterior intercostals), brachiocephalic veins |
| No valves | Blood flows by negative intrathoracic pressure |

## 🔑 Azygos System
The azygos vein (right) and hemiazygos veins (left) drain the posterior thoracic wall, esophagus, and bronchi → empty into the SVC. The azygos is a crucial collateral pathway if the SVC or IVC is obstructed.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **SVC syndrome** — obstruction of SVC → facial/arm swelling, dilated neck veins, headache, cyanosis
  - Most common cause: **lung cancer** (right-sided) or lymphoma compressing SVC
  - Other causes: thrombosis from central lines/pacemakers
  - Collateral veins visible on chest wall; reversed flow in jugular veins
- **Central venous catheter (CVC)** — tip should lie in SVC/cavoatrial junction for accurate CVP monitoring
- **Pacemaker leads** — traverse SVC to right ventricle
