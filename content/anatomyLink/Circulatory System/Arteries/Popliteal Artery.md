# Popliteal Artery

> The popliteal artery is the **continuation of the [Femoral Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Femoral-Artery.md)** through the popliteal fossa, behind the knee, before dividing into the anterior tibial artery and the tibioperoneal trunk (→ posterior tibial + fibular arteries).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Continues from: [Femoral Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Femoral-Artery.md) (at adductor hiatus)
- Divides into: anterior tibial artery + tibioperoneal trunk (posterior tibial + fibular arteries)
- Relates to: [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) (posterior, in popliteal fossa), [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibia.md) (posterior, at lower border of popliteus)
- Accompanies: tibial nerve ([Tibial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Tibial-Nerve.md)) — most medial in popliteal fossa
- Overlying: [Gastrocnemius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gastrocnemius.md) heads

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Adductor hiatus in [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) |
| Termination | Lower border of popliteus → anterior tibial artery + tibioperoneal trunk |
| Branches | Genicular arteries (×5 — supply knee joint); sural arteries (supply [Gastrocnemius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gastrocnemius.md), soleus) |
| Pulse | Popliteal pulse: deep in popliteal fossa — patient prone, knee slightly flexed; press firmly |

## 🔑 Popliteal Fossa Contents (Surface to Deep)
Biceps femoris (lateral), semitendinosus/membranosus (medial); then superficial to deep: tibial nerve → popliteal vein → popliteal artery (deepest, next to bone)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Popliteal artery aneurysm** — most common peripheral artery aneurysm; often bilateral; presents with claudication, thrombosis, or distal embolisation; 50% associated with AAA
- **Popliteal artery entrapment syndrome** — young athletes; fibromuscular band compresses artery → exertional claudication; abnormal popliteal artery course around medial head of [Gastrocnemius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gastrocnemius.md)
- **Knee dislocation** — popliteal artery injury in ~30% of posterior dislocations; must assess vascular status and consider angiography/CT angiogram
- **Genicular arteries** — extensive anastomotic network around knee; collateral supply if femoral artery occluded slowly
- **Below-knee amputation level** — popliteal vessel status determines feasibility
