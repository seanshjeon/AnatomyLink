# Ulnar Artery

> The ulnar artery is the **medial and larger terminal branch of the [Brachial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Brachial Artery.md)**, running alongside the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md) and [Ulnar Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Ulnar Nerve.md) in the forearm, forming the superficial palmar arch in the hand.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Arises from: [Brachial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Brachial Artery.md) (at cubital fossa)
- Runs alongside: [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md) and [Ulnar Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Ulnar Nerve.md) (medial forearm)
- Anastomoses with: [Radial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Radial Artery.md) (via palmar arches)
- Forms: superficial palmar arch (primary)

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | [Brachial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Brachial Artery.md) bifurcation at cubital fossa |
| Course | Passes deep to pronator teres → medial forearm with [Ulnar Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Upper Limb Nerves/Ulnar Nerve.md) → enters hand via Guyon's canal |
| Key Branch | Superficial palmar arch → common palmar digital arteries → proper digital arteries |
| Pulse | Palpable at wrist medial to flexor carpi ulnaris tendon |

## 🔑 Palmar Arches

| Arch | Formed By | Depth | Supplies |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Superficial palmar arch** | Ulnar artery + superficial radial branch | Superficial to long flexor tendons | Common digital arteries to medial 3.5 digits |
| **Deep palmar arch** | [Radial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Radial Artery.md) + deep ulnar branch | Deep to long flexor tendons | Palmar metacarpal arteries |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Allen's test** — tests ulnar artery patency (see [Radial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Radial Artery.md)); essential before radial access procedures
- **Hypothenar hammer syndrome** — repetitive trauma to heel of hand (Guyon's canal) → ulnar artery thrombosis → digital ischemia; seen in mechanics, carpenters
- **Ulnar artery aneurysm** — rare; pulsatile hypothenar mass
