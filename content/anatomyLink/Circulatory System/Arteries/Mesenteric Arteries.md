# Mesenteric Arteries

> The **superior mesenteric artery (SMA)** and **inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)** are unpaired branches of the abdominal [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Aorta.md) supplying the midgut and hindgut respectively. They correspond to the embryological divisions of the gut and are the primary blood supply to the small and large intestine.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Circulatory System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Circulatory System.md)
- Arise from: Abdominal [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Aorta.md)
- Digestive supply: [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Small Intestine.md), [Large Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Lower GI/Large Intestine.md), [Rectum and Anal Canal](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Lower GI/Rectum and Anal Canal.md)
- Embryological correspondence: SMA (midgut) + IMA (hindgut); [Celiac Trunk](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Celiac Trunk.md) (foregut)
- Venous return: SMA vein + IMV → [Portal System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Veins/Portal System.md) → [Liver](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Accessory Organs/Liver.md)

## 🔬 Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA)

**Origin:** Abdominal aorta at **L1** (just below celiac trunk; passes behind neck of [Pancreas](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Accessory Organs/Pancreas.md))

| Branch | Supplies |
|--------|---------|
| Inferior pancreaticoduodenal | Duodenum (3rd/4th parts), head of pancreas |
| Jejunal + ileal arteries | Jejunum and ileum (via mesenteric arcades) |
| Ileocolic artery | Terminal ileum, cecum, appendix |
| Right colic artery | Ascending colon |
| Middle colic artery | Transverse colon |

**SMA syndrome** — duodenum compressed between SMA and aorta → vomiting/obstruction

## 🔬 Inferior Mesenteric Artery (IMA)

**Origin:** Abdominal aorta at **L3**

| Branch | Supplies |
|--------|---------|
| Left colic artery | Descending colon |
| Sigmoid arteries | Sigmoid colon |
| Superior rectal artery | Upper rectum |

**Marginal artery of Drummond** — anastomosis between SMA and IMA branches along the mesenteric border of colon; watershed area at splenic flexure (most vulnerable to ischaemia)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Acute mesenteric ischaemia** — SMA embolism (AF most common) or thrombosis; severe abdominal pain "out of proportion" to exam; elevated lactate; CT angiography; surgical emergency
- **Chronic mesenteric ischaemia** — "intestinal angina" — postprandial pain; weight loss; atherosclerosis
- **IMA at risk in colorectal surgery** — ligated during sigmoid colectomy; risk of left colon ischaemia if marginal artery poorly developed
- **Splenic flexure ischaemia** — watershed between SMA and IMA; vulnerable in hypotension, aortic surgery
- **SMA syndrome** — duodenum compressed by SMA; weight loss, vomiting
