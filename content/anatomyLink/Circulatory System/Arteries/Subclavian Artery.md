# Subclavian Artery

> The subclavian artery is the **primary arterial supply to the upper limb**, arising from the brachiocephalic trunk (right) or [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md) directly (left), passing over the first [Ribs](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Ribs.md) and becoming the axillary artery at the lateral border of the first rib.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Arises from: [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md) (left) / brachiocephalic trunk (right)
- Continues as: axillary artery → [Brachial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Brachial-Artery.md)
- Key branch: vertebral artery → [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) (posterior circulation)
- Passes over: first rib ([Ribs](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Ribs.md)) — landmark for name change
- Relation to: [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md) (passes beneath), [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md) (via suprascapular artery)

## 🗂️ Key Branches

| Branch | Supply |
|--------|--------|
| **Vertebral artery** | Ascends through cervical transverse foramina ([Cervical Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Cervical-Vertebrae.md)) → basilar artery → posterior [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) |
| **Internal mammary (thoracic) artery** | Anterior thoracic wall; used as coronary bypass graft for LAD |
| **Thyrocervical trunk** | Thyroid (inferior thyroid a.), [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md) (suprascapular a.), neck |
| **Costocervical trunk** | Posterior upper intercostals; deep cervical muscles |

## 🔑 Name Changes of the Upper Limb Artery
Subclavian → **Axillary** (at lateral border of 1st rib) → **[Brachial](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Brachial-Artery.md)** (at lower border of teres major) → [Radial Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Radial-Artery.md) + [Ulnar Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Ulnar-Artery.md) (at cubital fossa)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Subclavian steal syndrome** — proximal subclavian stenosis → retrograde vertebral flow → posterior circulation ischemia with arm exercise (dizziness, diplopia, drop attacks)
- **Thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS)** — compression between clavicle and first rib → arm pain, pallor, weakness; can compress [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md) (neurogenic TOS, most common) or subclavian artery/vein
- **Subclavian line** — central venous access; risk of pneumothorax (lung apex nearby)
- **Blood pressure measurement** — BP measured in brachial artery; difference >20 mmHg between arms suggests subclavian stenosis
