# Femoral Artery

> The femoral artery is the **main arterial supply of the lower limb**, continuing from the external iliac artery at the inguinal ligament and descending through the femoral triangle and adductor canal to become the [Popliteal Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Popliteal-Artery.md) at the adductor hiatus.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Continues from: [Iliac Arteries](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Iliac-Arteries.md) (external iliac, at inguinal ligament)
- Continues as: [Popliteal Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Popliteal-Artery.md) (at adductor hiatus / [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) distal)
- Accompanies: [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) (lateral in femoral triangle)
- Runs through: femoral triangle → adductor canal → adductor hiatus

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Origin | Inguinal ligament (continuation of external iliac) |
| Termination | Adductor hiatus in adductor magnus → [Popliteal Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Popliteal-Artery.md) |
| Key Branch | **Profunda femoris (deep femoral artery)** — largest branch; supplies [Quadriceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadriceps-Femoris.md), [Hamstrings](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Hamstrings.md), adductors; **medial circumflex femoral artery** → femoral head blood supply |
| Pulse | Femoral pulse: mid-inguinal point (midpoint between ASIS and pubic symphysis) |

## 🔑 Femoral Triangle Contents (lateral to medial)
**VAN** — Vein, Artery, Nerve (femoral vein medial to artery; [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) lateral to artery)

## 🔑 Femoral Head Blood Supply
The **medial circumflex femoral artery** (branch of profunda femoris) is the main supply to the femoral head via the retinacular vessels. **Disrupted in femoral neck fractures → avascular necrosis of the femoral head**.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Peripheral arterial disease (PAD)** — atherosclerosis → intermittent claudication (thigh/calf) → rest pain → ulceration/gangrene; ABI <0.9 diagnostic
- **Femoral artery aneurysm** — pulsatile groin mass; often associated with popliteal aneurysm
- **Femoral access** — cardiac catheterization, angiography, TAVI; risk of haematoma, pseudoaneurysm, AV fistula
- **Femoral pseudoaneurysm** — arterial blood leaks into a tissue cavity (post-procedure); pulsatile swelling; treated with ultrasound-guided compression or thrombin injection
- **Femoral vein — DVT** — femoral DVT → PE risk; DVT propagates from calf veins
