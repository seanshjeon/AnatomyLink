# Large Intestine

> The large intestine is a **1.5-metre frame** surrounding the small bowel, responsible for **water and electrolyte absorption**, vitamin K and B production by gut flora, and formation and storage of faeces. It extends from the ileocaecal valve to the rectum.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Receives from: [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md) (at ileocaecal valve, right iliac fossa)
- Continues to: [Rectum and Anal Canal](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Lower-GI/Rectum-and-Anal-Canal.md)
- Blood supply:
  - **SMA**: cecum, ascending colon, transverse colon (right and middle colic arteries)
  - **IMA**: descending colon, sigmoid colon (left colic + sigmoid arteries)
- Venous drainage: → [Portal System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Veins/Portal-System.md)
- Relations: [Iliac Arteries](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Iliac-Arteries.md) (sigmoid crosses over), [inguinal region](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) (inguinal hernias near cecum)

## 🗂️ Segments

| Segment | Length | Location | Key Feature |
|---------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Cecum** | 6 cm | Right iliac fossa, intraperitoneal | Receives [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md) via ileocaecal valve; appendix attached |
| **Appendix** | 2–20 cm | Variable (retrocaecal most common) | Lymphoid organ; vestigial; McBurney's point |
| **Ascending colon** | 15 cm | Right side; **retroperitoneal** | Right colic (hepatic) flexure at liver |
| **Transverse colon** | 50 cm | Intraperitoneal; mobile | Attached by transverse mesocolon; hangs inferior |
| **Descending colon** | 25 cm | Left side; **retroperitoneal** | Left colic (splenic) flexure at spleen |
| **Sigmoid colon** | 40 cm | Intraperitoneal; S-shaped | Most mobile; site of diverticular disease + volvulus |

## 🔑 Distinguishing Features from Small Bowel
- **Taeniae coli** — 3 longitudinal muscle bands (gather wall into haustra)
- **Haustra** — sacculations between taeniae
- **Appendices epiploicae** — fat tags on outer wall
- **Wider diameter** — 6–8 cm vs. 2–4 cm (small bowel)

## 🔑 Splenic vs. Hepatic Flexure (Watershed Areas)
The **splenic flexure** is the watershed between SMA and IMA territories — most vulnerable to ischaemia in low-flow states (e.g., post-AAA repair).

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Colorectal cancer (CRC)** — 3rd most common cancer; adenocarcinoma; Dukes/TNM staging; right-sided: iron deficiency anaemia (occult bleeding); left-sided: change in bowel habit, obstruction; CEA marker; treat: surgical resection ± adjuvant chemotherapy
- **Diverticular disease** — outpouchings of mucosa through muscle wall (false diverticula); sigmoid most common; low-fibre diet; complications: diverticulitis (LLQ pain, fever), haemorrhage (painless rectal bleed), perforation, fistula
- **Appendicitis** — inflammation of appendix; central colicky pain migrates to RIF (McBurney's point); Rovsing's sign, psoas sign, Rebound tenderness; treat: appendicectomy
- **Sigmoid volvulus** — twist of sigmoid on its mesentery → obstruction; elderly + constipated; "coffee bean" sign on AXR; treat: flexible sigmoidoscopy decompression or surgery
- **Ulcerative colitis** — mucosal inflammation (continuous, from rectum proximally); bloody diarrhoea; crypt abscesses; lead-pipe colon on imaging; risk of CRC; treat: aminosalicylates, steroids, biologics, colectomy
- **Hirschsprung's disease** — congenital absence of ganglion cells (from anus upward); failure to pass meconium; abdominal distension; treat: pull-through surgery
