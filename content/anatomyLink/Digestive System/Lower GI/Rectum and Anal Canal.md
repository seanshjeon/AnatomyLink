# Rectum and Anal Canal

> The rectum is the **15-cm terminal segment of the large bowel**, serving as a faecal reservoir. The anal canal (~4 cm) is the final segment, controlled by internal (involuntary) and external (voluntary) anal sphincters.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Receives from: [[Large Intestine]] (sigmoid colon at S3 level)
- Blood supply:
  - Superior rectal artery (from IMA — hindgut)
  - Middle rectal artery (from internal iliac — [[Iliac Arteries]])
  - Inferior rectal artery (from internal pudendal — [[Iliac Arteries]])
- Venous drainage: **portosystemic anastomosis**
  - Superior rectal vein → inferior mesenteric vein → [[Portal System]]
  - Middle + inferior rectal veins → internal iliac → [[Inferior Vena Cava]]
- Relations: [[Sacrum]] + [[Coccyx]] (posterior), [[Hip Bone]] (lateral — levator ani)

## 🗂️ Key Anatomy

| Structure | Level | Key Feature |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| **Rectum** | S3 → tip of coccyx | 3 lateral curves (Houston's valves); no taeniae coli; no haustra; ampulla = faecal reservoir |
| **Anorectal junction** | Tip of coccyx | Anorectal ring (puborectalis + sphincters); palpable on PR exam |
| **Anal canal** | ~4 cm | Upper 2/3: columnar epithelium (visceral innervation); lower 1/3: squamous epithelium (somatic innervation) |
| **Dentate (pectinate) line** | Mid-anal canal | **Embryological junction** (endoderm above, ectoderm below); critical landmark |
| **Internal anal sphincter (IAS)** | Around upper anal canal | Smooth muscle; **involuntary**; tonic contraction (sympathetic); relaxes with rectal filling (RAIR) |
| **External anal sphincter (EAS)** | Surrounds IAS | Skeletal muscle; **voluntary**; pudendal nerve (S2–S4); maintains continence |

## 🔑 Dentate Line — The Key Landmark

| Feature | Above Dentate Line | Below Dentate Line |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Epithelium | Columnar | Squamous |
| Sensation | Visceral (stretch only — not painful) | Somatic (pain, temperature, touch) |
| Arterial supply | Superior rectal (IMA) | Inferior rectal (pudendal) |
| Venous drainage | Portal system | Systemic (IVC) |
| Lymph drainage | Internal iliac nodes | Inguinal nodes |
| Haemorrhoids | Internal (painless) | External (painful) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Haemorrhoids (piles)** — dilated anal cushion veins; internal (above dentate — painless bright rectal bleeding) vs. external (below — painful, thrombosed); 3, 7, 11 o'clock positions; treat: dietary fibre → banding → haemorrhoidectomy
- **Rectal cancer** — most rectal cancers are in lower 1/3 (within reach of finger); presents with rectal bleeding + change in habit; requires MRI staging; treat: chemoradiotherapy + TME (total mesorectal excision)
- **Anal fissure** — linear tear in anoderm, usually posterior midline; severe pain after defecation; spasm of IAS → ischaemia → poor healing; treat: GTN cream, diltiazem, botox, lateral sphincterotomy
- **Anorectal abscess / fistula-in-ano** — infected anal gland → abscess → fistula tracking; Goodsall's rule predicts fistula course; association with Crohn's disease
- **Faecal incontinence** — pudendal nerve injury (obstetric trauma), sphincter disruption, rectal prolapse; treat: pelvic floor rehab, sphincteroplasty, sacral nerve stimulation
- **Rectal prolapse** — full-thickness rectum protrudes through anus; older women; treat: Altemeier (perineal) or Delorme's procedure
