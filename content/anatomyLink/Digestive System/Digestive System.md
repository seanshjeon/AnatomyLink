# Digestive System

> The digestive system is a **9-metre muscular tube and its accessory organs**, responsible for ingestion, mechanical and chemical digestion, absorption of nutrients, and excretion of waste. It is divided into the GI tract and accessory organs.

## 🔑 Peritoneum & Mesenteries → [Peritoneum](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Peritoneum.md)
Most GI organs are covered by peritoneum. Mesenteries carry vessels and nerves to mobile organs. Retroperitoneal organs (duodenum, ascending/descending colon, [Pancreas](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Pancreas.md), kidneys) are fixed.

## 🔑 Embryological Divisions & Blood Supply (Key Exam Topic)
- **Foregut** (mouth → proximal duodenum + liver, gallbladder, pancreas) → **[Celiac Trunk](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Celiac-Trunk.md)**
- **Midgut** (distal duodenum → proximal 2/3 transverse colon) → **Superior mesenteric artery (SMA)**
- **Hindgut** (distal 1/3 transverse colon → upper anal canal) → **Inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)**

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Upper GI bleed** — haematemesis / melaena; causes: peptic ulcer, varices, Mallory-Weiss tear
- **Lower GI bleed** — haematochezia; causes: diverticulosis, colorectal cancer, IBD, haemorrhoids
- **Bowel obstruction** — small (adhesions, hernia) vs. large (cancer, volvulus)
- **Peritonitis** — peritoneal inflammation → surgical emergency
