# Gallbladder

> The gallbladder is a **pear-shaped sac (~8 cm)** on the inferior surface of the [Liver](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Liver.md), storing and concentrating bile (up to 10×) produced by the liver. It releases bile into the duodenum in response to cholecystokinin (CCK) after a fatty meal.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Attached to: [Liver](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Liver.md) (inferior surface, between right and quadrate lobes)
- Drains via: cystic duct → common bile duct → ampulla of Vater → 2nd part of duodenum ([Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md))
- Blood supply: cystic artery (branch of right hepatic artery — within Calot's triangle)
- Relations: [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md) (duodenum, first part — inferior), transverse colon

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Parts | Fundus (projects below liver edge) + body + neck → cystic duct |
| Capacity | ~30–50 mL (stores; concentrates 5–10×) |
| Emptying trigger | CCK (released by fat + protein in duodenum) → gallbladder contraction + Oddi relaxation |
| Inhibition | Somatostatin; fasting |

## 🔑 Calot's Triangle
Bounded by: **cystic duct** (inferior), **common hepatic duct** (medial), **inferior surface of liver** (superior). Contains the **cystic artery** — must be identified and clipped in cholecystectomy to avoid bleeding.

## 🔑 Bile Composition & Enterohepatic Circulation
Bile = water + bile salts + bilirubin + cholesterol + phospholipids. Bile salts are reabsorbed in the terminal ileum ([Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md)) → portal blood → liver → re-secreted (enterohepatic circulation, ~95% recycled per cycle).

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Gallstones (cholelithiasis)** — cholesterol (80%, Western), pigment (haemolytic anaemia), mixed; risk: **4 F's** (Female, Fat, Forty, Fertile)
  - Biliary colic — transient cystic duct obstruction → RUQ/epigastric pain radiating to right shoulder tip (diaphragmatic irritation); nausea/vomiting
  - Acute cholecystitis — impacted stone → inflammation; Murphy's sign (RUQ tenderness + inspiratory arrest); treat: laparoscopic cholecystectomy
  - Choledocholithiasis — stone in CBD → obstructive jaundice + raised ALP/GGT; treat: ERCP + stone extraction
  - Ascending cholangitis — Charcot's triad: RUQ pain + fever + jaundice; Reynold's pentad (+ septic shock + confusion); surgical emergency; treat: IV antibiotics + urgent ERCP
  - Gallstone ileus — large stone erodes into duodenum → small bowel obstruction; "Rigler's triad" on AXR
- **Porcelain gallbladder** — calcification of GB wall; associated with gallbladder cancer
- **Gallbladder cancer** — rare; poor prognosis; risk: gallstones, porcelain GB, typhoid carrier
- **Mirizzi syndrome** — gallstone impacted in Hartmann's pouch / cystic duct compresses common hepatic duct → jaundice
