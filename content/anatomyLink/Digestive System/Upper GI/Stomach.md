# Stomach

> The stomach is a **J-shaped muscular organ** in the left upper quadrant, serving as a reservoir and initiating protein digestion via gastric acid (HCl) and pepsinogen. It can hold ~1–1.5 litres.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Receives from: [Esophagus](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Esophagus.md) (via gastro-oesophageal junction)
- Empties into: [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Small Intestine.md) (duodenum, via pylorus)
- Blood supply: all from [Celiac Trunk](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Arteries/Celiac Trunk.md)
  - Lesser curvature: left gastric + right gastric arteries
  - Greater curvature: left + right gastroepiploic arteries
  - Fundus: short gastric arteries (from splenic artery)
- Venous drainage: → [Portal System](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Veins/Portal System.md)
- Relations: liver (right), spleen (left), [Pancreas](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Accessory Organs/Pancreas.md) (posterior), transverse colon (inferior)

## 🗂️ Regions

| Region | Location | Key Feature |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Cardia** | Near GO junction | Receives food from [Esophagus](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Esophagus.md) |
| **Fundus** | Superior dome | Gas bubble on X-ray; short gastric arteries |
| **Body** | Main region | Rugae (folds for expansion); parietal cells (HCl + intrinsic factor); chief cells (pepsinogen) |
| **Antrum** | Distal | G cells (gastrin); mixes food; antral pump |
| **Pylorus** | Junction with duodenum | Pyloric sphincter (muscular); controls gastric emptying |

## 🗂️ Gastric Secretory Cells

| Cell Type | Location | Secretion | Function |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Parietal (oxyntic)** | Body / fundus | HCl + intrinsic factor | Protein denaturation; B12 absorption |
| **Chief (peptic)** | Body / fundus | Pepsinogen | Converted to pepsin by HCl → protein digestion |
| **G cells** | Antrum | Gastrin | Stimulates parietal cells; increases motility |
| **Mucous neck cells** | Throughout | Mucus + HCO₃⁻ | Mucosal protection |
| **Enterochromaffin-like (ECL)** | Body | Histamine | Stimulates parietal cells |

## 🔑 Gastric Acid Regulation
- **Stimulated by**: gastrin (G cells), histamine (ECL cells), acetylcholine (vagus)
- **Inhibited by**: somatostatin, secretin (duodenal acidification), CCK
- **Proton pump** (H⁺/K⁺-ATPase) on parietal cells — target of PPIs (omeprazole)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Peptic ulcer disease (PUD)** — gastric or duodenal ulcer; causes: H. pylori (70%), NSAIDs, excess acid
  - Gastric ulcer: pain worse with eating; weight loss; malignancy risk → biopsy all
  - Duodenal ulcer (proximal duodenum): pain relieved by eating; no malignancy risk; posterior wall → erodes [gastroduodenal artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory System/Heart/Coronary Arteries.md) → haemorrhage
- **Gastric cancer** — adenocarcinoma (95%); risk: H. pylori, salt-preserved food, blood group A; Virchow's node (left supraclavicular), Sister Mary Joseph nodule (umbilical); Krukenberg tumour (ovarian mets)
- **Gastroparesis** — delayed gastric emptying; causes: diabetes (autonomic neuropathy), post-vagotomy
- **Pyloric stenosis** — infantile hypertrophic pyloric stenosis; projectile non-bilious vomiting at 2–8 weeks; olive-shaped mass in epigastrium; treat: Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy
- **Pernicious anaemia** — autoimmune destruction of parietal cells → no intrinsic factor → B12 deficiency → megaloblastic anaemia + subacute combined degeneration of spinal cord
- **Zollinger-Ellison syndrome** — gastrin-secreting tumour (gastrinoma) → massive acid production → recurrent severe peptic ulcers + diarrhoea
