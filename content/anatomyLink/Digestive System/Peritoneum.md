# Peritoneum

> The peritoneum is the **serous membrane lining the abdominal cavity** (parietal peritoneum) and covering abdominal organs (visceral peritoneum). The potential space between them — the **peritoneal cavity** — normally contains ~50 mL of lubricating fluid.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Contains: [Stomach](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Stomach.md), [Small Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Small-Intestine.md), [Large Intestine](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Lower-GI/Large-Intestine.md) (transverse, sigmoid), [Liver](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Liver.md), [Gallbladder](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Gallbladder.md), spleen, uterus
- Retroperitoneal organs (NOT covered): [Pancreas](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Accessory-Organs/Pancreas.md), ascending colon, descending colon, duodenum (2nd–4th parts), kidneys, adrenals, [Aorta](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Aorta.md), [Inferior Vena Cava](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Veins/Inferior-Vena-Cava.md)
- Relations: [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (posterior), [Ribs](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Ribs.md) + diaphragm (superior), [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) (pelvic peritoneum)

## 🗂️ Key Peritoneal Folds & Spaces

| Structure | Description | Contents |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Greater omentum** | Large fatty apron hanging from greater curvature of [Stomach](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Stomach.md) | Fat, lymph nodes; "policeman of the abdomen" — walls off infection |
| **Lesser omentum** | From liver → lesser curvature of [Stomach](./anatomyLink/Digestive-System/Upper-GI/Stomach.md) + duodenum | Hepatogastric + hepatoduodenal ligaments; hepatoduodenal contains portal triad (portal vein, hepatic artery, CBD) |
| **Mesentery (small bowel)** | Fan-shaped; attaches jejunum + ileum to posterior abdominal wall | SMA + SMV, lymphatics, nerves |
| **Transverse mesocolon** | Attaches transverse colon | Middle colic vessels |
| **Sigmoid mesocolon** | Attaches sigmoid colon | Sigmoid vessels; inverted V root |
| **Hepatorenal recess (Morrison's pouch)** | Between liver and right kidney | Most dependent space in supine position → fluid collects here first |
| **Pouch of Douglas (rectouterine)** | Between rectum and uterus (or bladder in males) | Most dependent pelvic space → blood/pus in pelvic pathology |

## 🔑 Retroperitoneal Structures — Mnemonic: **SAD PUCKER**
Suprarenal glands, Aorta/IVC, Duodenum (2nd–4th), Pancreas, Ureters, Colon (ascending + descending), Kidneys, Esophagus (abdominal), Rectum

## 🔑 Peritoneal Attachments & Surgical Significance
- **Pringle manoeuvre** — compresses hepatoduodenal ligament (in lesser omentum) to control hepatic inflow → controls liver haemorrhage
- **Hartmann's pouch** — recess at GB neck; site of stone impaction
- **Mesenteric ischaemia** — SMA embolism → sudden severe abdominal pain; "pain out of proportion to tenderness"; surgical emergency

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Peritonitis** — infection/inflammation of peritoneum; causes: perforated peptic ulcer, appendicitis, diverticulitis, Boerhaave; generalised guarding + rigidity + rebound tenderness; surgical emergency
- **Ascites** — fluid accumulation in peritoneal cavity; causes: cirrhosis (portal hypertension + low albumin), malignancy, cardiac failure, TB; diagnostic tap (SBP if neutrophils >250/mm³)
- **Primary peritoneal cancer** — serous carcinoma arising from peritoneum; similar to ovarian cancer; treat: platinum-based chemo
- **Pneumoperitoneum** — free air under diaphragm on erect CXR → perforated viscus until proven otherwise
- **Hernia** — protrusion of peritoneum (peritoneal sac) with contents through abdominal wall defect; inguinal (most common), femoral, umbilical, incisional
