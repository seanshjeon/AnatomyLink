# Tonsils

> The tonsils are **aggregates of lymphoid tissue** forming **Waldeyer's tonsillar ring** around the pharyngeal inlet, providing the first line of mucosal immune defence against inhaled and ingested antigens.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Lymphatic System](./anatomyLink/Lymphatic System/Lymphatic System.md)
- Located in: Pharynx (upper aerodigestive tract) → [Oral Cavity](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Oral Cavity.md), [Larynx](./anatomyLink/Digestive System/Upper GI/Larynx.md)
- Lymphatics: Drain to upper deep cervical nodes (jugulodigastric — "tonsillar node")
- Blood supply: Tonsillar branch of facial artery (palatine); ascending pharyngeal artery

## 🔬 Waldeyer's Ring Components

| Tonsil | Location | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Pharyngeal tonsil (adenoids)** | Posterior nasopharynx roof | Hypertrophy → nasal obstruction, OSA, glue ear |
| **Tubal tonsils** | Around Eustachian tube orifice (×2) | |
| **Palatine tonsils** | Tonsillar fossa between pillars (×2) | Most commonly infected; "tonsillitis" |
| **Lingual tonsils** | Base of tongue (×2) | |

**Crypts** — deep invaginations in palatine tonsils; trap debris → tonsilloliths

## ⚙️ Function
- Antigen sampling at mucosal surface
- Lymphocyte activation → IgA secretion into mucosa
- Immunologically most active in early childhood

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tonsillitis** — viral (EBV, adenovirus) or bacterial (*S. pyogenes* — Group A Strep); exudative pharyngitis; Centor criteria; quinsy (peritonsillar abscess) is a complication
- **Adenoid hypertrophy** — children; nasal obstruction, snoring, conductive hearing loss (glue ear via Eustachian tube); adenoidectomy
- **OSA (obstructive sleep apnoea)** — tonsillar + adenoid hypertrophy is common cause in children; tonsillectomy often curative
- **Tonsillectomy indications** — recurrent tonsillitis (≥7/year or ≥5/year × 2 years or ≥3/year × 3 years), peritonsillar abscess, OSA
- **Tonsillar cancer** — squamous cell carcinoma; HPV-associated; presents with neck mass (metastasis)
