# Ventricular System & CSF

> The **ventricular system** is a series of interconnected cavities within the [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) filled with **cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)**. CSF cushions the brain, removes waste, and maintains ionic balance. Total CSF volume ~150 mL; produced at ~500 mL/day → replaced 3–4× daily.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) — ventricles are embedded within brain tissue
- [Spinal Cord](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Spinal-Cord.md) — central canal continuous with 4th ventricle; subarachnoid space surrounds cord
- [Meninges](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Meninges.md) — CSF flows in the subarachnoid space between arachnoid and pia

## 🗂️ CSF Flow Pathway

```
Choroid plexus (lateral ventricles) → Interventricular foramen (Foramen of Monro)
→ Third ventricle → Cerebral aqueduct (Aqueduct of Sylvius)
→ Fourth ventricle → Foramina of Luschka (×2, lateral) + Foramen of Magendie (×1, median)
→ Subarachnoid space → Arachnoid granulations → Dural venous sinuses → Venous blood
```

## 🗂️ Ventricles

| Ventricle | Location | Key Connections |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Lateral ventricles** (×2) | Within cerebral hemispheres; C-shaped | → 3rd ventricle via foramen of Monro |
| **Third ventricle** | Midline diencephalon; between thalami | → 4th ventricle via cerebral aqueduct |
| **Cerebral aqueduct** (Aqueduct of Sylvius) | Through midbrain | Connects 3rd ↔ 4th ventricle |
| **Fourth ventricle** | Between pons/medulla and cerebellum | → Subarachnoid space via Luschka + Magendie |

### Lateral Ventricle Anatomy
| Horn | Location | Adjacent Structure |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| Anterior (frontal) horn | Frontal lobe | Caudate nucleus (lateral wall) |
| Body | Parietal lobe | Thalamus (floor), corpus callosum (roof) |
| Posterior (occipital) horn | Occipital lobe | Calcarine sulcus |
| Inferior (temporal) horn | Temporal lobe | Hippocampus (floor) |

## 🔑 CSF Composition & Production
| Property | Normal Value |
|----------|-------------|
| Color | Clear, colorless ("gin clear") |
| Pressure | 70–180 mmH₂O (opening pressure) |
| Volume | ~150 mL total (25 mL ventricles, 125 mL subarachnoid) |
| Production rate | ~0.35 mL/min = 500 mL/day |
| Production site | Choroid plexus (70%) — lateral > 3rd > 4th ventricle |
| Protein | 15–45 mg/dL |
| Glucose | 60–80 mg/dL (60–70% of blood glucose) |
| WBC | 0–5 lymphocytes/μL |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

### Hydrocephalus
| Type | Mechanism | Cause | Treatment |
|------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **Obstructive (non-communicating)** | Blockage within ventricular system | Aqueductal stenosis, tumor, hemorrhage | VP shunt; ETV |
| **Communicating** | Impaired CSF reabsorption at arachnoid granulations | Post-meningitis, post-SAH, idiopathic | LP shunt; serial LPs |
| **Normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH)** | Enlarged ventricles, normal pressure | Idiopathic (elderly) | **Triad: Wet, Wobbly, Wacky** (incontinence, gait ataxia, dementia); LP + VP shunt |
| **Hydrocephalus ex vacuo** | Brain atrophy → ventricles enlarge | Alzheimer's, stroke | No treatment (not true hydrocephalus) |

### Lumbar Puncture (LP)
- **Level**: L3–L4 or L4–L5 (below spinal cord at L1–L2)
- **Landmarks**: Iliac crest → L4 vertebra
- **Contraindication**: Raised ICP with mass lesion (risk of tonsillar herniation)
- **Opening pressure**: Measured with patient lateral decubitus

### Xanthochromia
- Yellow discoloration of CSF due to RBC breakdown products (oxyhemoglobin → bilirubin)
- Distinguishes **true SAH** from traumatic tap
- Appears 2–4 hours after SAH, persists up to 2 weeks
