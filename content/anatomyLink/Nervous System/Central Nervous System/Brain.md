# Brain

> The brain is the **command center of the CNS**, housed within the [[Skull]], composed of the **cerebrum (4 lobes), cerebellum, and brainstem**, and giving rise to all **12 [[Cranial Nerves]]**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Nervous System]] — parent system
- [[Spinal Cord]] — inferior continuation via foramen magnum
- [[Cranial Nerves]] — 12 pairs originating from the brainstem and brain
- [[Skull]] — bony protection (cranial vault)
- [[Vertebral Column]] — continuation of CNS protection

## 🗂️ Key Information

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Weight | ~1,400 g (adult) |
| Protection | Skull, meninges (dura, arachnoid, pia), CSF |
| Blood supply | Internal carotid arteries + vertebral arteries (Circle of Willis) |
| Major divisions | Cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem (midbrain, pons, medulla) |
| Ventricular system | 2 lateral ventricles, 3rd ventricle, cerebral aqueduct, 4th ventricle |

## 🔑 Cerebral Lobes and Functions

| Lobe | Location | Key Functions |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Frontal** | Anterior; anterior to central sulcus | Primary motor cortex, prefrontal cortex (executive function), Broca's area (speech production), personality |
| **Parietal** | Posterior to central sulcus | Primary somatosensory cortex, spatial awareness, integration of sensory input |
| **Temporal** | Lateral (Sylvian fissure) | Primary auditory cortex, Wernicke's area (speech comprehension), memory (hippocampus) |
| **Occipital** | Posterior pole | Primary visual cortex, visual processing |

### Deep Structures
- **[[Basal Ganglia]]** — motor coordination, habit formation; damaged in Parkinson's disease
- **Thalamus** — sensory relay station ("gateway to cortex")
- **Hypothalamus** — autonomic and endocrine control; see [[Limbic System]]
- **Hippocampus** (temporal lobe) — explicit memory formation; see [[Limbic System]]
- **Amygdala** (temporal lobe) — fear, emotional memory

## 🔑 Brainstem and Cranial Nerve Origins

The [[Brainstem]] consists of midbrain, pons, and medulla oblongata. It contains all cranial nerve nuclei except CN I and CN II.

| Brainstem Level | Cranial Nerves | Key Functions |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Midbrain** | CN III (oculomotor), CN IV (trochlear) | Eye movement, pupillary light reflex |
| **Pons** | CN V (trigeminal), CN VI (abducens), CN VII (facial), CN VIII (vestibulocochlear) | Facial sensation/movement, hearing, balance |
| **Medulla** | CN IX (glossopharyngeal), CN X (vagus), CN XI (accessory), CN XII (hypoglossal) | Swallowing, cardiac/respiratory control, tongue movement |
| **Olfactory bulb** | CN I (olfactory) | Smell (not from brainstem proper) |
| **Diencephalon** | CN II (optic) | Vision (not from brainstem proper) |

### [[Cerebellum]]
Located in the posterior fossa. Coordinates movement, balance, and fine motor control. Receives input from [[Spinal Cord]] and motor cortex; outputs to thalamus. Damage causes ipsilateral ataxia, dysmetria, intention tremor.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

- **Stroke (CVA)** — ischemic (thrombotic/embolic) or hemorrhagic. Middle cerebral artery (MCA) stroke: contralateral hemiplegia + hemianesthesia + aphasia (if dominant hemisphere). Posterior cerebral artery (PCA) stroke: contralateral homonymous hemianopia.
- **Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)** — epidural hematoma (lucid interval → rapid deterioration, lenticular/biconvex hematoma on CT, middle meningeal artery tear); subdural hematoma (bridging veins, crescent-shaped on CT, more chronic in elderly).
- **Transtentorial (uncal) herniation** — expanding mass forces uncus over tentorium → compresses ipsilateral CN III ("blown pupil" — mydriasis, ptosis, "down and out" eye) → contralateral hemiplegia.
- **Tonsillar herniation** — cerebellar tonsils forced through foramen magnum → brainstem compression → respiratory arrest.
- **Raised ICP** — Cushing's triad: hypertension + bradycardia + irregular respirations.
- **Broca's aphasia** — expressive (non-fluent) aphasia; frontal lobe lesion (dominant hemisphere).
- **Wernicke's aphasia** — receptive (fluent but nonsensical) aphasia; temporal lobe lesion (dominant hemisphere).
