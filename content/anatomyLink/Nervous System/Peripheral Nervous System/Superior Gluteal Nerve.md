# Superior Gluteal Nerve

> The **superior gluteal nerve** (L4, L5, S1) exits through the [[Greater Sciatic Notch]] **above** the piriformis and supplies [[Gluteus Medius]], [[Gluteus Minimus]], and [[Tensor Fasciae Latae]] — the primary hip abductors.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [[Sacral Plexus]] (L4, L5, S1)
- **Exit** — Greater sciatic foramen, superior to piriformis
- **Muscles supplied** — [[Gluteus Medius]], [[Gluteus Minimus]], [[Tensor Fasciae Latae]]

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Trendelenburg gait** — Damage causes hip abductor weakness; pelvis drops on unsupported side
- **Hip arthroplasty** — Superior gluteal nerve at risk in superior approaches to the hip
