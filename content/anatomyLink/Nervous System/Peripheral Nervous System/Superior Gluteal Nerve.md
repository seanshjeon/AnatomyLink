# Superior Gluteal Nerve

> The **superior gluteal nerve** (L4, L5, S1) exits through the [Greater Sciatic Notch](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Greater Sciatic Notch.md) **above** the piriformis and supplies [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Medius.md), [Gluteus Minimus](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Minimus.md), and [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tensor Fasciae Latae.md) — the primary hip abductors.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Sacral Plexus.md) (L4, L5, S1)
- **Exit** — Greater sciatic foramen, superior to piriformis
- **Muscles supplied** — [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Medius.md), [Gluteus Minimus](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Minimus.md), [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tensor Fasciae Latae.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Trendelenburg gait** — Damage causes hip abductor weakness; pelvis drops on unsupported side
- **Hip arthroplasty** — Superior gluteal nerve at risk in superior approaches to the hip
