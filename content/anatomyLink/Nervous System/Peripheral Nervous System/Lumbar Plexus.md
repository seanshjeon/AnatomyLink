# Lumbar Plexus

> The lumbar plexus is formed by the **anterior rami of L1–L4** (with a contribution from T12) within the substance of [Iliopsoas](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliopsoas.md), supplying the anterior and medial compartments of the thigh and the inguinal region.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Root values: L1–L4 (+ T12 contribution)
- Formed within: [Iliopsoas](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliopsoas.md) (psoas major muscle)
- Relation to: [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (roots exit intervertebral foramina)
- Joins with: [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Sacral-Plexus.md) (via lumbosacral trunk L4–L5) → lumbosacral plexus
- Key terminal nerve: [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md)

## 🗂️ Branches

| Nerve | Roots | Course | Supply |
|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Iliohypogastric** | L1 (T12) | Above inguinal ligament | Skin of hypogastrium + lateral buttock |
| **Ilioinguinal** | L1 | Inguinal canal | Skin of medial thigh, scrotum/labia majora, mons pubis |
| **Genitofemoral** | L1–L2 | On anterior surface of [Iliopsoas](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliopsoas.md) → inguinal canal | Genital branch: cremaster reflex; femoral branch: skin of femoral triangle |
| **Lateral femoral cutaneous (LFCN)** | L2–L3 | Exits beneath inguinal ligament (medial to ASIS) | Lateral thigh sensation only (purely sensory) |
| **Obturator nerve** | L2–L4 | Through obturator foramen ([Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)) | Adductor muscles; medial thigh sensation |
| **[Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md)** | L2–L4 | Exits below inguinal ligament (lateral to femoral artery) | [Quadriceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadriceps-Femoris.md), [Iliopsoas](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliopsoas.md), sartorius, pectineus; anterior thigh + medial leg (saphenous) |
| **Lumbosacral trunk** | L4–L5 | Crosses pelvic brim → joins [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Sacral-Plexus.md) | Contribution to sciatic + superior/inferior gluteal nerves |

## 🔑 Femoral Triangle and the Lumbar Plexus
In the femoral triangle, branches emerge **lateral to medial**: [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md) → femoral artery ([Femoral Artery](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Femoral-Artery.md)) → femoral vein — mnemonic **NAVY** (Nerve, Artery, Vein, Y-fronts/lymphatics) lateral to medial.

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Meralgia paraesthetica** — entrapment of lateral femoral cutaneous nerve (LFCN) at inguinal ligament → burning/tingling/numbness of lateral thigh; risk: obesity, tight belts, pregnancy; purely sensory (no motor loss)
- **Femoral nerve palsy** — see [Femoral Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Femoral-Nerve.md); most commonly from inguinal ligament compression, haematoma, pelvic mass, or during hip surgery
- **Obturator nerve injury** — pelvic surgery, obturator hernia, childbirth → adductor weakness + medial thigh numbness; difficulty crossing legs
- **Ilioinguinal nerve entrapment** — post-hernia repair pain (groin + inner thigh + scrotum/labia); common complication of open inguinal herniorrhaphy
- **Psoas abscess** — infection within [Iliopsoas](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Iliopsoas.md) can compress lumbar plexus → leg pain + hip flexion deformity; caused by TB of [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Lumbar-Vertebrae.md) (Pott's disease) or Crohn's disease
