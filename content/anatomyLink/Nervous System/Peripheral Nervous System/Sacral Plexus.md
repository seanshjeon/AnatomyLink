# Sacral Plexus

> The sacral plexus is formed by the **lumbosacral trunk (L4–L5) + anterior rami of S1–S3** on the anterior surface of piriformis, deep in the pelvis. It supplies the posterior thigh, entire leg and foot, gluteal muscles, and perineum.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- Root values: L4–S3 (via lumbosacral trunk from [Lumbar Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Lumbar-Plexus.md))
- Formed on: anterior surface of piriformis muscle, against [Sacrum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Sacrum.md) and [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)
- Exits via: greater sciatic foramen (most branches)
- Key terminal nerve: [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md) (largest nerve in the body)
- Relation to: [Iliac Arteries](./anatomyLink/Circulatory-System/Arteries/Iliac-Arteries.md) (internal iliac vessels cross anterior to plexus)

## 🗂️ Branches

| Nerve | Roots | Exit | Supply |
|-------|-------|------|--------|
| **[Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md)** | L4–S3 | Greater sciatic foramen (below piriformis) | Entire lower limb below knee via [Tibial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Tibial-Nerve.md) + [Common Fibular Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Common-Fibular-Nerve.md); posterior thigh muscles ([Hamstrings](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Hamstrings.md)) |
| **Superior gluteal nerve** | L4–S1 | Greater sciatic foramen (above piriformis) | [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Medius.md), gluteus minimus, tensor fasciae latae |
| **Inferior gluteal nerve** | L5–S2 | Greater sciatic foramen (below piriformis) | [Gluteus Maximus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Maximus.md) |
| **Nerve to piriformis** | S1–S2 | Direct | Piriformis |
| **Nerve to obturator internus** | L5–S2 | Lesser sciatic foramen | Obturator internus, superior gemellus |
| **Nerve to quadratus femoris** | L4–S1 | Below piriformis | Quadratus femoris, inferior gemellus |
| **Posterior cutaneous nerve of thigh** | S1–S3 | Below piriformis | Skin of posterior thigh, perineum, posterior scrotum/labia |
| **Pudendal nerve** | S2–S4 | Lesser sciatic foramen (via Alcock's canal) | External anal sphincter, external urethral sphincter, perineal muscles, genitalia; somatic sensation of perineum |
| **Pelvic splanchnics** | S2–S4 | Direct into pelvis | Parasympathetic to pelvic viscera (bladder, rectum, uterus — "erection and erection" S2–S4) |

## 🔑 The Piriformis Divide
The piriformis muscle is the key landmark — almost all sacral plexus branches exit the greater sciatic foramen either **above** (superior gluteal) or **below** (everything else) piriformis.

## 🔑 Parasympathetic Output (S2–S4)
The pelvic splanchnic nerves carry the **sacral parasympathetic** outflow — responsible for:
- Bladder detrusor contraction (micturition)
- Rectal motility + defecation
- Penile/clitoral erection ("point, shoot, score" S2–S4 erection; T10–L2 emission; S2–S4 ejaculation)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Sciatic nerve injury** — see [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md); posterior hip dislocation most common cause at this level
- **Piriformis syndrome** — sciatic nerve irritation by piriformis (especially if nerve passes through muscle); buttock pain + sciatica; positive FAIR test
- **Superior gluteal nerve injury** — pelvic surgery or posterior approach hip arthroplasty; [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Medius.md) paralysis → **Trendelenburg gait** (pelvis drops on opposite side when weight-bearing)
- **Pudendal nerve injury** — childbirth (stretching over ischial spine); faecal + urinary incontinence; sexual dysfunction; perineal anaesthesia
- **Cauda equina syndrome** — technically involves lumbar + sacral roots before they join the plexus; see [Spinal Cord](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Spinal-Cord.md)
- **Sacral insufficiency fracture** — elderly + osteoporotic; H-pattern fracture on bone scan; can compress sacral plexus roots
- **Pudendal nerve block** — used in obstetric analgesia; needle directed toward ischial spine (pudendal nerve wraps around it)
