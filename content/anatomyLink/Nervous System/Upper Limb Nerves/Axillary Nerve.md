# Axillary Nerve

> The axillary nerve (C5, C6) is a terminal branch of the posterior cord of the [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md) that **innervates the deltoid and teres minor** and provides sensation to the regimental badge area of the lateral upper arm.

## 🔗 Origin
- **Root values**: C5–C6
- **From plexus**: [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md)
- **Cord**: Posterior cord

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Root Values | C5–C6 |
| Plexus Origin | [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md) (posterior cord) |
| Course | Exits axilla through the quadrangular space with the posterior circumflex humeral artery; winds around the surgical neck of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) |
| Sensory Territory | Regimental badge area — lateral upper arm (over lower deltoid) |
| Terminal Branches | Anterior branch (motor to deltoid), posterior branch (motor to teres minor + superior lateral cutaneous nerve of arm) |

## 💪 Muscles Innervated
- [Deltoid](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Deltoid.md) (anterior, middle, and posterior parts) — shoulder abduction (middle), flexion (anterior), extension (posterior)
- Teres Minor (part of [Rotator Cuff](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Rotator Cuff.md)) — lateral rotation of the arm

## 🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships
- Passes through the **quadrangular space**, bounded by:
  - Superiorly: teres minor / subscapularis
  - Inferiorly: teres major
  - Medially: long head of [Triceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Triceps Brachii.md)
  - Laterally: surgical neck of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md)
- Travels with the posterior circumflex humeral artery
- Lies in close contact with the surgical neck of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) — the most clinically important relationship

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Axillary nerve palsy** — caused by surgical neck fracture of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) or anterior shoulder dislocation
  - Motor deficit: deltoid paralysis → flat (squared-off) shoulder, loss of shoulder abduction (0–15° arc), weak flexion and extension
  - Sensory deficit: loss of sensation over the regimental badge area (lateral upper arm)
  - Test: inability to initiate abduction against resistance
- **Anterior shoulder dislocation** — humeral head displaces anteriorly and inferiorly, stretching the axillary nerve
- **Quadrangular space syndrome** — rare compression causing posterior shoulder pain and deltoid weakness
- Distinguish from **suprascapular nerve** injury (supraspinatus + infraspinatus weakness, different sensory territory)
