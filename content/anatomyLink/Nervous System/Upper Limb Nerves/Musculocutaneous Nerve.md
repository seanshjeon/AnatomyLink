# Musculocutaneous Nerve

> The musculocutaneous nerve (C5, C6) arises from the lateral cord of the [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md), **innervates the three anterior arm muscles** (coracobrachialis, [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md), brachialis), and continues as the lateral cutaneous nerve of the forearm.

## 🔗 Origin
- **Root values**: C5–C6
- **From plexus**: [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md)
- **Cord**: Lateral cord

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Root Values | C5–C6 |
| Plexus Origin | [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md) (lateral cord) |
| Course | Pierces coracobrachialis → descends between [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) and brachialis → emerges lateral to [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) tendon at the elbow → continues as lateral cutaneous nerve of the forearm |
| Sensory Territory | Lateral forearm (anterior and posterior surfaces) via lateral cutaneous nerve of forearm |
| Terminal Branches | Lateral cutaneous nerve of the forearm (purely sensory continuation) |

## 💪 Muscles Innervated
- Coracobrachialis — weak arm flexion + adduction at shoulder
- [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) — elbow flexion (primary in supination) + forearm supination; long head also assists shoulder flexion/abduction
- Brachialis — **primary elbow flexor** in all forearm positions (the "workhorse" of elbow flexion)

## 🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships
- Pierces the coracobrachialis muscle shortly after arising from the lateral cord (useful anatomical landmark)
- Travels between [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) (anterior) and brachialis (posterior) in the anterior compartment of the arm
- Emerges lateral to the [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) tendon just above the elbow — becomes lateral cutaneous nerve of forearm
- Passes lateral to the brachial artery and median nerve in the axilla
- The lateral cutaneous nerve of the forearm travels with the cephalic vein on the lateral forearm

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Musculocutaneous nerve palsy** — relatively rare due to the nerve's protected course deep to [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md)
  - Mechanism: penetrating axillary trauma, forced shoulder hyperextension, prolonged carrying heavy bags on shoulder, surgical positioning
  - Motor deficit: weak elbow flexion (brachialis partially compensates as it also receives some C5 contribution via radial nerve); weak supination
  - Sensory deficit: lateral forearm numbness/tingling
  - Reflex: biceps jerk diminished or absent (C5–C6)
- **Biceps reflex testing** — tests C5–C6 via musculocutaneous nerve; diminished in musculocutaneous nerve injury or C5/C6 radiculopathy
- **Coracobrachialis entrapment** — nerve can be compressed as it pierces the muscle, especially in hypertrophied athletes
- **Lateral cutaneous nerve of forearm entrapment** — isolated sensory syndrome causing lateral forearm paraesthesia; can occur at the elbow with hyperextension or compression by [Biceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Biceps-Brachii.md) tendon
- Distinguish from **C5–C6 radiculopathy** (also causes biceps weakness + diminished biceps reflex, but with neck pain and additional muscles affected)
