# Median Nerve

> The median nerve (C6–T1), formed from the lateral and medial cords of the [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md), **innervates most anterior forearm flexors and the LOAF thenar muscles**; it is the nerve most commonly compressed at the wrist (carpal tunnel syndrome).

## 🔗 Origin
- **Root values**: C6–T1
- **From plexus**: [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md)
- **Cord**: Lateral cord (C6–C7) + Medial cord (C8–T1) — forms an "M" shape around the axillary artery

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Root Values | C6–T1 |
| Plexus Origin | [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Brachial Plexus.md) (lateral + medial cords) |
| Course | No branches in the arm → enters forearm between two heads of pronator teres → gives off anterior interosseous nerve (AIN) → runs deep to FDS → gives palmar cutaneous branch (5 cm above wrist) → passes through carpal tunnel with 9 flexor tendons → divides into recurrent motor branch and digital branches |
| Sensory Territory | Palmar surface of lateral 3.5 digits + nail beds; palm via palmar cutaneous branch (not affected in CTS) |
| Terminal Branches | Recurrent (thenar) motor branch, common palmar digital nerves, anterior interosseous nerve (AIN) |

## 💪 Muscles Innervated
**Forearm (anterior compartment):**
- Pronator teres — forearm pronation
- Flexor carpi radialis (FCR) — wrist flexion + radial deviation
- Palmaris longus — wrist flexion (absent in ~15%)
- Flexor digitorum superficialis (FDS) — PIP joint flexion (all 4 fingers)

**Via Anterior Interosseous Nerve (AIN):**
- Flexor digitorum profundus (FDP) to digits 1–2 — DIP joint flexion of index + middle fingers
- Flexor pollicis longus (FPL) — thumb IP flexion
- Pronator quadratus — forearm pronation (primary pronator)

**Hand (LOAF muscles — via recurrent motor branch):**
- Lumbricals 1 and 2 — MCP flexion + IP extension of index and middle fingers
- Opponens pollicis — thumb opposition
- Abductor pollicis brevis (APB) — thumb abduction
- Flexor pollicis brevis (FPB, superficial head) — thumb MCP flexion

## 🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships
- Passes between the two heads of pronator teres (site of pronator teres syndrome)
- Runs beneath the fibrous arch of FDS in the forearm
- Palmar cutaneous branch exits 5 cm proximal to the wrist — travels superficial to the flexor retinaculum (NOT through carpal tunnel — preserved in CTS)
- Passes through the **carpal tunnel** (beneath the flexor retinaculum) with 9 tendons: FDS ×4, FDP ×4, FPL ×1
- Recurrent motor branch hooks back into the thenar eminence — vulnerable in carpal tunnel release

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS)** — most common peripheral nerve compression
  - Mechanism: compression of median nerve under flexor retinaculum
  - Motor deficit: thenar wasting (especially APB); weak opposition
  - Sensory deficit: tingling/numbness in lateral 3.5 digits; worse at night; palm spared (palmar cutaneous branch not compressed)
  - Tests: **Tinel's sign** (tapping over carpal tunnel reproduces symptoms), **Phalen's test** (wrist flexion for 60 seconds), **reverse Phalen's**, **carpal tunnel compression test**
  - Causes: idiopathic, pregnancy, hypothyroidism, rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes, repetitive use
- **AIN palsy** — compression of anterior interosseous nerve
  - Deficit: cannot make "OK sign" — loss of FPL (thumb IP flexion) and FDP to index (DIP flexion)
  - Pinch becomes pulp-to-pulp rather than tip-to-tip
  - No sensory loss
- **High median nerve lesion (above elbow)** — "Hand of benediction" at rest
  - Ring and little fingers flex (FDP intact via ulnar nerve) but index and middle fingers remain extended (FDP to digits 1-2 lost)
  - True "benediction" posture on attempted grip
- **Pronator teres syndrome** — compression at pronator teres; similar to CTS but palm also affected (palmar cutaneous branch involved)
- **Ape hand deformity** — thenar wasting + loss of opposition (chronic median nerve injury)
