# Ulnar Nerve

> The ulnar nerve (C8, T1), the terminal branch of the medial cord of the [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md), **innervates most intrinsic hand muscles and produces the classic claw hand deformity** when injured; it is the most commonly injured nerve at the elbow.

## 🔗 Origin
- **Root values**: C8, T1
- **From plexus**: [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md)
- **Cord**: Medial cord

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Root Values | C8, T1 |
| Plexus Origin | [Brachial Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Peripheral-Nervous-System/Brachial-Plexus.md) (medial cord) |
| Course | Descends medial arm → passes posterior to medial epicondyle of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) (cubital tunnel) → enters forearm between two heads of FCU → descends with ulnar artery → gives dorsal cutaneous branch (8 cm above wrist) → enters hand through Guyon's canal (ulnar tunnel) → divides into superficial (sensory) and deep (motor) branches |
| Sensory Territory | Medial 1.5 digits (palmar + dorsal), medial palm, dorsal medial hand |
| Terminal Branches | Superficial branch (sensory to medial 1.5 digits), deep branch (motor to hypothenar + intrinsics) |

## 💪 Muscles Innervated
**Forearm:**
- Flexor carpi ulnaris (FCU) — wrist flexion + ulnar deviation
- Flexor digitorum profundus (FDP) to digits 4–5 (medial half) — DIP flexion of ring and little fingers

**Hand (via deep branch):**
- All hypothenar muscles (abductor digiti minimi, flexor digiti minimi, opponens digiti minimi) — little finger abduction, flexion, opposition
- Adductor pollicis — thumb adduction (key pinch)
- All 4 dorsal interossei — finger abduction (DAB)
- All 3 palmar interossei — finger adduction (PAD)
- Lumbricals 3 and 4 — MCP flexion + IP extension of ring and little fingers
- Flexor pollicis brevis (FPB, deep head) — thumb MCP flexion

## 🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships
- Passes behind the **medial epicondyle** of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) in the cubital tunnel — palpable and vulnerable to direct trauma ("funny bone")
- Lies between FCU and FDP in the forearm
- Dorsal cutaneous branch given off 8 cm proximal to the wrist (travels dorsomedially — not through Guyon's canal, so preserved in Guyon's canal lesions)
- Enters hand through **Guyon's canal** — bounded by pisiform (medially), hook of hamate (laterally), palmar carpal ligament (roof), flexor retinaculum (floor)
- Deep branch winds around the hook of hamate

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Cubital tunnel syndrome** — most common ulnar nerve entrapment (at medial epicondyle)
  - Mechanism: compression/stretch at elbow, especially with prolonged flexion
  - Motor deficit: weak grip, weak key pinch, interosseous wasting, hypothenar wasting
  - Sensory deficit: medial 1.5 digits, medial palm and dorsum (dorsal cutaneous branch also affected)
  - Deformity: **claw hand** (ring and little fingers) — intrinsic minus posture: MCP hyperextension, PIP/DIP flexion (most pronounced in little and ring fingers)
- **Ulnar paradox**: High lesion (above elbow) → less clawing than low lesion (at wrist)
  - High lesion: FDP to digits 4–5 is also paralysed → less DIP flexion → less severe claw
  - Low lesion: FDP intact → full DIP flexion → more pronounced claw
- **Guyon's canal syndrome (cyclist's palsy)** — compression at the wrist
  - If superficial to bifurcation: both motor + sensory (medial 1.5 digits only — dorsal cutaneous branch spared)
  - If deep branch only: pure motor deficit (no sensory loss)
  - Causes: prolonged cycling, ganglion cyst, hook of hamate fracture
- **Froment's sign** — adductor pollicis paralysis → patient uses FPL (median) to compensate during key pinch → thumb IP flexes rather than adducts
- **Wartenberg's sign** — little finger held in abduction at rest due to unopposed abductor digiti minimi (EDM also pulls it out) and paralysed 3rd palmar interosseous
- **Medial epicondyle fracture** — common in children; ulnar nerve at risk
