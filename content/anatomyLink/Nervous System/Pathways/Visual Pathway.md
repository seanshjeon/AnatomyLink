# Visual Pathway

> The **visual pathway** transmits visual information from the **retina** → **optic nerve (CN II)** → **optic chiasm** → **optic tract** → **lateral geniculate nucleus (LGN)** → **optic radiation** → **primary visual cortex (V1)** in the occipital lobe.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Cranial Nerves](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Cranial Nerves.md) — CN II (optic nerve) is the first segment
- [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Central Nervous System/Brain.md) — occipital lobe (V1), temporal/parietal lobes (V2+), thalamus (LGN)
- [Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Skull Region/Skull.md) — optic canal transmits CN II

## 🗂️ The Pathway Step by Step

```
Retina (nasal + temporal fibers)
        ↓
Optic Nerve (CN II) — ipsilateral
        ↓
Optic Chiasm — nasal fibers CROSS; temporal fibers stay ipsilateral
        ↓
Optic Tract — carries contralateral visual field
        ↓
Lateral Geniculate Nucleus (LGN) — thalamus relay
        ↓
Optic Radiation — Meyer's loop (inferior) + Baum's loop (superior)
        ↓
Primary Visual Cortex V1 (calcarine sulcus, occipital lobe)
```

### Fiber Decussation at Optic Chiasm
| Fiber | Origin | Crosses? | Ends Up In |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Nasal retinal fibers** | Medial retina (temporal visual field) | ✅ Crosses | Contralateral optic tract |
| **Temporal retinal fibers** | Lateral retina (nasal visual field) | ❌ Stays | Ipsilateral optic tract |

> **Result**: Each optic tract carries the **contralateral visual field** from **both eyes**

### Meyer's Loop (Temporal Lobe)
- Lower optic radiation sweeps through temporal lobe
- Carries **upper visual field** information
- Temporal lobe lesion (e.g., tumor) → **"pie in the sky"** defect (upper contralateral quadrantanopia)

## 🔑 Visual Field Defects — Localizing Lesions

```
         LEFT EYE        RIGHT EYE
         [Temporal|Nasal] [Nasal|Temporal]
                   ↓ ↓
              OPTIC CHIASM
                   ↓
         L optic tract  R optic tract
                   ↓
              L LGN      R LGN
                   ↓
         L optic radiation  R optic radiation
                   ↓
         L visual cortex  R visual cortex
```

| Lesion Site | Visual Field Defect | Cause |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Optic nerve (A)** | Monocular blindness (ipsilateral eye) | MS, optic neuritis, glaucoma |
| **Optic chiasm (B)** | **Bitemporal hemianopia** ("tunnel vision") | Pituitary adenoma compressing chiasm |
| **Optic tract (C)** | **Contralateral homonymous hemianopia** | Stroke (MCA), tumor |
| **Meyer's loop (D)** | **Contralateral upper quadrantanopia** ("pie in sky") | Temporal lobe surgery/tumor |
| **Superior optic radiation (E)** | **Contralateral lower quadrantanopia** ("pie on floor") | Parietal lobe lesion |
| **Occipital cortex (F)** | **Contralateral homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing** | PCA stroke (macula = dual supply) |

## 🔑 Pupillary Light Reflex
```
Light → Retina → CN II → Pretectal nucleus (midbrain) → Edinger-Westphal nucleus (CN III)
→ Ciliary ganglion → Pupillary constrictor (both eyes via crossing)
```
- **Direct reflex**: light in one eye → that eye constricts
- **Consensual reflex**: light in one eye → other eye also constricts
- **RAPD (Relative Afferent Pupillary Defect)**: CN II lesion → swinging flashlight test positive

## 🔑 Accommodation Reflex (Near Triad)
Convergence + Accommodation + Miosis — mediated by CN III
- Lesion: **Argyll Robertson pupil** (accommodates but doesn't react to light) — neurosyphilis

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Optic neuritis** — Demyelination of CN II (MS); painful vision loss; afferent pupillary defect; spontaneous recovery
- **Pituitary macroadenoma** — Bitemporal hemianopia from chiasm compression; superior temporal fields lost first
- **PCA stroke** — Contralateral homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing (occipital pole has MCA supply)
- **Glaucoma** — Progressive optic nerve damage → arcuate field defects → tunnel vision → blindness
- **Retinal detachment** — Monocular visual field loss; "curtain coming down"
