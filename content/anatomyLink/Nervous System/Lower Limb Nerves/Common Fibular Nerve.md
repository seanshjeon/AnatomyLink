# Common Fibular Nerve

> The common fibular nerve (L4–S2), the smaller lateral terminal division of the [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md), **winds around the fibular neck making it the most commonly injured nerve in the lower limb**; injury causes foot drop with characteristic steppage gait.

## 🔗 Origin
- **Root values**: L4–S2
- **From plexus**: [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Sacral Plexus.md) (via [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md))
- **Cord**: Lateral division of [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md)

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Root Values | L4–S2 |
| Plexus Origin | [Sacral Plexus](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Peripheral Nervous System/Sacral Plexus.md) via [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Lower Limb Nerves/Sciatic Nerve.md) |
| Course | Diverges from tibial nerve at apex of popliteal fossa → runs along the medial border of biceps femoris → winds around the **neck of the [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md)** (most vulnerable point) → pierces the fibularis longus → divides into deep fibular nerve (anterior compartment) and superficial fibular nerve (lateral compartment) |
| Sensory Territory | Lateral leg and dorsum of foot (superficial fibular nerve); first web space (dorsal — deep fibular nerve); lateral knee (lateral cutaneous nerve of calf) |
| Terminal Branches | Deep fibular nerve (anterior compartment — dorsiflexion), superficial fibular nerve (lateral compartment — eversion), lateral cutaneous nerve of calf |

## 💪 Muscles Innervated
**Via deep fibular nerve (anterior compartment of leg):**
- [Tibialis Anterior](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tibialis Anterior.md) — **dorsiflexion + inversion** (primary dorsiflexor; critical for foot clearance in swing phase)
- Extensor digitorum longus (EDL) — dorsiflexion + toe extension (MTP extension, toes 2–5)
- Extensor hallucis longus (EHL) — dorsiflexion + hallux extension
- Fibularis (peroneus) tertius — dorsiflexion + eversion

**Via deep fibular nerve (dorsum of foot — intrinsic):**
- Extensor digitorum brevis (EDB) — toe extension
- Extensor hallucis brevis (EHB) — hallux extension

**Via superficial fibular nerve (lateral compartment of leg):**
- Fibularis (peroneus) longus — **eversion** + plantarflexion; supports lateral longitudinal arch
- Fibularis (peroneus) brevis — **eversion** + plantarflexion; supports lateral ankle

## 🦴 Key Anatomical Relationships
- Lateral border of popliteal fossa — lies along the medial border of biceps femoris tendon (palpable)
- Winds subcutaneously around the **neck of the [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md)** — only ~1–2 cm of adipose tissue between nerve and bone; extremely vulnerable to compression here
- Superficial to fibularis longus as it curves around the fibular neck
- Deep fibular nerve runs with the anterior tibial artery in the anterior compartment
- Superficial fibular nerve becomes subcutaneous in the distal third of the lateral leg — vulnerable to ankle inversion injury
- The common fibular nerve contributes to the **sural nerve** via the lateral sural cutaneous nerve

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Foot drop** — hallmark of common fibular nerve injury; most common cause of foot drop
  - Mechanism: compression at the fibular neck
  - Common causes: habitual leg crossing, plaster cast, prolonged squatting, weight loss (loss of protective fat pad), fibular neck fracture, knee dislocation, traction during hip or knee surgery
  - Motor deficit: loss of dorsiflexion ([Tibialis Anterior](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tibialis Anterior.md) + EDL + EHL) and eversion (fibularis longus and brevis)
  - Gait: **steppage gait** (high stepping to clear the foot; foot slaps on ground)
  - Deformity: **equinovarus** — foot plantarflexed and inverted (unopposed tibialis posterior + gastrocnemius-soleus complex)
  - Sensory deficit: dorsum of foot and toes, lateral leg
- **Deep fibular nerve (anterior tarsal tunnel syndrome)** — compression under the inferior extensor retinaculum on the dorsum of the foot
  - Sensory: first web space (dorsal)
  - Motor: EDB/EHB weakness (if proximal enough)
  - Causes: tight shoe laces, osteophytes
- **Superficial fibular nerve entrapment** — compression as nerve pierces the deep fascia in distal lateral leg; lateral leg and dorsal foot paraesthesia; can follow ankle sprains
- **Common fibular nerve vs. L4–L5 radiculopathy differentiation**:
  - Fibular nerve palsy: spares tibialis posterior (inversion intact) and [Hamstrings](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Hamstrings.md)
  - L5 root lesion: tibialis posterior also weakened (inversion lost); back pain; dermatomal sensory pattern differs
- **Ankle inversion sprain** — can stretch the superficial fibular nerve as it crosses the lateral ankle
- **Knee dislocation** — high energy injury; always assess common fibular nerve (popliteal artery also at risk)
