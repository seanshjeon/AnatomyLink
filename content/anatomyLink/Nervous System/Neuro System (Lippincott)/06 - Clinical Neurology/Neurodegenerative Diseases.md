# рҹ§¬ Neurodegenerative Diseases

> **Progressive, irreversible neuronal loss defined by protein misfolding and aggregation вҖ” each disease targets a specific protein, pathway, and brain region, producing characteristic clinical syndromes.**

## рҹ”— Key Connections
- [[Basal Ganglia]] вҖ” nigrostriatal degeneration in Parkinson's; striatal loss in Huntington's
- [[Hippocampus]] вҖ” earliest target in Alzheimer's disease
- [[Cerebral Cortex]] вҖ” widespread cortical atrophy in AD, FTD, ALS-FTD
- [[Motor Pathway]] вҖ” upper + lower motor neuron degeneration in ALS
- [[Neurotransmitters]] вҖ” dopamine (PD), acetylcholine (AD), glutamate (excitotoxicity)
- [[Glial Cells]] вҖ” demyelination in MS; reactive gliosis in all
- [[Synaptic Transmission]] вҖ” synaptic loss = best correlate of cognitive decline in AD
- [[Sleep and Wakefulness]] вҖ” REM sleep behaviour disorder as prodromal marker for PD/DLB
- [[Amygdala]] вҖ” Lewy bodies in DLB; tau in AD
- [[Epilepsy]] вҖ” seizures occur in late AD and some genetic forms
- [[Stroke Syndromes]] вҖ” vascular dementia differential; multi-infarct

## рҹ”‘ Alzheimer's Disease (AD)

### Epidemiology
- Most common dementia (60-70%); 2nd most common neurodegenerative disease overall
- Prevalence doubles every ~5 years after age 65; affects ~50 million worldwide

### Pathology
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Amyloid plaques | AОІ42 from APP cleavage (BACE1 вҶ’ Оі-secretase/presenilin); AОІ oligomers most toxic; plaques = amyloid core + dystrophic neurites + reactive glia |
| Neurofibrillary tangles (NFTs) | Hyperphosphorylated tau вҶ’ microtubule destabilization вҶ’ intraneuronal filaments |
| Braak staging | I-II: entorhinal/hippocampal; III-IV: limbic; V-VI: neocortical |
| Synaptic loss | Best correlate of cognitive decline (more than plaque/tangle burden) |
| Cholinergic deficit | Nucleus basalis of Meynert (Ch4) вҶ’ cortex/hippocampus; basis for ChEI therapy |
| Neuroinflammation | Microglial activation, complement, TREM2 variants (AD risk gene вҖ” microglial AОІ clearance) |

### Amyloid Cascade Hypothesis
```
APP вҶ’ BACE1 cleavage вҶ’ AОІ42 overproduction OR вҶ“clearance
    вҶ“
AОІ oligomers (most toxic) вҶ’ synaptic dysfunction
    вҶ“
Amyloid plaques вҶ’ neuroinflammation
    вҶ“
Tau hyperphosphorylation вҶ’ NFT spread (Braak staging)
    вҶ“
Neuronal/synaptic loss вҶ’ cognitive decline вҶ’ dementia
```

### Genetics
| Gene/Factor | Chromosome | Effect |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| APOE Оө4 | 19q13 | Most common genetic risk; вҶ‘AОІ aggregation, вҶ“clearance; Оө4/Оө4 ~10Г— risk; Оө2 protective |
| APP mutation | 21q21 | Familial early-onset; trisomy 21 (Down syndrome) вҶ’ AD by 40s from extra APP copy |
| PSEN1 | 14q24 | Most common familial mutation; early-onset (~45y); encodes presenilin-1 (Оі-secretase) |
| PSEN2 | 1q42 | Rare familial; later onset than PSEN1 |

### Clinical Progression
| Stage | Features |
|-------|---------|
| Preclinical | AОІ accumulation (PET+) years before symptoms; no cognitive deficit |
| MCI (amnestic) | Episodic memory impairment > expected for age; hippocampal atrophy on MRI |
| Mild AD | Memory loss + anomia; can still manage ADLs; insight present |
| Moderate AD | Language, visuospatial, executive deficits; gets lost; dressing apraxia; loses insight |
| Severe AD | Non-ambulatory, incontinence, dysphagia, myoclonus; death from aspiration pneumonia |

### Diagnosis
- MRI: hippocampal + entorhinal atrophy; posterior parietal/temporal thinning
- FDG-PET: hypometabolism in posterior cingulate, precuneus, parietal, temporal cortex
- Amyloid PET (florbetapir/florbetaben): positive amyloid burden
- CSF biomarkers: вҶ“AОІ42 (sequestered in plaques), вҶ‘total tau, вҶ‘phospho-tau (p-tau181)
- Blood biomarkers (emerging): plasma p-tau217, AОІ42/40 ratio, GFAP, NfL

### Treatment
| Drug | Class | Mechanism | Use |
|------|-------|-----------|-----|
| Donepezil | ChEI | Inhibits AChE вҶ’ вҶ‘synaptic ACh | Mild-severe AD |
| Rivastigmine | ChEI | Inhibits AChE + BuChE | Mild-moderate; patch for PDD |
| Galantamine | ChEI | AChE inhibition + nicotinic allosteric modulation | Mild-moderate |
| Memantine | NMDA antagonist | Blocks tonic NMDA activation вҶ’ вҶ“excitotoxicity | Moderate-severe |
| Lecanemab | Anti-AОІ mAb | Clears AОІ protofibrils; ~27% slowing of decline | Early AD (FDA 2023); risk: ARIA |

## рҹ”‘ Parkinson's Disease (PD)

### Epidemiology & Pathology
- 2nd most common neurodegenerative disease; 1-2% prevalence >65 years
- **Lewy bodies**: cytoplasmic inclusions of Оұ-synuclein + ubiquitin; dopaminergic neurons of substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc) most affected
- Loss of nigrostriatal DA вҶ’ imbalance of direct (D1/"Go") vs indirect (D2/"Stop") pathways вҶ’ вҶ‘STN вҶ’ вҶ‘GPi/SNr вҶ’ вҶ‘thalamic inhibition вҶ’ вҶ“motor cortex activity

### Braak Staging (Lewy body propagation вҖ” prion-like spread)
| Stage | Regions | Symptoms |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1-2 | Dorsal motor nucleus CN X; olfactory bulb | Constipation, anosmia, RBD (prodromal) |
| 3-4 | Substantia nigra; locus coeruleus; amygdala | Motor symptoms (TRAP), depression |
| 5-6 | Neocortex | Dementia, hallucinations (PDD) |

### Motor Features вҖ” TRAP
| Sign | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **T**remor | Rest tremor, 4-6 Hz, "pill-rolling," asymmetric onset, disappears with action |
| **R**igidity | Cogwheel (tremor on lead-pipe), throughout ROM, "gegenhalten" |
| **A**kinesia/Bradykinesia | Slowness, reduced amplitude вҖ” micrographia, hypomimia, hypophonia, festinating gait |
| **P**ostural instability | Late feature; pull test positive; retropulsion; falls |

### Non-Motor Features
- **Prodromal** (precede motor by years): anosmia, constipation, RBD (acts out dreams вҖ” strongest predictor), depression, orthostatic hypotension
- **Later**: cognitive impairment вҶ’ PD dementia (PDD, >1 year after motor onset; if dementia вүӨ1 year = DLB), hallucinations (visual), psychosis, autonomic (OH, urinary urgency, sweating), pain, fatigue

### Treatment
| Drug/Therapy | Mechanism | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| L-DOPA + carbidopa | DA precursor; carbidopa = peripheral DDC inhibitor | Gold standard; long-term: wearing-off, dyskinesias, on-off |
| Dopamine agonists (ropinirole, pramipexole) | D2/D3 agonist | First-line younger patients; impulse control disorder risk |
| MAO-B inhibitors (rasagiline, selegiline) | вҶ“DA catabolism | Mild benefit; possible neuroprotection (unproven) |
| COMT inhibitors (entacapone) | вҶ“L-DOPA peripheral breakdown | Extends L-DOPA effect; wearing-off |
| Amantadine | NMDA antagonist + DA release | Reduces peak-dose dyskinesias |
| DBS (STN or GPi) | High-frequency stimulation (~130 Hz) functionally inhibits target | Controls tremor/rigidity/bradykinesia; not dementia or autonomic |

### Parkinson-Plus Syndromes (atypical parkinsonism вҖ” poor L-DOPA response)
| Syndrome | Protein | Key Features |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| PSP (Progressive Supranuclear Palsy) | Tau (4R) | Vertical gaze palsy (downward first), falls backward, Richardson's syndrome, "hummingbird sign" MRI |
| MSA (Multiple System Atrophy) | Оұ-synuclein | Autonomic failure + parkinsonism/cerebellar; "hot cross bun" MRI pons |
| DLB (Dementia with Lewy Bodies) | Оұ-synuclein | Dementia вүӨ1y of parkinsonism; fluctuating cognition; visual hallucinations; RBD; neuroleptic sensitivity |
| CBS/CBD (Corticobasal Syndrome) | Tau (4R) | Asymmetric, alien limb, apraxia, cortical sensory loss |

## рҹ”‘ Huntington's Disease (HD)

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Inheritance | Autosomal dominant; full penetrance |
| Gene | HTT (huntingtin), chromosome 4p16.3 |
| Mutation | CAG trinucleotide repeat expansion: normal вүӨ26, HD вүҘ36; anticipation (especially paternal) |
| Onset | Inversely proportional to CAG repeat length; mean ~40y; juvenile HD >60 repeats |
| Pathology | Mutant huntingtin (mHTT) toxic gain-of-function; striatal medium spiny neuron loss (indirect pathway MSNs first вҶ’ chorea) |
| MRI | Caudate atrophy вҶ’ "boxcar/bat-wing" ventricles |

### Clinical Triad
| Domain | Features |
|--------|---------|
| Motor | Chorea (early вҖ” involuntary irregular flowing movements); later: rigidity, dystonia, bradykinesia; oculomotor (saccade initiation delay); dysarthria; dysphagia |
| Psychiatric | Depression (вҶ‘suicide 4Г—), irritability/aggression, apathy, anxiety, OCD-like, psychosis вҖ” often precede motor symptoms |
| Cognitive | Subcortical dementia: slowed processing, executive dysfunction, memory retrieval deficit, visuospatial decline; insight preserved until late |

### Treatment
- No disease-modifying treatment
- **Tetrabenazine / Deutetrabenazine**: VMAT2 inhibitor вҶ’ вҶ“presynaptic DA вҶ’ reduces chorea; risk: depression/suicidality; contraindicated with MAO inhibitors
- SSRIs/SNRIs for depression; antipsychotics for psychiatric; MDT care

## рҹ”‘ ALS (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis)

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Pathology | Combined UMN + LMN degeneration; TDP-43 cytoplasmic inclusions (97%); SOD1 mutations (familial 20%); C9orf72 expansion (most common familial + sporadic genetic cause; also FTD) |

| Fasciculations | Absent | Present |
| Atrophy | Mild | Severe |

| Drug | Mechanism | Benefit |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Riluzole | вҶ“Presynaptic glutamate release (Na+ channel) | +2-3 months survival |
| Edaravone | Free radical scavenger | Slows functional decline in selected patients |
| Tofersen | Antisense oligonucleotide (SOD1-ALS) | вҶ“SOD1 protein + NfL (biomarker) |
