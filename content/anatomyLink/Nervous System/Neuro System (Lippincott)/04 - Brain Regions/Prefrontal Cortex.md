# 🧠 Prefrontal Cortex

> **The most anterior region of the frontal lobe, anterior to the premotor cortex (area 6); comprising ~29% of the human neocortex — the largest proportion in any primate — and the last cortical region to reach full maturity (~age 25). It is the phylogenetically newest cortex and the seat of executive function, working memory, decision-making, and social cognition.**

## 🔗 Key Connections

- [Cerebral Cortex](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Cerebral Cortex.md) — PFC is the anterior association cortex of the frontal lobe; heavily interconnected with all lobes
- [Amygdala](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Amygdala.md) — OFC and vmPFC ↔ amygdala via stria terminalis and ventral amygdalofugal pathway; critical for emotional regulation and fear extinction
- [Hippocampus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Hippocampus.md) — vmPFC ↔ hippocampus; memory-guided value-based decisions and fear extinction consolidation
- [Basal Ganglia](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Basal-Ganglia.md) — PFC → caudate head → globus pallidus → thalamus → PFC (five parallel cortico-striatal-thalamo-cortical loops)
- [Thalamus](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Thalamus.md) — DLPFC ↔ mediodorsal (MD) thalamus (bidirectional reciprocal); gateway for subcortical → prefrontal input
- [Language and Speech](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/05 - Higher Functions/Language and Speech.md) — left DLPFC and Broca's area (area 44/45) underlie phonological loop and verbal working memory
- [Memory Systems](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/05 - Higher Functions/Memory Systems.md) — PFC provides top-down control for encoding and retrieval; vmPFC indexes consolidated memories
- [Neurotransmitters](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/01 - Cellular Neuroscience/Neurotransmitters.md) — mesocortical DA pathway (VTA → PFC); D1 dominant in DLPFC; NE via locus coeruleus (α2A receptors)
- [Limbic System](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Limbic-System.md) — mPFC/ACC and OFC are key limbic-prefrontal integration zones
- [Neurodegenerative Diseases](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/06 - Clinical Neurology/Neurodegenerative Diseases.md) — bvFTD preferentially targets OFC/vmPFC/ACC

## 🔑 Subdivisions

| Subdivision | Brodmann Areas | Key Functions |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| **DLPFC** (dorsolateral PFC) | 9, 46 | Working memory (sustained delay-period firing), cognitive flexibility, planning, abstraction, verbal fluency, attention top-down control |
| **vmPFC** (ventromedial PFC) | 10, 11, 25 | Emotion regulation, value-based decision-making, social cognition, fear extinction, somatic marker integration |
| **OFC** (orbitofrontal cortex) | 11, 13, 47 | Reward valuation, stimulus-reinforcement learning, impulse control, reversal learning, olfactory/gustatory integration |
| **Medial PFC / Dorsal ACC** | 24, 32 (dorsal) | Conflict monitoring, error detection, response selection under competition (Stroop, flanker tasks) |
| **Subgenual ACC** | 25 (ventral) | Mood regulation, autonomic output to hypothalamus/brainstem, HPA axis regulation; hyperactive in depression |
| **Frontopolar cortex** | 10 | Prospective memory, multi-task branching ("hold subgoal while pursuing main goal"), relational integration |

## 🔑 Working Memory (Goldman-Rakic Model)

> Working memory is the ability to actively maintain and manipulate information over seconds in the absence of external stimuli — the "mental scratchpad."

- **Cellular basis**: Layer III pyramidal cells in DLPFC exhibit sustained **delay-period firing** bridging stimulus and response; dependent on recurrent excitatory microcircuits
- **Dopamine (D1) modulation**: D1 receptor stimulation on dendritic spines of layer III DLPFC neurons stabilizes WM representations via **inverted-U dose-response curve**:
  - Too little DA → excessive noise, poor signal-to-noise
  - Optimal DA → stable persistent activity
  - Too much DA → loss of selectivity, WM degradation
- **Norepinephrine (α2A) modulation**: NE via locus coeruleus acts on **α2A adrenergic receptors** in PFC postsynaptic spines; guanfacine and clonidine (α2A agonists) enhance WM and attention — clinical basis for ADHD treatment
- **Baddeley's Model of Working Memory**:

| Component | Neural Substrate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Phonological loop (verbal rehearsal) | Left DLPFC + Broca's area (BA 44/45) |
| Visuospatial sketchpad | Right PFC + right posterior parietal cortex |
| Central executive (attentional controller) | DLPFC bilaterally |
| Episodic buffer (multimodal integration) | Frontopolar cortex (BA 10) |

## 🔑 Executive Functions

| Function | Test / Task | Key Area | Lesion Effect |
|----------|------------|----------|---------------|
| **Inhibitory control** | Stop-signal task, Go/No-Go | Right inferior frontal cortex (IFC, BA 44/47) | Impulsivity, response disinhibition |
| **Cognitive flexibility** | Wisconsin Card Sorting Test (WCST) | DLPFC | **Perseveration** — stuck on previous sorting rule despite feedback |
| **Planning** | Tower of London / Tower of Hanoi | DLPFC + frontopolar | Reduced look-ahead, inefficient move sequences |
| **Verbal fluency** | Letter fluency (FAS), Category fluency | Left DLPFC + Broca's | Reduced word generation, intrusion errors |
| **Attention (top-down)** | Stroop test, sustained attention | DLPFC + ACC (conflict monitoring) | Increased Stroop interference, distractibility |
| **Response selection** | Stroop, flanker | Dorsal ACC (BA 24/32) | Poor conflict resolution |

## 🔑 Decision-Making and Reward

### Somatic Marker Hypothesis (Damasio)
- **Core idea**: OFC and vmPFC encode the **emotional/somatic value** of past outcomes; these "somatic markers" bias future choices via visceral/autonomic signals ("gut feelings")
- **Iowa Gambling Task (IGT)**: Four decks — two advantageous (small frequent rewards, rare small losses), two disadvantageous (large infrequent rewards, large losses)
  - Normal subjects: shift to advantageous decks → skin conductance response (SCR) before choosing bad decks (anticipatory somatic signal)
  - **vmPFC patients**: continue choosing disadvantageously despite verbally knowing bad decks; absent anticipatory SCR — somatic markers are not generated
- **OFC in reward**: encodes expected value, tracks reward prediction errors (alongside VTA DA neurons); OFC lesion → perseveration, stimulus-bound behavior, failure of reversal learning

### Prefrontal Dopamine
| Pathway | Origin → Target | Receptors | Function |
|---------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| **Mesocortical** | VTA → DLPFC/PFC | D1 (dominant), D2 | WM, executive function, motivation |
| **Mesolimbic** | VTA → NAc, amygdala, hippocampus | D2 (dominant) | Reward, salience, addiction |

- **Schizophrenia dual-DA hypothesis**: DLPFC D1 **hypofunction** (hypofrontality) + mesolimbic D2 **hyperfunction** (psychosis)

## 🔑 Social Cognition and Theory of Mind

| Network Node | Role |
|-------------|------|
| Medial PFC (vmPFC/mPFC) | Self-referential processing; default mode hub; mentalizing |
| Temporoparietal junction (TPJ) | Representing others' beliefs, perspective-taking |
| Posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) | Biological motion, gaze direction, social intent |
| Anterior insula | Empathy, interoception, emotional mirroring |

- **False-belief tasks** (Sally-Anne test): activate medial PFC + TPJ
- **mPFC lesions**: impaired recognition of social faux pas, reduced empathy, poor moral judgment (especially personal moral dilemmas)

## 🔑 Development and Adolescence

| Developmental Stage | PFC Status |
|--------------------|-----------|
| Childhood (~12y) | Grey matter volume peaks; synaptic density maximum |
| Adolescence (12–18y) | Pruning and myelination ongoing; limbic system (reward, emotion) matures earlier than PFC |
| Early adulthood (~18–22y) | Substantial prefrontal maturation, but connectivity not complete |
| Full maturity (~25y) | Myelination of prefrontal white matter complete |

- **Adolescent risk-taking**: Limbic/reward circuitry (NAc, amygdala) matures before prefrontal regulatory control → "dual-systems model" (Casey et al.)
- **Implication**: adolescents are biologically predisposed to reward-seeking, impulsivity, peer influence sensitivity

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

| Condition | Mechanism | Key Features |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Frontal lobe syndrome** | Bilateral PFC damage (trauma, tumor, infarct) | Disinhibition, perseveration (WCST), utilization behavior (compulsive use of objects in view), environmental dependency, loss of social norms, confabulation, abulia |
| **Phineas Gage** | Tamping rod through OFC/vmPFC (1848) | Personality change (disinhibited, profane, impulsive), poor decisions, preserved IQ and memory — seminal case for PFC-personality link; Damasio's vmPFC patient series reproduced findings |
| **ADHD** | DLPFC + prefrontal-striatal circuit hypofunction; DA and NE deficiency | Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity; treatments: methylphenidate (DAT blocker), amphetamine (DA/NE releaser), guanfacine (α2A agonist — especially inattentive/anxious subtype) |
| **Schizophrenia** | DLPFC hypofrontality; D1 hypofunction | Reduced N-back activation on fMRI; negative symptoms (alogia, avolition, blunted affect, anhedonia) and cognitive symptoms (WM, executive dysfunction) alongside positive symptoms |
| **OCD** | OFC-caudate **hyperactivity** → runaway error-detection loop | Intrusive obsessions + compulsive rituals to relieve anxiety; SSRIs + CBT (ERP); DBS of anterior limb IC or NAc in refractory cases |
| **Major Depression** | Subgenual ACC (area 25) **hyperactivity**; vmPFC hypoactivity; reduced serotonin and NE | Anhedonia, vegetative symptoms, guilt, suicidality; **DBS of area 25** (Mayberg protocol) → remission in treatment-resistant depression |
| **PTSD** | Reduced vmPFC/mPFC suppression of amygdala → hyperactive fear responses; reduced mPFC thickness on MRI | Hyperarousal, re-experiencing, avoidance; impaired fear extinction (requires vmPFC → amygdala inhibition); EMDR, prolonged exposure target this circuit |
| **Frontotemporal dementia (bvFTD)** | OFC/vmPFC/ACC atrophy; TDP-43 or tau pathology; C9orf72, MAPT, GRN mutations | Disinhibition, apathy, social dysfunction, hyperphagia, compulsive behaviors, loss of empathy; typically age 45–65; FDG-PET: frontal hypometabolism |
| **Alien hand syndrome** | Medial frontal cortex + anterior corpus callosum lesion (post-callosotomy or ACA infarct) | One hand acts autonomously contrary to patient's will; frontal variant: compulsive grasping/groping |
