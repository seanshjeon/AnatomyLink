# 🧬 Glial Cells

> Glial cells are the **non-neuronal supporting cells of the nervous system**, comprising ~50% of brain cells, performing essential roles in **myelination, blood-brain barrier maintenance, ion buffering, synaptic modulation, immune surveillance, and structural scaffolding**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Neuron](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Neuro System (Lippincott)/01 - Cellular Neuroscience/Neuron.md) — primary cell type supported by glia
- [Action Potential](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Neuro System (Lippincott)/01 - Cellular Neuroscience/Action Potential.md) — conduction velocity depends on oligodendrocyte/Schwann cell myelination
- [Meninges](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Central Nervous System/Meninges.md) — astrocytic endfeet contribute to the glia limitans at the CNS surface
- [Ventricular System](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Central Nervous System/Ventricular System.md) — ependymal cells line ventricles and produce/circulate CSF
- [Nervous System](./anatomyLink/Nervous System/Nervous System.md) — parent system

## 🔑 Astrocytes

Astrocytes are the most abundant glial cell in the CNS. They are star-shaped (astro = star) and express **GFAP (Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein)** as a hallmark marker.

### Subtypes
| Subtype | Location | Morphology |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Fibrous astrocytes | White matter | Long, sparse processes |
| Protoplasmic astrocytes | Gray matter | Short, bushy processes; contact synapses |

### Functions
| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Blood-brain barrier (BBB) maintenance** | Astrocytic endfeet encircle ~99% of CNS capillary surface; induce/maintain tight junctions between endothelial cells via Angiopoietin-1, VEGF |
| **K⁺ spatial buffering** | Kir4.1 channels on endfeet absorb extracellular K⁺ released during AP firing; redistribute K⁺ to distant sites; prevents excitotoxic depolarization |
| **Glutamate uptake** | EAAT1 (GLAST) and EAAT2 (GLT-1) transporters remove synaptic glutamate; convert to glutamine via glutamine synthetase; return to neurons (glutamate-glutamine cycle) |
| **Glycogen storage** | Provide lactate to neurons during high activity (astrocyte-neuron lactate shuttle) |
| **Tripartite synapse** | Astrocytic processes surround synapses; modulate transmission via gliotransmitters (glutamate, D-serine, ATP) |
| **Water transport** | AQP4 (aquaporin-4) channels on endfeet regulate CNS water homeostasis |
| **Reactive gliosis / scar formation** | After injury: hypertrophy, upregulate GFAP, form glial scar (barrier to axon regeneration but also protective) |
| **Neuronal migration guidance** | Radial glia (specialized astrocyte precursors) serve as scaffolding during cortical development |

## 🔑 Oligodendrocytes

Oligodendrocytes are the **myelinating cells of the CNS**.

### Key Features
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Processes per cell | One oligodendrocyte myelinates **up to 50 axon segments** |
| Axons per cell | Many axons simultaneously |
| Key protein | **MOG** (Myelin Oligodendrocyte Glycoprotein) — outermost layer; target in autoimmune disease |
| Other myelin proteins | MBP (Myelin Basic Protein), PLP (Proteolipid Protein) |
| Precursor | Oligodendrocyte Precursor Cell (OPC) — widely distributed in adult CNS; capable of remyelination |

### Myelination Process
1. OPC migrates to target axon
2. Oligodendrocyte process contacts axon via NRG1 (Neuregulin-1) signaling through ErbB2/3 receptors on axon
3. Process wraps around axon in concentric spirals, extruding cytoplasm
4. Compact myelin forms — alternating protein-lipid-protein layers
5. Nodes of Ranvier left bare — Nav channel clusters maintained by paranodal Caspr/contactin interactions

## 🔑 Schwann Cells

Schwann cells are the **myelinating cells of the PNS**. They are derived from neural crest cells.

### Comparison: Oligodendrocytes vs. Schwann Cells
| Property | Oligodendrocyte (CNS) | Schwann Cell (PNS) |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Origin | Neural tube (neuroepithelium) | Neural crest |
| Axons per cell | Many (up to 50 segments) | **One axon segment** (1:1 relationship) |
| Myelin proteins | MBP, PLP, MOG | MBP, P0 (MPZ), PMP22 |
| Basal lamina | Absent | Present (surrounds each cell) |
| Regeneration after injury | Poor; inhibitory signals (Nogo, MAG, OMgp) | Good; Bands of Büngner guide regenerating axon |
| Key signaling | NRG1-ErbB4 | NRG1-ErbB2/3 |

### Non-myelinating Schwann Cells
- Ensheath multiple unmyelinated C-fiber axons (Remak bundles)
- Provide trophic support and maintain axonal integrity

## 🔑 Microglia

Microglia are the **resident immune cells (macrophages) of the CNS**.

### Origin
- Derived from **yolk sac progenitors** (not bone marrow); enter CNS during early embryogenesis
- Distinct from circulating monocytes/macrophages — maintained by local self-renewal

### Morphological States
| State | Morphology | Function |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Ramified (resting)** | Small soma, long branching processes; constantly surveying environment | Homeostatic monitoring via process motility; synaptic pruning; phagocytosis of debris |
| **Activated** | Retracted processes, enlarged soma, amoeboid | Phagocytosis, antigen presentation, cytokine release |

### Functional Phenotypes
| Phenotype | Stimulus | Actions |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| M1 (pro-inflammatory) | LPS, IFN-γ | Releases IL-1β, TNF-α, IL-6, ROS; promotes neuroinflammation |
| M2 (anti-inflammatory) | IL-4, IL-13 | Releases IL-10, TGF-β; promotes tissue repair, phagocytosis of debris |

> Note: M1/M2 is a simplified model; microglia exist on a continuum of activation states in vivo.

### Key Microglial Functions
| Function | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Synaptic pruning | During development; mediated by complement (C1q, C3) tagging synapses for phagocytosis |
| Phagocytosis | Clear apoptotic cells, debris, excess myelin, protein aggregates (Aβ plaques) |
| Surveillance | Process tips sample extracellular space every few hours |
| Neuroprotection | Release BDNF, IGF-1 to support neuronal survival |

## 🔑 Ependymal Cells

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Location | Line ventricles and central canal of spinal cord |
| Morphology | Columnar; ciliated apical surface; microvilli |
| Primary function | **CSF production** (choroid plexus variant: cuboidal, highly vascular) and **circulation** (cilia beat to move CSF) |
| Tight junctions | Absent at ventricular surface (unlike BBB); present at choroid plexus (blood-CSF barrier) |
| Tanycytes | Specialized ependymal cells in hypothalamic floor; transport hormones between CSF and portal blood |

---

## 🔑 Radial Glia

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Timing | Transient — present during embryonic CNS development |
| Function | Scaffold for neuronal migration from ventricular zone to cortical plate |
| Fate | Transform into astrocytes after migration complete; retain stem cell capacity |
| Markers | RC2, vimentin, nestin, GFAP (late) |
| Neural stem cells | In adult brain (subventricular zone, dentate gyrus), radial glia-like cells persist as neural progenitors |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

| Condition | Cell Type Involved | Mechanism | Key Features |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Multiple sclerosis (MS)** | Oligodendrocytes | Autoimmune demyelination; T-cells and antibodies target MBP, MOG, PLP | Relapsing-remitting attacks; demyelination plaques; slowed conduction velocity; Uhthoff's phenomenon |
| **Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)** | Schwann cells | Post-infectious autoimmune attack on PNS myelin; anti-GM1, anti-GQ1b antibodies | Ascending paralysis, areflexia, albuminocytologic dissociation in CSF |
| **Glioblastoma (GBM)** | Astrocytes (glia in general) | Grade IV malignant glioma; GFAP+; IDH wild-type; EGFR amplification | Most common primary brain tumor in adults; poor prognosis; pseudopalisading necrosis |
| **Reactive gliosis** | Astrocytes | Injury-triggered hypertrophy and proliferation; upregulate GFAP, form glial scar | Neuroprotective short-term; inhibits axonal regeneration long-term |
| **Neuromyelitis Optica (NMO)** | Astrocytes | Anti-AQP4 antibodies destroy astrocytic endfeet → secondary oligodendrocyte loss | Optic neuritis + transverse myelitis; distinct from MS |
| **Alzheimer's disease** | Microglia | Impaired Aβ clearance; chronic neuroinflammation; TREM2 mutations increase risk | Senile plaques, NFTs, progressive dementia |
| **Charcot-Marie-Tooth (CMT)** | Schwann cells | Mutations in PMP22, MPZ, connexin-32; dysmyelination/demyelination | Distal motor and sensory neuropathy; pes cavus, hammer toes |
