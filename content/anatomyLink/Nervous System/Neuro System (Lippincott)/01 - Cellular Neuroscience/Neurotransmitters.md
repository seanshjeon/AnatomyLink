# 💊 Neurotransmitters

> Neurotransmitters are **endogenous chemical messengers** released from presynaptic terminals upon Ca²⁺ influx, diffusing across the synaptic cleft to act on **ionotropic or metabotropic receptors** on postsynaptic cells, producing **fast excitatory, inhibitory, or modulatory effects** that underlie all neural communication.

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Criteria for NT status | Synthesized in neuron; released on stimulation; acts on specific receptors; mechanism for inactivation |
| Fast NTs | Amino acids (glutamate, GABA, glycine); act via ionotropic receptors |
| Slow NTs | Monoamines, ACh, neuropeptides; act via metabotropic receptors |
| Reuptake transporters | SLC (solute carrier) family; drug targets (SSRIs, SNRI, DAT blockers) |
| Vesicle types | Small clear vesicles (amino acids, ACh, monoamines); large dense-core vesicles (neuropeptides) |

## 🔑 Glutamate (Glu)

**Major excitatory neurotransmitter of the CNS**

### Overview
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Distribution | Widespread throughout cortex, hippocampus, cerebellum, spinal cord |
| Synthesis | α-ketoglutarate (TCA cycle) → glutamate (via GAST); or glutamine → glutamate (via glutaminase) in neurons |
| Reuptake | EAAT1–5 (GLAST, GLT-1) on astrocytes and neurons → converted to glutamine → returned to neurons |

### Receptors
| Receptor | Type | Ion/Mechanism | Notes |
|----------|------|---------------|-------|
| **AMPA** (GluA1–4) | Ionotropic | Na⁺/K⁺; fast | Mediates fast EPSP; GluA2 subunit impermeable to Ca²⁺; trafficking key for LTP |
| **NMDA** (GluN1/2/3) | Ionotropic | Na⁺/K⁺/Ca²⁺ | Requires glutamate + glycine; Mg²⁺ voltage block; Ca²⁺ influx mediates LTP/excitotoxicity |
| **Kainate** (GluK1–5) | Ionotropic | Na⁺/K⁺ | Pre- and postsynaptic; role in pain, seizures |
| **mGluR I** (1, 5) | Metabotropic Gq | PLC → IP3/DAG | Postsynaptic; potentiates NMDA; target in fragile X |
| **mGluR II/III** (2–4, 6–8) | Metabotropic Gi | ↓cAMP | Presynaptic autoreceptors; inhibit glutamate release |

### Clinical
- **Excitotoxicity**: ischemia/TBI → excess glutamate → NMDA overactivation → Ca²⁺ overload → mitochondrial dysfunction → cell death
- **Ketamine**: NMDA antagonist → dissociative anesthesia; antidepressant (subanesthetic)
- **Memantine**: NMDA antagonist → Alzheimer's treatment

## 🔑 GABA (γ-Aminobutyric Acid)

**Major inhibitory neurotransmitter of the CNS**

### Overview
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Distribution | GABAergic interneurons throughout cortex, cerebellum (Purkinje cells), basal ganglia, hippocampus |
| Synthesis | Glutamate → GABA via **GAD** (Glutamic Acid Decarboxylase; requires pyridoxal-5-phosphate/B6) |
| Reuptake | GAT-1 (predominantly astrocytes and neurons) |
| Degradation | GABA-T (GABA transaminase) → succinic semialdehyde → enters TCA cycle |

### Receptors
| Receptor | Type | Ion/Mechanism | Notes |
|----------|------|---------------|-------|
| **GABA-A** | Ionotropic (pentameric Cys-loop) | Cl⁻ channel | Fast IPSP; target for benzodiazepines, barbiturates, alcohol, neurosteroids, general anesthetics |
| **GABA-B** | Metabotropic Gi/Go | Opens K⁺ channels; closes Ca²⁺ channels | Slow IPSP; pre- and postsynaptic; baclofen agonist |
| **GABA-C** (GABA-ρ) | Ionotropic | Cl⁻ channel | Retina; now reclassified as GABA-A ρ |

### Allosteric Modulators of GABA-A
| Drug | Mechanism | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Benzodiazepines** | ↑ frequency of Cl⁻ channel opening (require GABA to be present) | Anxiolysis, sedation, anticonvulsant, muscle relaxation |
| **Barbiturates** | ↑ duration of Cl⁻ channel opening; high doses → open directly | Sedation, anesthesia; narrow therapeutic window |
| **Alcohol (ethanol)** | Potentiate GABA-A; also inhibit NMDA | CNS depression; withdrawal seizures |
| **Neurosteroids** (allopregnanolone) | Positive allosteric modulator | Endogenous; basis for brexanolone (post-partum depression treatment) |
| **Propofol** | Potentiates GABA-A (at β-subunit) | IV anesthetic |

## 🔑 Glycine (Gly)

**Inhibitory neurotransmitter of spinal cord and brainstem**

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Distribution | Renshaw cells in spinal cord; brainstem interneurons |
| Function | Fast inhibition via glycine receptor (GlyR) — ligand-gated Cl⁻ channel (strychnine-sensitive) |
| Receptor (GlyR) | Ionotropic; pentameric; Cl⁻ channel; α1–4 and β subunits |
| Role in NMDA | Obligatory co-agonist at NMDA receptor (binds GluN1 glycine-binding site) |
| Reuptake | GlyT1 (astrocytes, CNS — controls NMDA co-agonism); GlyT2 (presynaptic glycinergic neurons) |

### Clinical
- **Strychnine poisoning**: blocks GlyR → loss of spinal inhibition → hyperekplexia, opisthotonus, tonic-clonic convulsions; mimics tetanus
- **Hyperekplexia**: GlyR α1-subunit mutation → exaggerated startle response

## 🔑 Acetylcholine (ACh)

### Overview
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Synthesis | Choline + Acetyl-CoA → ACh via **ChAT** (choline acetyltransferase); in cytoplasm |
| Transport | VAChT loads ACh into vesicles |
| Degradation | **AChE** (acetylcholinesterase) at synapse → choline + acetate; choline recycled by high-affinity choline transporter (CHT1) |

### Cholinergic Pathways and Receptors
| Location | Receptor Type | Effect |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| **NMJ** (skeletal muscle) | nAChR (nicotinic, N_M subtype: α1₂β1δε) | Depolarization → muscle contraction |
| **Autonomic ganglia** (sympathetic + parasympathetic) | nAChR (N_N subtype: α3β4) | Ganglionic transmission |
| **Parasympathetic end-organs** | Muscarinic (M1–M5) | M2: ↓HR (heart); M3: smooth muscle contraction, glandular secretion |
| **CNS — basal forebrain** (nucleus basalis of Meynert) | Muscarinic (M1) | Cortical arousal, attention, memory encoding; projects to neocortex |
| **CNS — septal nuclei** | Muscarinic | Projects to hippocampus; memory formation |
| **CNS — brainstem** (pedunculopontine, laterodorsal tegmental nuclei) | Nicotinic + muscarinic | REM sleep, arousal, reward |

### Clinical
- **Alzheimer's disease**: cholinergic neurons in nucleus basalis of Meynert degenerate; ↓ACh in cortex/hippocampus → AChE inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) provide symptomatic benefit
- **Organophosphate poisoning**: irreversible AChE inhibition → ACh accumulates → SLUDGE (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI distress, Emesis) + bradycardia + miosis + seizures; treat with atropine + pralidoxime

## 🔑 Dopamine (DA)

### Synthesis Pathway
**Tyrosine → L-DOPA → Dopamine → Norepinephrine → Epinephrine**
- Tyrosine hydroxylase (TH): rate-limiting enzyme; tyrosine → L-DOPA
- DOPA decarboxylase (AADC): L-DOPA → dopamine
- Dopamine-β-hydroxylase: dopamine → norepinephrine (in NE/Epi neurons only)

### Dopaminergic Pathways
| Pathway | Origin | Projection | Function |
|---------|--------|------------|----------|
| **Nigrostriatal** | Substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc) | Striatum (caudate + putamen) | Motor control, movement initiation |
| **Mesolimbic** | Ventral tegmental area (VTA) | Nucleus accumbens, amygdala, hippocampus | **Reward, motivation, addiction** |
| **Mesocortical** | VTA | Prefrontal cortex | Cognition, working memory, executive function |
| **Tuberoinfundibular** | Arcuate nucleus (hypothalamus) | Anterior pituitary (median eminence) | Inhibits prolactin secretion (via D2) |

### Dopamine Receptors
| Receptor | Family | G-protein | Location | Function |
|----------|--------|-----------|----------|----------|
| D1 | D1-like | Gs → ↑cAMP | Striatum, PFC | Excitatory modulation; motor, cognition |
| D2 | D2-like | Gi → ↓cAMP | Striatum, anterior pituitary | Inhibitory; target of antipsychotics |
| D3 | D2-like | Gi | Limbic system | Reward, emotion |
| D4 | D2-like | Gi | PFC | Cognition |
| D5 | D1-like | Gs | Hippocampus | Memory |

### Degradation
| Enzyme | Location | Products |
|--------|----------|---------|
| MAO-A/B (monoamine oxidase) | Intraneuronal (mitochondria) | DOPAC; then HVA |
| COMT (catechol-O-methyltransferase) | Extracellular, liver, postsynaptic | 3-MT; then HVA |
| Final metabolite | Homovanillic acid (HVA) | Measured in CSF/urine |

### Reuptake: DAT (dopamine transporter; SLC6A3)

## 🔑 Norepinephrine (NE / Noradrenaline)

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Synthesis | From dopamine → NE via dopamine-β-hydroxylase (requires ascorbic acid, O₂, Cu²⁺) |
| Storage | Vesicles via VMAT2 |
| Reuptake | NET (norepinephrine transporter; SLC6A2) |
| Degradation | MAO, COMT → MHPG (major CNS metabolite), VMA (urine) |

### Noradrenergic Pathways
| Origin | Projection | Function |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **Locus coeruleus** (LC, pons) | Diffuse: cerebral cortex, hippocampus, cerebellum, spinal cord, thalamus | Arousal, attention, vigilance, stress response, fear, pain modulation |
| Lateral tegmental neurons | Hypothalamus, amygdala | Autonomic regulation |

### Receptors
| Receptor | G-protein | Effect |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| α1 | Gq → PLC | Vasoconstriction, mydriasis |
| α2 | Gi → ↓cAMP | Presynaptic autoreceptor (inhibit NE release); also postsynaptic |
| β1 | Gs → ↑cAMP | ↑ HR, ↑ contractility |
| β2 | Gs → ↑cAMP | Bronchodilation, vasodilation |

### Clinical
- **Depression**: ↓NE (and ↓5-HT); treated with SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine), TCAs, MAOIs
- **ADHD**: NE and DA deficiency in PFC; treated with atomoxetine (NET inhibitor), stimulants

## 🔑 Serotonin (5-HT, 5-Hydroxytryptamine)

### Synthesis
**Tryptophan → 5-HTP → 5-HT**
- Tryptophan hydroxylase (TPH1 in gut, TPH2 in brain): rate-limiting
- AADC: 5-HTP → 5-HT
- Degradation: MAO-A → 5-HIAA (urine marker; elevated in carcinoid syndrome)

### Serotonergic Pathways
| Origin | Projection | Function |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **Raphe nuclei** (B1–B9, midbrain/pons/medulla) | Cortex, limbic system, basal ganglia, spinal cord | Mood, anxiety, appetite, sleep, circadian rhythm, pain modulation |

### Key Receptors
| Receptor | Type | Function |
|----------|------|----------|
| 5-HT1A | Gi → ↓cAMP | Autoreceptor (raphe soma); anxiolytic; buspirone partial agonist |
| 5-HT1B/D | Gi → ↓cAMP | Presynaptic autoreceptor; triptans act here (migraine) |
| 5-HT2A | Gq → PLC | Psychedelic actions (LSD); cortical excitation; antipsychotics block |
| 5-HT3 | Ionotropic (cation channel) | Nausea/vomiting (area postrema); gut motility; ondansetron blocks → antiemetic |
| 5-HT4 | Gs → ↑cAMP | GI motility; cognitive enhancement |

### Clinical
- **Depression**: ↓5-HT; SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline) block SERT → ↑synaptic 5-HT
- **Serotonin syndrome**: excess 5-HT (SSRI + MAOI, tramadol, linezolid) → hyperthermia, rigidity, clonus, agitation, diaphoresis
- **Carcinoid syndrome**: serotonin-secreting tumor → flushing, diarrhea, bronchoconstriction; ↑urine 5-HIAA

## 🔑 Histamine

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Synthesis | Histidine → histamine via histidine decarboxylase |
| Main CNS source | **Tuberomammillary nucleus (TMN)** of posterior hypothalamus |
| Projections | Widespread: entire cerebral cortex, hippocampus, basal ganglia, brainstem, spinal cord |
| Primary CNS function | **Wakefulness, arousal, attention** |
| Degradation | MAO-B; histamine-N-methyltransferase (HMT) |

### Receptors
| Receptor | G-protein | Effect |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| H1 | Gq → PLC | CNS: wakefulness; periphery: bronchoconstriction, vasodilation, allergic response |
| H2 | Gs → ↑cAMP | Gastric acid secretion; cardiac effects |
| H3 | Gi → ↓cAMP | Presynaptic autoreceptor; also heteroreceptor inhibiting other NTs |
| H4 | Gi | Immune cells; itch |

### Clinical
- **First-generation H1 antihistamines** (diphenhydramine, chlorphenamine): cross BBB → block H1 → sedation, cognitive impairment
- **Second-generation H1 antihistamines** (cetirizine, loratadine): poor BBB penetration → non-sedating
- **Pitolisant**: H3 inverse agonist → ↑HistamineCNS release → wakefulness (narcolepsy treatment)

## 🔑 Opioid Peptides

### Endogenous Opioids
| Peptide | Precursor | Receptor Preference | Location |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| **β-Endorphin** | POMC (pro-opiomelanocortin) | μ (mu) | Hypothalamus, pituitary; stress-induced analgesia |
| **Enkephalins** (Met-, Leu-) | Proenkephalin | δ (delta) and μ | Striatum, brainstem, spinal cord dorsal horn |
| **Dynorphins** | Prodynorphin | κ (kappa) | Striatum, hypothalamus, spinal cord; dysphoria |
| **Endomorphins** | Unknown precursor | μ | High μ affinity; brainstem |
| **Nociceptin/Orphanin FQ** | Prepronociceptin | ORL1 (NOP) | Pain modulation, anxiety |

### Opioid Receptors
| Receptor | G-protein | Mechanism | Effects |
|----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **μ (MOR)** | Gi/Go | ↓cAMP; ↑K⁺ conductance; ↓Ca²⁺ conductance | Analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, constipation, physical dependence |
| **δ (DOR)** | Gi/Go | ↓cAMP | Analgesia, antidepressant-like, modulates μ effects |
| **κ (KOR)** | Gi/Go | ↓cAMP | Analgesia, sedation, **dysphoria**, diuresis |

### Clinical
- **Exogenous opioids** (morphine, heroin, oxycodone): primarily μ agonists; analgesia, euphoria (reward via mesolimbic DA ↑), respiratory depression (pre-Bötzinger complex)
- **Naloxone**: μ/δ/κ antagonist → opioid overdose reversal; ultra-short acting
- **Addiction**: repeated opioid use → receptor desensitization (GRK/β-arrestin), downregulation, compensatory cAMP upregulation → withdrawal on discontinuation

## 🔑 Substance P

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Family | Tachykinin neuropeptide (11 amino acids) |
| Receptor | **NK1 receptor** (neurokinin-1; Gq-coupled) |
| Synthesis | DRG neurons and CNS neurons; large dense-core vesicles |
| Function | **Pain transmission** — released in spinal dorsal horn by Aδ and C fibers; amplifies pain signal (central sensitization) |
| Other roles | Neurogenic inflammation (mast cell degranulation, vasodilation), emesis (area postrema) |

### Clinical
- **Aprepitant (NK1 antagonist)**: antiemetic for chemotherapy-induced nausea; also studied in depression
- **Capsaicin**: activates TRPV1 on substance P-containing neurons → initial pain → depletion of substance P → pain relief

## 🏥 Clinical Significance Summary

| Condition | NT Change | Key Drug Targets |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Parkinson's disease** | ↓DA in nigrostriatal pathway (SNc degeneration) | L-DOPA/carbidopa; DA agonists (pramipexole); MAO-B inhibitors (selegiline); COMT inhibitors |
| **Schizophrenia** | Hyperdopaminergia (mesolimbic: positive sx); hypodopaminergia (mesocortical: negative/cognitive sx) | Typical antipsychotics: D2 blockers; Atypical: D2 + 5-HT2A blockers |
| **Depression** | ↓5-HT, ↓NE; possible ↓DA | SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs; ketamine (NMDA); bupropion (DA/NE reuptake inhibitor) |
| **Addiction** | Mesolimbic DA system dysregulation; ΔFOSB accumulation | DA antagonists; opioid agonist therapy (methadone, buprenorphine); naltrexone |
| **Alzheimer's disease** | ↓ACh (nucleus basalis of Meynert); also ↓5-HT, ↓DA | AChE inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine); memantine (NMDA antagonist) |
| **Anxiety disorders** | ↑noradrenergic tone (LC); ↓GABAergic inhibition | Benzodiazepines, SSRIs, buspirone (5-HT1A), beta-blockers |
| **Epilepsy** | ↑excitation (glutamate) or ↓inhibition (GABA) | Valproate (↑GABA); levetiracetam; lamotrigine (Na⁺ channel); benzodiazepines |
| **Narcolepsy** | ↓hypocretin/orexin (lateral hypothalamus) | Modafinil; sodium oxybate; pitolisant (H3 inverse agonist) |
