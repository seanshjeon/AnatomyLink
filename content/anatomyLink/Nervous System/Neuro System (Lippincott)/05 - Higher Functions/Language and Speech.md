# 🗣️ Language and Speech

> **Language is a left-hemisphere-dominant, distributed cortical network involving frontal, parietal, and temporal regions linked by white matter tracts**, with Broca's area governing speech production and grammar and Wernicke's area governing comprehension and word selection; lesions at any node produce a characteristic aphasia syndrome.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Cerebral Cortex](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Cerebral Cortex.md) — primary language cortices (Brodmann 44/45, 22, 39, 40) in left hemisphere
- [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) — hemispheric lateralization; left MCA territory houses most language areas
- [Motor Pathway](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Pathways/Motor-Pathway.md) — primary motor cortex face area → lip, tongue, laryngeal muscles for articulation
- [Memory Systems](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/05 - Higher Functions/Memory Systems.md) — verbal working memory via phonological loop; semantic memory storage
- [Prefrontal Cortex](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Neuro-System-(Lippincott)/04 - Brain Regions/Prefrontal Cortex.md) — verbal working memory, phonological loop, language executive control

## 🗂️ Key Information
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Dominant hemisphere | Left hemisphere: >95% of right-handers, ~70% of left-handers |
| Broca's area | Brodmann areas 44 (pars opercularis) + 45 (pars triangularis), left inferior frontal gyrus |
| Wernicke's area | Brodmann area 22, left posterior superior temporal gyrus |
| Connecting tract | Arcuate fasciculus / superior longitudinal fasciculus (SLF) |
| Angular gyrus | Brodmann area 39, inferior parietal lobule — reading and writing integration |
| Supramarginal gyrus | Brodmann area 40, inferior parietal lobule — phonological processing |
| Blood supply | Left MCA (superior division → Broca; inferior division → Wernicke) |
| Right hemisphere role | Prosody, pragmatics, discourse, metaphor, emotional tone of speech |


## 🔑 Language Network: Core Nodes

### Broca's Area (Brodmann 44/45)
- Located in the **left inferior frontal gyrus** (pars opercularis + pars triangularis)
- Functions: **speech production, grammatical processing (syntax), verbal working memory, phonological encoding, Broca's area also involved in action observation (mirror neurons)**
- Receives input from Wernicke's area via arcuate fasciculus; projects to primary motor cortex (face/larynx area)
- Also active during **sign language** in deaf signers — not purely auditory-speech

### Wernicke's Area (Brodmann 22)
- Located in the **left posterior superior temporal gyrus** (planum temporale region)
- Functions: **phonological decoding, lexical-semantic access, word selection for production, comprehension of spoken and written language**
- Planum temporale is typically larger on the left — anatomical substrate of language asymmetry
- Feeds selected words forward via arcuate fasciculus to Broca's area for encoding and articulation

### Angular Gyrus (Brodmann 39)
- Left inferior parietal lobule
- **Cross-modal integration: links written symbols (visual) ↔ spoken words (auditory) ↔ meaning (semantic)**
- Critical for **reading and writing** — damage causes alexia WITH agraphia
- Also involved in arithmetic, semantic processing, and metaphor

### Supramarginal Gyrus (Brodmann 40)
- Left inferior parietal lobule, just anterior to angular gyrus
- **Phonological awareness, phonological working memory, conduction aphasia pathway**
- Implicated in repetition — part of the perisylvian phonological circuit

### Arcuate Fasciculus / Superior Longitudinal Fasciculus
- White matter tract arcing around the lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure)
- **Directly connects Broca's area ↔ Wernicke's area** (and extends to angular/supramarginal gyri)
- Essential for **repetition** — lesion here → conduction aphasia
- Part of the dorsal language stream (phonological → motor mapping)


## 🔑 Dual-Stream Language Model

| Stream | White Matter | Direction | Function |
|--------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **Dorsal stream** | Arcuate fasciculus / SLF | Temporal → Frontal | Sound-to-articulation mapping; phonological processing; repetition; syntax |
| **Ventral stream** | Uncinate fasciculus + extreme capsule | Temporal → Frontal/subcortical | Sound-to-meaning mapping; lexical-semantic access; comprehension |


## 🔑 Aphasia Classification Table

| Aphasia Type | Fluency | Comprehension | Repetition | Lesion Site | Key Features |
|-------------|---------|--------------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Broca's** | Non-fluent (effortful, telegraphic, agrammatic) | Relatively intact (simple commands understood) | Impaired | Brodmann 44/45, left inferior frontal (MCA superior division) | Aware of deficit; frustration; written language also affected; often with right hemiparesis |
| **Wernicke's** | Fluent (normal rate/melody but paraphasic/jargon) | Impaired | Impaired | Brodmann 22, left posterior STG (MCA inferior division) | Unaware of errors (anosognosia for language); paraphasias (phonemic/semantic/neologistic); comprehension severely impaired |
| **Conduction** | Fluent | Intact | Severely impaired | Arcuate fasciculus / supramarginal gyrus (parietal operculum) | Frequent paraphasias during repetition; conduite d'approche (self-corrections); lesion disconnects Broca ↔ Wernicke |
| **Global** | Non-fluent | Impaired | Impaired | Large left MCA territory (Broca + Wernicke + connecting tracts) | Most severe aphasia; no meaningful output; may retain emotional language; often with right hemiplegia + hemianopia |
| **Transcortical Motor (TCM)** | Non-fluent | Intact | **Intact** | Left ACA territory or watershed between ACA/MCA (anterior superior frontal/SMA) | Can repeat but cannot initiate; echolalia possible; often with ACA stroke (leg > arm weakness) |
| **Transcortical Sensory (TCS)** | Fluent | Impaired | **Intact** | Left posterior watershed (MCA/PCA); angular gyrus region | Can repeat echolalically; comprehension impaired; similar to Wernicke's but repetition spared |
| **Mixed Transcortical** | Non-fluent | Impaired | **Intact** | Watershed areas surrounding Broca + Wernicke (isolation of speech area) | Only islands of speech spared; can repeat but not initiate or comprehend; seen in carbon monoxide poisoning, cardiac arrest watershed |
| **Anomic** | Fluent | Intact | Intact | Angular gyrus or diffuse (non-localizing alone) | Word-finding difficulty (tip-of-tongue); circumlocutions; mildest aphasia; can occur as recovery stage of others |

> **Mnemonic for transcortical aphasias — REPETITION IS INTACT** (the perisylvian zone is preserved; only the watershed tissue surrounding it is damaged)


## 🔑 Disconnection Syndromes

### Alexia Without Agraphia (Pure Alexia)
- **Can write but CANNOT read** — paradoxical because patient can read with their finger what they just wrote
- Lesion: **left occipital lobe (primary visual cortex → visual input to left hemisphere lost) + splenium of corpus callosum** (visual information from intact right occipital cannot cross to left language areas)
- Right visual field defect (left homonymous hemianopia) is invariably present
- Etiology: left PCA infarct

### Alexia With Agraphia
- Cannot read OR write
- Lesion: **left angular gyrus** (Brodmann 39) — destroys the cross-modal visual-linguistic integration
- Often with Gerstmann syndrome (angular gyrus): alexia + agraphia + acalculia + finger agnosia + right-left disorientation

### Pure Word Deafness
- Cannot understand spoken speech, but **hearing is intact** (pure tones normal), **reading is intact**, **writing is intact**
- Lesion: **bilateral auditory cortex (Heschl's gyri) OR disconnection of bilateral auditory input from left Wernicke's area**
- Speech sounds like foreign language or meaningless noise
- Differentiates from Wernicke's aphasia: reading comprehension preserved

### Callosal Disconnection (Corpus Callosum Section)
- **Left hand agraphia**: patient cannot write with left hand on verbal command (left hand not connected to language areas)
- **Left hand apraxia**: cannot carry out verbal commands with left hand
- Alien hand syndrome (callosal variant)


## 🔑 Subcortical Language Areas

| Structure | Role in Language | Damage |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Basal ganglia** (putamen, caudate) | Subcortical aphasia — hypophonic, dysarthric speech; word-finding difficulty; involved in language automaticity | Subcortical aphasia (striatal): fluent but hypophonic, possible paraphasias; seen in striatal strokes |
| **Thalamus** (pulvinar, dorsomedial) | Thalamic aphasia: decreased verbal output, semantic paraphasias; preserved repetition; reduced alertness/spontaneity | Thalamic aphasia — fluent with paraphasias; good repetition; often with fluctuating alertness |
| **Cerebellum** | Prosody, rhythm, speech rate, timing of articulatory movements | Cerebellar dysarthria: slurred, scanning, ataxic speech; no true aphasia |
| **Brainstem** | Cranial nerve nuclei (V, VII, IX, X, XII) for articulation | Dysarthria (bulbar/pseudobulbar); anarthria in locked-in syndrome |


## 🔑 Aphasia vs. Dysarthria vs. Apraxia of Speech

| Feature | Aphasia | Dysarthria | Apraxia of Speech |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Definition | Language processing disorder | Motor execution of speech (muscle weakness/incoordination) | Motor planning/programming disorder for speech (not weakness) |
| Language content | Paraphasias, agrammatism, word-finding errors | Normal language content, impaired execution | Normal language content; inconsistent articulatory errors |
| Reading/writing | Often affected | Normal (can write fluently) | Normal writing |
| Lesion | Left perisylvian cortex | Brainstem, cerebellum, bilateral UMN lesions | Broca's area (left posterior inferior frontal) |
| Key feature | Language system impaired | Muscles work but poorly | Knows what to say, programs incorrectly |


## 🔑 Right Hemisphere Language Functions
- **Prosody** (emotional tone of speech): production (right frontal) and comprehension (right temporal)
- **Pragmatics**: understanding indirect speech, humor, sarcasm, metaphor
- **Discourse coherence**: maintaining narrative structure and topic
- Right hemisphere lesion → **aprosodia** (flat, monotone speech; cannot interpret emotional tone of others' speech)


## 🔑 Special Topics

### Bilingual Aphasia
- Both languages typically affected, but not always equally
- Language learned first (L1) tends to be more resistant to damage
- Paradoxical recovery: one language recovers first (not always L1)
- Bilingual language control involves DLPFC and basal ganglia for language switching

### Assessment Tools
| Tool | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Boston Diagnostic Aphasia Exam (BDAE)** | Gold standard; profiles fluency, comprehension, repetition, naming, reading, writing |
| **Western Aphasia Battery (WAB)** | Shorter; produces Aphasia Quotient |
| **Token Test** | Sensitive test of auditory comprehension; follows commands with colored tokens |
| **Boston Naming Test** | 60-item confrontation naming test |


## 🏥 Clinical Significance

| Condition / Lesion | Aphasia Type | Key Bedside Findings | Etiology / Notes |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Left MCA superior division infarct | Broca's aphasia | Non-fluent, effortful; right face + arm > leg weakness; comprehension intact for simple commands | Embolic most common |
| Left MCA inferior division infarct | Wernicke's aphasia | Fluent jargon; patient unaware; no hemiplegia (motor cortex spared) | Superior temporal gyrus; often misdiagnosed as psychiatric |
| Large left MCA infarct | Global aphasia | No meaningful speech or comprehension; right hemiplegia + hemianopia | Worst prognosis for language recovery |
| Left PCA infarct (occipital + splenium) | Alexia without agraphia | Right homonymous hemianopia; reads by tracing letters | Classic disconnection syndrome |
| Left angular gyrus infarct | Alexia with agraphia + Gerstmann | Cannot read or write; right-left disorientation, finger agnosia, acalculia | Angular gyrus syndrome |
| Left ACA / watershed infarct | Transcortical motor aphasia | Non-fluent but repeats well; ACA stroke: leg weakness | ACA territory or ACA/MCA watershed |
| Bilateral temporal / splenium lesion | Pure word deafness | Cannot understand speech; reads and writes normally; pure tones intact | Disconnection of auditory cortex from Wernicke's |
| Primary progressive aphasia (PPA) | Non-fluent/agrammatic (FTLD-tau) or semantic (FTLD-TDP) | Slowly progressive language deterioration; behavior preserved early | Neurodegenerative; FTLD spectrum |
| Stroke recovery | Anomic → resolving aphasia | Word-finding difficulty as the final residual; circumlocutions | Best language recovery in first 3–6 months; plasticity allows right hemisphere compensation |
