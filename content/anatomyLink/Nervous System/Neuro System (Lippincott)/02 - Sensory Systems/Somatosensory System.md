# 🧠 Somatosensory System

> The somatosensory system comprises **ascending neural pathways that convey tactile, proprioceptive, pain, and temperature information from the body and face to the cerebral cortex**, organized into two principal systems: the **dorsal column–medial lemniscal pathway** (fine touch, vibration, proprioception) and the **spinothalamic (anterolateral) tract** (pain, temperature, crude touch).

## 🔑 Dorsal Column–Medial Lemniscal (DCML) Pathway

Carries: **fine touch, vibration, two-point discrimination, conscious proprioception, graphesthesia, stereognosis**.

### First-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | Dorsal root ganglion (DRG) — pseudounipolar neuron |
| Peripheral process | Travels in peripheral nerve to receptor (skin, muscle, joint) |
| Central process | Enters spinal cord at dorsal root entry zone → ascends IPSILATERALLY in dorsal columns |
| Fasciculus gracilis | Medial; carries fibers from lower limb and sacral/lumbar/thoracic cord (T7 and below) |
| Fasciculus cuneatus | Lateral; carries fibers from upper limb and upper thorax (T6 and above) |
| First synapse | Nucleus gracilis (lower body) and nucleus cuneatus (upper body) — both in medulla oblongata |

> Somatotopic organization in dorsal columns: sacral fibers most medial, cervical fibers most lateral. The gracile and cuneate nuclei maintain this somatotopy.

### Second-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | Nucleus gracilis / nucleus cuneatus (medulla) |
| Axons | **Internal arcuate fibers** — sweep ventromedially |
| Decussation | Cross midline in the caudal medulla → form the **medial lemniscus** |
| Medial lemniscus | Ascends through brainstem (rotates 90° in pons); terminates in VPL of thalamus |
| Somatotopy in lemniscus | Medulla: leg medial, arm lateral. Pons/midbrain: leg lateral, arm medial |

### Third-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | Ventral posterolateral (VPL) nucleus of thalamus |
| Axons | Travel through posterior limb of internal capsule → corona radiata |
| Termination | Primary somatosensory cortex (S1), postcentral gyrus |

## 🔑 Spinothalamic Tract (Anterolateral System)

Carries: **pain, temperature, crude touch, itch, tickle, sexual sensation**.

### First-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | Dorsal root ganglion (DRG) |
| Peripheral process | Aδ fibers (fast pain, cold) and C fibers (slow pain, warmth) from periphery |
| Central process | Enters cord via dorsal root → bifurcates and ascends/descends 1–2 segments in **Lissauer's tract** (dorsolateral fasciculus) before synapsing |
| First synapse | Dorsal horn: Rexed **lamina I** (marginal zone; Aδ and C), **lamina II** (substantia gelatinosa; C fibers), **lamina V** (wide-dynamic-range neurons; Aδ and visceral) |

### Second-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | Dorsal horn (primarily laminae I, IV–V) |
| Decussation | Axons cross midline via **anterior white commissure** within **1–2 spinal segments** above the entry level |
| Tract location | Contralateral anterolateral quadrant of spinal cord (lateral spinothalamic: pain/temp; anterior spinothalamic: crude touch) |
| Ascent | Through spinal cord, medullary reticular formation, pons, midbrain → terminates at thalamus |
| Thalamic target | VPL nucleus (discriminative pain → S1 cortex); intralaminar nuclei + medial thalamus (affective-motivational component) |

### Third-Order Neuron
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Cell body | VPL thalamus (discriminative); intralaminar/posterior group (affective) |
| Termination | S1 cortex (discriminative: location, quality) and insular cortex, anterior cingulate (affective: suffering) |

> **Key clinical point**: Because the spinothalamic tract decussates in the cord, a **unilateral cord lesion** causes **contralateral** loss of pain and temperature, typically beginning 1–2 segments below the lesion.

## 🔑 Trigeminothalamic Pathways (Face)

The face has its own parallel somatosensory organization via CN V (trigeminal nerve):

| Pathway | Carries | 1st Order | 1st Synapse | 2nd Order | Thalamic Target |
|---------|---------|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Dorsal trigeminothalamic** (principal sensory nucleus pathway) | Fine touch, vibration, proprioception | Trigeminal ganglion | Principal (chief) sensory nucleus — pons | Crosses midline → trigeminal lemniscus | VPM thalamus |
| **Ventral trigeminothalamic** (spinal trigeminal pathway) | Pain, temperature | Trigeminal ganglion | Spinal trigeminal nucleus (pars caudalis, medulla–C2) | Crosses midline → trigeminal lemniscus | VPM thalamus |
| **Proprioceptive** | Jaw muscle proprioception | Mesencephalic trigeminal nucleus (cell bodies IN brainstem — unique) | Bypasses ganglion | Direct to motor nucleus | N/A |

## 🔑 Primary Somatosensory Cortex (S1)

Located in the **postcentral gyrus** (parietal lobe). Composed of four Brodmann areas with distinct functional roles:

| Brodmann Area | Location | Primary Input | Function |
|---------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| **3a** | Fundus of central sulcus | Deep proprioception (muscle spindle Ia afferents via VPL) | Proprioceptive awareness, limb position |
| **3b** | Posterior bank of central sulcus | Cutaneous SA1/RA1 (Merkel, Meissner) | Texture and size perception (primary processing) |
| **1** | Crown of postcentral gyrus | Rapidly adapting cutaneous (Meissner, Pacinian) | Texture (high-frequency detail) |
| **2** | Posterior face of postcentral gyrus | Deep pressure, joint position (from 3a, 3b) | Size, shape discrimination, stereognosis |

> Area 3b is considered the primary somatosensory receiving area and is most severely affected by postcentral lesions. Areas 1 and 2 receive secondary input from area 3b and contribute to higher-order tactile processing.

### Somatotopic Organization (Homunculus)
- Medial-to-lateral organization on postcentral gyrus: **sacral/leg (medial, on medial surface)** → trunk → arm → hand → face (lateral)
- **Disproportionate representation**: fingertips, lips, tongue, and face occupy far more cortical area than trunk or proximal limbs, reflecting receptor density and behavioral importance
- **Cortical magnification factor**: ratio of cortical surface area to body surface area; highest for fingertips (~100×), lowest for back

### Secondary Somatosensory Cortex (S2)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Location | Parietal operculum (superior bank of lateral sulcus) |
| Input | Receives from S1 (areas 1, 2) and VPL/VPM directly |
| Representation | **Bilateral** body representation (unlike S1 which is contralateral) |
| Function | Pain processing, tactile learning, object recognition through touch |
| Damage | Tactile learning deficits; contralateral tactile agnosia |

### Posterior Parietal Cortex (Areas 5 and 7)
| Area | Function |
|------|----------|
| Area 5 (superior parietal lobule) | Somatosensory association; integrates S1 inputs; limb position sense |
| Area 7 (inferior parietal lobule) | Multimodal integration (somatosensory + visual + proprioceptive); spatial awareness; visuospatial attention |
| Damage to areas 5/7 | **Contralateral neglect**, tactile agnosia, astereognosis, loss of spatial awareness |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance

| Lesion | Pathway Damaged | Deficits | Distinguishing Features |
|--------|----------------|----------|------------------------|
| **Dorsal column lesion** (e.g., MS plaque, syphilis) | DCML ipsilateral | Ipsilateral loss of fine touch, vibration, proprioception BELOW lesion | Positive Romberg sign; sensory ataxia; normal pain/temp |
| **Spinothalamic lesion** (e.g., anterolateral cordotomy, ALS) | STT contralateral | Contralateral loss of pain and temperature 1–2 levels below lesion | Fine touch, proprioception preserved |
| **Complete cord transection** | All pathways | Complete loss of all modalities below lesion; UMN paralysis; autonomic dysfunction | Level determined by dermatomal mapping |
| **Brown-Séquard syndrome** (hemisection) | Ipsilateral DCML + corticospinal; contralateral STT | Ipsilateral: fine touch/proprioception loss + UMN weakness; contralateral: pain/temp loss (1–2 segments lower) | Classic dissociated sensory loss |
| **Syringomyelia** (central cord cavity) | Crossing STT fibers at anterior white commissure | Bilateral "cape distribution" loss of pain and temperature across shoulders/arms; dorsal columns and motor spared early | Loss of pain/temp with preserved touch = dissociated sensory loss |
| **Tabes dorsalis** (syphilis, 3°) | Dorsal columns (degeneration of large myelinated fibers) | Bilateral proprioception/vibration loss; sensory ataxia; Romberg positive; lancinating pain; Argyll Robertson pupils | Positive VDRL/TPHA; loss of DTRs |
| **Subacute combined degeneration** (B12 deficiency) | Dorsal columns + lateral (corticospinal) columns | Proprioception loss, vibration loss (legs > arms) + UMN signs; may have peripheral neuropathy | MCV↑; serum B12↓; responds to B12 supplementation |
| **Medial lemniscus lesion** (brainstem stroke) | DCML after decussation | Contralateral fine touch, vibration, proprioception loss (body) | No pain/temp loss; may accompany cranial nerve findings |
| **Thalamic stroke** (VPL) | All modalities, contralateral body | Contralateral hemisensory loss (all modalities); may develop thalamic pain syndrome (Dejerine-Roussy) | Spontaneous burning pain in affected area; post-stroke weeks to months |
| **Postcentral gyrus lesion** | S1 | Contralateral loss of discriminative sensation; astereognosis; impaired 2-point discrimination | Pain/temp may be partially preserved (bilateral S2 representation) |
