# Metacarpals

> The metacarpals are **5 long bones** forming the palm of the hand. Numbered 1 (thumb) to 5 (little finger).

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Carpals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Carpals.md) — **Carpometacarpal joints (CMC joints)**; 1st CMC is a saddle joint enabling thumb opposition
- [Phalanges of Hand](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Phalanges of Hand.md) — **Metacarpophalangeal joints (MCP joints)**

## 🗂️ Structure

Each metacarpal has a **Base** (proximal; articulates with carpals), **Shaft**, and **Head** (distal; forms the knuckle).

### 1st Metacarpal (Thumb)
- Shortest and stoutest
- 1st CMC = **saddle joint** → opposition possible (hallmark of primates)

## 🔑 Arches of the Hand
- **Transverse arch** — Across the metacarpal heads
- **Longitudinal arch** — Carpal bones to phalanges
- Collapse reduces grip strength

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Boxer's fracture** — 5th metacarpal neck fracture (closed-fist punch)
- **De Quervain's tenosynovitis** — 1st compartment; thumb extensor/abductor tendon pain
- **Rheumatoid arthritis** — MCP joint involvement; ulnar deviation deformity
