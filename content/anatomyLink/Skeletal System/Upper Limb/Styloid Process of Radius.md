# Styloid Process of Radius

> The **styloid process of the radius** is the pointed bony projection at the distal lateral end of the [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Radius.md), extending ~1 cm more distally than the ulnar styloid. It is the attachment for the brachioradialis tendon.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Radius.md) — distal lateral radius
- [Brachioradialis](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Brachioradialis.md) — inserts on the radial styloid
- [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md) — ulnar styloid is ~1 cm proximal to the radial styloid (important in Colles' fracture assessment)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Colles' fracture** — Distal radius fracture with dorsal displacement; radial styloid displaced → loss of normal radial inclination (normally 23°)
- **De Quervain's tenosynovitis** — APL and EPB tendons pass through first dorsal compartment adjacent to the radial styloid
- **Chauffeur's fracture** — Isolated radial styloid fracture from direct blow
