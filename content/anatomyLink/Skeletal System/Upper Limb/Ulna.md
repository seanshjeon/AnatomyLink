# Ulna

> The ulna is the **medial (little finger-side) bone of the forearm** and the **primary stabilizer of the elbow joint**.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) — **Humeroulnar joint** — elbow flexion/extension
- [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Radius.md) — **Proximal & distal radioulnar joints** — forearm rotation
- [Carpals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Carpals.md) — Indirect via the triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC)

## 🔗 Associated Muscles
- [Triceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Upper Limb/Triceps Brachii.md) — Inserts on the olecranon

## 🗂️ Key Structures

| Structure | Location | Description |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| [Olecranon](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Olecranon.md) | Proximal | Posterior elbow prominence; triceps insertion |
| [Coronoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Coronoid Process.md) | Proximal anterior | Anterior elbow stability |
| Trochlear notch | Proximal | Articulates with the humeral trochlea |
| Radial notch | Proximal lateral | Articulates with the radial head |
| [Styloid Process of Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Styloid Process of Ulna.md) | Distal | Medial wrist prominence |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Monteggia fracture** — Ulnar shaft fracture + proximal radioulnar joint dislocation
- **Olecranon fracture** — Direct trauma or triceps avulsion
- **Cubital tunnel syndrome** — Ulnar nerve entrapment; ring & little finger numbness
- **UCL tear** — Medial elbow instability in throwing athletes (Tommy John surgery)
