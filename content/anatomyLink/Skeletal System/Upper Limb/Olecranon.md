# Olecranon

> The **olecranon** is the large posterior bony projection at the proximal end of the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md) that forms the point of the elbow. It fits into the **olecranon fossa** of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) during full elbow extension and is the insertion of the [Triceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Triceps-Brachii.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md) — proximal posterior ulna
- [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md) — articulates in the olecranon fossa (full extension)
- [Triceps Brachii](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Upper-Limb/Triceps-Brachii.md) — inserts on the olecranon (elbow extension)
- [Ulnar Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Upper-Limb-Nerves/Ulnar-Nerve.md) — passes in the cubital tunnel just medial to the olecranon

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Function | Forms elbow point; locks into olecranon fossa in extension |
| Muscle insertion | Triceps brachii |
| Palpation | Prominent bony point of elbow |
| Olecranon bursa | Superficial bursa overlies the olecranon |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Olecranon fracture** — Usually direct blow or fall on outstretched hand; gap palpable; triceps avulsion
- **Olecranon bursitis (student's elbow)** — Inflamed bursa; soft, fluctuant swelling at elbow point
- **Triceps tendon rupture** — Rare; inability to extend elbow; often at olecranon insertion
