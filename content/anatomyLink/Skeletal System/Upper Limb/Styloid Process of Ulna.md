# Styloid Process of Ulna

> The **styloid process of the ulna** is the pointed bony projection at the distal end of the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md), on the medial side of the wrist, serving as an attachment for the articular disc of the distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md) — distal medial ulna
- [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Radius.md) — distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ) — radius rotates around the ulnar head
- Triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC) — attaches at the base and tip of the ulnar styloid

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Ulnar styloid fracture** — Common with distal radius (Colles') fractures; if at base, TFCC is disrupted → DRUJ instability
- **TFCC injury** — Ulnar-sided wrist pain; positive ulnar grind test
- **Ulnar impaction syndrome** — Ulnar styloid impinges on triquetrum with ulnar-positive variance
