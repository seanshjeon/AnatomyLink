# Medial Collateral Ligament (Elbow UCL)

> The **medial collateral ligament** (MCL) of the elbow — also called the **ulnar collateral ligament (UCL)** — is the primary stabilizer against valgus stress at the elbow, connecting the [Medial Epicondyle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Medial Epicondyle.md) of the [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md) to the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Medial Epicondyle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Medial Epicondyle.md) of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Humerus.md)
- **Insertion** — Medial [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Ulna.md) (sublime tubercle of coronoid process)
- **Three bundles** — Anterior (strongest), posterior, transverse

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tommy John surgery (UCL reconstruction)** — UCL rupture in overhead throwing athletes (baseball pitchers); reconstructed with palmaris longus or gracilis tendon graft
- **Valgus stress test** — Tests UCL integrity; medial elbow pain/gapping
- **Medial elbow pain** — UCL sprain/tear from repetitive valgus load in throwing athletes
