# Acromion

> The **acromion** is a bony projection of the spine of the [[Scapula]] that forms the "roof" of the shoulder joint, articulating with the [[Clavicle]] at the **acromioclavicular (AC) joint**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Scapula]] — extension of the spine of the scapula
- [[Clavicle]] — forms the **acromioclavicular (AC) joint**
- [[Deltoid]] — origin on the lateral acromion
- [[Rotator Cuff|Supraspinatus]] — passes under the acromion through the **subacromial space**

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Type | Bony process (extension of scapular spine) |
| Joint formed | Acromioclavicular (AC) joint |
| Landmark | Highest point of the shoulder — easily palpable |
| Subacromial space | Between acromion and supraspinatus tendon (~1 cm) |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **AC joint separation** — Sprain/rupture of AC ligaments; step deformity at shoulder tip
- **Subacromial impingement** — Supraspinatus tendon compressed under the acromion → shoulder pain with abduction (painful arc 60–120°)
- **Shoulder tip pain** — Often indicates diaphragmatic irritation (referred via phrenic nerve C3–5)
- **Acromial morphology** — Type I (flat), II (curved), III (hooked — highest risk of impingement)
