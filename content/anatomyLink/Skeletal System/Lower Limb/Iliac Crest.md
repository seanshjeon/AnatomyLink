# Iliac Crest

> The **iliac crest** is the superior curved border of the ilium (part of the [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md)), running from the anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS) to the posterior superior iliac spine (PSIS). It is the most commonly used bone graft donor site.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md) — superior border of the ilium
- [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tensor Fasciae Latae.md) — origin on the anterior iliac crest
- [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Medius.md) — origin on the lateral iliac crest
- [Gluteus Maximus](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gluteus Maximus.md) — origin on the posterior iliac crest
- [Quadratus Lumborum](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Trunk/Quadratus Lumborum.md) — inserts on the iliac crest
- [Iliotibial Band](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Iliotibial Band.md) — attaches to the tubercle of the iliac crest

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Palpation | Easily palpable "hip bones"; level of L4 vertebra (used for lumbar puncture landmark) |
| ASIS | Anterior endpoint of the iliac crest |
| PSIS | Posterior endpoint; dimples of Venus; level of S2 |
| Bone graft | Primary donor site for autologous bone grafts |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Iliac crest bone graft** — Most common donor site for spinal fusion, nonunion repair
- **L4 landmark** — Intercristal line (between highest points of iliac crests) passes through L4 vertebral body → used for lumbar puncture needle insertion level (L3–L4 or L4–L5)
- **Iliac crest apophysitis** — In adolescents; avulsion injury at apophysis from muscle traction
