# Anterior Superior Iliac Spine (ASIS)

> The **anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS)** is the anterior bony projection at the front of the [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md)'s iliac crest, serving as the origin of the [Sartorius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Sartorius.md) and the attachment of the inguinal ligament.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) — anterior iliac crest endpoint
- [Sartorius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Sartorius.md) — originates from the ASIS (longest muscle in the body)
- [Tensor Fasciae Latae](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Tensor-Fasciae-Latae.md) — originates just posterior to the ASIS
- Inguinal ligament — extends from ASIS to pubic tubercle

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Palpation | Prominent bony point at front of hip |
| Inguinal ligament | ASIS → pubic tubercle |
| AIIS | Anterior inferior iliac spine — 2 cm below ASIS; origin of rectus femoris |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **ASIS avulsion fracture** — Sartorius or TFL avulsion in adolescents; sprinting injury
- **Meralgia paresthetica** — Lateral femoral cutaneous nerve compressed at ASIS/inguinal ligament → burning thigh pain
- **Anatomical landmark** — Used to measure true leg length (ASIS to medial malleolus)
