# Femoral Head

> The **femoral head** is the spherical proximal end of the [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md), covered with hyaline cartilage, articulating with the [Acetabulum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Acetabulum.md) to form the hip joint. Its blood supply is precarious, making it vulnerable to avascular necrosis.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md) — proximal end, connected via the femoral neck
- [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md) — articulates with the acetabulum (hip joint)
- Ligamentum teres — connects femoral head to acetabular fossa; carries a small artery in children

## 🗂️ Blood Supply (Critical)
| Source | Route | Importance |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Medial femoral circumflex artery (MFCA) | Retinacular vessels along femoral neck | Primary supply (90%) |
| Lateral femoral circumflex artery | Minor contribution | |
| Obturator artery (artery of ligamentum teres) | Via ligamentum teres | Significant only in children |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Avascular necrosis (AVN) of femoral head** — Disruption of MFCA → bone death; causes: femoral neck fracture, dislocation, steroids, alcohol, sickle cell, decompression sickness
- **Femoral neck fracture** — Disrupts retinacular vessels → AVN risk; intracapsular fractures highest risk
- **Legg-Calvé-Perthes disease** — Idiopathic AVN in children (4–8 years)
- **Total hip replacement** — Femoral head replaced with metal/ceramic prosthesis
