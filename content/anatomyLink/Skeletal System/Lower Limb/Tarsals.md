# Tarsals

> The tarsals are **7 short bones** forming the posterior and middle foot, essential for weight bearing and locomotion.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) & [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibula.md) — **Talocrural joint (Ankle joint)** — talus sits in the tibiofibular mortise
- [Metatarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Metatarsals.md) — **Tarsometatarsal joints (Lisfranc joint)**

## 🔗 Associated Muscles
- [Gastrocnemius](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Gastrocnemius.md) — Inserts via Achilles tendon onto the calcaneal tuberosity
- [Tibialis Anterior](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tibialis Anterior.md) — Inserts on the medial cuneiform and 1st metatarsal base

## 🗂️ The 7 Tarsal Bones

| Bone | Location | Key Feature |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Talus | Ankle | Forms the ankle joint; central axis of the foot |
| Calcaneus | Heel | Largest tarsal; Achilles tendon insertion |
| Navicular | Medial midfoot | Medial longitudinal arch keystone |
| Cuboid | Lateral midfoot | Articulates with 4th & 5th metatarsals |
| Medial cuneiform | Medial | Articulates with 1st metatarsal |
| Intermediate cuneiform | Middle | Articulates with 2nd metatarsal |
| Lateral cuneiform | Lateral | Articulates with 3rd metatarsal |

## 🔑 Key Joints
- **Ankle (Talocrural)** — Tibial plafond + malleoli = mortise around talus; Dorsiflexion / Plantarflexion
- **Subtalar** — Talus + Calcaneus; Inversion / Eversion
- **Lisfranc** — Cuneiforms + Cuboid → Metatarsal bases

## 🔑 Arches of the Foot
- **Medial longitudinal arch** — Calcaneus-Talus-Navicular-Cuneiforms-Metatarsals 1–3 (highest)
- **Lateral longitudinal arch** — Calcaneus-Cuboid-Metatarsals 4–5 (lower, rigid)
- **Transverse arch** — Across cuneiforms and cuboid

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Ankle sprain** — ATFL injury most frequent sports injury
- **Calcaneal fracture** — High-energy fall from height; "lover's fracture"
- **Pes planus (Flatfoot)** — Loss of medial longitudinal arch
- **Pes cavus (High arch)** — Associated with neuromuscular disorders
- **Lisfranc injury** — Tarsometatarsal instability; easy to miss on X-ray
