# Greater Sciatic Notch

> The **greater sciatic notch** is a large concave notch on the posterior [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md), converted into the **greater sciatic foramen** by the sacrospinous ligament. The **sciatic nerve** and multiple other neurovascular structures pass through it into the gluteal region.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md) — posterior ilium and ischium
- [Sciatic Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Sciatic-Nerve.md) — exits through the greater sciatic foramen (below piriformis in 85%)
- [Piriformis](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Piriformis.md) — passes through the greater sciatic foramen; key landmark
- Superior gluteal nerve/vessels — exit above piriformis
- Inferior gluteal nerve/vessels — exit below piriformis
- Pudendal nerve — exits here, then re-enters via lesser sciatic foramen

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Piriformis syndrome** — Sciatic nerve compressed at the greater sciatic notch/piriformis; sciatica-like symptoms
- **Posterior hip dislocation** — Femoral head can injure sciatic nerve at the notch level
- **Gluteal injection safety** — Injections must avoid the greater sciatic notch (upper outer quadrant)
