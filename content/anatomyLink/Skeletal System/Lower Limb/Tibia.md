# Tibia

> The tibia is the **medial, primary weight-bearing bone of the lower leg**. Its entire anterior surface is subcutaneous and palpable.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) — **Knee joint** — articulates with medial & lateral femoral condyles
- [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Patella.md) — Patellar tendon attaches to the tibial tuberosity
- [Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Fibula.md) — **Proximal & distal tibiofibular joints** + interosseous membrane
- [Tarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tarsals.md) — **Ankle joint (Talocrural joint)** — distal tibia articulates with the talus

## 🔗 Associated Muscles
- [Quadriceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadriceps-Femoris.md) — Inserts on the tibial tuberosity via patellar tendon
- [Tibialis Anterior](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Tibialis-Anterior.md) — Originates from the lateral tibial shaft; dorsiflexion
- [Hamstrings](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Hamstrings.md) — Semitendinosus inserts medially on the tibia (pes anserinus)
- [Gastrocnemius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gastrocnemius.md) — Crosses the knee but does not attach to the tibia directly

## 🗂️ Key Structures

### Proximal
| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Medial condyle | Articulates with medial femoral condyle |
| Lateral condyle | Articulates with lateral femoral condyle |
| [Tibial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Tibial-Tuberosity.md) | Patellar tendon insertion |

### Shaft
| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Anterior border | Sharp ridge — the "shin"; subcutaneous |
| Interosseous border | Interosseous membrane attachment |

### Distal
| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| [Medial Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Medial-Malleolus.md) | Medial ankle prominence |
| Fibular notch | Articulates with distal fibula |
| Inferior articular surface | Articulates with the talus |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tibial stress fracture** — Distance runners, military recruits
- **Tibial plateau fracture** — Valgus knee trauma
- **Tibial shaft fracture** — High open fracture risk (subcutaneous location)
- **Osgood-Schlatter disease** — Tibial tuberosity apophysitis in adolescents
