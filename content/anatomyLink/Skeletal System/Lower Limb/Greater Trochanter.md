# Greater Trochanter

> The **greater trochanter** is the large, square bony prominence at the proximal-lateral [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md), serving as the insertion for the gluteal muscles and external rotators of the hip.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) — proximal lateral femur
- [Gluteus Medius](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Medius.md) — inserts on the lateral and superior surfaces (primary hip abductor)
- [Gluteus Minimus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Gluteus-Minimus.md) — inserts on the anterior surface
- [Piriformis](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Piriformis.md) — inserts on the superior facet
- [Obturator Internus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Obturator-Internus.md) + gemelli — insert on the medial surface
- [Quadratus Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadratus-Femoris.md) — inserts on the intertrochanteric crest

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Location | Proximal lateral femur; palpable laterally at hip |
| Trochanteric fossa | Medial deep pit; obturator externus insertion |
| Greater trochanteric bursa | Between gluteus maximus and greater trochanter |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Greater trochanteric pain syndrome (GTPS)** — Gluteus medius/minimus tendinopathy ± bursitis; lateral hip pain with lying on the side
- **Greater trochanteric bursitis** — Inflamed bursa; lateral hip pain; Trendelenburg gait
- **Trochanteric fracture** — Intertrochanteric or subtrochanteric fractures in elderly; treated with ORIF or IM nail
- **Trendelenburg sign** — Gluteus medius weakness (insertion on greater trochanter); pelvis drops to contralateral side when standing on affected leg
