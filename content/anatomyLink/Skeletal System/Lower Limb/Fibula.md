# Fibula

> The fibula is the **slender lateral bone of the leg**, bearing minimal weight (~10%) but critical for **lateral ankle stability**.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) — **Proximal & distal tibiofibular joints** + interosseous membrane
- [Tarsals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tarsals.md) — **Ankle joint** — lateral malleolus forms the lateral wall of the ankle mortise

## 🔗 Associated Muscles
- [Tibialis Anterior](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Tibialis Anterior.md) — Runs along the anterior compartment lateral to the fibula

## 🗂️ Key Structures

| Structure | Location | Description |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| [Fibular Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Fibular Head.md) | Proximal | Articulates with lateral tibial condyle; common fibular nerve wraps here |
| Fibular neck | Proximal | Common fibular (peroneal) nerve at risk |
| Shaft | Middle | Slender; interosseous membrane attachment |
| [Lateral Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Lateral Malleolus.md) | Distal | Lateral ankle prominence; sits lower than medial malleolus |

## 🔑 Lateral Ankle Ligaments
- **ATFL** — Fibula anterior → talus; most commonly injured in ankle sprains
- **CFL** — Fibula inferior → calcaneus
- **PTFL** — Fibula posterior → talus; strongest of the three

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Lateral malleolus fracture** — Ankle inversion; one of the most common fractures overall
- **Common fibular nerve injury** — At fibular neck → foot drop
- **Fibular free flap** — Reconstructive surgery with vascular pedicle
- **Maisonneuve fracture** — Proximal fibula + distal tibiofibular disruption
