# Linea Aspera

> The **linea aspera** ("rough line") is a prominent longitudinal ridge on the posterior surface of the [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) midshaft, serving as an attachment for multiple thigh muscles and intermuscular septa.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md) — posterior midshaft ridge
- [Vastus Medialis](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadriceps-Femoris.md) — originates from the medial lip
- [Vastus Lateralis](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Quadriceps-Femoris.md) — originates from the lateral lip
- [Adductor Magnus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Adductor-Magnus.md) — inserts on the medial lip
- [Adductor Longus](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Adductor-Longus.md) — inserts on the medial lip
- [Adductor Brevis](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Adductor-Brevis.md) — inserts on the medial lip
- [Biceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Lower-Limb/Biceps-Femoris.md) — short head originates from the lateral lip

## 🗂️ Key Facts
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Location | Posterior femoral midshaft; bifurcates above and below |
| Superior extension | Spiral line → lesser trochanter; pectineal line |
| Inferior extension | Medial + lateral supracondylar lines → medial/lateral condyles |
| Profunda femoris | Deep femoral artery passes near the linea aspera via perforating branches |
