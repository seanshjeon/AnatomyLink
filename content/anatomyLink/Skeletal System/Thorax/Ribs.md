# Ribs

> The ribs comprise **12 pairs (24 bones)** connecting the thoracic vertebrae to the sternum, forming the **thoracic cage** that protects the heart, lungs, and major vessels.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Thoracic Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Thoracic-Vertebrae.md) — **Costovertebral joints** — posterior articulation (all 12 pairs)
- [Sternum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Thorax/Sternum.md) — **Sternocostal joints** — anterior articulation (ribs 1–7 directly)
- [Vertebral Column](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Vertebral-Column.md) — Connected via thoracic vertebrae

## 🔗 Associated Muscles
- [Pectoralis Major](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Pectoralis-Major.md) — Originates from anterior rib surfaces (costal cartilages 1–6)
- [Erector Spinae](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Erector-Spinae.md) — Iliocostalis inserts on ribs
- [Latissimus Dorsi](./anatomyLink/Muscular-System/Trunk/Latissimus-Dorsi.md) — Originates from ribs 9–12

## 🗂️ Classification

| Type | Number | Characteristic |
|------|--------|----------------|
| True ribs | 1–7 | Costal cartilage attaches directly to sternum |
| False ribs | 8–10 | Attach indirectly via 7th costal cartilage |
| Floating ribs | 11–12 | No anterior attachment |

## 🦴 Typical Rib Structure (Ribs 3–9)
| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Head | 2 articular facets; articulates with thoracic vertebral bodies |
| Neck | Narrow portion posterior to the head |
| Tubercle | Articulates with transverse process of thoracic vertebra |
| Angle | Point of greatest curvature |
| Costal groove | Inferior border; transmits intercostal nerve, artery & vein (VAN order: vein-artery-nerve) |

### Atypical Ribs
- **Rib 1** — Shortest and flattest; subclavian vessels and brachial plexus pass over it
- **Rib 2** — Serratus anterior attachment
- **Ribs 11–12** — No neck or tubercle; no anterior articulation

## 🫁 Role in Respiration
- **Bucket-handle motion** — Middle ribs; increases transverse diameter
- **Pump-handle motion** — Upper ribs; increases anteroposterior diameter
- Ribs rotate superiorly during inspiration → increases thoracic volume

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Rib fractures** — Most common thoracic injury in trauma (ribs 4–9 most vulnerable)
- **Intercostal neuralgia** — Herpes zoster, post-traumatic
- **Thoracentesis** — Insert needle over the superior rib margin (avoids neurovascular bundle)
- **Rib graft harvest** — Used as bone graft donor site in reconstructive surgery
