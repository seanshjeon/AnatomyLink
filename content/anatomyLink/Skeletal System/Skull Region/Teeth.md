# Teeth (Dentition)

> Humans have **two sets of teeth**: 20 deciduous (baby) teeth and **32 permanent teeth**. Teeth are anchored in the alveolar bone of the [[Mandible]] (lower) and maxilla of the [[Skull]] (upper), connected by the periodontal ligament.

## 🗂️ Permanent Dentition — 32 Teeth

| Type | Upper | Lower | Total | Function |
|------|-------|-------|-------|----------|
| [[Incisors]] | 4 | 4 | 8 | Cutting / biting |
| [[Canines]] | 2 | 2 | 4 | Tearing / piercing |
| [[Premolars]] | 4 | 4 | 8 | Crushing / grinding |
| [[Molars]] | 6 | 6 | 12 | Grinding |
| **Total** | **16** | **16** | **32** | |

### Dental Formula (Permanent)
`2.1.2.3 / 2.1.2.3` × 2 = **32 teeth**
*(Incisors.Canines.Premolars.Molars per quadrant)*

### Dental Formula (Deciduous — 20 teeth)
`2.1.0.2 / 2.1.0.2` × 2 = **20 teeth** *(no premolars)*

## 🔑 Tooth Anatomy

```
        Crown
    ┌───────────┐
    │  Enamel   │  ← Hardest substance in the body (96% mineral)
    │  Dentin   │  ← Bulk of tooth; slightly elastic; sensitive
    │  Pulp     │  ← Nerves + blood vessels; pain sensation
    └─────┬─────┘
          │ Neck (CEJ — cementoenamel junction)
    ┌─────┴─────┐
    │  Cementum │  ← Covers root; PDL attachment
    │  PDL      │  ← Periodontal ligament; shock absorber
    │  Root     │
    └───────────┘
```

| Structure | Composition | Function |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Enamel** | 96% hydroxyapatite | Hardest body substance; protects crown |
| **Dentin** | 70% mineral, 30% collagen | Bulk of tooth; transmits pain |
| **Pulp** | Nerves, vessels, connective tissue | Nutrition, pain detection |
| **Cementum** | 45–50% mineral | Anchors PDL to root surface |
| **Periodontal ligament (PDL)** | Collagen fibers | Anchors tooth to alveolar bone; shock absorption |
| **Alveolar bone** | Cancellous bone | Tooth socket (remodels with orthodontic force) |

## 🔑 Tooth Numbering
### Universal System (USA)
- Upper right → Upper left: **1–16** (Upper right 3rd molar = #1)
- Lower left → Lower right: **17–32** (Lower right 3rd molar = #32)

### FDI / ISO System (International)
- Quadrant 1 (upper right), 2 (upper left), 3 (lower left), 4 (lower right)
- e.g., **36** = lower left 1st molar

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [[Mandible]] — lower teeth embedded in mandibular alveolar bone
- [[Skull]] — upper teeth embedded in maxillary alveolar bone
- [[Oral Cavity]] — teeth are the primary structures of the oral cavity
- [[Masseter]] — primary muscle closing the jaw onto teeth
- [[Cranial Nerves]] — CN V3 (inferior alveolar nerve) innervates lower teeth; CN V2 innervates upper teeth

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Dental caries (cavities)** — Bacterial acid demineralizes enamel → dentin → pulp exposure
- **Pulpitis** — Pulp inflammation; irreversible → root canal therapy
- **Periodontitis** — Bacterial infection of PDL + alveolar bone → tooth mobility + bone loss
- **Gingivitis** → **Periodontitis** — Progressive gum disease pathway
- **Malocclusion** — Malalignment of teeth; classified by Angle's classification (Class I/II/III)
