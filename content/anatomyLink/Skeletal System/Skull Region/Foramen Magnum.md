# Foramen Magnum

> The **foramen magnum** ("great opening") is the large oval opening in the **occipital bone** at the base of the skull through which the brainstem transitions to the spinal cord.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Skull-Region/Skull.md) — located in the occipital bone, posterior cranial fossa
- [Brain](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Brain.md) — brainstem passes through to become [Spinal Cord](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Spinal-Cord.md)
- [Spinal Cord](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Central-Nervous-System/Spinal-Cord.md) — begins at the foramen magnum (continuous with medulla oblongata)
- [Vertebral Column](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Spine/Vertebral-Column.md) — upper border of spinal canal

## 🗂️ Contents Passing Through
| Structure | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Medulla oblongata | Brainstem → spinal cord transition |
| Vertebral arteries (×2) | Supply brainstem and posterior brain |
| Anterior spinal artery | Supplies anterior spinal cord |
| Posterior spinal arteries (×2) | Supply posterior spinal cord |
| Accessory nerve roots (CN XI) | Ascending spinal roots enter skull |
| Meninges | Dura, arachnoid, pia continue into spinal canal |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Tonsillar herniation** — Increased ICP forces cerebellar tonsils through foramen magnum → brainstem compression → respiratory arrest
- **Chiari malformation** — Cerebellar tonsils displaced below foramen magnum; causes headache, syringomyelia
- **Atlanto-occipital dislocation** — Traumatic separation at the joint just above the foramen magnum
