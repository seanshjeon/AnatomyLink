# Incisors

> **Incisors** are the 8 front teeth (4 upper, 4 lower) designed for **cutting and biting** food. They are the most visible teeth and have a single root with a chisel-shaped crown.

## 🗂️ Types

| Type | Location | Count | Features |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| Central incisor | Midline (teeth #8, #9 upper; #24, #25 lower) | 4 | Widest, most prominent |
| Lateral incisor | Adjacent to central (#7, #10 upper; #23, #26 lower) | 4 | Narrower; upper often congenitally absent |

## 🔑 Key Anatomy
- **Crown shape** — Blade-like (chisel); incisal edge for cutting
- **Roots** — Single, conical root
- **Upper central incisor** — Largest anterior tooth; 2nd most commonly fractured tooth
- **Innervation** — Upper: CN V2 (anterior superior alveolar nerve); Lower: CN V3 (inferior alveolar nerve)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Dental trauma** — Maxillary central incisors most commonly fractured (crown fractures, Ellis classification)
- **Congenitally missing** — Upper lateral incisors are the 2nd most commonly missing permanent teeth
- **Overjet / overbite** — Horizontal / vertical protrusion of upper incisors over lower
- **Class II malocclusion** — Protruding upper incisors ("buck teeth")
- **Esthetic zone** — Incisors dominate smile esthetics; focus of cosmetic dentistry
