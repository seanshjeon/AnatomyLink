# Sella Turcica

> The **sella turcica** ("Turkish saddle") is a saddle-shaped depression in the **sphenoid bone** forming the hypophyseal fossa, housing the **pituitary gland (hypophysis)**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- [Skull](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Skull-Region/Skull.md) — located in the sphenoid bone, middle cranial fossa
- Pituitary gland — housed within the sella (endocrine master gland)
- Optic chiasm — lies just anterior to the sella
- Cavernous sinus — borders the sella laterally (contains CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI + ICA)

## 🗂️ Anatomical Landmarks
| Structure | Location | Note |
|-----------|----------|------|
| Tuberculum sellae | Anterior border | Ridge of sphenoid |
| Dorsum sellae | Posterior border | Elevated ridge |
| Hypophyseal fossa | Central depression | Contains pituitary |
| Posterior clinoid processes | Posterior dorsum | Dural attachment |
| Anterior clinoid processes | Anterior | Dural attachment |

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Pituitary adenoma** — Most common sella lesion; expands sella on X-ray ("ballooned sella")
- **Bitemporal hemianopia** — Pituitary tumor compresses optic chiasm → loss of temporal visual fields
- **Empty sella syndrome** — CSF fills sella compressing pituitary; often incidental
- **Transsphenoidal surgery** — Pituitary tumors accessed surgically through the sphenoid sinus via the nose
