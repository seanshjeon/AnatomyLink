# Coccyx

> The coccyx is the **terminal segment of the vertebral column**, formed by the fusion of **3–5 rudimentary coccygeal vertebrae**. It represents the vestigial tail and serves as an attachment point for pelvic floor muscles.

## 🔗 Connected Structures
- [Sacrum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Sacrum.md) — Articulates at the **sacrococcygeal joint**
  - Allows slight flexion/extension — important during childbirth

## 🗂️ Structure
- Typically 3–5 segments fused in adulthood
- Co1 has the most developed transverse processes
- **Coccygeal cornua** — articulate with sacral cornua

## 🔑 Muscle & Ligament Attachments
- **Anococcygeal ligament** — supports the anal sphincter
- **Coccygeus muscle** — part of the pelvic floor
- Part of the **levator ani** muscle group

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Coccydynia** — Tailbone pain after falls or prolonged sitting
- **Childbirth** — Coccyx can deflect posteriorly to allow fetal passage
- **Pressure ulcers** — Risk area in bedridden patients
- **Coccygectomy** — Surgical removal for refractory coccydynia
