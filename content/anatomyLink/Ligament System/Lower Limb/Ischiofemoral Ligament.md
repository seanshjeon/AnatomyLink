# Ischiofemoral Ligament

> The **ischiofemoral ligament** reinforces the **posterior** hip capsule, arising from the ischial acetabular rim and spiraling to the femoral neck. It limits **medial rotation** and extension of the hip.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Ischial part of acetabular rim ([[Acetabulum]])
- **Insertion** — Greater trochanter + femoral neck (posterior)
- **Limits** — Medial (internal) rotation + extension
- **Related ligaments** — [[Iliofemoral Ligament]], [[Pubofemoral Ligament]]

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Posterior hip dislocation** — Most common hip dislocation type; ischiofemoral ligament often ruptures
- **Piriformis syndrome** — Posterior hip structures including ischiofemoral ligament in the region of [[Piriformis]]
- **Ischiofemoral impingement** — Narrowing between ischium and lesser trochanter; quadratus femoris compressed → deep gluteal pain
