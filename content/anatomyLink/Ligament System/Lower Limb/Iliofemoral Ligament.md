# Iliofemoral Ligament

> The **iliofemoral ligament** (Y-ligament of Bigelow) is the **strongest ligament in the human body**, spanning the anterior hip joint. It prevents hip hyperextension and allows humans to stand erect with minimal muscle activity.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Anterior inferior iliac spine (AIIS) + acetabular rim ([Acetabulum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Acetabulum.md))
- **Insertion** — Intertrochanteric line of [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md) (two bands: superior → [Greater Trochanter](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Greater Trochanter.md), inferior → [Lesser Trochanter](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Lesser Trochanter.md))
- **Hip capsule** — Anterior reinforcement of hip joint capsule
- **Related ligaments** — [Pubofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Pubofemoral Ligament.md), [Ischiofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Ischiofemoral Ligament.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Anterior hip dislocation** — Rare; requires rupture of iliofemoral ligament
- **Hip hyperextension** — Iliofemoral tightness limits hyperextension → useful in standing posture (postural sway control)
- **Femoral neck fracture risk** — Falls in elderly often involve hip hyperextension mechanism
- **Thomas test** — Tests hip flexor tightness; when hip extends, iliofemoral ligament comes under tension
