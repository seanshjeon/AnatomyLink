# Pubofemoral Ligament

> The **pubofemoral ligament** reinforces the inferior and anterior hip capsule, arising from the pubic part of the acetabular rim and blending with the [Iliofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Iliofemoral-Ligament.md). It limits **abduction and extension**.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Iliopubic eminence + superior pubic ramus ([Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md))
- **Insertion** — Anterior intertrochanteric fossa; blends with [Iliofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Iliofemoral-Ligament.md)
- **Limits** — Abduction + extension + lateral rotation
- **Related ligaments** — [Iliofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Iliofemoral-Ligament.md), [Ischiofemoral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Ischiofemoral-Ligament.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Hip dislocation** — Inferior dislocation involves pubofemoral + iliofemoral rupture
- **Hip replacement (THA)** — Anterior approach must release or work around pubofemoral region
- **Hip labral tears** — Inferior labral tears in region of pubofemoral attachment
