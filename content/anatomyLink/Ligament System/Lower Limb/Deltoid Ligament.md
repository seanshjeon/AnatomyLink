# Deltoid Ligament

> The **deltoid ligament** is a strong triangular **medial ankle ligament complex** connecting the [Medial Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Medial Malleolus.md) to the talus, navicular, and calcaneus. It resists **eversion** and is far stronger than the lateral ligaments.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Medial Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Medial Malleolus.md) ([Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md))
- **Four components** — Tibionavicular, tibiocalcaneal, anterior tibiotalar, posterior tibiotalar (superficial + deep)
- **Insertion** — Talus ([Talus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Talus.md)), navicular, sustentaculum tali of calcaneus
- **Counterpart** — Lateral complex: [Anterior Talofibular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Anterior Talofibular Ligament.md) + [Calcaneofibular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Calcaneofibular Ligament.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Medial ankle sprain** — Rare (only ~15% of ankle sprains); requires greater force than lateral
- **Ankle fracture** — Medial malleolus fracture or deltoid ligament rupture in bimalleolar or Maisonneuve fractures
- **Pronation-abduction injury** — Deltoid tears in pronation-type ankle fractures (Lauge-Hansen classification)
- **Medial ankle swelling** — Deltoid tear → medial side bruising + tender medial malleolus region
