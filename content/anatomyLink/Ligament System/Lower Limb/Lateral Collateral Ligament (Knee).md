# Lateral Collateral Ligament (Knee)

> The **lateral collateral ligament (LCL)** (fibular collateral ligament) resists **varus stress** at the knee, connecting the lateral femoral condyle to the fibular head. It is part of the **posterolateral corner (PLC)** complex.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Lateral femoral condyle ([Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md))
- **Insertion** — [Fibular Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Fibular-Head.md)
- **Does NOT attach** — Lateral meniscus (unlike MCL and medial meniscus)
- **PLC structures** — LCL + popliteus tendon + popliteofibular ligament + posterolateral capsule
- **Related structures** — [Common Fibular Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Common-Fibular-Nerve.md) (wraps around fibular head nearby!)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Varus stress test** — At 0° and 30° flexion; lateral gapping
- **PLC injury** — LCL often injured as part of posterolateral corner complex; combined with ACL or PCL
- **Peroneal nerve injury** — [Common Fibular Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Lower-Limb-Nerves/Common-Fibular-Nerve.md) at risk with LCL/PLC injuries → foot drop
- **Dial test** — Increased external rotation at 30° flexion suggests PLC injury
- **Rarely isolated** — LCL injury usually occurs with PLC or cruciate injuries
