# Anterior Talofibular Ligament (ATFL)

> The **anterior talofibular ligament (ATFL)** is the **most commonly sprained ligament in the body**, connecting the anterior [Lateral Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Lateral-Malleolus.md) to the talar neck. It resists **anterior talar displacement** and **inversion** in plantar flexion.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Anterior border of [Lateral Malleolus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Lateral-Malleolus.md) ([Fibula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Fibula.md))
- **Insertion** — Lateral talar neck ([Talus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Talus.md))
- **Lateral ankle complex** — ATFL + [Calcaneofibular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Calcaneofibular-Ligament.md) + PTFL (posterior talofibular ligament)
- **Medial counterpart** — [Deltoid Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Lower-Limb/Deltoid-Ligament.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Lateral ankle sprain** — ATFL is first ligament torn in inversion sprain (Grade I = stretch, Grade III = complete tear)
- **Anterior drawer test (ankle)** — Anterior talar translation; ATFL integrity
- **Talar tilt test** — Combined ATFL + CFL injury; increased inversion
- **Chronic ankle instability** — Recurrent sprains from lax ATFL
- **Broström repair** — Anatomic ATFL reconstruction for chronic instability
