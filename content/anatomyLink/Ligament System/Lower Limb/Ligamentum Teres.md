# Ligamentum Teres

> The **ligamentum teres** (round ligament of the femur) connects the **fovea capitis** of the femoral head to the acetabular notch. It carries the **artery to the femoral head** (branch of obturator artery), critically important in children's vascular supply.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Acetabular notch + transverse acetabular ligament ([Acetabulum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Acetabulum.md))
- **Insertion** — Fovea capitis of [Femoral Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femoral-Head.md)
- **Neurovascular** — Carries medial circumflex femoral / obturator artery branch → supplies femoral head
- **Related structures** — [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Hip-Bone.md), [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Lower-Limb/Femur.md), acetabular labrum

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Avascular necrosis (AVN)** — In children, ligamentum teres is critical blood supply; disruption (e.g., hip dislocation, Perthes disease) → AVN of femoral head
- **Hip dislocation** — Ligamentum teres tears in traumatic hip dislocation
- **Adults** — Less critical vascular role than in children; small contribution only
- **MRI hip** — Ligamentum teres tears identified on MRI; associated with hip instability
