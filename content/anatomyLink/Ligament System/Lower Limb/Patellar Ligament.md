# Patellar Ligament

> The **patellar ligament** (often called patellar tendon) is the distal continuation of the [Quadriceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Quadriceps Femoris.md) tendon, connecting the inferior pole of the [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Patella.md) to the [Tibial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibial Tuberosity.md). It transmits quadriceps force for knee extension.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Inferior pole of [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Patella.md)
- **Insertion** — [Tibial Tuberosity](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibial Tuberosity.md)
- **Proximal** — Continuous with [Quadriceps Femoris](./anatomyLink/Muscular System/Lower Limb/Quadriceps Femoris.md) tendon
- **Related structures** — Infrapatellar fat pad (Hoffa's fat pad), infrapatellar bursa, [Patella](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Patella.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Patellar tendinopathy ("jumper's knee")** — Overuse injury at inferior pole of patella; pain with loading
- **Patellar tendon rupture** — Disrupts knee extension mechanism; gap below patella + patella alta on X-ray
- **Osgood-Schlatter disease** — Traction apophysitis at tibial tuberosity in adolescents
- **ACL reconstruction** — Bone-patellar tendon-bone (BTB) graft harvested from central third of patellar ligament
- **Hoffa's syndrome** — Impingement of infrapatellar fat pad adjacent to patellar ligament
