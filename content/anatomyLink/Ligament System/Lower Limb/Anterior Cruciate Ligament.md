# Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL)

> The **anterior cruciate ligament (ACL)** runs from the **anterior intercondylar area of the tibia** to the **lateral femoral condyle**, preventing **anterior tibial translation** and rotational instability. It is one of the most commonly injured ligaments in sports.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Anterior intercondylar area of [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md) (between tibial spines)
- **Insertion** — Medial surface of **lateral femoral condyle** ([Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md))
- **Two bundles** — Anteromedial (AM, taut in flexion) + Posterolateral (PL, taut in extension)
- **Related structures** — [Posterior Cruciate Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Posterior Cruciate Ligament.md), [Medial Collateral Ligament (Knee)](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Lower Limb/Medial Collateral Ligament (Knee).md), lateral meniscus, [Femur](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Femur.md), [Tibia](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Tibia.md)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **ACL tear** — Common in cutting sports (soccer, basketball, skiing); "pop" + hemarthrosis + instability
- **Pivot shift test** — Lateral tibial plateau subluxes anteriorly in extension, reduces in flexion
- **Anterior drawer test** — Anterior tibial translation at 90° flexion
- **Lachman test** — Most sensitive; anterior tibial translation at 20–30° flexion
- **ACL reconstruction** — Patellar tendon (BTB), hamstring, or quadriceps tendon graft; no reliable healing when torn
- **Unhappy triad** — ACL + MCL + medial meniscus (O'Donoghue); from valgus-rotation force
