# Iliolumbar Ligament

> The **iliolumbar ligament** is a strong fan-shaped ligament connecting the transverse processes of **L4–L5** to the **iliac crest**. It stabilizes the lumbosacral junction and limits side-bending and rotation.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Transverse processes of L4 and L5 ([Transverse Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Transverse Process.md), [Lumbar Vertebrae](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Lumbar Vertebrae.md))
- **Insertion** — Iliac crest (anterior and posterior bands)
- **Related structures** — [Sacrum](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Spine/Sacrum.md), [Hip Bone](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Hip Bone.md), [Iliac Crest](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Lower Limb/Iliac Crest.md), sacroiliac joint

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Low back pain** — Iliolumbar ligament strain is a common source of mechanical low back pain
- **Lumbosacral instability** — Resists excessive motion at the L5–S1 junction
- **Iliac crest bone graft** — Harvest site can damage iliolumbar ligament → postoperative pain
- **Referred pain** — Can refer pain to groin, testis, or posterior thigh
