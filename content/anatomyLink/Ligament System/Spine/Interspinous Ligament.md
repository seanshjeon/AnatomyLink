# Interspinous Ligament

> The **interspinous ligaments** are thin fibrous bands connecting adjacent **spinous processes** throughout the spine. They blend anteriorly with the [Ligamentum Flavum](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Ligamentum-Flavum.md) and posteriorly with the [Supraspinous Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Supraspinous-Ligament.md).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Attachment** — Between adjacent spinous processes (root to tip)
- **Levels** — T1 to L5 (poorly developed in cervical spine)
- **Adjacent structures** — [Ligamentum Flavum](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Ligamentum-Flavum.md) (anterior), [Supraspinous Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Supraspinous-Ligament.md) (posterior)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Flexion injuries** — Rupture in hyperflexion (e.g., posterior ligamentous complex injury)
- **Lumbar puncture landmark** — Needle passes through supraspinous → interspinous → ligamentum flavum → epidural → dural space
- **Posterior ligamentous complex (PLC)** — Interspinous + supraspinous + facet capsules = PLC; injury here = spinal instability
