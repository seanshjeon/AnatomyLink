# Supraspinous Ligament

> The **supraspinous ligament** is a strong fibrous cord running along the **tips of spinous processes** from C7 to the sacrum. It is continuous superiorly with the [Ligamentum Nuchae](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Ligamentum-Nuchae.md) in the cervical spine.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Attachment** — Tips of spinous processes (C7 → sacrum)
- **Superior continuation** — [Ligamentum Nuchae](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Ligamentum-Nuchae.md) (C7 and above)
- **Adjacent** — [Interspinous Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Spine/Interspinous-Ligament.md) (deep to it between each level)
- **Part of** — Posterior Ligamentous Complex (PLC)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Lumbar puncture** — First ligament pierced when needle enters intervertebral space
- **Flexion injury** — Ruptures in severe hyperflexion/distraction injuries (seatbelt fractures)
- **Posterior instability** — PLC failure (including SSL) indicates need for posterior spinal fusion
