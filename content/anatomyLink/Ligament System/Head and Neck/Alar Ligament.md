# Alar Ligament

> The **alar ligaments** ("check ligaments") are two strong bands connecting the **dens (odontoid)** to the **occipital condyles** bilaterally. They limit **axial rotation and lateral flexion** of the head at the atlantoaxial joint.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Posterolateral aspect of dens ([[Cervical Vertebrae]] C2)
- **Insertion** — Medial surface of occipital condyles ([[Skull]])
- **Limits** — Contralateral rotation (e.g., left alar taut in right head rotation) + ipsilateral lateral flexion
- **Related** — [[Transverse Atlantal Ligament]], apical ligament, atlantoaxial joint

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Whiplash injury** — Alar ligaments can stretch or tear in high-speed flexion-extension; source of chronic cervical pain
- **Atlantoaxial rotatory fixation** — Alar ligament involvement in pediatric atlantoaxial rotatory subluxation
- **High cervical instability** — Combined alar + TAL injury = severe upper cervical instability
- **MRI** — Alar ligament tears visible on high-resolution MRI; controversial in medicolegal settings
