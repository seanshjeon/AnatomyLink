# Temporomandibular Ligament

> The **temporomandibular ligament** (lateral ligament of TMJ) is the primary intrinsic ligament of the **temporomandibular joint (TMJ)**, reinforcing the lateral capsule. It limits **posterior and inferior mandibular displacement** during jaw opening.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — Articular tubercle of temporal bone ([[Skull]])
- **Insertion** — Posterior surface of mandibular neck ([[Mandible]])
- **TMJ accessory ligaments** — Stylomandibular + sphenomandibular ligaments (extracapsular)
- **Related structures** — TMJ disc (articular disc), condylar process, [[Masseter]]

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **TMJ dysfunction (TMD)** — Ligament laxity contributes to disc displacement and TMJ pain
- **TMJ dislocation** — Condyle moves anterior to articular eminence; ligament overstretched → "locked open jaw"
- **Bruxism** — Excessive clenching loads TMJ capsule + ligament
- **Referred pain** — TMJ dysfunction can refer pain to temple, ear, and neck
- **Arthroscopy** — TMJ arthroscopy performed through lateral approach near TMJ ligament
