# Annular Ligament

> The **annular ligament** is a strong ring-shaped band that encircles the **radial head**, holding it against the radial notch of the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md) and allowing the radius to rotate freely during pronation/supination.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Attachment** — Both ends attach to the anterior and posterior margins of the radial notch of the [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md)
- **Wraps around** — [Radial Head](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Radial-Head.md)
- **Related structures** — [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Radius.md), [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md), radiocapitellar joint, proximal radioulnar joint

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Nursemaid's elbow (radial head subluxation)** — Annular ligament slips over radial head in children < 5 years when arm is pulled; treated by supination + flexion maneuver
- **Radial head fracture** — May tear the annular ligament; assess stability after ORIF
- **Posterior interosseous nerve (PIN)** — Deep branch of [Radial Nerve](./anatomyLink/Nervous-System/Upper-Limb-Nerves/Radial-Nerve.md) passes through the arcade of Frohse near the annular ligament
