# Lateral Collateral Ligament (Elbow)

> The **lateral collateral ligament complex** of the elbow provides varus stress resistance and rotatory stability. The most important component is the **lateral ulnar collateral ligament (LUCL)**, which prevents posterolateral rotatory instability (PLRI).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Lateral Epicondyle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Lateral-Epicondyle.md) of [Humerus](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Humerus.md)
- **Components** — Radial collateral ligament (RCL), lateral ulnar collateral ligament (LUCL), annular ligament ([Annular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Upper-Limb/Annular-Ligament.md))
- **LUCL insertion** — Supinator crest of [Ulna](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Ulna.md)
- **Counterpart** — [Medial Collateral Ligament](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Medial-Collateral-Ligament.md) (medial side)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **PLRI (posterolateral rotatory instability)** — LUCL insufficiency; elbow subluxes posterolaterally with supination + extension
- **Lateral pivot shift test** — Reproduces PLRI
- **Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)** — Extensor carpi radialis brevis origin at lateral epicondyle; LCL complex nearby
- **Elbow dislocation** — LCL complex usually fails first in posterior elbow dislocation (Horii circle)
