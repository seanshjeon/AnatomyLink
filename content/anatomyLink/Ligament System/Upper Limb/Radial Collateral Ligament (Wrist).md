# Radial Collateral Ligament (Wrist)

> The **radial collateral ligament (RCL)** of the wrist extends from the radial styloid to the scaphoid and trapezium, limiting **ulnar deviation** and providing radial-side stability to the radiocarpal joint.

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Styloid Process of Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Styloid Process of Radius.md)
- **Insertion** — [Carpals](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Carpals.md) (scaphoid, trapezium)
- **Counterpart** — [Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist)](./anatomyLink/Ligament System/Upper Limb/Ulnar Collateral Ligament (Wrist).md)
- **Related structures** — [Radius](./anatomyLink/Skeletal System/Upper Limb/Radius.md), radiocarpal joint, scaphoid

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **Radial styloid fracture** — May avulse the RCL origin
- **De Quervain's tenosynovitis** — Radial-sided wrist pain (EPB/APL tendons); RCL region proximity causes confusion
- **Wrist ligament injury** — RCL sprain from radial wrist trauma
