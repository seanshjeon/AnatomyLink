# Coracoclavicular Ligament

> The **coracoclavicular (CC) ligament** suspends the scapula from the clavicle, providing **vertical stability** to the AC joint. It has two components: the **conoid** (medial, cone-shaped) and **trapezoid** (lateral, quadrilateral).

## 🔗 Key Connections
- **Origin** — [Coracoid Process](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Coracoid-Process.md) of [Scapula](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Scapula.md)
- **Insertion** — Inferior surface of distal [Clavicle](./anatomyLink/Skeletal-System/Upper-Limb/Clavicle.md)
- **Two parts** — Conoid (posteromedial) + Trapezoid (anterolateral)
- **AC stabilizer** — [Acromioclavicular Ligament](./anatomyLink/Ligament-System/Upper-Limb/Acromioclavicular-Ligament.md) (horizontal component)

## 🏥 Clinical Significance
- **AC separation Grade III+** — CC ligament rupture leads to inferior scapular displacement and "step deformity"
- **Coracoclavicular distance** — X-ray measurement (normal < 13 mm); increased in Grade III+
- **AC reconstruction** — CC ligament reconstructed with tendon graft (e.g., gracilis) in high-grade separations
- **Piano key sign** — Distal clavicle springs back up when pushed down
