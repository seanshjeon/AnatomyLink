# 🫀 AnatomyLink

## Understanding the Human Body Through Connected Knowledge

Welcome to **AnatomyLink** — an interactive, interconnected reference guide to human anatomy. Unlike traditional anatomy textbooks that present information in isolation, AnatomyLink leverages Obsidian's powerful linking system to show how every bone, muscle, nerve, and organ connects to and influences every other structure.

Explore the human body as an integrated system. Click any anatomical structure to discover its neighbors, its blood supply, its nerve innervation, and its functional relationships.

---

## 🗂️ Explore by System

AnatomyLink organizes the human body into 11 major physiological systems. Choose a system below to begin exploring.

### 🦴 Structural Support Systems

- **[[Skeletal System]]** — Bones, vertebrae, joints, and skeletal architecture
  - Lower Limb, Skull Region, Spine, Thorax, Upper Limb
- **[[Muscular System]]** — Muscles, tendons, and the fascial system  
  - Head and Neck, Lower Limb, Trunk, Upper Limb
- **[[Ligament System]]** — Ligaments and connective tissue structures
  - Head and Neck, Lower Limb, Spine, Upper Limb

### 🧠 Control & Sensory Systems

- **[[Nervous System]]** — Brain, spinal cord, nerves, and neural pathways
  - Central Nervous System, Peripheral Nervous System, Autonomic Nervous System
  - Detailed regional sections: Head/Neck, Upper Limb, Lower Limb Nerves
  - Specialized content: Pathways, Neuro System (Lippincott)
- **[[Endocrine System]]** — Hormone-secreting glands and endocrine organs
  - Pituitary, Thyroid, Parathyroid, Adrenal, Pineal Glands

### 🫀 Circulation & Gas Exchange Systems

- **[[Circulatory System]]** — Heart, blood vessels, and blood flow
  - Arteries, Veins, Heart, Pulmonary Circulation, Systemic Circulation
- **[[Respiratory System]]** — Lungs, airways, and gas exchange
  - Trachea, Bronchi, Lungs, Pleura, Diaphragm
- **[[Lymphatic System]]** — Lymph circulation and immune defense
  - Lymph Nodes, Spleen, Thymus, Tonsils

### 🔄 Processing & Elimination Systems

- **[[Digestive System]]** — Entire alimentary tract from mouth to anus
  - Upper GI, Lower GI, Accessory Organs, Peritoneum
- **[[Urinary System]]** — Kidneys, bladder, and waste elimination
  - Kidney, Ureter, Urinary Bladder, Urethra
- **[[Reproductive System]]** — Reproductive organs and hormone regulation
  - Female, Male reproductive structures

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Quick Start (Web View)
1. Select a body system from the list above
2. Click on any structure to learn more
3. Follow links to explore related anatomy
4. Use the explorer sidebar for hierarchical browsing

### Full Experience (Obsidian)
For the best experience with full linking and graph visualization:
1. [Download Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) (Free desktop app)
2. Clone or download this AnatomyLink vault
3. Open it as a local vault in Obsidian
4. Enable Graph View to visualize the entire anatomy network
5. Use quick search (`Ctrl/Cmd + K`) to find structures instantly
6. Explore backlinks to see what references each structure

## 📚 How to Use AnatomyLink

### 1️⃣ System-Based Learning
Choose a system above to deeply understand one functional area of the body (e.g., learn all muscles in the upper limb, all bones in the thorax).

### 2️⃣ Structure-Based Learning
Search for a specific structure (bone, muscle, organ) and follow its connections throughout the body.

### 3️⃣ Relationship-Based Learning
Ask anatomical questions like:
- "What muscles attach to this bone?"
- "What nerves innervate this muscle?"
- "What organs are in the abdominal cavity?"
- Follow the links to answer these questions.

### 4️⃣ Visual Exploration
Use Obsidian's Graph View to see the entire anatomical network and explore based on visual connections.

## 💡 Tips & Tricks

**🔍 Quick Search**
- Press `Ctrl+K` (Windows) or `Cmd+K` (Mac) to instantly find any structure

**🔗 Following Links**
- Click blue links to navigate to related structures
- Check the **Backlinks** section to see what references each structure
- Use breadcrumbs to understand hierarchy and relationships

**📊 Graph Visualization**
- In Obsidian, open **Graph View** to see the network of all structures
- Hover over nodes to highlight connected structures
- Click nodes to navigate while keeping the graph open
- Notice which structures are "hubs" (highly connected)

**🎯 Thematic Browsing**
- Browse by anatomical region (head, neck, upper limb, trunk, lower limb)
- Explore by function (circulation, movement, protection, sensation)
- Follow regional pages to learn all structures in one area at once
